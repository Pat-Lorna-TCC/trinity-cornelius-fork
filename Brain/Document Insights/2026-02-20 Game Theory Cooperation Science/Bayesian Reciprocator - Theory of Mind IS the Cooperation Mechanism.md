## Core Framework

The Bayesian Reciprocator model proposes that theory of mind - inferring others' hidden mental states and latent utility functions - is the missing ingredient in evolutionary models of cooperation. Classical strategies like tit-for-tat are "inflexible automata" that respond to observed behavior without modeling why an agent behaves as it does. The Bayesian Reciprocator cooperates conditionally based on inferred intent, not just observed behavior, enabling both direct and indirect reciprocity from a single unified mechanism.

---

## How It Works

The Bayesian Reciprocator:
1. **Infers opponents' latent utility functions** - Are they cooperators or defectors by disposition?
2. **Updates beliefs Bayesianly** based on observed behavior
3. **Cooperates conditionally** based on inferred reciprocity probability, not just what they did
4. **Covers both direct and indirect reciprocity** from the same mechanism - a unification no prior model achieved
5. **Forgives errors** - if someone defected due to noise rather than intent, it recognizes this and continues cooperation

## Why This Is Significant

Tit-for-tat was always too rigid: it punishes genuine mistakes the same as strategic defection. The Bayesian Reciprocator solves this because it models intent. If someone defected because of a stochastic mistake, the Bayesian agent detects this and continues cooperation. If someone defected strategically, it responds with appropriate retaliation.

This implies that **theory of mind is not just a social nicety - it is functionally essential for robust cooperation at scale**. Cognitive sophistication and the ability to model others' minds did not evolve for social intelligence as a side effect; they evolved because they directly solve the cooperation problem.

**For AI alignment**: Building AI agents with theory of mind may be the path to genuinely cooperative AI, not value alignment alone.
