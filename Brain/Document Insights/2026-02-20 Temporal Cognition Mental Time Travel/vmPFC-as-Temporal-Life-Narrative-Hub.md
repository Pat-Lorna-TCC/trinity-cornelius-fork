# vmPFC as Temporal Life Narrative Hub

**Source**: "The Role of vmPFC in Accessing the Temporality of Life Events for Mental Time Travel" - Scientific Reports (Nature) - April 24, 2025 - DOI: 10.1038/s41598-025-97861-z; also bioRxiv preprint January 2026 - DOI: 10.64898/2026.01.20.700521

## Core Insight

The ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC) is not merely activated during mental time travel - it is causally required for constructing a **coherent temporal life narrative**. Patients with vmPFC lesions systematically misplace the timing of life events, associating typical experiences with earlier-than-normal ages across both past memory and future planning tasks. The vmPFC is the brain's temporal orientation system for the self.

Additionally, within the vmPFC there is a **valence asymmetry**: the vmPFC is specifically associated with **positive** experiences and simulations (both past and future), while the dmPFC handles negative affect. This means our capacity to construct positive futures is more neurologically fragile than our capacity to simulate negative ones.

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Causal lesion study (2025 Scientific Reports) + fMRI valence study (2026 bioRxiv preprint)
**Key Results**:
- vmPFC patients showed systematic "temporal anticipation" - associating typical life events with earlier-than-normal ages
- Effect appeared across past MTT tasks, future MTT tasks, AND a separate timeline placement task - confirming it's not domain-specific
- Control patients with lesions elsewhere did not show this effect
- fMRI study confirmed vmPFC valence asymmetry: stronger activation for positive than negative simulations across both past and future

**Two Interpretations of Temporal Compression**:
1. vmPFC patients have a **shortened time perspective** with reduced future representation, causing events to cluster toward the present
2. A **compressed mental timeline** with the future on the "right side" analogically leads to systematic leftward bias

**Neural Context**:
- vmPFC shows asymmetric gradient organization favoring episodic autobiographical memory over EFT, consistent with lesion data
- vmPFC is the hub for "activating temporal schemata" - the cognitive frames that let us locate ourselves and events in time
