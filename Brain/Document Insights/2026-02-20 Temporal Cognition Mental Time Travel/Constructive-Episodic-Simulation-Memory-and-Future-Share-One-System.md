# Constructive Episodic Simulation - Memory and Future Share One System

## Core Framework

Memory and future simulation are not separate cognitive faculties but expressions of a single **constructive episodic simulation system**. The brain does not "record" the past or "predict" the future - it constantly recombines experiential fragments into adaptive representations. This is why imagining the future and remembering the past activate nearly identical neural networks.

Mahr & Schacter (2024) introduced a crucial refinement: the **temporal component** of mental time travel (the sense of moving forward or backward in subjective time) is separable from the **episodic retrieval and recombination processes** that generate the content of both memories and imagined futures.

---

## Evidence Base

**Neural Evidence:**
- Shared core network for both episodic autobiographical memory (EAM) and episodic future thinking (EFT): bilateral hippocampus/parahippocampus, mPFC, PCC, angular gyrus, aMTG, and left SFG
- EFT engages hippocampal regions *more strongly* than EAM, reflecting additional cognitive demands for recombining past details into novel scenarios
- ALE meta-analysis (Teghil, Wiener & Boccia, 2025, Neuropsychology Review) confirmed cortical gradient organization, not segregated systems

**Dissociation Evidence:**
- Patients with hippocampal amnesia can sometimes simulate future events even when they cannot remember the past - demonstrating that the temporal "vector" of mental time travel can dissociate from the episodic content machinery
- This dissociation is theoretically important: it means the sense of "being in the future" is not the same as the ability to construct rich episodic futures

**Vulnerability Evidence (Schacter & Thakral, 2024):**
- The same neural processes that make future simulation flexible and adaptive also create systematic vulnerabilities to memory distortion (false memories)
- Constructive flexibility = adaptive power AND liability

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Widely accepted - the constructive episodic simulation hypothesis has been a dominant framework since Schacter's original proposals
**Evidence Base**: Convergent fMRI, lesion studies, behavioral experiments, meta-analyses
**Extension**: The 2024-2025 work adds: (1) the temporal/episodic dissociation, (2) affective dimensions of future simulation, (3) conscious experience aspects
