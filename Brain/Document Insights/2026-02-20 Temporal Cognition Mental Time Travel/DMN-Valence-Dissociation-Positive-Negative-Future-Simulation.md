# DMN Valence Dissociation - Positive and Negative Future Simulations Have Distinct Neural Hardware

**Source**: "Shared and Distinct Default Mode Systems in Mental Time Travel and Affective Experience over Time" - bioRxiv preprint - January 21, 2026 - DOI: 10.64898/2026.01.20.700521

## Core Insight

The Default Mode Network is NOT a monolithic "mental time travel module" - it contains functionally specific subregions with opposite emotional orientations:
- **vmPFC** (ventromedial PFC): specifically processes **positive** experiences and simulations across BOTH past and future temporal domains
- **dmPFC** (dorsomedial PFC): specifically processes **negative** affect across BOTH temporal domains

This means imagining a positive future and imagining a negative future recruit different neural hardware, even though both involve the DMN and both involve future simulation.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: fMRI study directly evaluating shared and distinct neural systems in episodic memory retrieval (EMR) vs. episodic future thinking (EFT), and their interaction with affective experience
**Key Results**:
- DMN core components (mPFC, PCC, MTL) confirmed as central to BOTH EMR and EFT
- Critical new finding: functional dissociation along valence within the mPFC
  - vmPFC: stronger association with positive experiences (both past and future)
  - dmPFC: consistently engaged during negative affect processing (both past and future)
- Hippocampus showed differential processing of valence AND arousal across temporal domains

---

## Why This Is Clinically Significant

This finding has immediate implications for understanding depression:
- Depression involves vmPFC hypoactivation + dmPFC hyperactivation
- This predicts EXACTLY the pattern observed clinically: impaired positive future simulation, intact or enhanced negative future simulation
- Antidepressants that restore vmPFC function should specifically restore positive future simulation capacity
- The anhedonia-specific impairment in positive future simulation (Finding 10 / separate note) now has a neural substrate

**Asymmetric Resilience**:
- Negative future simulations (dmPFC) are neurologically more robust than positive ones (vmPFC)
- This creates an asymmetric default: in low-resource states (depression, fatigue, stress), negative futures are more accessible than positive ones
- This may be adaptive (negative scenarios warrant more attention in threatening environments) but maladaptive in clinical depression
