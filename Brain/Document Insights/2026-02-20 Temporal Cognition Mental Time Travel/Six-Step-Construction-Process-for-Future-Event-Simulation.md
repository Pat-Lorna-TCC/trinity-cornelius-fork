## Core Framework

Based on 2024-2025 research, the brain constructs future event simulations through at least **six distinct, dissociable components**. Each component has identifiable neural substrates, and each can be disrupted independently, producing specific impairments in future thinking.

---

## The Six Steps

**Step 1: Temporal Orientation (vmPFC)**
- Activating a temporal schema that orients the self toward a specific future time period
- "Locating" the simulation in time - establishing WHEN in the future this scenario will occur
- vmPFC lesion impairment: patients lose the ability to correctly locate themselves or events in time → systematic temporal compression

**Step 2: Episodic Retrieval (hippocampus, PCC, MTL)**
- Retrieving relevant fragments from autobiographical past experiences
- These fragments are the "raw materials" for constructing the future scenario
- The constructive process uses past as building blocks, not templates

**Step 3: Recombination (hippocampus, mPFC)**
- Flexibly combining retrieved elements into novel, coherent scenarios that did not previously exist
- This step makes future simulation creative: it's not replaying the past but reassembling it
- The same process that generates false memories (creative recombination is the dual-use mechanism)

**Step 4: Scene Construction (parahippocampal cortex, hippocampus)**
- Building a spatially and contextually coherent "mental scene" for the future event
- Creating the sensorimotor context: what it will look, sound, feel like
- Disruption: some patients can describe future events abstractly but cannot construct vivid scenes

**Step 5: Affective Simulation (vmPFC for positive, dmPFC for negative)**
- Generating anticipatory emotional responses to the imagined scenario
- POSITIVE future simulations: vmPFC (ventromedial PFC)
- NEGATIVE future simulations: dmPFC (dorsomedial PFC)
- This valence dissociation is new: positive and negative futures have distinct neural hardware

**Step 6: Self-Projection (posterior cingulate, medial parietal)**
- Placing the SELF as an experiencer within the imagined future scene
- The "first-person" quality of future imagination vs. third-person description
- Connects to DMN's self-referential function

---

## Time Cells as Temporal Scaffolding

Hippocampal time cells provide the temporal scaffolding across all steps - encoding the sequential structure that gives the simulation its "placement in time" quality. Without time cell support, future scenarios would be temporally unanchored (consistent with vmPFC orientation lesion data: the orientation step requires intact temporal scaffolding).
