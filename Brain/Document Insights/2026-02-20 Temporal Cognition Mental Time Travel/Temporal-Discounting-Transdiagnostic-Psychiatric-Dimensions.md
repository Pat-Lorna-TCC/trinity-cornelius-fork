# Temporal Discounting Links Two Transdiagnostic Psychiatric Dimensions

**Source**: "Association of Temporal Discounting with Transdiagnostic Symptom Dimensions" - npj Mental Health Research (Nature) - 2024 - N=731
**Document Type**: Large-Scale Clinical Study (Peer-Reviewed)

## Core Insight

In a large (N=731) clinical study, temporal discounting (the tendency to prefer immediate over future rewards) was significantly associated with **two distinct transdiagnostic symptom dimensions**:
1. **Anxious-depression** dimension
2. **Inattention-impulsivity-overactivity** dimension

These two dimensions span multiple psychiatric diagnoses (they cut across the traditional diagnostic categories), suggesting temporal discounting may be a transdiagnostic biomarker or mechanism - not specific to one disorder but underlying a broad range of psychopathology through two different pathways.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Large clinical sample (N=731), transdiagnostic dimensional assessment
**Key Results**:
- Temporal discounting associated with anxious-depression dimension AND inattention-impulsivity dimension
- Hyperbolic and exponential models outperformed quasi-hyperbolic models in predictive accuracy
- Hyperbolic discounting yielded slightly better accuracy than exponential
- **Magnitude effect**: TD was lower (better long-term choice) with higher reward magnitudes, especially in those scoring higher on inattention-impulsivity - suggesting these individuals are specifically sensitive to magnitude framing

**Neural Basis Update (Supporting Evidence)**:
- Dopaminergic system remains central: dopamine agonists produce duration overestimation and antagonists produce underestimation
- This connects temporal perception directly to the reward system
- Two pathways to high temporal discounting:
  1. Through depressive/anxious states (reduced capacity to experience anticipated future rewards as vivid)
  2. Through attentional/impulsive traits (reduced cognitive engagement with future consequences)

---

## Why Two Pathways Matter

The two transdiagnostic dimensions represent different mechanisms:
- **Anxious-depression pathway**: Future feels threatening or empty (consistent with anhedonia finding) - temporal discounting results from impaired positive future simulation
- **Inattention-impulsivity pathway**: Future fails to capture attention even when it could be rewarding - temporal discounting results from attentional capture by immediate stimuli

This distinction has intervention implications: EFT interventions targeting vivid positive future simulation would address the anxious-depression pathway, while attention training or simplification interventions would address the impulsivity pathway.
