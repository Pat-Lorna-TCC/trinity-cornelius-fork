# Hippocampal Time Cells Form a Cognitive Map of Temporal Experience

## Core Insight

The hippocampus contains specialized "time cells" - CA1 neurons that fire at specific moments within a temporal sequence - that function as a **cognitive map of time**, analogous to how place cells form a cognitive map of space. These time cells are not learned but are intrinsic to hippocampal organization, and they **dynamically adapt** their representations based on whether the cognitive task demands generalization across contexts or discrimination between them.

Additionally, hippocampal **theta sequences** - rhythmic sweeps of activity at 6-12 Hz - generate mental time travel *during decision-making itself*, not just during memory encoding. At decision points, the hippocampus sweeps forward to simulate possible futures before a choice is made.

## Evidence

**Time Cell Dynamics (University of Utah, 2025 preprint):**
- Two-photon calcium imaging in mice during learning
- Time cells present from the outset of training - intrinsic organizational feature, not learned
- At key curriculum moments, time cell representations adapted to reflect whether mice were generalizing across contexts or discriminating between them
- Parallel to place cell behavior in spatial learning: time cells form the temporal equivalent of a spatial map

**Time Cells vs. Place Cells (Neuron, 2024):**
- Time cells and place cells are largely distinct neuronal populations in the hippocampus
- They coexist in the same task phase during virtual reality tasks
- The two systems can simultaneously encode "where" and "when" aspects of experience

**Theta Sequences and Decision-Making (Redish, Hippocampus, 2025):**
- Hippocampal theta sequences sweep forward to goals during deliberation
- During vicarious trial and error (VTE) - pausing at decision points - the sweeps extend further ahead, representing more extensive simulation of future paths
- These sequences can be pharmacologically manipulated: clonidine narrows the sweep; cannabinoids disrupt sequences and increase VTE behaviors
- This is the neural substrate of "imagining possible futures" at decision points

**Time Cell Generation Mechanism:**
- Ramping cells in the lateral entorhinal cortex provide input to hippocampal CA1
- Competitive hippocampal networks convert ramping inputs into orthogonal temporal representations
- Architecture enables both forward sequences AND reverse replay during memory consolidation
