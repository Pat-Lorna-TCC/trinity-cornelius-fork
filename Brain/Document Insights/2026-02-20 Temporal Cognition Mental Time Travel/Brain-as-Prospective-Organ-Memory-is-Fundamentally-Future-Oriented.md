## Core Framework

The brain's mnemonic system is not a record-keeping device but a **prospective organ** - it is fundamentally designed for adaptive future orientation, not archival accuracy. Memory is adaptively biased toward flexible, future-oriented construction rather than veridical record.

This extends to collective cognition: "transpersonal mental time travel" involves shared temporal representations that support "projection and coordination of futures across individuals and generations." Mental time travel is not only individual but a foundation of **human language, cultural evolution, and social coordination**.

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging consensus - the "memory for the future" framing is gaining traction but remains contested by those who emphasize memory's historical functions
**Evidence Base**: Multidisciplinary (psychology, linguistics, cognitive neuroscience, anthropology) - Cambridge review synthesizes across disciplines
**Alternative View**: Traditional memory science treats memory as primarily record-keeping with adaptive forgetting; the prospective organ view inverts this priority

---

## Theoretical Implications

**Individual Level**:
- Every act of remembering is preparation for future scenarios
- Memory distortions (false memories, reconstructive errors) are features, not bugs: they prioritize future usefulness over historical accuracy
- "We don't remember events, we remember what we might need next time"

**Collective Level - Transpersonal MTT**:
- Humans can coordinate around shared future projections (social contracts, cultural narratives, institutional plans)
- Language evolved partly to enable transpersonal temporal projection - communicating about non-present times
- Cultural artifacts (calendars, histories, prophecies) are physical extensions of transpersonal MTT

**Evolutionary Interpretation**:
- Natural selection favored brains that use the past to navigate the future, not brains that accurately record the past
- Memory accuracy has reproductive value only insofar as it improves future outcomes
