# Dopamine Directly Modulates Temporal Perception

## Core Insight

The dopaminergic system has a direct and causal relationship with temporal perception: dopamine agonists produce **duration overestimation** (time feels to pass more slowly, durations feel longer) and dopamine antagonists produce **duration underestimation** (time feels to pass faster, durations feel shorter). This is confirmed pharmacologically, establishing dopamine not just as a reward signal but as a fundamental **time-coding neurotransmitter**.

The supplementary motor area (SMA) is implicated in subjective duration perception alongside the dopaminergic striatum - suggesting timing involves both motor-predictive systems and reward systems.

---

## Evidence

**Pharmacological Evidence**:
- Dopamine agonists: duration overestimation (things feel longer than they are)
- Dopamine antagonists: duration underestimation (things feel shorter than they are)
- This dose-dependent bidirectional relationship establishes causal direction

**Neural Systems**:
- Striatum/dopaminergic system: temporal discounting and reward-temporal integration
- Supplementary Motor Area (SMA): motor timing and subjective duration
- The SMA connection suggests the motor system's predictive timing is dopaminergically modulated

**Transdiagnostic Implications**:
- Disorders with dopamine dysregulation (ADHD, schizophrenia, addiction, Parkinson's) characteristically involve time perception distortions
- This may be a mechanism, not merely a correlation

## Why This Matters

This finding closes a theoretical loop in the vault:
- Mental discounting might be caused by Dopamine cycles (vault hypothesis) → Confirmed: dopamine literally controls how long the future "feels" away
- High dopamine states make durations feel longer → future rewards feel more temporally distant → present rewards seem relatively more attractive
- Low dopamine states (depression, anhedonia) make durations feel shorter subjectively but make future rewards seem less vivid → different pathway to temporal discounting

The connection is: **dopamine affects BOTH what rewards feel like AND how far away in time they feel** - a dual mechanism for temporal discounting.
