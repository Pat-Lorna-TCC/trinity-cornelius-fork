# Anhedonia Drives Positive Future Simulation Deficit - Not Depression Per Se

**Source**: (1) "Episodic Future Thinking and Psychopathology: A Focus on Depression and Suicide Risk" - ScienceDirect - 2024; (2) Bogaert et al. - "The Potential of FEST to Decrease Anhedonia" - Applied Psychology: Health and Well-Being - 2024; (3) 2025 ScienceDirect review on EFT in psychopathology

## Core Insight

The inability to imagine positive futures in depression is specifically driven by **anhedonia** (the inability to experience pleasure or anticipate reward) - not by the broader syndrome of depression per se. Anhedonia predicts reduced positive future simulation vividness **above and beyond general depressive symptoms**. This creates a therapeutic catch-22: the patients who most need to practice imagining positive futures are neurologically least able to do so.

---

## Evidence

**Key Finding (2024 systematic review)**:
- Depression is associated with reduced **specificity and vividness for positive** future events (not negative or neutral ones)
- Anhedonia - not depression broadly - remained a significant predictor of reduced positive imagery vividness after controlling for other depressive symptoms
- The deficit holds for both EFT AND autobiographical memory of positive events - suggesting a core dysfunction in positive mental simulation broadly

**Quantified Effect Size**:
- Psychiatric diagnosis reduces EFT specificity: meta-analytic g = -0.84 (large effect)
- This is the negative of the intervention effect, confirming both that EFT deficits are large and that clinical populations have significant positive simulation impairment

**FEST - Future Event Specificity Training (2024 RCT)**:
- Specifically designed to address this deficit
- RCT results: reduced anhedonia, changes in transdiagnostic factors, higher likelihood of depression remission
- Mechanism: scaffolded training in generating specific (not vague) positive future scenarios

**The Negative Feedback Loop (2025 ScienceDirect review)**:
- EFT deficits may interfere with the very therapeutic interventions that patients need
- Standard CBT asks patients to imagine future scenarios; patients with anhedonia cannot do this effectively
- This identifies a previously unrecognized barrier to treatment efficacy
