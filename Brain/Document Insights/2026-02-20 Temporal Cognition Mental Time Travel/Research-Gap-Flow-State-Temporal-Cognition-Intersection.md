## The Gap

The temporal cognition research comprehensively maps the neural systems for mental time travel (past and future simulation), but there is **no systematic research on what happens to these temporal systems during flow states**. The vault knows that:
- Flow involves DMN suppression and reduced self-referential processing
- Mental time travel is driven by the DMN (mPFC, PCC, hippocampus)
- Flow creates a "timeless" subjective experience

But the mechanistic link between flow and temporal cognition is unexplored.

---

## Why This Matters

If flow involves DMN suppression, and the DMN implements mental time travel, then flow should systematically reduce mental time travel - particularly the self-projection steps (Steps 1, 5, 6 of the six-step construction process). This would explain the phenomenological "timelessness" of flow from a temporal cognition perspective.

**The untested hypothesis**: Flow achieves altered temporal experience not through enhanced time perception but through **suspension of the self-projection system** - the vmPFC and PCC components of mental time travel that anchor experiences to a specific point in the personal timeline.

In flow, you are not "in the present moment" in a spiritual sense - you are neurologically unable to project yourself into the future or retrieve from the past, because the systems that do this are suppressed.

---

## What We Know (Related Research)

- **Flow research**: DMN deactivates during flow; reduced self-referential processing; PFC deactivation (transient hypofrontality)
- **Temporal cognition**: vmPFC = temporal orientation; PCC = self-projection; hippocampus = scene construction
- **Missing**: No study has measured mental time travel ability, temporal discounting, or future simulation vividness during vs. after flow states

---

## Testable Predictions

If flow suppresses temporal cognition:
1. People in flow should score lower on delay discounting tasks if administered during (or immediately after) flow states
2. Future simulation vividness should be temporarily reduced following flow
3. The phenomenological "timelessness" of flow should correlate with vmPFC and PCC deactivation specifically
4. Expert flow practitioners (athletes, musicians) may show distinctive temporal cognition profiles compared to novices
