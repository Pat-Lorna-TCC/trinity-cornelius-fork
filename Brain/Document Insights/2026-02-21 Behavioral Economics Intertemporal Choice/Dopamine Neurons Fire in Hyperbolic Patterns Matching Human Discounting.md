# Dopamine Neurons Fire in Hyperbolic Patterns Matching Human Discounting

## Core Insight

Dopamine neurons show decreased firing in response to cues predicting longer reward delays, and this firing pattern approximates a hyperbolic discounting function that closely matches human behavioral data. The relationship is bidirectional and receptor-specific: L-DOPA (increasing dopamine) increases discounting rates (more impulsive choices), while D2 receptor blockade reduces discounting - suggesting D1 and D2 pathways have opposing effects on intertemporal choice.

---

## Evidence

**Study 1 - Pharmacological RCT (Journal of Neuroscience, December 2025)**:
**Study Design**: Large-sample, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, preregistered
**Sample Size**: N = 76
**Key Results**:
- Dopamine neurons fire in hyperbolic patterns matching human behavioral discounting
- L-DOPA (dopamine precursor) increased discounting rates - more impulsive choices
- D2 receptor blockade reduced discounting - more patient choices
- Direction of effect depends on baseline dopamine levels and which receptor is targeted
- Individual differences in baseline dopamine explain why dopamine's effect on discounting was previously contradictory across studies

**Study 2 - Delay vs. Effort Discounting (PLOS Biology, 2024)**:
**Key Finding**: Dopamine selectively modulates DELAY discounting. Acetylcholine governs EFFORT discounting. These are neurobiologically dissociable - prior conflation of delay and effort discounting explains years of confusing results.

**Study 3 - Policy Complexity and Dopamine (Journal of Neuroscience, January 2025)**:
**Key Finding**: Dopamine responses to reward outcomes are suppressed by policy complexity - directly connecting dopamine's temporal discounting role to Enke et al.'s cognitive complexity findings. Complex decision environments suppress the very dopaminergic signals that encode temporal value.

## The Unified Dopamine-Discounting Mechanism

This evidence supports a mechanistic account:

1. Dopamine neurons encode the value of future rewards through a hyperbolic firing pattern
2. More dopamine (or D1 activation) makes immediate rewards relatively more attractive (higher impulsivity)
3. D2 pathway activation has the opposing effect
4. Cognitive complexity suppresses dopamine response, further impairing long-horizon planning
5. Individual baseline dopamine levels determine which direction pharmacological manipulation will push
