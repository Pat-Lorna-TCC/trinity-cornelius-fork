# Future Self-Continuity Work Is a Behavioral Economics Intervention

**Source**: Yang et al. (2024) - Frontiers in Psychology; ScienceDirect (2025) - "Modeling Temporal Self-Continuity and Its Association with Temporal Discounting"; Yi Lu - GSA 2024 Conference

## Core Insight

Future self-continuity - the degree to which a person feels connected to and similar to their future self - directly reduces temporal discount rates. Showing participants age-progressed renderings of themselves (increasing perceived self-continuity) decreases their preference for immediate rewards. This means the domain of identity and personal narrative work is not separate from behavioral economics - it IS behavioral economics, targeting the mechanism that drives intertemporal choice.

---

## Evidence

**Yang et al. (2024, Frontiers in Psychology)**:
**Study Design**: Experimental manipulation of future self-continuity using age-progression technology and narrative prompts
**Key Results**:
- High future self-continuity directly reduces temporal discount rates
- Effect is mediated by increased perceived similarity to future self
- Factors beyond mere connectedness (perceived uncertainty about future self, temporal focus) modulate the relationship
**Citation**: Yang et al. (2024). "The Effect of Future Self-Continuity on Intertemporal Decision Making." Frontiers in Psychology. https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1437065

**ScienceDirect (2025)**:
- Temporal discounting is modeled as an increasing tendency to benefit the current self over the distancing future self
- The future self is evaluated with increasing uncertainty as temporal distance grows
- This uncertainty itself (not just distance) contributes to discounting

**Yi Lu, GSA (2024)**:
- Both past and future self-continuity increase naturally with age
- This explains why older adults show less discounting - not biology but identity structure
- Hyperbolic models best fit self-continuity across temporal distances

**Age-Progression Technology Finding**:
- Showing participants photographs of themselves aged by computer software reduces temporal discounting
- Effect is substantial enough to be meaningful for financial decision contexts
- Requires perceiving the aged person as "me" - identity continuity is the mechanism

## The Mechanism

The mechanism is straightforward: if the future beneficiary of my patient behavior is perceived as "me," I will sacrifice for them. If they feel like a stranger, I won't. Age-progression technology, narrative identity exercises, letter-writing to future self, and vivid future-self visualization all increase the perceived "me-ness" of the future self, making patient choices feel like self-investment rather than sacrifice.

This connects directly to the vault's identity hub: identity is not just a belief system with emotional consequences - it is a functional cognitive variable that determines the scope of your temporal self-interest.
