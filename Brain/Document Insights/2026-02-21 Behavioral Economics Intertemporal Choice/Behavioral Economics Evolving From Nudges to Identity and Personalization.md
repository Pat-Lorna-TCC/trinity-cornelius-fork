## Core Framework

The field of behavioral economics is undergoing a generational transition: the first generation (1980s-2010s) focused on cataloging biases and designing one-size-fits-all nudges; the second generation (2015-2025) is discovering that identity-based, personalized, and emotionally salient interventions produce the strongest and most durable results. This transition is driven by both positive evidence (what works better) and negative evidence (nudge effects collapse under scrutiny).

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging consensus - backed by 500+ intervention meta-review and OECD institutional adoption

---

## What the Meta-Review Found

A 2025 meta-review of 500+ behavioral interventions found:
- **Identity-based interventions**: Strongest and most durable results
- **Personalized interventions**: Second strongest (higher signal-to-noise; matched to individual)
- **Emotionally salient interventions**: Third strongest
- **Generic nudges**: Weakest; collapse at scale; publication bias correction removes apparent effect

---

## The Transition in Practice

**Generation 1 (Nudge Theory Era)**:
- Assumption: Universal biases → universal nudge solutions
- Approach: Default enrollment, loss framing, social proof
- Problem: Minimal effects at scale after publication bias correction; voltage drop

**Generation 2 (Identity & Personalization Era)**:
- Assumption: Heterogeneous people → heterogeneous interventions
- Approach: Identity salience, future-self continuity, personalized framing
- Tools: AI-enabled personalization, behavioral typing, individual differences measurement
- Evidence: 500+ intervention review confirms superiority

---

## Institutionalization Trajectory

- SEBI and RBI (India): Initiating nudge units modeled on UK/US approaches
- OECD Behavioural Insights Unit: Now tracking global nudging against standards of autonomy, feedback, and fairness
- Shift from laboratory to field: Representative sampling, large-scale RCTs, preregistration becoming expected

---

## AI Enabling the Transition

Personalized behavioral economics at scale was previously impossible - you couldn't tailor interventions to each individual. AI enables this:
- Real-time behavioral profiling
- Dynamic intervention matching
- Continuous feedback and adjustment
- But also: unprecedented manipulation potential and autonomy concerns

The same infrastructure that enables beneficial personalized behavioral economics enables potentially harmful behavioral manipulation.
