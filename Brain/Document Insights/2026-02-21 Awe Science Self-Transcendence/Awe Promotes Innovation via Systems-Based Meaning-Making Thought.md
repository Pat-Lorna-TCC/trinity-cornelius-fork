# Awe Promotes Innovation via Systems-Based Meaning-Making Thought

## Core Insight

Awe is distinct from adjacent emotions (beauty, interest, admiration, fear) because it specifically orients individuals toward *systems-based, meaning-making thought* - perceiving patterns, connections, and larger wholes rather than isolated details. This cognitive reorientation is the mechanism for awe's relationship to innovation: by inducing wonder and curiosity about the underlying structure of things, awe creates fertile ground for cultural and creative innovation.

---

## Evidence

**Source**: Keltner (2025) conceptual model in *Journal of Consumer Psychology*
**Key Claims**:
- Awe is distinct from beauty (aesthetic appreciation), interest (novelty detection), admiration (witnessing excellence), and fear (threat response)
- Awe orients individuals toward systems-based thought: perceiving larger patterns, connections, underlying structures
- Awe leads to wonder and curiosity - the psychological states that drive creative representation, symbolic expression, ritualization, and object design
- Awe produces ambivalent effects on ambiguity aversion: can DECREASE it (via self-transcendence, openness) or INCREASE it (via small-self, feeling overwhelmed) - context-dependent
- Cultural creations "touched by awe" - designed to evoke it - fare well in consumer choice

## The Awe-Innovation Pathway

```
Environmental Vastness
        ↓
Awe (Vastness + Need for Accommodation)
        ↓
Systems-Based Meaning-Making Thought
        ↓
Wonder + Curiosity (epistemic motivation)
        ↓
Cultural Innovation (representation, symbolism, ritual, design)
```

This pathway explains why humanity's most enduring cultural innovations often arise in contexts of awe: religious architecture designed to overwhelm, great music that generates chills, scientific discoveries that reveal vast underlying order. Awe is not merely a response to innovation - it is a generator of it.

---

## The Ambiguity Aversion Paradox

The finding that awe can both increase AND decrease ambiguity aversion is theoretically significant:

**Awe DECREASES ambiguity aversion when**:
- Self-transcendence pathway dominates
- Person feels connected and part of something larger
- Openness and curiosity are foreground

**Awe INCREASES ambiguity aversion when**:
- Small-self pathway dominates
- Person feels overwhelmed and dwarfed
- Uncertainty feels threatening rather than interesting

This context-dependence has design implications: awe interventions intended to promote innovation should foreground the wonder-curiosity pathway (awe walks, music, expansive art) rather than the threat-overwhelm pathway (extreme natural events, confronting mortality).
