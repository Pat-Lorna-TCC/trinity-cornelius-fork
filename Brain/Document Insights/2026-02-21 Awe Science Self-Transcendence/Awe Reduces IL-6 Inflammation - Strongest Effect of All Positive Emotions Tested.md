# Awe Reduces IL-6 Inflammation - Strongest Effect of All Positive Emotions Tested

**Source**: Stellar JE et al. Awe and IL-6 cytokines study. *Emotion*, 2015 (widely cited through 2025, confirmed and extended by 2025 RCT). Keltner lab, UC Berkeley. https://greatergood.berkeley.edu/article/item/awe_boosts_health

## Core Insight

Among all positive emotions tested - including amusement, compassion, contentment, joy, love, and pride - awe showed the most robust reduction in Interleukin-6 (IL-6), a pro-inflammatory cytokine. This finding positions awe as the most biologically potent positive emotion for immune health. The 2025 RCT (Finding 6) provides clinical confirmation of awe's biological effects through real-world intervention.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Study of 200+ young adults measuring positive emotions and cytokine levels
**Key Results**:
- Awe was the strongest predictor of lower proinflammatory cytokine IL-6 levels
- Effect held across all seven positive emotions tested (amusement, awe, compassion, contentment, joy, love, pride)
- Awe outperformed all other positive emotions in IL-6 reduction

**Why IL-6 Matters**:
- Chronic IL-6 elevation is a biomarker associated with cardiovascular disease
- IL-6 elevation linked to type-2 diabetes, Alzheimer's disease, and depression
- IL-6 is a primary driver of the chronic low-grade inflammation underlying many modern diseases

---

## Neurochemical Profile of Awe

The biological mechanisms involve multiple neurochemical systems:
- **Dopamine**: Reward and motivation circuits activated by awe's mixed-valence surprise
- **Oxytocin**: Social bonding - particularly relevant for the prosocial aftermath of awe
- **Serotonin**: Mood regulation and long-term thinking enhancement

This neurochemical profile explains why awe outperforms other positive emotions for IL-6 reduction: awe engages more neurochemical systems simultaneously than more purely positive emotions, producing broader physiological effects.
