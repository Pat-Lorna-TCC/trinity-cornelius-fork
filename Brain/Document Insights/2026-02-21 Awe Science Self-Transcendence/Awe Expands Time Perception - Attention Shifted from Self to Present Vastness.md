# Awe Expands Time Perception - Attention Shifted from Self to Present Vastness

**Source**: Rudd M, Vohs KD, Aaker J. "Awe Expands People's Perception of Time, Alters Decision Making, and Enhances Well-Being." *Psychological Science*, 2012 (foundational, widely cited through 2025). DOI: 10.1177/0956797612438731. Confirmed and extended in 2024-2025 awe literature.

## Core Insight

Awe significantly expands subjective time perception - people experiencing awe report having more time available and feel less impatient. This occurs because awe shifts attention from self-referential rumination (which creates a sense of time scarcity) to present-moment absorption in the vast stimulus. The time-expansion effect of awe has downstream consequences for decision quality, well-being, and preference for experiential over material goods.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Multiple experiments on awe and time perception
**Key Results**:
- Awe experience produces significant expansion in perceived time availability
- Awe-induced time expansion led to greater preference for *experiential* over material goods
- Awe increased willingness to volunteer time
- Awe enhanced overall well-being ratings

**Mechanism**: Attention pulled into the present moment (absorbed by vastness) removes the self-referential future-orientation and "time urgency" that normally compress perceived time.

---

## The Mechanism: Self-Reference and Time Scarcity

The subjective sense of "not having enough time" is partly a consequence of self-referential processing: thinking about what one should be doing, what one has done, and what remains. DMN activity is temporally extended - it projects into past and future. When awe suppresses the DMN, temporal projection reduces, and the present moment expands.

This is structurally identical to the Buddhist insight that suffering (including time pressure) arises from self-referential conceptualization. Awe produces a secular, immediate version of the Buddhist "being fully present" that comes from stopping self-referential thought.

---

## Decision-Making Implications

Time expansion from awe alters decision-making in predictable ways:
- **Reduced impatience**: Less preference for smaller-sooner vs. larger-later options (reduced temporal discounting)
- **Greater generosity**: More willing to volunteer time (time feels abundant rather than scarce)
- **Experiential preference**: Prefer experiences over possessions (consistent with present-moment orientation)

This connects to the awe-as-System-2-override insight: when time feels less scarce, there is less pressure to rely on heuristic (System 1) shortcuts.
