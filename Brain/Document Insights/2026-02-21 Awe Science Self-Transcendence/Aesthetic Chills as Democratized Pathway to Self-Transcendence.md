# Aesthetic Chills as Democratized Pathway to Self-Transcendence

**Source**: Christov-Moore L, Schoeller F, Lynch C, Sacchet M, Reggente N. "Self-transcendence accompanies aesthetic chills." *PLOS Mental Health*, 1(5), e0000125, October 2024. DOI: 10.1371/journal.pmen.0000125
**Document Type**: Empirical Research - Large-scale study
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-21
**Session**: 2026-02-21 Awe Science Self-Transcendence

---

## Core Insight

Music and art-induced aesthetic chills ("goosebumps," "shivers") reliably accompany self-transcendent states, even after controlling for demographics, traits, and prior affective state. This positions aesthetic chills as a *low-barrier, democratized proxy* for self-transcendent experience - accessible without meditation training, chemical agents, or exceptional natural environments. Practically anywhere with music is a potential site of self-transcendence.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Large-scale exposure study with diverse participants
**Sample Size**: N = 2,937
**Stimuli**: Chills-eliciting music and audiovisual content
**Key Results**:
- Both chills likelihood and intensity positively associated with self-transcendence measures
- Effect held after controlling for demographics, traits, and prior affective state
- Aesthetic chills enhance mood, awe, and prosociality - mirroring outcomes of traditional self-transcendence practices
- Aesthetic chills may *denote* sufficiently intense feelings of self-transcendence (not merely correlate with them)

---

## Why This Is Significant: Accessibility

The landscape of self-transcendence practices has historically required:
- **Meditation**: Years of practice
- **Psychedelics**: Legal access, controlled setting, facilitation
- **Extreme nature**: Geographic access, physical capacity
- **Religious ritual**: Community and belief commitment

Aesthetic chills require only exposure to moving music or art. They are:
- Available anywhere with a sound system
- Require no prior training or belief
- Cross-cultural (documented across diverse populations)
- Inducible on demand via curated playlists
- Deliverable through headphones in any setting

This democratization of self-transcendence has profound clinical and design implications.
