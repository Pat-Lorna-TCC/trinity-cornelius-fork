# First RCT Confirms Awe Reduces Depression - Effect Size d=0.78-0.96

**Source**: "Awe reduces depressive symptoms and improves well-being in a randomized-controlled clinical trial." *Scientific Reports*, May 2025. DOI: 10.1038/s41598-025-96555-w
**Document Type**: Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) - Single-blind, waitlist-control
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-21
**Session**: 2026-02-21 Awe Science Self-Transcendence

---

## Core Insight

The first randomized controlled trial of awe as a clinical intervention demonstrates medium-to-large effect sizes for depression reduction (d = 0.78-0.96) - effects comparable to many established psychological interventions. A brief, online, synchronous awe intervention produced a 17% decrease in depression symptoms vs. a 2% increase in the control group, and a 16% increase in well-being. Awe is now an evidence-based intervention, not merely a theoretical construct.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Single-blind, waitlist-control RCT
**Sample Size**: N = 68; mean age 54.09; 82.4% female; long COVID patients across the United States
**Groups**: Awe intervention (N = 30) vs. waitlist control (N = 38)

**Key Results**:
- Depression reduction: up to 17% decrease for intervention group vs. 2% *increase* for control
- Effect sizes: d = 0.78-0.96 (medium to large - clinically robust)
- Well-being increase: up to 16% for intervention group
- Stress: significantly decreased in intervention group vs. control
- Anxiety: no significant difference (awe specifically targets depression/stress, not generalized anxiety)

**Format**: Brief, online, synchronous group awe intervention
**This is the first RCT documenting awe's effects on depression symptoms**

---

## Clinical Significance

Effect sizes of d = 0.78-0.96 place awe in the range of:
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for depression: d = 0.73-1.12
- Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT): d = 0.43-0.60
- Exercise as antidepressant: d = 0.43-0.80

Awe matches or exceeds established interventions, while being:
- Brief (short session)
- Online-deliverable (no specialist facility required)
- Low-cost
- Without pharmacological side effects
- Accessible to populations with limited mobility (long COVID patients)
