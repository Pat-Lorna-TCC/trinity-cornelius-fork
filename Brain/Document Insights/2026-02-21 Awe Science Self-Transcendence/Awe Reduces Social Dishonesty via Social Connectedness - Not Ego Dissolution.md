# Awe Reduces Social Dishonesty via Social Connectedness - Not Ego Dissolution

**Source**: Luo L, Yuan J. "Social connectedness mediates the effect of awe in reducing dishonest behavior." *Scientific Reports*, 15, 33130, 2025. DOI: 10.1038/s41598-025-18367-2
**Document Type**: Empirical Research - Three studies with mediation analysis
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-21
**Session**: 2026-02-21 Awe Science Self-Transcendence

---

## Core Insight

Awe reduces dishonest behavior, but the operative mechanism is *social connectedness* - not the small self effect, universe connectedness, or authenticity. People become more ethical after awe primarily because they feel more connected to others, not because they feel cosmically small or spiritually aligned. This mechanistic precision has important implications: the ethical effects of awe are fundamentally social, not transcendental.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Three studies, total N = 714
- Study 1 (N = 347): Dispositional awe measurement
- Study 2 (N = 157): Experimental awe induction vs. amusement and neutral conditions
- Study 3 (N = 210): Mediation analysis with multiple candidate mediators

**Key Results**:
- Dispositional awe negatively related to dishonest tendencies (Study 1)
- Experimental awe induction produced lower dishonest behavior vs. amusement and neutral conditions (Study 2)
- Mediation analysis: social connectedness was the *only* significant mediator
  - β = -0.15, 95% CI [-0.25, -0.04]
  - Proportion mediated = 20%
- Small self, universe connectedness, and authenticity were NOT significant mediators
- Bayesian mediation analysis confirmed these findings

---

## The Mechanism Refinement: Social Not Cosmic

This study distinguishes between several theoretical accounts of why awe promotes prosocial behavior:

| Proposed Mechanism | Status |
|-------------------|--------|
| Small self (feeling diminished) | NOT confirmed as mediator |
| Universe connectedness (feeling cosmically connected) | NOT confirmed as mediator |
| Authenticity (feeling true to self) | NOT confirmed as mediator |
| Social connectedness (feeling part of community) | CONFIRMED as mediator |

The implication is clear: awe improves ethics by making people feel *part of* a human community. The mechanism is social bonding and felt connection - not ego dissolution, not cosmic significance, not identity authenticity.

This does not mean the small self effect doesn't exist - it means the small self effect doesn't drive the ethical behavior change. These may be separable downstream consequences of awe.

---

## Design Implication: Social Context of Awe Matters

If social connectedness is the operative mechanism for awe's ethical effects, then the *social context* of awe experiences becomes crucial:
- Awe experienced in community (concerts, collective ritual, nature groups) may produce stronger ethical effects than solitary awe
- Awe that foregrounds human connection (witnessing virtue, communal beauty) may be more ethics-enhancing than awe of impersonal vastness
- Interventions should include social elements to activate the connectedness mechanism
