# Collective Effervescence Mediated by Awe Expands Identity to All-of-Humanity

**Source**: Da Costa Dutra SC et al. "Ritual's Collective Effervescence, Awe, and Social Identity: Psychosocial Effects of the Pasto Carnival." *Frontiers in Psychology*, 2025. DOI: 10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1566499
**Document Type**: Empirical Research - Field Study
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-21
**Session**: 2026-02-21 Awe Science Self-Transcendence

---

## Core Insight

Collective effervescence - the shared heightened emotional state of group ritual participation - expands social identity to include all of humanity, but this expansion is *mediated by awe*. Simply participating in a collective ritual is not enough; it is the quality of effervescence (the awe generated) that triggers superordinate identity expansion. This makes awe the proximate psychological mechanism for reducing tribalism through collective experience.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Field study of the 2023 Black and White Carnival (Carnaval de Negros y Blancos), Colombia
**Sample Size**: N = 163 participants (dancers, actors, artists)
**Key Results**:
- Collective effervescence quality (not merely participation intensity) was associated with communal, national, AND superordinate identification with all of humanity
- Awe during the carnival correlated with social identification and identification with all of humanity
- Mediation analyses: collective effervescence influences community identity and parochial altruism *through awe*
- Awe does NOT directly mediate national or all-of-humanity identity - suggesting a hierarchical mechanism where awe first creates immediate social bonding, which then extends upward

**Related Finding**: A *Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin* (2024) study of live music concerts found collective effervescence predicted enjoyment and meaning more strongly than awe, flow, or basic positive mood alone, with effects on happiness lasting days after the event.

---

## The Hierarchy of Identity Expansion

The mediation finding is theoretically important:

```
Collective Effervescence Quality
        ↓ (via Awe)
Community/In-Group Identity
        ↓ (sequential)
National Identity
        ↓ (sequential)
All-of-Humanity Superordinate Identity
```

Awe appears to be the catalyst that makes collective experience identity-expanding rather than merely identity-reinforcing. Without awe, a collective ritual can intensify in-group bonding while *strengthening* tribalism. With awe, the expansion continues beyond the group boundary to universal identification.

---

## Implications for Tribalism and Polarization

Political polarization and tribalism involve strongly contracted in-group identity with heightened out-group threat perception. The Carnaval study suggests a practical pathway:
- Collective awe experiences (shared music, art, nature, cultural events) that generate genuine effervescence
- Effervescence quality (depth of awe) determines whether identity expands beyond the in-group
- Designing high-awe collective experiences may be more effective for reducing polarization than direct persuasion or exposure

The awe-prosociality connection (r = 0.40 meta-analysis) combined with this collective expansion finding suggests awe is a scalable tool for social cohesion.
