# AMEDIA-Model - Digital Technology Co-Constructs Autobiographical Memory (Not Just Records It)

## Core Framework

Autobiographical remembering in the digital age is an **iterative process of combining information stored in the mind with information stored in the digital environment**. Memory reconstruction "crucially depends on the way the information has been encoded and curated" - and **digital curation happens via platform algorithms, not personal judgment**.

Smartphones and social media are no longer tools for recording memory - they are **co-constructors of autobiographical narrative**.

---

## Context and Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - 2024 theoretical proposal with experimental validation
**Supporting Evidence**: Think-aloud experimental study showing participants repeatedly switched between internal memories and digital resources when recalling both important and random days
**Convergent Support**: Consistent with neuroscience of memory reconsolidation (memories are rewritten each time they are retrieved)
**Alternative Theories**: Traditional view: internal cognitive process (now superseded for digital-native populations)

---

## Key Mechanisms Identified

1. **Algorithmic memory curation**: "Your memories from X years ago" features decide which life events get re-consolidated and which fade - not personal significance, but platform optimization
2. **Iterative reconstruction**: People repeatedly switch between internal memories and digital records, making the boundary between internal and digital memory blurry
3. **Encoding dependency**: How memories were encoded (what was photographed, posted, tagged) shapes what can be recalled and how
4. **Deepfake risk**: Synthetic media represents a genuine threat to personal memory integrity at scale
5. **Identity fragmentation risk**: Increased density of recorded episodes creates both new identity construction opportunities and fragmentation risks

---

## Why This Is Significant

The standard model of autobiographical memory: it's an internal cognitive process. This framework says: **for anyone with a smartphone and social media presence, autobiographical memory is now hybrid cognitive-digital**.

This means:
- Platform design choices have identity-level consequences
- The "memories" people access are algorithmically filtered, not personally curated
- Identity constructed from digital-mediated memory is shaped by optimization functions (engagement), not psychological health
