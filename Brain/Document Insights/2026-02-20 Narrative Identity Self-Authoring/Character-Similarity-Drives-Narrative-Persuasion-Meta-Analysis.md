# Character Similarity Drives Narrative Persuasion - Meta-Analysis Shows Identification and Belief Change

**Source**: Chen, M., Dong, Y., & Wang, J. (2024). "A Meta-Analysis Examining the Role of Character-Recipient Similarity in Narrative Persuasion." *Communication Research*, published online. https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00936502231204834

## Core Insight

A 2024 meta-analysis of 19 empirical papers (123 effect sizes) demonstrated that **character-recipient similarity is a significant predictor of narrative persuasion outcomes**. A similar story character leads to stronger identification, stronger self-referencing, marginally stronger transportation, and reduced counter-arguing.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Meta-analysis
**Sample**: 123 effect sizes from 19 empirical papers
**Key Results**:
- Identification: d=0.14 (similar character → stronger character identification)
- Self-referencing: d=0.16 (similar character → stronger self-referencing during story processing)
- Transportation: d=0.13 (marginally stronger, positive direction)
- Counter-arguing: Reduced when character is similar

**Citation**: Chen, Dong & Wang (2024), *Communication Research*, https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00936502231204834

---

## The Mechanism

Character similarity triggers self-referencing (the audience maps the story onto their own experience). This self-referencing:
1. Activates autobiographical memory channels (as per the shared neural representations finding)
2. Reduces analytical processing / counter-arguing
3. Increases identification with the character's beliefs and outcomes
4. Produces belief change aligned with the story's message

The effect is small but reliable (d=0.14-0.16 is consistent with many persuasion findings where even small effects have large population-level impact).

---

## The AI Storytelling Exception

A companion finding from *Journal of Communication* (2024): three experiments compared human vs. ChatGPT-generated narratives.

**When stories were labeled as AI-generated**:
- Lower transportation
- Higher counter-arguing
- Lower story-consistent beliefs

**When AI-generated stories were unlabeled**:
- Reduced counter-arguing similarly to human stories

**Implication**: Authenticity attribution (perceived human vs. AI authorship) - not content quality - determines story persuasiveness. The psychological mechanism is identity-level: can I identify with a story authored by a non-human? The label activates analytical mode; without the label, the story is processed experientially.
