## Core Framework

Narrative transportation - the experience of being absorbed in a story - changes beliefs more effectively than arguments because it **reduces counter-arguing by shifting processing from analytical to experiential mode**. This is the core mechanism by which stories change beliefs more effectively than logical arguments.

When transported into a narrative, the analytical system that would evaluate and refute claims is bypassed - the audience is processing the story experientially, not argumentatively.

## Key Evidence

From the 2024-2025 research synthesis:
- Narrative transportation reduces counter-arguing (the primary defense against persuasion)
- The narrative vs. argument neural distinction (from Cerebral Cortex 2025): narratives engage DMN while arguments engage frontoparietal control systems - different processing modes at the neural level
- Applied study: storytelling interventions produced **28% increase** in self-efficacy expectancies vs. controls (climate communication study, *Journal of Agricultural Education* 2025)
