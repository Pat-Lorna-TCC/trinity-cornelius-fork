# Narrative Identity Disruption Sequence - Motivational Disturbances Precede Psychosis Onset

**Source**: (1) Raffles Cowan, H., et al. (2024). ## Core Insight

Three dimensions of narrative identity are disrupted in schizophrenia: motivational/affective themes, narrative structure, and autobiographical reasoning. Critically, a **prospective study** found that **motivational/affective disturbances appear before psychosis onset**, while structural narrative disruptions emerge closer to or after the first psychotic episode - providing a potential early marker system.

---

## Evidence

**Study 1 (Systematic Review)**:
- 30 studies, N=3,859
- Three dimensions of narrative identity disrupted in schizophrenia spectrum:
  1. Motivational/affective themes - distorted emotional tone of self-narratives
  2. Narrative structure - disjointed temporal sequencing and coherence
  3. Autobiographical reasoning - inability to draw lessons or meaning from events

**Study 2 (CHR Prospective Study, Schizophrenia Bulletin 2024)**:
- Youth at Clinical High Risk (CHR) for psychosis
- **Key Finding**: Motivational/affective theme disturbances appear *before* psychosis onset
- Structural narrative disruptions emerge closer to or after the first psychotic episode
- Implies a temporal sequence that could guide early intervention timing

**Study 3 (Journal of Mental Health 2025)**:
- Even a single narrative identity interview (prompting reflection on life story) produced significant increases in positive affect
- Therapeutic implications for case formulation and collaborative treatment planning

---

## The Temporal Sequence

```
Pre-psychosis: Motivational/affective narrative disturbances
    ↓ (develops toward onset)
Onset: Structural narrative disruptions appear
    ↓ (post-onset)
Full psychosis: All three dimensions disrupted
```

This sequence suggests that **narrative assessment could enable earlier intervention** than current symptom-based approaches, since motivational/affective narrative disturbances are assessable before florid psychosis develops.
