## The Gap

No study has yet used fMRI to measure **narrative identity constructs during flow states** - specifically whether DMN deactivation during flow suppresses exactly the situation-model-maintenance regions identified in the 2025 Cerebral Cortex study.

---

## Why This Matters

Two parallel bodies of literature exist without empirical connection:
- **Flow research**: DMN deactivates during deep task absorption; flow is "selfless"
- **Narrative identity neuroscience**: DMN maintains situation model / narrative self-coherence; its suppression equals narrative dissolution

The mechanistic link is obvious but empirically unverified. Confirming it would:
1. Validate the synthesis in Flow as Phenomenology of Narrative Dissolution
2. Provide the empirical bridge between peak performance psychology and narrative identity psychology
3. Connect Buddhist no-self practices and peak performance psychology through a shared neural substrate

---

## What We Know (Related Research)

- **Flow domain**: DMN deactivates during flow states (multiple fMRI studies); transient hypofrontality is well-established
- **Narrative identity domain**: DMN maintains situation model (Cerebral Cortex 2025); DMN coupling strength predicts narrative memory
- **Missing**: Cross-domain study measuring narrative identity variables (via NISE subscales or equivalent) in flow states and correlating with DMN activity in situation-model-maintenance regions specifically

---

## Proposed Study Design

**What it would measure**:
- fMRI during flow-inducing tasks (musical performance, video gaming at peak skill, athletic performance)
- Specifically: activity in midline cortical DMN regions (situation model maintenance)
- Pre/post: narrative identity measures (autobiographical reasoning, narrative coherence)
- Control: non-flow performance of same tasks

**What it would show**:
- Whether flow specifically suppresses situation-model-maintenance DMN regions (not just general DMN)
- Whether post-flow periods show temporarily elevated narrative flexibility scores
- Mechanism for why flow feels like "being out of your story" rather than just "focused"
