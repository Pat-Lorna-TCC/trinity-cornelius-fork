## Core Framework

AI systems are no longer passive platforms for self-expression - they are **active co-constructors of the narratives people tell themselves**. Algorithm-curated content determines which versions of the self get rehearsed and reinforced. The "Algorithmic Self" is the AI-mediated version of identity construction through digital platforms.

## Specific Mechanisms Identified

1. **Echo chambers of self-narrative**: Personalized feeds create selective exposure to identity-confirming content - not just confirming political beliefs but confirming the user's self-concept
2. **Algorithmic memory curation**: "Your memories from X years ago" features decide which life events get re-consolidated and which fade - optimizing for engagement, not psychological coherence
3. **AI as co-author**: AI chatbots and coaches are becoming co-authors of personal narratives as people increasingly process their experiences through them
4. **Narrative flattening**: Optimized, algorithmically edited stories "flatten the richness of human experience" and may prevent psychological integration

---

## The Central Paradox

Growth, resilience, and self-definition require **contradiction, change, and ambiguity** - precisely what algorithmic optimization removes. The AI-curated self is coherent but potentially psychologically impoverished.

Algorithms optimize for engagement and coherence; psychological growth often requires dissonance and challenge. The algorithm is, in this sense, **anti-therapeutic** by default.
