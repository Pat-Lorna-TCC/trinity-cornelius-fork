# Research Gap: Neural Signature Distinguishing Presence vs Search for Meaning

## The Gap

The specific neural signature distinguishing "**presence of meaning**" (experiencing life as meaningful right now) from "**search for meaning**" (actively seeking meaning because it feels absent) has not yet been directly imaged or characterized.

---

## Why This Matters

Meaning-in-life psychology distinguishes between two fundamentally different meaning states:
- **Presence of MIL**: The feeling that life is meaningful now
- **Search for MIL**: The motivation to seek and build meaning (often when presence is low)

These states are predictively different (search without presence predicts psychological distress; search that leads to presence predicts growth), yet their neural substrates remain uncharacterized. We know the DMN is involved in meaning construction generally, but we don't know which DMN activation patterns correspond to "currently meaningful" vs. "actively seeking meaning."

**Clinical Implication**: Without this distinction, neuroimaging cannot reliably differentiate between someone who is flourishing through meaning-seeking (search leading toward presence) vs. someone in meaning-crisis (search without progress toward presence).

---

## What We Know (Related Research)

- **DMN activation**: Associated with self-referential thought and meaning construction generally
- **DMN network specialization**: Higher in people with higher meaning
- **Presence of MIL correlates**: Positive affect, sense of coherence, lower depression, lower anxiety
- **Search for MIL correlates**: Mixed (can indicate growth orientation OR existential crisis)
- **Missing**: The specific neural contrast between these two states

---

## What Would Help

**Critical Studies**:
- fMRI paradigm contrasting presence-induction vs. search-induction conditions with validated MIL measures
- Real-time neural monitoring during meaning-generation vs. meaning-seeking tasks
- TFUS (transcranial focused ultrasound) of DMN regions to experimentally induce/disrupt presence vs. search states
