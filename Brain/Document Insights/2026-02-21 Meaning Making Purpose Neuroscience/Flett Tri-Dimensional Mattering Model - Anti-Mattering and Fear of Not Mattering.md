## Core Framework

Gordon Flett's mattering framework advances beyond the simple positive/negative binary to a tri-dimensional model that better captures the full phenomenology of significance:

**Three Mattering Constructs:**
1. **General Mattering (GM)**: Positive sense of significance to others - feeling valued, noticed, depended upon
2. **Anti-Mattering (AM)**: Active experience of being insignificant or irrelevant - not just absence of mattering but its opposite (feeling invisible, dismissed, like a burden)
3. **Fear of Not Mattering (FNM)**: Future-oriented anxiety about losing significance - anticipatory dread of becoming irrelevant

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - growing empirical support across populations and cultures
**Study Scale**: 9,170 students across four educational levels in 2025 *Nature* portfolio study
**Clinical Validation**: Validated in Palestinian participants experiencing extreme adversity (2024)
**Sensitivity Differences**: General mattering responds to positive experiences; AM and FNM respond immediately to social disruptions

---

## Key Research Findings

- Disparities in mattering constructs are more pronounced **between** educational stages than within them
- Anti-mattering and FNM are specific risk factors for youth suicidality - not merely low mattering (Flett, 2024)
- Under traumatic circumstances, FNM elevates significantly alongside anxiety and depression
- The Mattering Movement is translating research into organizational design: environments must deliberately ensure people feel valued, seen, and significant

---

## Why This Matters (Practical Implications)

The tri-dimensional model explains why "you matter" interventions sometimes fail: addressing positive mattering without reducing active anti-mattering feelings leaves the core problem untreated. Anti-mattering is not merely the absence of significance but an active felt state of irrelevance - requiring specific targeted interventions.

**Clinical Design Principle**: Don't just try to increase how much people feel they matter - actively reduce their experience of anti-mattering (being treated as invisible or burdensome).
