## Core Insight

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) is the most empirically supported meaning-based clinical intervention. Its mechanism - **psychological flexibility** (the ability to contact the present moment fully and change or persist in behavior in service of chosen values) - directly addresses all three dimensions of meaning-in-life theory (coherence, purpose, significance). ACT is grounded in Relational Frame Theory (RFT), which posits that human language creates complex relational networks that can either constrain or expand meaning.

---

## Evidence

**COMMEND Trial** (*The Lancet*, 2024):
- RCT of ACT for motor neuron disease (MND) - extreme test case (degenerative, terminal condition)
- ACT significantly improved quality of life through values clarification and psychological flexibility
- Demonstrates ACT can generate meaning-enhancement even when circumstances cannot be changed

**Social Functioning Meta-Analysis** (2025, *PMC*):
- Systematic review of ACT across PTSD, insomnia, psychosis, autism spectrum disorder
- ACT beneficial through:
  - Reducing experiential avoidance
  - Enhancing mindfulness
  - Promoting long-term emotional regulation improvements

**ACT Mechanism on Meaning**:
- Coherence: Values clarification creates interpretive framework for experience
- Purpose: Values-aligned behavior creates goal direction
- Significance: Defusion from self-narrative allows connection to larger significance

---

## Theoretical Foundation - Relational Frame Theory

ACT's neuroscience foundation (RFT) is significant: human language creates relational networks that are both the source of meaning and the source of suffering. Language allows us to relate any event to any other event, including pain to meaning. ACT interventions reshape these relational frames to align behavior with values - this is literally meaning as neural network restructuring.

**Implication**: Meaning is not just found - it is constructed through the relational networks we build with language and experience. Therapy for meaning-loss is therefore also language/narrative restructuring.
