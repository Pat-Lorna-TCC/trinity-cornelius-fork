## Core Framework

Paul Wong's Existential Positive Psychology (EPP) challenges conventional happiness-focused psychology by arguing that suffering is not an obstacle to meaning but a **pathway** to it. True positivity means "seeing and being the light in darkness" - not avoiding darkness. Effective coping requires dialectical/paradoxical rather than binary logic. Positive outcomes require working through suffering, not around it.

**Core EPP Tenets:**
1. True positivity means seeing and being the light in darkness
2. Effective coping depends on dialectical/paradoxical rather than binary thinking
3. Positive outcomes require working **through** suffering, not avoiding it

**The Positive Suffering Mindset (PSM)**: Proposed as essential for healing and flourishing - the capacity to find meaning within suffering rather than only after suffering ends.

**Self-Transcendence**: Connecting beyond the ego to something greater is identified as a core meaning mechanism - the direction of meaning points outward and upward, beyond personal benefit.

**PURE Model**: Purpose, Understanding, Responsible Action, Enjoyment/Evaluation - operationalizing meaning in clinical practice.

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - challenges the dominant PERMA model (Seligman's Positive Psychology) which focuses on positive emotions, engagement, relationships, meaning, accomplishment without centrally addressing suffering
**Theoretical Strength**: Convergence with multiple traditions (Buddhist dukkha, Frankl's logotherapy, ACT's acceptance framework)
**Note**: Paul T.P. Wong died in October 2024 - a significant loss to the field; posthumous publications continue

## Why This Matters - The Contrarian Insight

Conventional positive psychology optimizes for positive experiences and minimizes negative ones. EPP argues this is insufficient and potentially counterproductive: **meaning requires the full spectrum of experience, including suffering**. A life optimized purely for pleasure (high liking/opioid activation, low suffering) may paradoxically produce less meaning than a life engaged with difficulty.

This is not just philosophical - it has implications for AI design: AI systems that optimize for user satisfaction and minimize friction may systematically erode meaningful experience by removing the productive suffering that generates growth.
