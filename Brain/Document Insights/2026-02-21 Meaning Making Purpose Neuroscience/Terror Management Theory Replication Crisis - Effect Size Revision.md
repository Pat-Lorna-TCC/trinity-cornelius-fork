# Terror Management Theory Replication Crisis - Effect Size Revision

**Source**: Chen, Benjamin, Guo, Lai, & Heine. (2025). "Managing the terror of publication bias: A systematic review of the mortality salience hypothesis." *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40193452/; Z-curve analysis (Replication Index, 2025); Arendsen et al. ## Core Insight

Terror Management Theory's mortality salience hypothesis - which posits that death awareness drives cultural worldview defense and meaning-seeking - faces serious replication challenges in 2024-2025. A meta-analysis of 643-825 studies found effects are highly heterogeneous, many studies underpowered, and individual effects potentially spurious. However, the core conceptual insight (death awareness activates meaning-seeking) retains validity; the specific experimental paradigm needs revision.

---

## Evidence

**Chen et al. (2025) Meta-Analysis** (*Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*):
- Reviewed 643-825 studies
- Used multiple bias-correction techniques (selection models, precision-effect tests, p-curve, z-curve)
- Finding: "nonzero effects underlying some studies... although the effects are **highly heterogeneous**, most studies are underpowered, and many individual effects may be spurious"
- Recommendation: Future replications assume smaller effect size (r = .18) and follow expert protocols

**Z-Curve Analysis** (Replication Index, 2025):
- False discovery rate point estimate: **43%** (95% CI: 20%-100%)
- "Not possible to rule out that most results COULD BE false positives"

**Consumer Domain Meta-Analysis** (2025, *IJCS*):
- 112 experiments, 70 studies
- Small but positive overall effect size (g = 0.21) for mortality salience on consumer behavior

**Important Counter-Evidence**: A 2025 *Frontiers in Psychiatry* study found that under mortality salience conditions, search for meaning **mediates** increased helping intentions - suggesting the underlying mechanism (existential threat → meaning-seeking → prosocial behavior) remains valid even if specific TMT predictions need revision.

---

## Significance for the Field

This is part of the broader replication crisis in psychology, now arriving specifically in existential/meaning psychology. The intellectual implication is important: **the conceptual insight that death awareness activates meaning-seeking and value affirmation is well-grounded**, but the specific experimental paradigm (mortality salience manipulation) may be producing inflated effect sizes due to demand characteristics and publication bias.
