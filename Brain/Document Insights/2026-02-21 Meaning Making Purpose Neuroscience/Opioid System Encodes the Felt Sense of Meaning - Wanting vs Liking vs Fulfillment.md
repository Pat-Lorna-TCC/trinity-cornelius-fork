## Core Framework

The "wanting vs. liking" distinction in reward processing extends to a three-tier neurochemical model of meaningful engagement:

- **Wanting** (dopamine): Motivational pull toward goals; anticipatory drive; "this might be meaningful"
- **Liking** (opioid system): Hedonic pleasure in the moment; "this feels good right now"
- **Fulfillment** (opioid + oxytocin convergence): The deeper "felt sense" of meaning - the satisfaction of having engaged with what matters, involving mu opioid receptor activation in NAc shell "hedonic hotspots"

## Why This Matters

This framework resolves a persistent puzzle in meaning research: why does meaningful activity sometimes feel effortful and unpleasant in the moment (low liking) yet leave one with deep satisfaction (fulfillment)? The three-tier model explains:

- You don't need to "like" every moment of meaningful work (opioid activation can come from completion, contribution, and connection - not constant hedonic pleasure)
- Meaningful engagement can involve dopamine-driven motivation without pleasure-in-the-moment
- The most meaningful experiences often involve sustained effortful engagement followed by opioid-mediated fulfillment at completion

**Design Implication**: Systems designed to maximize moment-to-moment pleasure (social media, entertainment) target the "liking" system. Systems designed for meaning need to also target the fulfillment dimension - which requires completion, contribution, and connection rather than continuous hedonic stimulation.
