# DMN Semantic Subsystems Map to Three Meaning Dimensions (SPECULATIVE SYNTHESIS)

**STATUS: SPECULATIVE SYNTHESIS - Connecting two well-established findings; the specific mapping is original interpretation**

**Source**: "Distinctive and Complementary Roles of Default Mode Network Subsystems in Semantic Cognition." (2024). *Journal of Neuroscience*, 44(20). https://www.jneurosci.org/content/44/20/e1907232024; Tripartite meaning framework (Martela & Steger, 2016-2025)

## Hypothesis

The three DMN subsystems identified in *Journal of Neuroscience* (2024) map functionally onto the three dimensions of meaning-in-life theory, suggesting that the tripartite meaning model has a structural neural basis:

| DMN Subsystem | Primary Function | Meaning Dimension |
|---------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Medial Temporal (MT)** | Scene construction + autobiographical memory | **Coherence**: "My life story makes sense" |
| **Core DMN** | Internally-oriented self-referential cognition | **Purpose**: "I have valued goals directing me" |
| **Frontotemporal (FT)** | Conceptual knowledge under cognitive control | **Significance**: "I have frameworks for why my existence matters" |

---

## Rationale

**Why This Might Be True**:

**MT Subsystem → Coherence**:
- Autobiographical memory constructs the narrative of "my life"
- Scene construction integrates episodic experiences into coherent sequences
- Coherence requires exactly this: life events integrated into an understandable story
- Evidence: Damage to MT subsystem disrupts narrative self-continuity

**Core DMN → Purpose**:
- The Core deactivates in response to external tasks, activates for internal self-reference
- Purpose involves the "internally-oriented" state: where am I going, what do I value, who am I becoming?
- The insulated core architecture (reduced sensory input) protects this purpose-contemplation from distraction

**FT Subsystem → Significance**:
- Manages conceptual knowledge and cognitive control
- Significance/mattering requires abstraction: "I contribute to something that transcends my immediate experience"
- This requires conceptual frameworks (FT) rather than autobiographical narrative (MT) or pure self-reference (Core)

**Confidence Level**: Medium - The subsystem functional properties align well with the meaning dimensions; however, direct empirical testing of this mapping has not been conducted

---

## Testable Predictions

**If this synthesis is correct, we would expect**:
1. Disruption of MT subsystem (but not Core or FT) should preferentially impair coherence sense, not purpose or significance
2. Conditions that suppress Core DMN (task absorption, flow) should impair purpose-sense but leave coherence intact
3. FT subsystem activity should correlate with abstract meaning-making but not autobiographical coherence

**Falsification Criteria**:
- If all three meaning dimensions correlate equally with all three DMN subsystems, the mapping is wrong
- If damage to MT disrupts all three meaning dimensions equally

---

## Research Needed

- Neuroimaging study with validated meaning-in-life measures (separate coherence/purpose/significance scores) correlated with subsystem-specific DMN activation
- Lesion studies examining which DMN subsystem damage most disrupts which meaning dimension
