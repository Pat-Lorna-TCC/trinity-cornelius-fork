# Meaning-in-Life Mediates Depression to Suicidal Ideation

**Source**: Perez Rodriguez et al. (2024). MIL mediates depressive symptoms and suicidal ideation (n=714). *Frontiers in Psychology*. https://public-pages-files-2025.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1349726/pdf

## Core Insight

Meaning in life (MIL) **mediates** the relationship between depressive symptoms and future suicidal ideation. This means: depression does not directly cause suicidal ideation; depression causes a collapse in meaning, and the collapsed meaning is what creates the pathway to suicidal ideation. Restoring meaning therefore interrupts the depression-to-suicidality pathway specifically - making MIL a targeted clinical intervention point, not just a general protective factor.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Cross-sectional mediation analysis with longitudinal follow-up
**Sample**: n=714
**Key Result**: MIL mediates (not merely moderates) the depression → suicidal ideation relationship
**Implication**: Controlling for MIL weakens the depression-suicidality pathway significantly
**Citation**: Perez Rodriguez et al. (2024). *Frontiers in Psychology*. DOI: 10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1349726

---

## Clinical Significance

The mediation finding has practical implications:
- Depression treatment that doesn't address MIL may reduce symptoms without reducing suicidality
- MIL-targeted interventions (ACT, logotherapy, meaning-centered therapy) may interrupt the suicide pathway directly
- Screening for low MIL in depressed patients is a specific suicide risk indicator

**Companion Finding (Flett, 2024)**: Anti-mattering (not just low meaning, but active experience of being insignificant) is specifically implicated in youth suicidality - narrowing the intervention target further.
