## Hypothesis

Because the four AI meaning-erosion pathways are now identified (cognitive offloading erodes agency, AI connection displaces authentic connection, AI deployment reduces workplace safety, attention hijacking disrupts DMN reflection), it is possible to derive **meaning-preserving AI design principles** that directly counter each pathway.

## Proposed Framework: Meaning-Preserving AI Design

**Principle 1: Agency Amplification** (counters cognitive offloading)
- Design AI to enhance human cognitive authorship, not replace it
- User should be able to trace their thinking through AI-assisted work
- Measure: Does user feel more capable after AI interaction or dependent on it?

**Principle 2: Relational Authenticity Preservation** (counters authentic connection erosion)
- AI companions should be transparent about their non-human nature
- Design limitations that preserve the space for authentic human connection
- Avoid optimizing AI relationships to be "better than" human ones in every dimension

**Principle 3: Psychological Safety by Design** (counters workplace safety erosion)
- AI implementation should be evaluated for its psychological safety impact
- Include employee meaning-assessment in AI deployment metrics
- Design AI to make human contributions more visible, not less

**Principle 4: Attention DMN Protection** (counters attention hijacking)
- AI systems should design for periods of unstructured reflection (DMN activation)
- Counterintuitive: best AI interactions may include intentional off-ramps from AI engagement
- Novelty streams should be bounded, not infinite
