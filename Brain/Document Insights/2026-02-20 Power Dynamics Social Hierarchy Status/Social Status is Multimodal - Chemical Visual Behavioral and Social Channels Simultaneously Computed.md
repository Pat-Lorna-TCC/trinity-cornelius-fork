## Core Framework

Social status perception integrates across at least four simultaneous sensory and social channels, each providing a distinct type of status information:

| Channel | Finding | Source |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Chemical (olfactory)** | Testosterone in body odor signals dominance pre-consciously; both sexes detect it equally | Hofer et al. (2025) |
| **Behavioral (visual)** | Amygdala extracts latent status variable from observed behavioral patterns in third-party interactions | Lee et al. (2024) |
| **Neural (brain-to-brain)** | Leader-to-follower prefrontal synchronization occurs after bonding; tracks inter-status dynamics | Ni et al. (2024) |
| **Social (feedback)** | Active inference sociometer tracks social feedback to continuously update status estimate | Albarracin et al. (2024) |

Status is not a single signal - it is a multi-channel inference computation that runs simultaneously across sensory and social modalities.

## Why This Is Significant

The multimodal nature of status computation has several implications:

1. **Pre-conscious layers**: The olfactory channel (testosterone in odor) operates below conscious awareness. Status assessments are partially completed before conscious perception even begins
2. **Social perception is automatically integrated**: The amygdala's latent variable computation runs on behavioral observation without deliberate attention
3. **Cross-channel consistency**: A leader who synchronizes neurally with followers AND signals competence behaviorally AND maintains congruent body chemistry will receive strong, consistent status signals across all channels
4. **Channel hijacking**: Digital environments can hijack the social feedback channel (active inference sociometer) while leaving other channels (chemical, behavioral) unengaged - creating status experiences that feel real but lack multimodal grounding
