# PACAP in BNST is Molecular Marker of Chronic Subordination Stress

**Source**: Meloni, E.G., Carlezon Jr., W.A., & Bolshakov, V.Y. (April 2024). ## Core Insight

Chronic social subordination is encoded in a specific stress peptide: PACAP (pituitary adenylate cyclase-activating polypeptide) is significantly elevated in the BNST (bed nucleus of the stria terminalis) of submissive animals compared to dominant and intermediate animals. Paradoxically, this elevation coincides with **blunted cortisol responses** - indicating chronic stress adaptation/exhaustion at the hormonal level while a specific peptide signal persists. The BNST-PACAP axis appears to be a molecular signature of chronic social defeat that survives beyond the acute stress response system.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: ELISA, immunohistochemistry, behavioral dominance ranking, plasma corticosterone assay
**Sample Size**: C57BL/6 male mice across dominance ranks
**Key Results**:
- PACAP expression significantly higher in BNST of submissive mice vs. dominant and intermediate
- No significant difference in PACAP in central nucleus of amygdala (CeA) - BNST-specific
- Corticosterone levels LOWEST in submissive mice - blunted hormonal stress response after chronic subordination
- No significant differences in body weight, motor coordination, or acoustic startle
**Replication**: Requires independent replication; novel peptide-rank correlation
**Citation**: Meloni, Carlezon & Bolshakov (2024), *Scientific Reports*

## Why This Is Significant

The dissociation between PACAP elevation (BNST) and blunted cortisol represents two stages of chronic subordination biology:

1. **Stage 1 (acute subordination)**: Elevated cortisol stress response
2. **Stage 2 (chronic subordination)**: Cortisol response blunted (adaptation/exhaustion), but BNST-PACAP axis remains activated - creating persistent anxiety-circuit activation without the acute hormonal signal

This explains the phenomenology of chronic social defeat: the person who has been subordinate for a long time may no longer feel acutely "stressed" in the conventional sense, but shows persistent anxiety and avoidance - the BNST-PACAP system maintaining low-grade anxiety signaling.

PACAP in the BNST is associated with anxiety disorders and PTSD in humans, suggesting this rodent finding has direct translational implications for social defeat-induced mental health conditions.
