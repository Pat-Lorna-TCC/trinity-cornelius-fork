## Core Framework

Social media platforms are "hyperstimulating digital environments" that systematically warp the brain's generative model - the predictive architecture used to navigate social reality. The exploitation is not metaphorical: the **same neural circuits that evolved to track real dominance hierarchies and prestige signals** (amygdala latent variable, MDT-OFC-cACC circuit) are being hijacked by algorithmically curated engagement metrics. The brain's status-tracking circuitry cannot distinguish authentic social rank from manufactured engagement. Social media anxiety is not psychological weakness - it is a systemic architectural mismatch between evolved neurobiology and digital feedback environments.

## Four Mechanisms of Hijacking

1. **Artificial hierarchy creation**: Follower counts and like metrics create numerical social rankings that activate the same amygdala latent variable system that tracks real dominance hierarchies
2. **Manageable prediction error waves**: Notifications provide waves of prediction error (who responded?) that feel rewarding to resolve - exploiting the brain's free energy minimization drive
3. **Generative model arrest**: Persistent platform engagement arrests the generative model's ability to flexibly track real-world social standing - the digital model becomes the reference point
4. **Signal substitution**: Authentic prestige signals (demonstrated competence, genuine social respect) are replaced by manufactured engagement signals (algorithmic amplification, bot interactions)

## Why This Is Significant

This framework synthesizes the new status neuroscience with the existing knowledge base on social media attention hijacking. The prior vault notes (e.g., Social Media feedback loops create spikes of Dopamine, Social Media uses Intermittent Variable Reinforcement (IVR)) focus primarily on the dopamine/reward hijacking mechanism. This adds a second, distinct mechanism: social rank tracking hijacking.

The distinction matters for intervention design. Anti-social-media interventions focused solely on reducing "screen time" address the dopamine hijacking but not the generative model warping. The latter requires redesigning feedback architectures to provide less distorted social standing signals - not just fewer signals.

This also explains why **content creators are especially vulnerable to social media pathology**: they are continuously receiving algorithmically curated status signals (engagement metrics) that override genuine social feedback, systematically warping their self-model toward the platform's metrics.
