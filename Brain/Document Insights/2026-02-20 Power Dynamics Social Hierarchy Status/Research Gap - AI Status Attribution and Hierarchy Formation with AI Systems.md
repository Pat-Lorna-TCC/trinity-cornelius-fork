## The Gap

No empirical studies (2024-2025) were identified that explicitly examine how humans attribute social status to AI systems, or how AI systems affect human hierarchy formation within groups. While digital status dynamics were studied (social media, follower counts), the specific question of AI-mediated status dynamics remains unexplored.

---

## Why This Matters

Given that:
1. The amygdala automatically computes social rank as a latent variable from any observed interaction (Lee et al., 2024)
2. Social rank tracking is continuous and applies to inferred status even in third-party observers
3. AI systems are increasingly present as interlocutors, assistants, and decision-support tools in human groups

The question of whether and how humans assign status to AI systems - and how AI adoption affects human hierarchy dynamics within teams - is both empirically tractable and practically important.

**Specific sub-questions**:
- Does the amygdala's latent variable status computation activate when observing AI-human interactions?
- Do humans attribute dominance or prestige (or neither) to AI systems?
- Does interacting with a high-performing AI threaten the status of domain experts (dominance/prestige disruption)?
- Do AI systems that demonstrate "skill" (prestige signal) or "authority" (dominance signal) receive different status attributions?

## What We Know (Related Research)

- **Hierarchy tracking**: Amygdala automatically computes rank even from behavioral observations (Lee et al., 2024)
