## The Synthesis

Three findings from this research report, combined with the vault's existing knowledge base, converge on a unified computational account of identity threat:

1. **Self-esteem = active inference sociometer** (Albarracin et al.): self-esteem is prediction error minimization about social standing
2. **Dopamine = social reward prediction error** (Batten et al.): dopamine tracks whether social outcomes are better or worse than expected
3. **Identity = set of beliefs** (vault): identity is constructed from belief priors with high precision/confidence

**The synthesis**: Status threat is computationally identical to any other high-precision prediction error. When your social standing is challenged, the active inference sociometer generates free energy (prediction error) that must be resolved through either action (defend status) or belief revision (accept lower status). Because identity is constituted by these status-beliefs (who you are partly depends on where you stand), the prediction error propagates into identity-level belief disruption. This is why status challenges feel existential: they ARE prediction errors in your fundamental model of yourself.
