## Core Framework

Polyvagal Theory and Karl Friston's Active Inference / Free Energy Principle are converging on a shared account of hierarchical autonomic regulation. PVT describes the phenomenological hierarchy (ventral vagal - sympathetic - dorsal vagal states); Active Inference provides the computational mechanism (precision weighting, free energy minimization, generative models). Together they describe the same system from two perspectives: PVT from the inside (what it feels like and what drives it), AIF from the outside (how it computes).

---

## The Convergence Points

**Both frameworks share:**
1. **Hierarchical models of brain-body regulation** - PVT's three-tier autonomic hierarchy maps onto AIF's hierarchical generative model layers
2. **Top-down predictions + bottom-up error signals** - PVT's neuroception (bottom-up) filtered by prior autonomic states (top-down) = AIF prediction-error dynamics
3. **Special status of autonomic/interoceptive signals** - Both treat ANS/interoceptive state as foundational to cognition and emotion, not epiphenomenal
4. **Safety/threat detection as predictive** - Both frameworks explain how threat is anticipated (not just reacted to) through ongoing probabilistic inference about environmental safety

**Formal mapping (from Harrison-Friston 2025):**
| PVT State | AIF Analog |
|-----------|-----------|
| Ventral vagal (safety, social engagement) | Low free energy; accurate generative model; minimal prediction error |
| Sympathetic activation | Prediction error spike; allostatic mobilization to reduce environmental uncertainty |
| Dorsal vagal (shutdown/dissociation) | Precision collapse; free energy reduction through disengagement from sensory flow |

## The Kotler-Friston Flow Synthesis (2025)

The landmark Kotler-Friston (2025) paper formally bridges polyvagal/interoceptive frameworks and computational neuroscience through flow and intuition:

**Key arguments:**
- Flow: Habitual priors become highly precise and tuned to context → reduces need for conscious deliberation → experienced as effortless action
- Intuition: "Gut sense" guides action by minimizing predicted uncertainty; **interoceptive signals (heart rate, visceral sensations) contribute to prediction** - the gut literally informs the brain's probabilistic model
- Both phenomena involve nonconscious processing, but serve different functions: intuition = rapid nonconscious decision; flow = optimal nonconscious performance

**Critical insight for interoception**: The role of interoception in intuition is explicitly underpinned by embodied cognition and FEP. The "gut feeling" is not metaphorical - it is the interoceptive prediction system contributing to uncertainty reduction.

## The Allostasis Bridge (Harrison-Friston 2025)

Formally integrates active inference with allostatic regulation - the same substrate PVT describes through vagal mechanisms:

- Allostasis = proactive prediction and management of anticipated physiological needs (vs. homeostasis = reactive correction)
- Polyvagal states map onto allostatic models within the FEP framework
- This paper identifies **four resilience phenotypes** based on allostatic active inference capacity - providing a computational taxonomy of why some people shift autonomic states more flexibly than others

**Resilience re-defined**: Allostatic resilience is the capacity to accurately predict and pre-emptively regulate autonomic state - not strength under stress, but the precision of the organism's generative model of its own regulatory needs.

## Why This Convergence Matters

1. **Resolves PVT's anatomical ambiguity**: If PVT's three-tier hierarchy is a computational architecture (not just anatomical structures), it doesn't need clean anatomical separation to be valid. The hierarchy is functional-computational, not merely structural.

2. **Provides mathematical grounding**: PVT's phenomenological descriptions gain formal specification through FEP mathematics - making them testable in ways they previously were not.

3. **Unifies multiple frameworks**: PVT + Active Inference + Interoceptive Inference (Anil Seth) + Flow neuroscience all become aspects of a unified hierarchical predictive regulation model.
