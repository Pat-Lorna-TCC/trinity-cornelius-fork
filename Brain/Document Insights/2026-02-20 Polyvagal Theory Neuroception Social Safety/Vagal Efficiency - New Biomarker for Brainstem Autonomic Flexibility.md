## Core Insight

**Vagal Efficiency (VE)** is a new biomarker introduced by Porges in 2024-2025 to track the brainstem's "switching" mechanism between cardiac and gut vagal regulation. Unlike RSA (which indexes respiratory-cardiac coupling), VE measures how efficiently the brainstem translates blood pressure feedback into vagal control of both heart and gut simultaneously. This opens clinical windows into autonomic regulatory capacity that RSA alone cannot capture.

---

## What Vagal Efficiency Measures

**The mechanism VE tracks:**
- The brainstem receives baroreceptor feedback about blood pressure
- It must dynamically allocate vagal output between two organ systems: heart and gut
- This "switching" between cardiac and enteric vagal regulation is the brain-gut interaction mechanism
- VE indexes how efficiently this switching operates - how much vagal benefit is produced per unit of baroreflex input

**Why this matters beyond RSA:**
| Metric | What It Measures | Limitation |
|--------|-----------------|------------|
| RSA | Respiratory-cardiac vagal coupling | Cardiac only; influenced by breathing pattern |
| HRV (SDNN, RMSSD) | Heart rate variability composite | Does not capture brain-gut coordination |
| Vagal Efficiency (VE) | Brainstem heart-gut vagal switching | Novel; requires specialized analysis |

## Gut-Brain Axis Application

Porges applied the VE framework to functional gastrointestinal disorders (DGBI - Disorders of Gut-Brain Interaction):

**The PVT-gastrointestinal connection:**
- **Safety state (VVC active)**: Efficient digestion, oxygenated blood flow to gut, optimal motility
- **Sympathetic activation**: Gastroparesis, reduced gut motility, inflammatory cascade, microbiome disruption
- **Chronic sympathetic state** (as in untreated trauma, chronic stress): IBS, functional dyspepsia, cyclic vomiting syndrome as symptoms of autonomic dysregulation - not purely psychosomatic

**Clinical insight**: IBS, fibromyalgia, chronic fatigue, and functional GI disorders may share a common mechanism - autonomic dysregulation (reduced vagal efficiency) rather than distinct disease pathologies. VE could become a unifying biomarker across these conditions.
