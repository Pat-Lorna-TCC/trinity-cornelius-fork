## Core Framework

Neuroception is not a fixed system - it is calibrated by history. Trauma **shifts the neuroceptive threshold toward threat detection**: after repeated experience of danger, the nervous system begins detecting threat in neutral environments. This creates a self-reinforcing cycle: trauma → threat-biased neuroception → reduced access to VVC → reduced resilience → increased vulnerability to future trauma.

**Resilience re-defined**: Not the absence of stress response, but the **adaptive flexibility to shift autonomic states** - specifically, the ability to return to VVC from sympathetic activation. Resilient individuals have neuroception calibrated toward detecting safety; less resilient individuals have it calibrated toward threat.

---

## The Self-Reinforcing Loop

```
Trauma or chronic threat
        ↓
Neuroception recalibrated toward threat detection
        ↓
More neutral cues classified as dangerous
        ↓
Autonomic system stays in sympathetic/dorsal vagal more often
        ↓
VVC less accessible → reduced social engagement, creativity, learning
        ↓
Reduced ability to form co-regulating relationships (safety source)
        ↓
Reduced resilience → greater vulnerability to future stressors
        ↓
More trauma experiences → loop repeats
```

---

## The Positive Loop (Resilience Building)

The loop runs in reverse under safety conditions:

```
Safe relationships / environments
        ↓
Neuroception detects safety cues
        ↓
VVC activates → social engagement, co-regulation
        ↓
Co-regulation with calm others recalibrates threat threshold
        ↓
Neutral cues begin to register as safe
        ↓
Increased resilience → better autonomic flexibility
        ↓
Broader access to cognitive/creative/social capacities
```

**Therapeutic implication**: The relationship is the treatment, not just the container. The therapist's calm presence and predictable attunement directly recalibrates the client's neuroceptive threshold through co-regulation.

## Neuroceptive Bias as Precision Prior

In Active Inference terms (connecting to existing knowledge base):
- Trauma creates a **high-precision prior for threat** - the generative model assigns very high confidence that environments are dangerous
- This prior overrides bottom-up sensory evidence (a neutral face is "seen" as threatening because the prior is strong enough to suppress the actual perception)
- Therapy works by accumulating **prediction error** in the safety direction: safe experiences that contradict the threat prior gradually reduce its precision
- **Interoception-based therapies** are particularly effective because they operate on the body (the prior's source), not just cognitive reappraisal (which can't easily override high-precision subcortical priors)

---

## Why Cognitive Therapy Alone Has Limits

This framework explains why "just think differently" fails for trauma:
1. Neuroception operates **subcortically** - below where cognitive reappraisal operates
2. High-precision threat priors actively suppress contrary sensory evidence
3. The body remains in sympathetic/DVC states even while the mind intellectually understands safety
4. Bottom-up approaches (somatic, interoceptive, breathwork) are needed because they change the physiological prior, not just the interpretation
