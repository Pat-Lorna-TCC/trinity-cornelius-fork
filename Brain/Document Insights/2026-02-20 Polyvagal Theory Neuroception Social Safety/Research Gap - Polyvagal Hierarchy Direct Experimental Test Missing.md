## The Gap

Despite 15,000+ citations, **no large-scale RCTs have directly tested Polyvagal Theory's full three-tier hierarchy as an experimental manipulation in humans**. Most empirical support comes from:
- HRV/RSA studies (indirect proxy measures)
- Clinical outcome data (does not disambiguate mechanism)
- Animal optogenetics models (Strain et al. 2024 - rodents only)
- Transcriptomics (molecular markers, not behavioral hierarchy)

The "hierarchy" itself - the claim that VVC, sympathetic, and DVC states are recruited in a specific order under escalating threat - has never been directly tested in a controlled human experiment.

---

## Why This Matters

This gap creates the paradox noted in the 2024-2025 literature: PVT has strong clinical adoption and some empirical support for individual predictions, but its core theoretical claim (hierarchical recruitment) remains unverified. This limits:
1. **Scientific confidence** in the specific hierarchy claim
2. **Therapeutic precision** - clinicians use PVT as heuristic, not evidence-based protocol
3. **New biomarker validation** - VE and pupillometry lack normative data from hierarchy-testing studies

---

## What We Know (Supporting Research)

**Individual predictions that have empirical support:**
- Higher vagal tone (HRV) → Better cognitive performance (2025 systematic review, n=24,390)
- HRV biofeedback reduces PTSD symptoms (meta-analysis, Hedges' g=-0.557)
- DMNX activation reduces anxiety-like behavior (optogenetics - Strain 2024)
- Prosodic voice produces calming effects (behavioral research)
- NPSS successfully measures neuroceptive safety (4 validation studies 2024-2025)

**What is missing:**
- Human neuroimaging during systematically varied threat levels showing vagal hierarchy recruitment
- Direct comparison: VVC vs. sympathetic vs. DVC state with multiple simultaneous neural/behavioral measures
- Longitudinal studies tracking neuroceptive threshold shifts as a function of therapeutic intervention

---

## What Research Would Fill This Gap

**Critical Studies:**

1. **Human fMRI + HRV study**: Systematically varied social threat levels (from safety to mild danger to life threat analog) with simultaneous fMRI (nucleus ambiguus vs. DMNX vs. sympathetic chain activation) + HRV + behavioral measures

2. **Neuroceptive threshold manipulation**: Compare populations with known neuroceptive differences (trauma, high attachment security, trained meditators) on the same environmental safety cue detection battery + physiological measures

3. **VVC direct activation in humans**: Non-invasive vagal nerve stimulation protocols (taVNS) targeting VVC-specific fibers, with behavioral safety/creativity outcomes
