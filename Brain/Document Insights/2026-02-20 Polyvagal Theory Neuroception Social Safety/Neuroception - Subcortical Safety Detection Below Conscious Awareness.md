## Core Framework

**Neuroception** is the nervous system's continuous, subcortical process of scanning the environment for cues of safety, danger, or life-threat - operating entirely below conscious awareness. Unlike conscious "perception," neuroception:

1. **Is pre-cognitive**: Safety/danger classification precedes any conscious thought about safety
2. **Is body-mediated**: Detects features like vocal prosody, facial expression, heart rate rhythms - not just explicit threats
3. **Shapes autonomic state first**: The body's autonomic response is triggered by neuroception, and only then does conscious interpretation follow
4. **Is bidirectional**: Environmental cues reach the body; body state feeds back to bias future neuroception

---

## The Neuroception Process

**What the nervous system detects (non-consciously):**
- Vocal prosody: Melodic, variable-pitch voice = safety signal; monotone or harsh = danger
- Facial expression: Engaged/animated faces with raised eyebrows = safety
- Heart rate rhythms: Shared RSA synchrony between co-regulators = safety
- Middle ear tuning: Ambient frequency spectrum shapes what frequencies are perceived
- Visceral signals: Internal body state feeds "safety confidence"

**Autonomic response hierarchy (Polyvagal Theory):**
1. **Ventral vagal complex (VVC) active** - Neuroception: SAFE. Social engagement, co-regulation, creative/cognitive function, digestion
2. **Sympathetic activation** - Neuroception: DANGER. Mobilization, fight/flight, suppression of higher functions
3. **Dorsal vagal complex (DVC) dominant** - Neuroception: LIFE-THREAT. Immobilization, dissociation, shutdown

---

## Why This Is Paradigmatically Important

**Prior model**: Conscious perception of safety/danger drives emotional response, which drives ANS state
**PVT model**: Subcortical neuroception drives ANS state, which then shapes conscious perception of safety/danger

This inverts the assumed causality: **The body decides safety before the mind knows it is deciding**.

Practical implications:
- Telling someone "you are safe" cognitively does not override neuroception of threat
- Safety must be established at the neuroceptive (bodily, relational, environmental) level
- Therapeutic environments work partly through neuroceptive cues, not just cognitive reframing
- Trauma responses make evolutionary sense: neuroception calibrated to past threat does not update easily from conscious reassurance

## Measurement: NPSS Validation (2024-2025)

The Neuroception of Psychological Safety Scale (NPSS) - first psychometric tool to operationalize neuroception as measurable construct:

- **3-factor structure**: Social Engagement, Compassion, Bodily Sensations
- **Cronbach's alpha**: 0.95 total; 0.92-0.94 subscales (Morton et al., 2024)
- **International validation**: UK community (n=2,035, Cogan et al. 2025), Italian (ICC=0.922, Poli & Miccoli 2024), UK healthcare workers (n=443, 2024)
- Enables cross-cultural research on neuroceptive safety processes
