## Core Framework

**Interoception is not merely "listening to the body" - it is the ongoing construction of selfhood.** The 2025 editorial by Ueno et al. positions interoception as the "cornerstone of human subjective experience": cardiac and proprioceptive signals modulate self-attribution, agency, and body ownership. Selfhood is constructed through continuous interoceptive inference - the brain's predictions about its own body.

The vagus nerve is the primary conduit: 80-90% of vagal fibers are **afferent** (body → brain), making the vagus the nervous system's primary channel for body-state information shaping the self-model.

---

## The Vagal Architecture of Self

**The vagus nerve's afferent role:**
- 80-90% of all vagal fibers carry information FROM the body TO the brain (afferent = body-to-brain)
- Only 10-20% are efferent (brain-to-body commands)
- The vagus is primarily an **intelligence-gathering organ**, not a command channel
- It continuously reports: heart rate, breathing depth, gut state, inflammatory status, immune activity

**What the brain does with this information:**
- Generates predictions about body state (active inference/interoceptive inference)
- Computes: "What is my body doing? What does that predict about the environment?"
- This continuous loop of body-state prediction is the neural substrate of the "felt sense of being"
- **Selfhood = continuous successful prediction of your own body state**

**When this fails (clinical implications):**
- Anxiety: Interoceptive signals overweighted - body sensations feel catastrophic, threatening
- PTSD: Interoceptive signals underweighted - body feels foreign, disconnected, numb
- Depression: Inflexible interoceptive precision - mood reflects stuck autonomic state
- Autism/Alexithymia: Degraded signal-to-noise in interoceptive processing - difficulty identifying emotional states from body signals
