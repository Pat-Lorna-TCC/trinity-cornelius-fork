# Abstract-Concrete Cognitive Flexibility Is the Mechanism of Wise Reasoning

**Source**: Grossmann, I., Peetz, J., Dorfman, A., Rotella, A., & Buehler, R. (2024). "The Wise Mind Balances the Abstract and the Concrete." *Open Mind*, MIT Press, 8, 826-858
**Document Type**: Research Paper (N = 1,151)

## Core Insight

What makes wise reasoning possible is not access to more information, nor a preference for abstract or concrete thinking - it is the ability to SWITCH FLEXIBLY between abstract and concrete mental representations. This cognitive flexibility is the mechanism explaining why Tetlock's "foxes" outforecast "hedgehogs."

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: N = 1,151 participants across four studies (North America and UK)
**Key Results**:
- Both abstract and concrete construals individually contribute to wise reasoning (intellectual humility, open-mindedness, perspective-taking, compromise-seeking)
- What predicts HIGHEST wisdom is the ability to switch flexibly between them - not to favor either consistently
- NLP validation: High-wisdom participants' semantic networks showed greater use of BOTH abstract and concrete themes - providing an automated marker for wise reasoning

**Study types**: Converging across behavioral experiments and computational text analysis

**Tetlock connection**: This directly provides the cognitive mechanism for why "foxes" who draw on both abstract principles and concrete specifics outforecast "hedgehogs" who rely on a single theoretical lens. Giving hedgehogs MORE information does not make them foxes - the difference is in construal flexibility, not information access.

**Citation**: Grossmann et al. (2024), *Open Mind*, MIT Press, 8, 826-858

## The Fox-Hedgehog Mechanism Clarified

Tetlock's famous distinction has often been interpreted as about "being open to multiple views." This research shows the mechanism is more specific: it is about the ability to shift cognitive level of abstraction.

- **Abstract construal**: Principles, patterns, generalizations, "why" questions
- **Concrete construal**: Specifics, context, details, "what exactly" questions
- **Wise reasoning**: Knowing which mode to use, and being able to switch fluidly

This has a practical implication: wisdom training should include explicit practice switching between abstraction levels, not just acquiring more diverse viewpoints.
