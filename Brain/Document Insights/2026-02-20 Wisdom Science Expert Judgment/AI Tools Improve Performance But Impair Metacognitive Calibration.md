# AI Tools Improve Performance But Impair Metacognitive Calibration

**Source**: Gignac, G. E. (2024/2025). Study on AI tool use and DK effect; synthesized from realkm.com (2025-11-19) and Gerlich (2025) AI literacy study

## Core Insight

Using AI tools produces a paradoxical dissociation: performance goes UP while metacognitive calibration (accurately knowing how well you performed) goes DOWN. Crucially, higher AI literacy is associated with LESS accurate self-assessment - the people who use AI most fluently may be most blind to what they've lost.

---

## Evidence

**The Finding**: When AI tools are introduced, the classic Dunning-Kruger graph pattern disappears - but not because calibration improved. AI users consistently overestimated their performance, and this overestimation was stronger among HIGH AI literacy users.

**Mechanism**: AI tools create a "cognitive prosthetic" that produces correct outputs without the user developing the internal knowledge structures that normally anchor accurate self-assessment. You know WHAT the answer is but not WHY - and that gap undermines metacognitive monitoring.

**Related finding**: Gerlich (2025) - strong negative correlation (r = -0.68, p < 0.001) between AI tool usage and critical thinking abilities in 666 participants. Cognitive offloading to AI degrades the cognitive muscles that ground wise judgment.

**Evidence Level**: Moderate - emerging finding, needs replication. The mechanism is plausible and consistent with cognitive offloading theory, but direct experimental confirmation is limited.

## Why This Is Significant

This finding sits at the intersection of three vault clusters:

1. **AI Agents**: The most enthusiastic AI users may be accumulating a hidden metacognitive debt
2. **Dopamine and Identity**: AI validation creates dopamine reward loops that feel like competence
3. **Extended Mind / Cognitive Autonomy**: There's a difference between extending cognition and replacing it

The implication is not "don't use AI" but rather: **AI tools require deliberate metacognitive check-ins** - periodic unassisted performance tests to recalibrate your sense of actual competence.
