# Metajudgment - Folk Standards of Good Judgment Are Context-Sensitive Not Universal

**Source**: Grossmann, I., & Eibach, R. E. (2024). "Metajudgment: Metatheories and Beliefs About Good Judgment Across Societies." *Current Directions in Psychological Science*, 33(4), 261-269

## Core Framework

"Metajudgment" is the meta-level framework people use to evaluate what COUNTS as good judgment - the beliefs people hold about how one SHOULD reason, prior to making any specific judgment. People flexibly combine multiple judgment standards based on context rather than applying a single universal standard.

---

## Key Dimensions of Metajudgment

Two key axes:
1. **Intuition vs. Deliberative reasoning** - Should I trust my gut or analyze?
2. **Rationality vs. Reasonableness** - Should I optimize for logical consistency or social context?

**Crucial distinction - Rationality vs. Reasonableness**:
- **Rationality** (as perceived by folk psychology): self-serving, logical, context-free
- **Reasonableness**: context-sensitive, socially conscious, balancing personal preferences with others' concerns

This distinction has major implications for professional and legal contexts: when we ask "was that a reasonable decision?" we are applying a different standard than "was that a rational decision?"

---

## Cross-Cultural Data

Intuitive strategies were selected only **27% of the time overall** across cultures (range: 13.2% in Morocco to 39.7% in Canada). This refutes the notion that people are dominated by intuitive/System 1 thinking - most of the time, people actively engage deliberative standards.

**Key finding**: Universal adaptive challenges produce largely consistent folk standards across cultures - but situational demands drive systematic within-person variability. The same person applies different metajudgment standards to different situations.

---

## What This Does to the System 1 / System 2 Debate

Standard dual-process debates (Kahneman, Stanovich) frame cognition as a contest between automatic and deliberate systems. Metajudgment theory shows this misses a meta-level: people actively choose which judgment standard to apply, and this meta-choice is itself a form of practical wisdom.

The sophisticated judgment practitioner is not one who always uses System 2 - it is one who accurately selects WHICH standard (intuition, rationality, reasonableness) is appropriate to the specific decision context.
