## Core Framework

Psychotic symptoms - hallucinations and delusions - are not random misfirings. The psychotic brain is performing **rational Bayesian inference on a corrupted input channel**. The mechanism, as formalized by Powers et al. (2025):

1. NMDA hypofunction drives ascending neural noise
2. This noise degrades the signal-to-noise ratio of incoming sensory evidence
3. The brain compensates by **downweighting noisy sensory evidence relative to prior beliefs**
4. Prior beliefs become relatively "hyper-precise" - the brain over-relies on what it already believes
5. Result: hallucinations arise from vivid prior predictions propagating without sensory correction; delusions crystallize as the brain generates coherent explanations for its own misfiring

**The psychotic patient is not irrational - they are rational with broken inputs.**

## Updated Dopamine Role

The 2025 framework substantially expands the role of dopamine beyond reward signaling:

**Old view**: Dopamine signals reward; psychosis = dopamine dysregulation causing reward system noise

**New view**: Dopamine signals **precision** - it determines how much weight the brain gives to incoming evidence vs. prior predictions. Psychosis = aberrant dopamine signaling that misattributes precision to irrelevant stimuli (making them feel significant) AND reduces the precision accorded to valid sensory inputs.

This is why antipsychotics (D2 blockers) work - they reduce the dopaminergic noise that misassigns precision to incoming signals, allowing sensory evidence to compete with priors again. But they do not fix the underlying NMDA hypofunction.

---

## Why Newer Treatments Are More Promising

If NMDA hypofunction is the root cause (not dopamine dysregulation per se), then:
- **Dopamine D2 blockers** are a downstream patch, not a cure
- **Glutamatergic agents** (KarXT - muscarinic agonist; glycine reuptake inhibitors) target the root mechanism
- Recent KarXT schizophrenia trial success validates the glutamate pathway
