# Transdiagnostic Precision Weighting Failure Across Psychiatric Disorders

**Source**: "Computational Approaches for Uncovering Interoceptive Mechanisms in Psychiatric Disorders and Their Biological Basis" (Mehta, Paulus & Smith, *Current Topics in Behavioral Neurosciences* Vol. 74, Springer, 2025); "Interoception in Anxiety, Depression, and Psychosis: A Review" (*eClinicalMedicine*, The Lancet, 2024); "Interoceptive Mechanisms and Emotional Processing" (Greenwood & Garfinkel, *Annual Review of Psychology* 76:59-86, 2025)

## Core Insight

A Bayesian computational model applied across depression, anxiety, eating disorders, and substance use disorders found a shared underlying deficit: **all groups failed to increase interoceptive precision estimates in response to physiological perturbation**, unlike healthy controls who appropriately upweighted cardiac signals during heightened arousal.

This is a **transdiagnostic computational deficit** - the same failure mode (inability to adaptively recalibrate precision weighting of bodily signals) appearing across diagnoses that are categorically distinct in DSM terms. This suggests a shared computational mechanism that cuts across diagnostic boundaries.

---

## Evidence

**Transdiagnostic finding (2025)**:
- Groups studied: depression, anxiety, eating disorders, substance use disorders
- Failure mode: Did not increase interoceptive precision estimates under perturbation
- Healthy control behavior: Appropriately upweighted cardiac signals during arousal

**GAD-specific finding**:
- GAD patients show amplified heart-evoked potential (HEP) amplitudes
- HEP = neural marker of interoceptive prediction error
- The failure in GAD is **amplified noise rather than suppressed signal** - the brain is getting louder signals but cannot translate them into reliable precision estimates

---

## Counterintuitive Reframing: Anxiety Is Not Hypersensitivity

The standard clinical description of anxiety: "overly sensitive to bodily sensations"

The computational reality: anxiety may reflect a **failure to accurately model those sensations**, causing the brain to generate increasingly alarming interpretations in the absence of reliable interoceptive evidence. The brain is not getting too much signal - it is getting noisy signal it cannot trust, and filling in the gap with catastrophic predictions.

This completely reframes treatment:
- Targeting exposure to the sensations themselves (interoceptive exposure in CBT for panic) is precision calibration training
- Mindfulness of body sensations is precision recalibration training
- Both are computationally justified by this framework
