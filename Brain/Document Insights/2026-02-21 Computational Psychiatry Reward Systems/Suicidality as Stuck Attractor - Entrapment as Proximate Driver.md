# Suicidality as Stuck Attractor - Entrapment as Proximate Driver

## Core Framework

Suicidal ideation is increasingly framed computationally as a **complex dynamical system** rather than a linear outcome of accumulated risk factors. Two findings from network analysis of symptom dynamics are particularly important:

1. **Entrapment and worrying** emerge as the symptoms most strongly linked to suicidal ideation in directed network analyses - not depression severity per se
2. Dynamic time warping (DTW) analyses of EMA data identify a cluster of co-occurring and temporally linked symptoms: sadness, hopelessness, feeling stuck, and worrying - this cluster collectively links to suicidal ideation

---

## The Active Inference Interpretation

From active inference perspective, suicidal ideation may represent a **computational solution to the problem of a stuck attractor**: if the brain cannot minimize free energy (reduce prediction error) through any available action in the world, and the high-free-energy state is experienced as unbearable suffering, then self-termination may be computed as the only path to reducing the mismatch between desired states and actual states.

This is not metaphorical - it is mathematically formalized: the brain generates action plans to minimize predicted free energy. If all available actions predict continued high free energy, and the agent is modeling future states as well as present ones, self-termination appears as the state with the lowest predicted long-term free energy.

**Critical implications**:
- Treatment must target the sense of entrapment and impossibility (the "stuck" computational state) - not just depressive symptom severity
- Interventions that create perceived alternative paths (options) should work better than those that reduce suffering while maintaining entrapment
- The sense of "no escape" is the proximate mechanism, not the severity of pain

---

## Empirical Evidence

**Network Analysis**: Directed temporal network analyses of EMA data show:
- Sadness, hopelessness, feeling stuck, and worrying cluster with shared temporal dynamics
- Entrapment emerges as the most proximate symptom to suicidal ideation
- Worrying (inability to escape mental content) links the cluster to suicidal ideation

**ML Systematic Review (2024) - Methodological Warning**:
- Studies mixing psychiatric and non-psychiatric populations produce inflated predictions
- Formal psychiatric diagnosis overshadows more subtle disorder-specific risk factors
- Computational biomarkers need disorder-specific development
