## Core Framework

The 2024-2025 computational psychiatry literature converges on a single unifying insight: **across all major psychiatric disorders, the core computational failure is disrupted precision weighting** - the brain's regulation of how much to weight prior beliefs versus incoming sensory evidence.

The key parameter is always the same: in which direction, and in which neural subsystem, is precision weighting disrupted?

| Disorder | Precision Weighting Failure | Mechanism |
|----------|---------------------------|-----------|
| Depression | Inflexible precision; prior model locked | Correct model of past environment persists; cannot update for improved present |
| Anxiety | Over-precise priors about threat; amplified interoceptive noise | Brain generates alarming predictions in absence of reliable evidence |
| Psychosis | Over-precise priors; under-precise sensory evidence | NMDA hypofunction drives ascending noise; sensory evidence discounted |
| OCD | Over-precise priors about threat/contamination | Priors resist update despite contradicting evidence; stuck attractor |
| PTSD | Under-precise priors for safety (hypervigilance) | Traumatic priors cannot be downweighted by subsequent safety evidence |
| Addiction | Distorted value landscape; relative drug value over-precise | Drug reward precision dominates; alternative reward signals suppressed |
| Anhedonia | Under-precise reward signals; high evidence threshold for action | Ventral striatal RPE signals blunted; effort commitment requires more evidence |

## Why Active Inference Is a Genuine Unifying Theory

Unlike previous "theories" that described different disorders independently, active inference provides:
1. **Common mathematics**: Free energy minimization governs all disorders - different disorders = different parameters, not different algorithms
2. **Mechanistic predictions**: Each parameter has identifiable neural substrates and measurable behavioral signatures
3. **Treatment implications**: Every disorder requires restoring appropriate precision weighting, but through different mechanisms at different scales
4. **Transdiagnostic validity**: Explains why the same pharmaceutical (SSRIs, antipsychotics) helps multiple disorders - they all modulate precision weighting through common neurotransmitter systems

## The Self as Active Inference

The Active Intersubjective Inference (AISI) framework extends this to identity: **the self is constituted through active inferences about how others perceive us** - second-order self-models extracted from social interactions. In MDD and PTSD, traumatic experiences corrupt these self-models, and the self becomes organized around distorted inferences about one's place in the social world.

This has direct Buddhist resonance: the self is not a fixed entity but an active inference process - a continuously updated model rather than a stable thing. When the inference process is corrupted, the "self" that emerges is distorted.
