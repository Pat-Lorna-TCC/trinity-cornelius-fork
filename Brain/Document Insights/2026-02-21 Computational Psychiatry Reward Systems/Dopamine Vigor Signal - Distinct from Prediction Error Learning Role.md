# Dopamine Vigor Signal - Distinct from Prediction Error Learning Role

**Source**: "The Neurobiology of Activational Aspects of Motivation: Exertion of Effort, Effort-Based Decision Making, and the Role of Dopamine" (Salamone & Correa, *Annual Review of Psychology*, 2024); "Differential Effects of Dopamine and Serotonin on Reward and Punishment Processes in Humans: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis" (UCL Applied Computational Psychiatry Lab, biorxiv 2025.01.08.631868, 2025); "Inflammation and Dopamine in Anhedonia" (biorxiv 2025.12.23.696320, December 2025)

## Core Insight

The 2024-2025 literature delivers a crucial precision upgrade to the understanding of dopamine: **dopamine plays two distinct roles** that must be disentangled for both theory and clinical practice.

**Role 1: Prediction Error (Learning)**
- Phasic dopamine bursts signal reward prediction errors
- Drives reinforcement learning - associating cues with outcomes
- Operates at individual event level (each reward experience)

**Role 2: Vigor (Motivational Activation)**
- Tonic dopamine signals **average reward rate in the current environment**
- Directly modulates response vigor - the speed and energetic intensity of motivated behavior
- Operates at the level of the overall behavioral state
- Mesolimbic system, gated by dynorphin-mediated κ-opioid stress tone

These are separable: you can have intact prediction error learning but collapsed vigor (as in depression), or elevated vigor but disrupted learning (as in mania).

---

## Evidence

**UCL Meta-Analysis (2025)**:
- Comprehensive review distinguishing dopaminergic vs. serotonergic effects
- Dopaminergic agents primarily affect **reward discounting and vigor**
- Serotonergic agents primarily affect **punishment learning and aspects of discounting**
- Through distinct neural mechanisms - directly informs treatment selection

**Inflammation-Dopamine Link (December 2025 preprint)**:
- Inflammatory states suppress dopaminergic vigor through **κ-opioid receptor upregulation**
- Anti-inflammatory strategies (e.g., κ-opioid antagonists) may restore dopaminergic vigor specifically in depression with inflammatory markers
- New treatment hypothesis: depression subtypes with elevated inflammatory markers may respond to anti-inflammatory agents as dopamine vigor restorers

## Clinical Implications

Not all antidepressants are appropriate for the same motivational symptoms:

| Symptom | Mechanism | Treatment Target |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Motivational anhedonia, anergia | Collapsed dopamine vigor | Bupropion, stimulants |
| Rumination, anxiety | Serotonergic punishment learning | SSRIs/SNRIs |
| Inflammatory anhedonia | κ-opioid suppression of vigor | κ-opioid antagonists, anti-inflammatories |

This explains why many depressed patients fail multiple SSRI trials but respond to dopaminergic augmentation - they have a vigor deficit, not a punishment learning excess.
