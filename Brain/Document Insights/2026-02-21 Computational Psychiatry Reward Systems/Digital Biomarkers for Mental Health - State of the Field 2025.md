## Core Findings

**State of the Field (2025 JMIR Scoping Review, N=42 studies)**:
- 55% of studies target depression; 21% target anxiety
- 76% use wrist-worn devices
- Key signals: heart rate (67%), movement index (60%), step count (40%)

**What Works Best**:
- HRV predicts depression traits
- Step counts correlate with mood symptoms
- Sleep digital biomarkers for bipolar disorder: within-night sleep variability achieves **0.87 balanced accuracy** for hypomanic episode prediction with **3-day advance warning**
- Speech patterns detect psychosis with up to 100% accuracy in some studies (though with serious ecological validity caveats)

---

## Why Speech Is a Particularly Promising Signal

Speech emerges as uniquely valuable because:
- Less demanding than explicit cognitive tests
- Less affected by motivational deficits (which would confound psychiatric assessments)
- Can be captured continuously in naturalistic settings
- Sensitive to psychotic thought disorder, depression, and mania through distinct acoustic and linguistic markers

---

## Current Barriers

No current psychiatric biomarker meets the APA standard:
- 80% sensitivity, 80% specificity, 80% accuracy required
- No existing biomarker achieves all three
- Conclusion: personalized computational models may need to **combine multiple weaker signals** rather than identify single reliable markers

**Key methodological problems**:
- Small sample sizes
- Data quality inconsistencies
- Model non-generalizability across populations
- Ethical concerns around passive psychiatric data collection from vulnerable populations
