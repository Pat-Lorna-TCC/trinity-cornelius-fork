## Core Argument

The serotonin deficiency theory of depression - the idea that depression is caused by a chemical imbalance that SSRIs correct - was **rejected by the scientific community decades before pharmaceutical marketing mainstreamed it**. Computational psychiatry does not replace it with "dopamine deficiency" or any other single-neurotransmitter story. Instead, it replaces all single-neurotransmitter theories with:

**Circuit-level, mathematically grounded models framing psychiatric disorders as disturbances in inference, belief updating, and decision-making under uncertainty.**

---

## What SSRIs Actually Do (Computational Account)

SSRIs likely work not by correcting a serotonin deficit but by **modulating the brain's flexibility for prior updating** - changing the precision assigned to the brain's existing generative models. Specifically:

- Serotonin modulates punishment learning and the precision assigned to negative prediction errors
- Higher serotonin availability may reduce the precision of negative priors, making them easier to update
- This is why SSRIs work best **with concurrent therapy** (which provides the new evidence to update on)
- It explains why SSRIs fail when used in isolation without environmental change - the prior update mechanism is loosened but no new evidence is provided

## The Paradigm Shift in One Sentence

**From**: "The brain is broken; fix the chemical"
**To**: "The brain has learned a maladaptive model; provide the conditions for updating it"

---

## Why This Matters for Treatment

The chemical imbalance framing creates specific clinical harms:
1. Medication becomes a passive correction of a fixed deficit (wait for balance to restore)
2. Therapy is seen as optional add-on rather than the essential evidence source
3. Environmental factors are treated as secondary rather than primary causes
4. Treatment resistance is mysterious rather than explicable (prior model not updating)

The precision weighting framing creates a fundamentally different treatment logic:
1. Medication modulates the brain's capacity to update (lower the threshold)
2. Therapy provides the disconfirming evidence required for actual prior updating
3. Environment must change to provide new evidence worth updating on
4. Treatment resistance = prior model too rigid or evidence too weak
