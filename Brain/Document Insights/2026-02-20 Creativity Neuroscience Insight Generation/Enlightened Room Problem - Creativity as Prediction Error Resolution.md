## Core Framework

**The Enlightened Room Problem (ERP)**: If brains are prediction error minimization machines (the Free Energy Principle / Predictive Processing), how can they be creative? Creativity requires generating novel, unpredictable outputs - the opposite of minimizing prediction error.

**The resolution**: Creativity emerges from brains and environments **co-constructing "exploration bubbles"** that temporarily shift the goalposts for prediction error minimization. Creativity is NOT a violation of the prediction error framework but its **highest expression** - building better predictive models by deliberately exploring novelty.

## The Problem Stated

**Predictive processing (PP)** says:
- Brains minimize free energy (surprise/prediction error)
- We perceive and act to make the world more predictable
- All cognition is fundamentally about reducing uncertainty

**The creativity paradox**:
- Creative output is by definition **novel and unexpected**
- If we only minimize prediction error, we'd always do what we've done before
- The most predictable (lowest surprise) world = completely habitual existence with zero creativity

---

## The Resolution: Exploration Bubbles

**Constant, Friston, Clark's solution**:

1. **Exploration bubbles**: Brains and environments co-construct temporary "safe spaces" for exploration
   - A blank canvas, a sketchbook, an improv session, a brainstorming meeting
   - Within these contexts, **novel output becomes the expected norm** (prediction error for NOT being novel)
   - The goalposts shift: creativity minimizes the meta-prediction "I am engaged in creative exploration"

2. **Higher-order priors**: At a higher level of the generative model, the brain holds a prior for "creative exploration" that makes unpredictability the expected state within that context
   - The creative context is a model within a model
   - Free energy is minimized by producing novel output (confirming the higher-order creative prior)

3. **Co-constructed with environment**: Physical environments (studio, workshop, sketchbook) signal to the brain which prediction-error regime is active
   - Context = Perspective + Information extends here: Environment = Exploration bubble signal

## Key Definition

**Creativity (Constant et al.)**: "A rolling process of hypothesizing solutions to problems, testing them, and evincing solutions that are both novel and apt"

Note: Both NOVEL (not predictable) and APT (not random) - this matches the finding that creative ideas require both associative ability (for novelty) AND executive control (for aptness).

## The "Divergent Predictive Perception" Extension

**Supporting 2024 paper** (Neuroscience of Consciousness):
- Three factors predict divergent perceptual experience:
  1. Confidence in sensory input
  2. **Maladaptive use of priors** (using wrong or overly rigid templates)
  3. **Mental imagery ability** (novel critical factor identified)

**Implication**: Creativity in perception requires the ability to deploy priors flexibly and to generate internal imagery that doesn't just replay past sensory experience

**Connection to HLVC findings**: The "maladaptive use of priors" factor maps onto what less organized white matter enables - fewer dominant interpretations means priors are deployed less rigidly.
