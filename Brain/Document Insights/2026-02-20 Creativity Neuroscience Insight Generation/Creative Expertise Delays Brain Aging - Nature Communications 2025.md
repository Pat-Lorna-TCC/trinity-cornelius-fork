# Creative Expertise Delays Brain Aging - Nature Communications 2025

**Source**: Coronel-Oliveros C., Migeot J., Lehue F., Amoruso L., Kowalczyk-Grębska N., Jakubowska N. et al. - "Creative experiences and brain clocks" - Nature Communications, Vol. 16(1), Article 8336 - October 3, 2025

## Core Insight

The first large-scale cross-national study to measure creativity's effect on **brain biological age** finds that creative expertise measurably produces **younger brains** across all creative domains tested (dance, music, visual arts, video games). The effect scales with expertise level and appears even after short-term learning - placing **creativity alongside diet and exercise** as a prescribable brain health intervention.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: M/EEG functional connectivity with machine learning "brain clocks" + pre/post learning study
**Sample Size (Cross-Sectional)**: N=1,240 creative experts vs. non-experts
**Geographic Diversity**: Argentina (dance), Canada (music), Germany (visual arts), Poland (video games) - 13 countries
**Learning Study**: N=232 participants measured before and after skill acquisition
**Brain Age Method**: Machine learning trained to predict chronological age from M/EEG - gap between predicted and actual age = "brain age delta"

**Key Results**:
- Creative expertise consistently linked to YOUNGER brain age profiles across ALL four domains
- Dose-response relationship: more years of practice = younger brain age (not just binary expert/novice)
- Short-term learning (pre/post) produced measurable brain age benefits - effects not limited to long-term experts
- Most pronounced effects in regions vulnerable to neurodegeneration: hippocampus, prefrontal cortex, parietal areas
- Computational modeling revealed mechanism: plasticity-driven increases in brain efficiency and biophysical coupling

**Citation**: Coronel-Oliveros et al. (2025), *Nature Communications*, Vol. 16(1), Article 8336

---

## The Dose-Response Finding

**Not just expert vs. novice**:
- The more expertise, the younger the brain
- This is a continuous dose-response relationship, not a threshold effect
- Implies accumulating benefits throughout a creative practice lifetime
- Even short-term learning showed measurable benefits (not only lifelong experts)

**This means**:
- Starting a creative practice at ANY age likely confers brain age benefits
- Continuing and deepening practice increases the protective effect
- No "too late" threshold identified

---

## Why Creativity Specifically (Not Just Skill Learning)?

**Mechanism proposed**:
- Creative practice demands constant novel problem-solving (not just repetitive skill)
- This continuous novelty requirement maintains high cognitive demand
- Engages hippocampus (for associative memory and novelty detection)
- Maintains prefrontal flexibility (for evaluation and selection of novel ideas)
- Parietal areas (for multi-modal integration in creative synthesis)

**Contrast with non-creative skill mastery**: Pure repetitive skill learning may not produce the same brain age benefits because it eventually becomes automatic and low-novelty.

---

## Implications for Health and Longevity

**Prescription-level evidence**:
- Effect size and dose-response relationship make creativity prescribable like exercise
- Domain-independent: dance, music, visual arts, video games all showed benefits
- No domain hierarchy - all creative domains appear to confer benefits
- Short-term learning shows effects → accessible to older adults starting new creative practices

**Neurodegeneration prevention angle**:
- Most pronounced effects in regions vulnerable to Alzheimer's and frontotemporal dementia
- Hippocampus: critical for memory consolidation, early Alzheimer's target
- Prefrontal cortex: executive function, late-stage dementia target
- Parietal areas: spatial reasoning, early decline marker

**2025 research frontier**: Actively being explored as dementia prevention intervention.
