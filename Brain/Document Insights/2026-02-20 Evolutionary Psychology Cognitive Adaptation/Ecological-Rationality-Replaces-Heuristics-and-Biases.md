# Ecological Rationality Replaces Heuristics-and-Biases as the Dominant Framework

## Core Framework

The dominant trend in decision science 2024-2025 is a paradigm shift from the **Kahneman/Tversky "heuristics and biases" framing** (cognitive biases as errors revealing irrationality) toward the **ecological rationality framing** (heuristics as environment-matched adaptive strategies). The Gigerenzen tradition is gaining ground.

The synthesis: heuristics are not universally rational or irrational but **conditionally adaptive** based on the match between cognitive design and environment. The mismatch framework provides the resolution to the Kahneman vs. Gigerenzen debate.

**The key shift**:
- Old: "Is this heuristic rational or irrational?"
- New: "In which environments does this heuristic outperform alternatives, and in which does it fail?"

## Context and Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - gaining significant ground in 2024-2025 evolutionary psychology and behavioral science literature; not yet mainstream in clinical or policy applications
**Tension**: Behavioral economics policy applications (nudge units, default choices) are built on heuristics-and-biases framework; ecological rationality challenges the normative foundation of these interventions
**Key Researchers**: Gerd Gigerenzen (ecological rationality), Leda Cosmides and John Tooby (evolutionary psychology of bias), and the PNAS 2025 team (evolutionary simulation)

---

## The Evidence Base (2024-2025)

1. **PNAS 2025 (positivity bias evolution)**: Evolutionary simulation shows positivity bias is an evolutionarily stable strategy in most environments - not an error but an optimal strategy for the environments that shaped human cognition
2. **Error Management Theory**: False-negative and false-positive errors carry asymmetric fitness costs; natural selection produces predictably biased responses (not random errors)
3. **Mismatch Resistance (Biological Theory 2024)**: Some traits evolved for variability itself and remain adaptive across novel environments - challenging the universal prediction of maladaptation
4. **Royal Society Open Science 2025**: Human cognitive biases reflect evolutionary optimization; LLM differences reflect optimization for different fitness landscape - neither is "the" rational standard

---

## The Practical Resolution

**"Which biases should we correct and which should we work with?"**

**Correct (context-mismatched biases)**:
- When evolved heuristic faces genuinely novel environment with different error cost structure
- When stakes are catastrophic and asymmetric (non-ergodic risks)
- When explicit deliberation is feasible and the bias generates systematic errors at scale

**Work with (ecologically rational heuristics)**:
- When the heuristic is optimal for the actual environment being navigated
- When cognitive load makes deliberation infeasible
- When the heuristic has been validated as adaptive (not just as common)

## Critical Tension with Knowledge Base

The vault has extensive coverage of cognitive biases from a Kahneman/decision-science framing (System 1/System 2, debiasing, noise reduction). This new framework **challenges the normative premise** of that literature:

- If optimism bias is an ESS, debiasing interventions may be counterproductive
- If confirmation bias reflects adaptive asymmetric updating, reducing it may reduce fitness-relevant behavior
- The goal should be **calibration to context**, not elimination of the bias

This does NOT mean abandoning the vault's Kahneman-derived insights - it means layering the ecological rationality lens on top of them.
