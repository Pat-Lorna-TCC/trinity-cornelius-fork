## Core Framework

"Neurocognitive Mismatch Theory" proposes that ADHD and autism are not fixed neurological disorders but **evolutionary variants destabilized by modern market-based civilization**. These traits are biologically conserved (not accidents of mutation) because they carried ancestral adaptive value. In post-industrial, digital, market-based societies, several environmental factors act as **transgenerational amplifiers** of neurocognitive mismatch, rendering these variants functionally dysfunctional:

- Rising psychosocial stress
- Post-industrial work structure requiring sustained attention and social conformity
- Digital overstimulation
- Cognitive suppression (standardized schooling, workplace compliance)
- Economic inequality

**Field Acceptance**: Controversial - radical reframing with significant clinical and policy implications; published in Frontiers in Psychology but not yet widely adopted in clinical literature
**Supporting Evidence**: Theoretical framework drawing on evolutionary psychology, anthropological evidence, and neurodevelopmental research
**Alternative Theories**: Genetic disorder model (ADHD/autism as mutations in specific genes); neurodiversity movement (difference without disorder framing)

## The Ancestral Adaptive Value Hypothesis

**ADHD (Exploration and Novelty-Seeking Advantage)**:
- In hunter-gatherer groups, traits associated with ADHD (high novelty seeking, distractibility, impulsivity) would confer exploration advantages
- Scouts and hunters benefit from attention that is not locked to a single task but instead responsive to environmental change
- The same traits that create dysfunction in standardized classroom settings would create fitness advantages in dynamic foraging environments
- ADHD traits are biologically conserved (maintained in population) - this requires ancestral fitness benefit, not just drift

**Autism (Specialized Pattern Recognition Advantage)**:
- High systemizing, pattern recognition, intense focus, and reduced social monitoring would confer advantages in craft-intensive societies
- Craft specialists, tool-makers, early astronomers, and technical innovators may have disproportionately carried autism-related traits
- In pre-industrial societies with specialist roles, autism-spectrum traits would be valued
- Reduced social monitoring creates cognitive space for technical depth

---

## The Modern Amplifier Hypothesis

The framework identifies modern civilization as specifically amplifying neurocognitive mismatch through:
1. **Standardization**: Schools and workplaces require uniform attention and behavior patterns
2. **Chronic stress**: Transgenerational stress transmission may epigenetically amplify neurodevelopmental expression
3. **Digital overstimulation**: Dopamine system overload particularly harms novelty-seeking architectures
4. **Inequality**: Economic precarity amplifies developmental disruption

Rising ADHD and autism diagnoses may partially reflect genuine population-level increases driven by these amplifiers, not simply expanded diagnostic criteria.

---

## Implications

**For Treatment**: Addressing the environmental signals driving dysfunction, not just the behavioral outputs; environmental redesign (reducing supernormal stimuli, increasing nature exposure, restoring social structures) rather than treating the individual as broken

**For Education**: Learning environments designed around neurocognitive diversity, not uniformity; situated, physical, community-embedded learning may be particularly important for neurodivergent learners

**For Policy**: ADHD/autism accommodation as environmental design problem, not individual deficit
