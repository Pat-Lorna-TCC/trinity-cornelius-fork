## Core Framework

"Mismatch resistance" is a 2024 refinement of the evolutionary mismatch hypothesis: some traits retain adaptive value in radically novel environments **because they evolved under variable selection pressures** - they were selected not for a specific ancestral environment but for handling environmental variability itself. This challenges the dominant narrative that modern humans are primarily fighting against their own cognitive architecture.

The mismatch hypothesis now has three distinct variants:
1. **Classic mismatch**: Traits adaptive in ancestral environments become maladaptive in modern ones (sugar cravings, status anxiety)
2. **Mismatch resistance** (new 2024): Traits evolved under variable selection remain adaptive across radically different environments
3. **Developmental mismatch**: Mismatches between ancestral developmental environments and modern ones - distinct from evolutionary mismatch

## Why This Is Counterintuitive

The standard "Stone Age brain in modern world" framing implies near-universal maladaptation. The mismatch resistance concept says: our ancestors did not live in a single stable environment for millions of years. They lived through ice ages, migrations, ecological shifts, and radical resource variability. **Variability selection** - selection for functioning well across variable conditions - would have produced mismatch-resistant traits.

This partially explains the paradox: why humans are so demonstrably capable of adapting to novel conditions despite the mismatch hypothesis predicting dysfunction.

---

## Implications for Modularity Debate

Classic evolutionary psychology assumes domain-specific cognitive modules (dedicated cheater detection, mating, kin selection modules). The mismatch resistance concept aligns with the "cognitive gadgets" hypothesis: rather than fixed modules, humans evolved flexible learning capacities that can be assembled by cultural transmission into domain-specific tools. This means:

- Culture is not merely adding to evolved cognition - it is **constituting** it
- Evolutionary psychology needs cultural evolution theory
- Cultural evolution needs evolutionary psychology
- Human adaptability is partially a design feature, not just a lucky accident
