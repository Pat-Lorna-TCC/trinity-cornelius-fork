## Core Insight

Dopamine reward prediction error (RPE) signals implement a recursive optimization process that continuously advances agents toward the **best available reward** in their environment - not merely signaling whether outcomes match expectations. The evolutionary function of dopamine is to **prevent settling for "good enough"**, driving continuous improvement toward the highest available reward. This reframes dopamine from a simple learning signal into an active optimization engine.

The critical implication: dopamine systems are designed to maximize in environments where supernormal stimuli did not exist. When they are exposed to artificially amplified stimuli (social media, drugs, junk food), they cannot distinguish between "improving toward best available" and "trapped by artificial ceiling." The hijacking is not an accident - it is the optimization engine working exactly as designed, applied to the wrong environment.

## The Recursive Optimization Loop

Standard RPE model:
- Cue triggers dopamine anticipation
- Reward exceeds expectation: dopamine spike (positive RPE)
- Reward below expectation: dopamine drop (negative RPE)
- Learning: Update future expectations

Evolutionary optimization model (adds):
- The system does not just update expectations - it **continuously searches for higher peaks**
- Dopamine drives exploration of better options when current rewards are predictable
- "Good enough" generates boredom (reduced dopamine) that motivates seeking alternatives
- Novelty seeking is not random - it is directed toward environments likely to yield better rewards

**Functional outcome**: Dopamine prevents optimization from stalling at local maxima. It is a biological implementation of simulated annealing - always probing for better solutions.

---

## Why Supernormal Stimuli Are So Destructive

The optimization engine fails in modern environments because:
1. Social media, gambling, drugs provide **artificially amplified stimuli** that exceed any ancestral reward level
2. The system cannot distinguish "best available in environment" from "artificially maximized"
3. Once exposed to supernormal peaks, normal rewards (genuine relationships, real accomplishment) generate negative RPE - they fall below the new baseline
4. The optimization engine now drives pursuit of the supernormal stimulus, not genuine fitness-relevant rewards

This is not a design flaw in dopamine - it is the correct behavior of an optimization engine in the wrong environment.

## The Evolutionary Basis of Duhkha

This insight provides a mechanistic evolutionary account of the Buddhist concept of duhkha (suffering from craving):

- **Buddhist insight**: Craving (tanha) creates suffering because desire is insatiable
- **Evolutionary mechanism**: Dopamine as optimization engine is designed to prevent settling - satiation is not the goal, continuous improvement is
- **The mismatch**: In ancestral environments, "continuous improvement" meant genuine fitness gains. In modern environments with supernormal stimuli, it means addiction loops
- **Buddhist solution** aligns with evolutionary understanding: reducing the intensity of the optimization signal (through meditation, detachment) is the only escape from the loop
