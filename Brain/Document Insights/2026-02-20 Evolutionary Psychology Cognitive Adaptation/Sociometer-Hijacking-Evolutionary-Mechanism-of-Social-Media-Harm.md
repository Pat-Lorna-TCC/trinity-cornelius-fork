## Core Insight

Social media platforms produce measurable psychological harm through three specific evolutionary mismatches - they provide "supernormal" versions of social feedback stimuli that ancient systems were not designed to handle at scale. This is the most comprehensive evolutionary framework for social media harm to date, providing testable predictions and design guidelines grounded in evolutionary theory.

The three hijacked systems:
1. **The Sociometer** (inclusionary status monitor) - calibrates self-esteem via real-time tracking of social acceptance/rejection; hijacked by like counts, follower metrics, and engagement rates that provide continuous status feedback at unnatural frequency and scale
2. **The Social Monitoring System** - evolved for tracking threats and opportunities in small social networks; hijacked by infinite feeds providing unprocessable volumes of social information from weak-tie networks
3. **Social Comparison Processes** - calibrated for local comparison within visible peer groups; hijacked by curated profiles that create artificially skewed comparison targets

## The Neural Evidence Confirms the Evolutionary Mechanism

"Developmental Changes in Brain Function" (Oxford, 2024) provides longitudinal neuroimaging validation:

- Developmental trajectories of **neural social feedback processing predict addiction-like social media use 2 years later**
- Adolescence identified as a critical neurobiological window of vulnerability
- Heightened reward sensitivity + immature cognitive control + persuasive platform design = measurable harm
- Measurable alterations in: prefrontal cortex, striatum, amygdala, and insula

**Data Points**:
- Global social media addiction prevalence: 14-17.9% of internet users; 21.8% showing moderate-to-severe symptoms
- 57% of people seeking help for internet addiction also report anxiety or depression

## The Active Inference Layer

"Digital Being: Social Media and the Predictive Mind" (Oxford, 2024) adds a computational neuroscience dimension:

Social media platforms constitute "hyperstimulating" digital environments that disrupt the predictive processing (active inference) machinery of cognition:
- Under active inference, the brain is a prediction machine that minimizes prediction error
- Social media provides unpredictable, high-variability social feedback - maximizing prediction error novelty
- This warps the **generative model** through which agents engage with the world
- Addiction emerges because the generative model becomes calibrated to social media's artificial statistics, not real-world social dynamics
