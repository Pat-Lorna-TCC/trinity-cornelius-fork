## Core Framework

Systematic differences between human and LLM reasoning patterns reflect **evolutionary mismatch operating through culturally evolved "cognitive gadgets"** rather than fixed genetic modules. This reframes the "humans vs. AI rationality" debate away from both "humans are irrational" and "AI is more rational" positions.

The key insight: humans did not evolve domain-specific cognitive modules (a dedicated logic module, a math module). Instead, humans evolved **flexible learning capacities** that are then assembled by cultural transmission into domain-specific cognitive tools - "cognitive gadgets." These gadgets are:
- Culturally transmitted, not genetically specified
- Subject to both cultural evolution and evolutionary psychology constraints
- Partially mismatched when applied to novel formal reasoning contexts

**LLMs, by contrast, are optimized for a fundamentally different "fitness landscape"**: the distribution of text in their training corpus, not evolutionary fitness in an ancestral environment. Both systems are "rational" relative to their respective optimization targets; neither is universally rational.

## The Cognitive Gadgets vs. Cognitive Modules Distinction

**Classic evolutionary psychology (modules)**:
- Humans evolved dedicated domain-specific modules (cheater detection, mating, kin detection)
- Culture adds to evolved architecture but does not constitute it
- Human cognitive errors = module misapplication

**Cognitive gadgets framework (this paper)**:
- Humans evolved flexible learning capacities (high-fidelity social learning, cultural transmission, teaching)
- Domain-specific tools (reading, mathematics, formal logic, scientific reasoning) are culturally evolved gadgets
- Culture is not merely adding to evolved cognition - it is constituting it
- Implication: **evolutionary psychology needs cultural evolution, and cultural evolution needs evolutionary psychology**

---

## Why Both Human and AI Reasoning Differ from "Pure Rationality"

**Human biases**: Optimization for ancestral fitness via culturally transmitted cognitive gadgets creates systematic patterns that differ from formal logic in predictable ways (loss aversion, confirmation bias, optimism bias)

**LLM "biases"**: Optimization for text prediction creates systematic patterns that differ from formal logic in different predictable ways (hallucination, pattern-matching over inference, token-by-token processing)

**The synthesis**: Neither is the rational gold standard. Both are locally optimized systems. Knowing the optimization target allows prediction of where each system will fail.
