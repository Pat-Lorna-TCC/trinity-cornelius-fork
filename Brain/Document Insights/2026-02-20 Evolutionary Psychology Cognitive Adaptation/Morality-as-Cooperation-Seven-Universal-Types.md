## Core Framework

Morality is fundamentally about **promoting cooperation toward the common good** - which makes it a tractable scientific problem via game theory. The framework: "What is good?" = "What promotes cooperation?" - and cooperation problems have game-theoretic structure. Seven types of cooperation are universally recognized as morally good across cultures:

1. **Kinship** - Care for family (kin selection)
2. **Mutualism** - Coordinate with allies (coordination games)
3. **Exchange** - Reciprocate favors (reciprocal altruism)
4. **Heroism** - Aid group members (collective action)
5. **Deference** - Respect superiors (hawk-dove)
6. **Fairness** - Divide resources equitably (negotiation)
7. **Property** - Respect ownership (bourgeois convention)

**Culturally variable moral norms** arise from combining these seven more basic elements in different proportions - not from fundamentally different moral foundations.

## Context and Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - significant theoretically, supported by cross-cultural data but full empirical base being compiled
**Building On**: Jonathan Haidt's Moral Foundations Theory (different taxonomy); Robert Axelrod's cooperation research
**Key Distinction from Haidt**: Where Haidt's 6 foundations are psychologically descriptive (harm, fairness, loyalty, authority, purity, liberty), Curry's 7 types are game-theoretically grounded - derived from types of cooperative problems evolution actually solves

## Why This Is Significant for the Knowledge Base

This framework enables morality to be treated as a **scientific problem** rather than purely a philosophical or cultural one. The implications:

1. **Cross-cultural moral psychology**: Moral diversity is variation on a small set of universal themes, not fundamental disagreement
2. **AI ethics frameworks**: If morality = cooperation maximization, AI ethics can be grounded in game theory rather than competing cultural values
3. **Political polarization**: Polarized groups may hold the same 7 moral types but weight them differently - providing intervention opportunities
4. **Evolutionary basis of identity**: In-group/out-group moral cognition is the mutualism + heroism + deference complex operating at group level
