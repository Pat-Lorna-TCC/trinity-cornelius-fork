# Optimism Bias Is Evolutionarily Stable - Not a Bug

**Source**: "Evolving Choice Hysteresis in Reinforcement Learning: Comparing the Adaptive Value of Positivity Bias and Gradual Perseveration"

## Core Insight

Using evolutionary simulation of reinforcement learning agents, positivity bias - the asymmetric updating toward positive outcomes - is an evolutionarily stable strategy (ESS) across a wide range of environments. Agents with symmetric updating (treating positive and negative feedback equally) are outcompeted by positivity-biased agents in repeated interaction contexts. This provides direct computational evidence that **optimism bias is a design feature, not a design flaw**.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Evolutionary simulation of reinforcement learning agents across varied environments
**Key Comparison**: Positivity bias (asymmetric update toward positive outcomes) vs. gradual perseveration (repeating past choices) vs. symmetric updating
**Key Results**:
- Positivity bias is evolutionarily stable across most simulated environments
- Gradual perseveration shows much less systematic evolutionary stability
- Symmetric updating is consistently outcompeted
- Result holds across varied noise levels and environmental conditions

**Significance**: First PNAS-level evolutionary simulation providing direct computational evidence that optimism bias is evolutionarily optimal in repeated interaction contexts.
