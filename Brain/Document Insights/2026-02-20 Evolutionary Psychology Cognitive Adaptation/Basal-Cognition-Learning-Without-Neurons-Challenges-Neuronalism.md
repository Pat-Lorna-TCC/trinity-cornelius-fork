# Basal Cognition - Learning Without Neurons Challenges Neuronalism

**Source**: "On the Prospects of Basal Cognition Research Becoming Fully Evolutionary: Promising Avenues and Cautionary Notes"

## Core Insight

Evidence from unicellular organisms - specifically the ciliate *Stentor coeruleus* and the slime mould *Physarum polycephalum* - demonstrates habituation (a basic form of learning: reduced response to repeated stimuli) without neuronal processing. This requires:

1. **Cognitive science to reconsider implementation constraints on learning**: If learning can occur without neurons, then neural architecture is not a necessary prerequisite for cognitive processes
2. **Evolutionary biology to broaden its concept of cognitive evolution beyond neural architecture**: The evolution of cognition extends back further and wider than brain evolution

---

## Evidence

**Study Organisms**:
- *Stentor coeruleus*: Single-celled ciliate that shows habituation to water jet stimuli - reduces avoidance response over repeated exposures without neurons
- *Physarum polycephalum*: Slime mould (acellular aggregation) that exhibits maze-solving, anticipatory behavior, and minimal learning behaviors without neurons

**What Is Demonstrated**:
- Habituation: A decrease in response to repeated stimuli (counted as associative-adjacent learning)
- Context-sensitivity: Organisms modify behavior based on past experience
- Memory-like processes: Carry information forward in time without neural substrate

**Significance**: If habituation (the simplest form of learning) exists in organisms without neurons, then neuronalism - the assumption that cognition requires neurons - is empirically falsified at the minimal cognition end.

---

## Context and Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging/controversial - the interpretation is contested; some argue habituation in unicellular organisms is not "cognition" but purely biochemical; the paper itself acknowledges cautionary notes
**Status**: Clear empirical evidence; interpretational debate ongoing
**The Conservative Position**: These are mechanistically different from neural learning and should not be called cognition
**The Progressive Position**: If functional behavior (adaptive response to past experience) is the criterion, then unicellular habituation meets it

## Why This Matters for the Knowledge Base

The vault has extensive coverage of consciousness (Buddhist no-self, neuroscience of consciousness) and AI cognition. The basal cognition finding connects to:

**For AI cognition**: If cognition is defined functionally (adaptive information processing based on past experience) rather than implementationally (requires neurons), then simple AI systems already meet some cognitive criteria - not as a metaphor but as a definitional issue

**For Buddhist epistemology**: Buddhist traditions generally do not require a self or neural substrate for experience - experience is a process, not a substance. Basal cognition research provides an empirical angle on this intuition: cognition as a functional property of information processing systems, not a neural property

**For evolutionary biology**: Cognitive evolution does not begin with nervous systems - it begins with any system that can carry adaptive information forward in time.
