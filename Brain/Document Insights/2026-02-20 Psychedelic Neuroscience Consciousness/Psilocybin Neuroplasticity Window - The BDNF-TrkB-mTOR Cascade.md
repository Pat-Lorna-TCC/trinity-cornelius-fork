## Core Insight

Psychedelics do not permanently rewire the brain - they create a window of **enhanced neural flexibility** that allows circuits to escape maladaptive patterns. A single oral dose of psilocybin induces approximately a **10% increase in dendritic spine density and size** in the medial prefrontal cortex and hippocampus, persisting for weeks. The mechanism is a shared cascade (with ketamine): glutamatergic activation → BDNF-TrkB → mTOR → synaptogenesis. This window is the neurobiological substrate for why the post-experience integration period is therapeutically critical.

---

## Evidence

**Established Mechanisms (2025 Review)**:
- Single oral dose psilocybin: **~10% increase** in dendritic spine density and size in medial PFC and hippocampus, persisting for weeks
- Psilocin (active metabolite) in human iPSC-derived cortical neurons: 5-HT2A-mediated augmentation of BDNF, enhanced neuronal complexity, increased synaptic protein expression
- LSD binds TrkB receptors with **greater affinity than SSRIs or ketamine**, acting as positive allosteric modulator of BDNF-TrkB signaling - partly independent of 5-HT2A

**Shared Cascade** (psychedelics AND ketamine):
```
Glutamatergic activation → BDNF-TrkB → mTOR → Synaptogenesis
```

**Epigenetic Effects (LSD)**:
- Repeated LSD significantly modulated methylation of **635 CpG sites**
- Expression of **178 proteins** in prefrontal cortex modulated (WGBS and proteomics)
- This is heritable epigenetic change, not just transient receptor occupancy

**Critical Biomarker Finding (2024 Meta-Analysis)**:
- **Peripheral blood BDNF does NOT reliably reflect CNS neuroplastic changes** in humans
- Peripheral BDNF as biomarker for psychedelic neuroplasticity is insufficient
- More direct neuroimaging measures are needed - this invalidates a common proxy measure used in earlier research

## The Psychoplastogen Framework

Rather than permanent structural rewiring, the most accurate framing is:

**Enhanced neural flexibility** - circuits gain the capacity to escape maladaptive attractors. The window of enhanced plasticity (days to weeks post-dose) is the therapeutic opportunity.

This reframes what psychedelic therapy actually does:
- Traditional antidepressants: suppress symptoms while maladaptive patterns persist
- Psilocybin: temporarily increases synaptic plasticity so circuits CAN reorganize, but reorganization requires the right inputs (integration therapy, new experiences, behavioral change)
- The drug creates opportunity; integration creates the outcome

**Why integration therapy matters**: Without directed experience during the plasticity window, the enhanced flexibility may not produce lasting change. The brain becomes more plastic but what it encodes depends on what it's exposed to.
