# REBAS - Psilocybin as Belief Revision Engine

## Core Framework

The REBUS model (Carhart-Harris & Friston, 2019) proposed that psilocybin works by **reducing the precision weighting of high-level priors** - temporarily weakening top-down beliefs so bottom-up information can update the world model. REBAS (2025) is the first empirical confirmation that this belief relaxation actually translates into post-acute **belief revision** - specifically, lasting reduction in negative self-beliefs.

**The Mechanism in One Sentence**: Psilocybin temporarily turns down the brain's dopaminergic "confidence signal" on existing beliefs, creating a window in which identity-constituting beliefs can update.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Double-blind, crossover; 11 healthy participants; 1 mg (active placebo) vs. 25 mg psilocybin, 4 weeks apart; EEG entropy measured during sessions; confidence ratings for personally held beliefs obtained before, during, and at 4-week follow-up
**Key Results**:
- Confidence in **negative self-beliefs significantly decreased** following 25 mg psilocybin (not 1 mg)
- Acute neural entropy (EEG) correlated with decreases in negative self-belief confidence **both acutely AND at 4-week follow-up**
- Intensity of subjective effects correlated with magnitude of belief revision
- Well-being improvements correlated with negative belief reduction
- Positive and neutral belief confidence showed **no significant change** - the effect is specific to negative self-beliefs

**Replication Status**: Small N (11); first empirical test of REBUS; requires replication
