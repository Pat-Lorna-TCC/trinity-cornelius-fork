# Psilocybin vs Escitalopram - The Existential Dimensions Advantage

**Source**: Barba T., Erritzoe D., Nutt D., et al. "Effect of psilocybin versus escitalopram on depression symptom severity... 6-month follow-up." eClinicalMedicine (The Lancet), September 21, 2024. https://www.thelancet.com/journals/eclinm/article/PIIS2589-5370(24)00378-X/fulltext

## Core Insight

At 6-month follow-up, psilocybin therapy and escitalopram (SSRI) show **equivalent** depression symptom reduction on the primary outcome measure. However, psilocybin shows significantly greater improvements on what can be called the "existential dimensions" of recovery - social functioning, psychological connectedness, meaning in life, and work functioning. This distinction - symptom suppression vs. existential reconfiguration - may be the most clinically important difference between the two treatment approaches.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Phase 2 double-blind RCT follow-up; 59 patients (30 psilocybin, 29 escitalopram); moderate-to-severe MDD; 6-month follow-up
**Primary Outcome**: QIDS-SR-16 score difference at 6 months: -1.51 (95% CI: -1.35, 4.38; p = 0.311)
- **No statistically significant difference** between treatments on primary depression symptom measure

**Secondary Outcomes Where Psilocybin Stood Out** (all with large effect sizes):
- Greater improvement in **social functioning**
- Greater improvement in **psychological connectedness**
- Greater improvement in **meaning in life**
- Greater improvement in **work functioning**
- Greater overall sense of **well-being**

**Expert Commentary (David Nutt)**: Psilocybin "disrupts the persistent ruminative loops of negative thinking that underpin depression."

## Why "Equivalent on Symptoms" Is Not the Full Story

The standard depression rating scales (MADRS, QIDS) measure symptom frequency and severity but do NOT measure:
- How people relate to their lives
- Sense of meaning and purpose
- Quality of social relationships
- Ability to engage in meaningful work
- Subjective sense of flourishing vs. merely not suffering

The REBAS mechanism predicts exactly this pattern:
- SSRIs suppress the neurochemical dysregulation that produces symptoms
- Psilocybin via REBAS changes the **identity-constituting beliefs** that organize the patient's relationship to themselves, others, and the world
- The result: equal symptom reduction, but fundamentally different quality of recovery
