## The Gap

The field of psychedelic medicine is bifurcating around a fundamental unresolved question: **Is the hallucinogenic experience necessary for therapeutic benefit, or is it delivery overhead that can be eliminated?** The two camps are supported by equally strong evidence, and the resolution of this tension will define the next decade of psychedelic pharmacology.

---

## Why This Matters

If the neuroplasticity camp is right:
- Non-hallucinogenic analogs could deliver therapeutic benefit without supervised 6-8 hour sessions
- Could be taken as daily oral pills like SSRIs
- Could be prescribed without specialized training or set/setting protocols
- Would democratize access dramatically

If the pro-experience camp is right:
- Non-hallucinogenic analogs will fail in larger trials despite positive molecular/animal data
- The supervised psychedelic therapy model is non-negotiable
- The identity transformation that produces lasting benefit requires ego dissolution
- We cannot shortcut the mechanism

---

## What We Know - The Two Camps

### Pro-Experience Camp
**Evidence**:
1. Mystical experience intensity is the strongest clinical predictor of positive outcomes (meta-analysis, 19,724 participants)
2. Experimentally disrupting the psychedelic experience (benzodiazepines) **significantly diminishes antidepressant effects**
3. REBAS: Negative self-belief reduction correlates with **subjective experience intensity**, not just dose
4. Psychological insights and emotional breakthroughs (not visual effects) predict outcomes - but these require a shifted state to access

**Key Notes**: [[Mystical Experience Is the Therapeutic Mechanism - Not a Side Effect]], [[REBAS - Psilocybin as Belief Revision Engine]]

### Neuroplasticity Camp
**Evidence**:
1. The 5-HT2A Gi pathway (hallucinogenic) and intracellular 5-HT2A (neuroplasticity) are structurally separable
2. Tabernanthalog (TBG) achieves cortical neuroplasticity via the same 5-HT2A → TrkB → mTOR → AMPA pathway as classic psychedelics, WITHOUT hallucinogenic effects
3. JRT (LSD analog with 2-atom transposition) promotes spinogenesis and depression/cognition effects without hallucinations in animal models
4. 10% increase in dendritic spine density is the molecular substrate - why would this require consciousness?

**Key Notes**: [[5-HT2A Receptor Gi Signaling - The Molecular Paradigm Shift]], [[Psilocybin Neuroplasticity Window - The BDNF-TrkB-mTOR Cascade]]

## The Possible Resolution

The most intellectually honest framing: these are not competing explanations but potentially **sequential stages of a multi-level mechanism**:

```
Mystical Experience (psychological level)
    → enables identity-level belief revision (REBAS)
    → neuroplasticity window encodes revision into structure
    → lasting therapeutic change
```

Removing the mystical experience may preserve step 3 (structural plasticity) while eliminating steps 1-2 (psychological transformation). Animal models cannot test this because animals cannot report identity-level belief revision.

**The critical test**: Non-hallucinogenic analog Phase 3 trials in humans with long-term psychological outcome measures (meaning, connectedness, identity) - not just symptom scales. This has NOT been done yet.
