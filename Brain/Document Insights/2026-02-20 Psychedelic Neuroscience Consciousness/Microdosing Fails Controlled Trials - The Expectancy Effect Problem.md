## Core Insight

The popular claims about microdosing - enhanced creativity, productivity, mood, and cognitive function - are not supported by controlled trial evidence. Controlled studies show a high null effect rate, and the meta-analysis (N=1,614) found a significant **decrease** in cognitive control with microdosing - the opposite of popular claims. The large gap between observational/naturalistic studies (consistently positive) and blinded RCTs (null or negative) is explained by **expectancy effects and confirmation bias**.

---

## Evidence

**Leiden University Trials (double-blind, placebo-controlled, longitudinal)**:
- Microdosing did NOT enhance self-reported mood or well-being vs. placebo
- No significant effects on behavioral or subjective measures vs. placebo
- Directly contradicts productivity and creativity claims

**Meta-Analysis (2025, 14 studies, N = 1,614, 59 effect sizes)**:
- Significant **decrease** in cognitive control with microdosing
- No detectable effects on other cognitive domains
- Effect NOT moderated by substance type (psilocybin vs. LSD), dose range, or duration

**Rapid Review (2024)**:
- Controlled studies show high null effect rate
- Large gap: naturalistic studies (positive) vs. controlled trials (null/negative)
- **Conclusion**: Gap explained by expectancy effects and confirmation bias in naturalistic settings

**Critical Distinction**:
- **High-dose psilocybin**: Large, significant antidepressant effects across multiple Phase 2-3 RCTs
- **Microdosing**: No comparable evidence in controlled conditions
- This is a dose-response relationship at the psychological level, consistent with mystical experience mediating outcomes

## Why This Is Epistemically Important

This is a textbook case of the knowledge base's dopamine-belief framework in action:

1. **Positive naturalistic reports** = confirmation bias in uncontrolled settings (people who believe in microdosing report positive effects - dopamine rewards confirmation)
2. **Blinded RCT null results** = when expectancy is controlled for, the effect disappears
3. **The cognitive control decrease finding** - this is the most striking: microdosing may actually **impair** certain cognitive functions while users believe they're being enhanced

The microdosing phenomenon also illustrates:
- Confirmation bias reinforces Identity through confirming Beliefs - Silicon Valley identity built around biohacking makes confirmation of microdosing benefits neurologically rewarding
- The gap between controlled and uncontrolled evidence = the exact size of the expectancy/confirmation bias effect

## The Mechanism Explanation

Why does microdosing fail to replicate benefits?

The REBAS and mystical experience research suggests the answer:
- Sub-perceptual doses don't produce neural entropy increases
- Without entropy increase, no precision-weighting reduction for priors
- Without precision-weighting reduction, no belief revision
- Without belief revision (especially of negative self-beliefs), no lasting therapeutic or cognitive benefit
- The mechanism requires disruption of the normal hierarchical prediction architecture - which microdoses don't achieve
