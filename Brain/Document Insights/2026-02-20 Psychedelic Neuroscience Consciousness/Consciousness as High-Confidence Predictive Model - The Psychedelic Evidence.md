## Core Framework

**Consciousness is a high-confidence predictive model. Psychedelics work by temporarily reducing the confidence weighting of that model - particularly the self-model - creating a window in which the brain can update its most fundamental beliefs.**

This framework, emerging from 2024-2025 psychedelic neuroscience, represents a testable, mechanistically grounded version of what contemplative traditions have described for millennia: the self is a construction, and releasing attachment to it (temporarily, via psychedelics; gradually, via meditation) is genuinely transformative.

## The Framework in Detail

### Normal Consciousness
- Brain generates a high-confidence predictive model of the world
- The "self" is the highest-confidence meta-prediction: "there is an agent experiencing these states"
- This self-model has strong prior weighting - it takes a lot of evidence to revise
- Dopaminergic confirmation loops maintain the confidence of existing beliefs (Finding a confirmation of the belief creates a spike of Dopamine)

### Psychedelic State
- 5-HT2A activation → Gi signaling → cortical hierarchy disruption
- Precision weighting of high-level priors is **reduced** (REBUS)
- Bottom-up information flows more freely; top-down control is attenuated
- Neural entropy increases (measurable via EEG)
- The self-model loses its confident status: "is there really a persistent experiencer here?"
- This is experienced as: boundary dissolution, unity, loss of time/space reference, mystical qualities

### Post-Acute Period
- The prior relaxation enables post-acute revision (REBAS)
- New information, new experiences, new framings can update the model more easily
- The most resistant beliefs (identity-constituting negative self-beliefs) are now updatable
- The neuroplasticity window (10% increase in dendritic spine density) provides structural substrate for encoding revisions
