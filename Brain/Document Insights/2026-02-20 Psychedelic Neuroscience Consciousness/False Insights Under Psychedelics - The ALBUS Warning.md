## Core Insight

The same mechanism that enables therapeutic belief revision (REBAS) can also entrench harmful false beliefs. Psychedelics increase both the **quantity AND subjective intensity** of insights, including false ones. An insight that "feels" profoundly true during a psychedelic experience has the same phenomenological signature as a therapeutically corrective insight - the brain cannot distinguish between a genuine belief correction and an entrenched false belief during the state of reduced top-down control.

---

## Evidence

**Communications Psychology, 2024**:
- Psychedelics increase both quantity and subjective intensity of insights - including FALSE ones
- The mechanism that relaxes precision weighting of valid priors ALSO relaxes the protective function of those priors against false information
- The heightened sense of "noetic quality" (feeling of profound truth, revelation) is pharmacologically generated - not evidence of truth
- Calls for research on minimizing risk of false/harmful belief formation in psychedelic contexts

**ALBUS Model (Oxford, 2025)**:
- At extreme doses: **breakdown of coherent metacognition** - the very system that normally evaluates insight quality
- Psychedelics can "both relax AND reinforce beliefs depending on dose, set, and setting" - this is NOT unidirectionally positive
- Set and setting (therapist, therapy room, music, pre-session priming) can direct what beliefs are formed during the window of reduced critical thinking

---

## The Mechanism Paradox

REBAS mechanism:
- Psilocybin relaxes precision weighting of high-level priors
- Valid negative self-beliefs update toward more accurate, positive representations
- This is therapeutic

False insight mechanism:
- The SAME relaxation of precision weighting removes critical evaluation of novel beliefs
- Whatever is encountered during the reduced-metacognition window can acquire "noetic quality" - feeling of profound truth
- If the content encountered is false, harmful, or manipulated, it can be encoded with the same confidence that was previously attached to the negative self-belief

**Example**: A therapist who primes a patient toward a specific belief before a session (intentionally or not) is leveraging the same mechanism that enables REBAS. This is simultaneously the therapeutic power AND the ethical risk of psychedelic therapy.

## Epistemic Implications

This finding connects directly to the vault's framework on belief and identity:

1. The brain's normal defense against false beliefs is the dopaminergic confirmation system - beliefs already held get rewarded, novel false beliefs get evaluated critically
2. Psychedelics temporarily disable this defense
3. During the window of disabled defense, the brain is maximally susceptible to belief formation - both corrective (therapeutic) AND false (potentially harmful)
4. The phenomenological indistinguishability of true and false insights under psychedelics makes this epistemically treacherous

**Practical implication**: The profound sense of revelation that many report after psychedelic experiences should not be automatically trusted as epistemically valid. The feeling of "I finally see the truth" has no special validity under these states - it is a pharmacological artifact that accompanies ALL belief formation during reduced precision weighting, regardless of accuracy.
