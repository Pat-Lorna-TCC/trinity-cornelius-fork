## Core Insight

Ego dissolution during psychedelic states has two distinct and newly measurable components: a **neural marker** (alpha wave collapse, now objectively measurable) and a **psychological bifurcation** (two qualitatively different experiences - ego loss vs. unity - with opposite emotional signatures). This bifurcation explains why ego dissolution sometimes produces fear and sometimes produces profound positive transformation.

---

## Evidence

### Alpha Wave Collapse (Journal of Neuroscience, November 2024)
- A clear **neurophysiological marker** of ego dissolution has been identified: collapse of alpha wave oscillations
- Alpha waves are associated with top-down cognitive control and self-referential processing
- During peak ego dissolution, alpha waves are reduced or eliminated
- This provides the first **objective, measurable neural correlate** for the subjective experience of boundary loss - dissolving the measurement problem in ego dissolution research

### Two-Subscale Model (Binghamton University, 2024)
Ego dissolution is NOT a single experience. Two qualitatively distinct subscales:

| Subscale | Neural Correlate | Emotional Signature | Outcome Prediction |
|----------|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Ego Loss** | Amygdala activation + anterior cingulate cortex (conflict monitoring) | Fear, threat, disorientation | Variable (set-dependent) |
| **Unity** | Positive valence circuits | Connectedness, oceanic boundlessness, awe | Therapeutic outcomes |

- **Ego Loss**: The system interprets self-dissolution as **existential threat** - the amygdala activates because the predictive self-model is crashing
- **Unity**: When dissolution is met with openness rather than resistance, the absence of self-boundary is experienced as expansion into connection
- Individual susceptibility to ego loss vs. unity predicted by **transliminality trait** - sensitivity to changes in internal and external environment

### ALBUS Model (Oxford, Neuroscience of Consciousness, 2025)
- At threshold doses: increased perceptual clarity, absorption (overlap with flow entry)
- At higher doses: near-complete blurring of imagination/reality
- At extreme doses: breakdown of coherent metacognition and objectified selfhood
- Critically: psychedelics can **both relax AND reinforce beliefs** depending on dose, set, and setting - not unidirectionally positive
