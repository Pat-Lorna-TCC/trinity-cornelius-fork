## Core Insight

Compass Pathways' COMP360 psilocybin has become the **first classic psychedelic to achieve statistically significant results across two Phase 3 trials** - crossing a landmark threshold in psychiatric drug development. With an NDA submission targeting Q4 2026, psilocybin therapy is positioned for FDA approval for treatment-resistant depression within the next 1-2 years.

---

## Evidence

**COMP005 (Phase 3, Trial 1, June 2025)**:
- Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; 258 participants; treatment-resistant depression; 32 US sites
- Result: Single dose COMP360 25 mg vs. placebo: **p < 0.001**; -3.6 points MADRS at Week 6
- Responder rate: **25%** showed clinically meaningful reduction (≥25% MADRS improvement)
- Safety: No new unexpected findings; adverse events mild-moderate, resolving within 24 hours
- Durability: Clinically meaningful responders maintained effect through Week 26 (COMP004 data)

**COMP006 (Phase 3, Trial 2, February 17, 2026)**:
- Design: Two fixed doses 3 weeks apart; COMP360 25 mg vs. 1 mg; multi-site international study
- Result: **p < 0.001**; -3.8 points MADRS at Week 6
- Responder rate: **39%** showed clinically meaningful reduction - significantly higher with two-dose protocol
- Average efficacy window: ~12 weeks per dose (COMP004 52-week follow-up)

**Historic Context**: First classic psychedelic to consistently achieve highly statistically significant results across two Phase 3 trials. Compass pursuing FDA NDA submission Q4 2026.

---

## Clinical Significance

For treatment-resistant depression (TRD) patients - defined as those who have failed at least 2 adequate antidepressant trials:
- Current options: ECT (electroconvulsive therapy), ketamine/esketamine (IV or nasal), MAOIs
- COMP360 would be the first oral, relatively long-lasting (12-week) option specifically targeting TRD
- The two-dose protocol achieving 39% responder rate is clinically meaningful for this notoriously difficult-to-treat population

**The 12-week window implication**: Average efficacy ~12 weeks per session; patients would likely need quarterly retreatment. This is a substantial practical and economic consideration, but similar to ketamine maintenance protocols.

---

## What Success Here Means for the Broader Field

The COMP360 success validates:
1. The trial design approach (learned from MDMA's failure)
2. That Phase 3 psychedelic trials are feasible and winnable
3. A regulatory roadmap that other developers can follow
4. That psilocybin's mechanism of action (REBAS, neuroplasticity) translates to clinical benefit in controlled conditions

The NDA approval pathway (targeting Q4 2026 NDA submission, approval potentially 2027-2028) would:
- Create the first Schedule I substance moving to Schedule II or lower for medical use since ketamine
- Establish reimbursement pathways and training requirements
- Legitimate the broader psychedelic therapy field

---

## Important Caveat

The COMP005/006 data is from **press releases** - full peer-reviewed publication pending. The -3.6 to -3.8 MADRS difference represents a moderate effect size. The 25-39% responder rate, while statistically significant, means 61-75% of TRD patients do NOT respond. The therapy is not a universal solution but a significant advancement for a subset of TRD patients.
