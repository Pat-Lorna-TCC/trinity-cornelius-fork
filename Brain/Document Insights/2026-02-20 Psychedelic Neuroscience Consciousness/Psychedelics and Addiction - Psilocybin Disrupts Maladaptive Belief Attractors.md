## Core Insight

Psilocybin shows significant effects in addiction, including 80% smoking cessation at 6 months (vs. <35% for conventional therapies) and meaningful alcohol use reductions. The mechanism is not substitution or aversive conditioning - it operates through REBAS-type belief revision targeting the **identity-constituting beliefs** that maintain addictive behavior ("I am an addict," "I need this to function"). A critical neural finding: psilocybin's AUD-specific effects correlate with blunted DMN hypoconnectivity driven by medial prefrontal regions.

---

## Evidence

**Alcohol Use Disorder (Phase 2 Data)**:
- Swiss single-center double-blind RCT (Lancet, March 2025): single 25 mg psilocybin + brief psychotherapy significantly reduced relapse rates vs. control
- Luquiens et al. (N=30 randomized): 25 mg vs. 1 mg; psilocybin's AUD effects correlated with **blunted DMN hypoconnectivity** in medial prefrontal regions; hypoconnectivity correlated **negatively** with alcohol relapse intensity
- Systematic review (16 studies): significant reductions in alcohol use across studies

**Smoking Cessation**:
- **80% cessation at 6 months** in psilocybin pilot (Johns Hopkins) - 10 of 15 at 12 months
- Comparison: <35% for other pharmacological therapies (varenicline, NRT, bupropion)
- The smoking cessation rate is the most striking single data point in the addiction literature

**Mechanisms in Addiction**:
- Psilocybin may relieve negative emotional states and stress (reducing the negative reinforcement cycle)
- Reduce cognitive inflexibility and compulsivity (the habitual response patterns)
- Update maladaptive beliefs about substance use via REBAS-type mechanisms ("I need alcohol to socialize" → revised with new evidence accessed during belief plasticity window)

## The Identity-Constituting Belief Theory of Addiction

Addiction can be understood through the REBAS framework and the vault's identity model:

1. "I am an addict" is an **identity-constituting belief** - it carries high dopaminergic precision weighting
2. The substance becomes entangled with identity ("drinking is who I am in my social group")
3. Standard treatments (CBT, 12-step, pharmacotherapy) work at the behavioral level but don't update the identity-level belief
4. Psilocybin via REBAS: temporarily reduces precision weighting on ALL high-level priors, including identity-constituting beliefs
5. During this window, the belief "I am an addict who needs this" loses its certainty, and new identity possibilities become accessible
6. The mystical experience at sufficient doses may enable patients to see themselves as entirely separate from the addiction identity

**The DMN connection**: Addiction involves blunted DMN connectivity (especially in AUD), which correlates with relapse. Psilocybin temporarily disrupts DMN patterns - this disruption appears to reset the maladaptive DMN attractor state that maintains the addiction loop.
