## Core Framework

By disrupting rigid neural patterns (specifically the high-precision-weighting maintenance of entrenched belief hierarchies), psychedelics foster **cognitive flexibility** - the capacity to consider alternative framings, update beliefs in light of new evidence, and escape cognitive attractors. This proposes therapeutic value for rigid thinking patterns including extremism, religious fundamentalism, and OCD.

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging; theoretical framework with limited direct human evidence for the extremism/fundamentalism application

---

## The Brain Network Framework

Cognitive rigidity is characterized by:
- Overactive top-down control networks (PFC maintaining high confidence in current beliefs)
- Underactive lateral/flexible processing
- Entrenched neural attractors - the brain keeps returning to the same thought patterns
- High precision weighting of current world model (especially identity-constituting beliefs)

Psychedelics disrupt this through:
- 5-HT2A activation at the apex of cortical hierarchy
- Reduction in precision weighting of high-level priors (REBUS)
- Increased neural entropy - the brain becomes less deterministic, more exploratory
- Disruption of default attractors

The result: **increased cognitive flexibility** - temporarily, but potentially allowing new patterns to be established during the plasticity window.
