# Social Learning Trade-Off - Value Shaping vs Decision Biasing Mechanisms

## Core Framework

Two distinct computational mechanisms govern how social information influences individual decision-making in collectives:

**Value Shaping (VS)**: Others' choices serve as pseudo-rewards that directly update your underlying valuations. You don't just change your behavior - you change what you think is good. Leads to rapid convergence but reduces flexibility when environments change.

**Decision Biasing (DB)**: Social information influences your behavior but not your underlying valuations. You act like others without updating what you value. Maintains flexibility but slows coordination.

Neither mechanism alone is optimal - **their coexistence is an evolutionarily stable outcome** that balances efficiency with adaptability.

## Why This Matters

In stable environments: Value Shaping wins - rapidly converge on what works, efficiency maximized.

In changing environments: Value Shaping becomes a liability - everyone has deeply updated their valuations toward the old solution, making adaptive re-learning extremely slow. Decision Biasing preserves the ability to shift when needed.

**Modern digital environments are unusually dynamic** - they change faster than VS can appropriately update valuations. This creates a systematic failure mode: populations using VS in changing environments become **maladaptively committed** to outdated solutions, with herding dynamics that accelerate rather than slow commitment.
