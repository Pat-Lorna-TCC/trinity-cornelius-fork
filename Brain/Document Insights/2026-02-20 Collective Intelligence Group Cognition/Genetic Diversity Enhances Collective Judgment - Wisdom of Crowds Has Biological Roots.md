# Genetic Diversity Enhances Collective Judgment - Wisdom of Crowds Has Biological Roots

**Source**: Barneron, M. (Hebrew University), "Genetically-diverse crowds are wiser" - Personality and Individual Differences, 2024. DOI: 10.1016/j.paid.2024.002836
**Document Type**: Research Paper
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-20

## Core Insight

Genetic diversity - not just cognitive or experiential diversity - directly enhances collective judgment quality. This grounds the "wisdom of crowds" mechanism at its deepest possible level: **diversity benefits operate even when controlling for environmental and experiential differences**. The biological basis for collective intelligence enhancement is now empirically established.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Two studies using twin pairs (both monozygotic and dizygotic); numerical judgment tasks
**Sample Size**: N=602 participants
**Key Results**:
- Unrelated pairs significantly outperformed co-twins on numerical judgment tasks
- Effect was present specifically for monozygotic (identical) twins - those with maximum genetic similarity showed maximum judgment correlation and thus minimum wisdom-of-crowds benefit
- First empirical demonstration that genetic diversity enhances collective judgment, controlling for environmental factors

**What This Rules Out**: Previous wisdom-of-crowds explanations could attribute benefits to experiential diversity (different backgrounds) or cognitive style diversity. This finding rules that out - even controlling for shared environment, greater genetic similarity reduces collective judgment quality.

---

## The Mechanism

Wisdom of crowds works because **independent errors cancel during aggregation**. For errors to be independent, the individuals making them must be genuinely uncorrelated in their processing. Genetic similarity creates correlated cognitive tendencies - similar biases, similar heuristics, similar blind spots. Even if two people have had very different life experiences, shared genetics produces residual cognitive correlation that limits the collective intelligence benefit.

The implication: **diversity requirements for collective intelligence have a hard floor at the biological level** that cannot be overcome by training, experience, or culture.

---

## Organizational Implication (Contrarian)

Hiring for "culture fit" likely destroys collective intelligence. Culture fit selects for cognitive similarity - similar values, similar thinking styles, similar intuitions. While this creates social cohesion, it maximizes the correlated errors that eliminate wisdom-of-crowds benefits.

**Diversity interventions should consider not just functional diversity (skills, roles) but fundamental cognitive diversity** that correlates with genetic background. The diversity research that focuses only on gender and race may be tracking the right variables (genetic diversity) for the wrong reasons.
