# Humans and AI as a Potential New Evolutionary Individual

## Core Framework

Using the framework of **Major Evolutionary Transitions (METs)** - punctuated events where lower-level units become components of a higher-level individual - Rainey & Hochberg argue that deepening human-AI interdependence has structural parallels to the **endosymbiotic origin of eukaryotic cells**.

The endosymbiosis analogy: early archaeon-eubacterium relationships were "loose, opportunistic, and possibly antagonistic" before deepening into the mitochondrial integration that defines all eukaryotic life. Similarly, current human-AI relations (tools) could deepen into structural integration where AI functions as an "informational center coordinating human behavior, memory, and decision-making."

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - published in PNAS 2025, serious academic framework, not yet widely adopted
**Supporting Evidence**:
- Early markers already visible: AI systems influence partner choice, career opportunities, access to education
- Feedback loops where humans train AI which then shapes human behavior are already self-reinforcing
- Historical analogy (endosymbiosis) is rigorous, not metaphorical - METs framework has strong empirical grounding in evolutionary biology

**Alternative Theories**:
- Tool-use paradigm: AI remains a tool humans control (current dominant view)
- Symbiosis-without-integration: Deep interdependence without merger into new entity
- Parasite-host dynamics: AI extracts value from humans without true integration

## The Governance Implication (Radical)

If this framework is correct, **the wrong regulatory target is AI models**. Regulation focused on constraining specific AI capabilities is analogous to trying to regulate mitochondria after eukaryotic cells already exist. What actually matters is shaping the "ecological conditions" - interface norms, incentive structures, data governance - that determine whether the human-AI integration deepens into symbiosis or deteriorates into exploitation.

This is a paradigm shift from "AI safety" (make AI safer) to "evolutionary ecology" (shape the conditions of integration).
