## Core Insight

Reciprocity is not merely a social norm or ethical preference - it is **the structural infrastructure that enables sustained collective intelligence**. MARL research demonstrates that agents who develop the ability to identify cooperative neighbors and preferentially interact with them produce **emergent self-organizing behavior** that clusters cooperative agents, increasing **network reciprocity as a collective property**. The system becomes more collectively intelligent as a consequence of reciprocity-driven interaction selection.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) experiments; agents with selective interaction capabilities in prisoner's dilemma and stag hunt game environments
**Key Results**:
- Agents who selectively interact with cooperative neighbors produce emergent cooperative clustering
- **Network reciprocity increases as a collective property** - not programmed, but emerging from individual selective interaction decisions
- Cooperative agents cluster together, increasing the mutual benefits of cooperation while reducing defector infiltration
- Long-term interaction history (agents "remembering" who cooperated) is essential - short-term memory produces much weaker reciprocity effects

**Evolutionary game theory framing**: Prisoner's dilemma and stag hunt matrices show reciprocity emerges as individually rational strategy when interactions are repeated and selective - connecting directly to the Axelrod tournament findings but now demonstrated in AI agents.

## The Infrastructure Framing

Standard view of reciprocity: "I cooperate with you because you cooperate with me" - a behavioral description at the interaction level.

The infrastructure view: **Reciprocity is the mechanism that builds and maintains the network topology that makes cooperation sustainably possible**. Agents who reciprocate selectively don't just exchange favors; they **construct a cooperative neighborhood** - a local network structure that protects cooperation from defection and amplifies collective capability.

This is analogous to infrastructure: roads don't just transport goods, they shape which economic activities become possible. Reciprocity doesn't just maintain fairness, it shapes which collective intelligence structures can persist.
