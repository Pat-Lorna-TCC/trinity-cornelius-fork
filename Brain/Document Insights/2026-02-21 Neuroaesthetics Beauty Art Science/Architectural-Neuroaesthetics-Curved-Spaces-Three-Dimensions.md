# Architectural Neuroaesthetics - Curved Spaces, Three Coherence-Fascination-Hominess Dimensions, and Neurosustainability

## Core Insight

Interior spaces with curved boundaries produce distinct EEG oscillation patterns compared to rectilinear spaces. Three psychological dimensions explain approximately 90% of variance in architectural aesthetic response. The "neurosustainability" framework proposes that designed environments can be systematically engineered to sustain hippocampal, cortical, and amygdala functioning through spatial, natural, aesthetic, and social enrichment.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: VR-EEG paradigm with curved vs. rectilinear interior spaces
**Key Results**:
- Curved boundaries: distinct EEG oscillation patterns vs. rectilinear spaces
- Three dimensions explain ~90% of architectural aesthetic response variance
- Curved forms systematically preferred over angular forms (cross-study consistent)

**The Three Dimensions:**
1. **Coherence** - Organizational clarity; does the space feel orderly and navigable?
2. **Fascination** - Visual richness and complexity; does the space engage sustained attention?
3. **Hominess** - Comfort and personalization; does the space feel safe and inhabited?

These three dimensions together explain why some spaces feel immediately inhabitable while others feel hostile.

---

## The Evolutionary Explanation for Curved Preference

Curved architectural forms are consistently preferred over angular forms. The evolutionary account: curved forms signal:
- Organic origin (plants, terrain, water) - associated with safety and resources
- Absence of threat (sharp edges signal weapons, predators, damage)
- Habitat abundance (smooth, rounded terrain = habitable environment)

This preference may be mediated by the amygdala's automatic threat-detection system: angular forms activate mild amygdala threat responses; curved forms suppress them.

---

## The Neurosustainability Framework

The "neurosustainability" framework proposes that designed environments should be evaluated not just on aesthetics but on their **long-term effects on neural functioning**:

- **Hippocampal health**: Spatial richness and navigational complexity maintain hippocampal engagement
- **Cortical engagement**: Visual complexity and fascination prevent cortical understimulation
- **Amygdala regulation**: Aesthetic safety cues (curves, nature elements, coherence) reduce chronic amygdala activation

Current built environments - hospitals, schools, offices - may be chronically **neurotoxic** in this framework: visually monotonous, rectilinear, lacking coherence or fascination, without nature elements. The aesthetic poverty of modern institutional environments may directly degrade cognitive and emotional functioning.

---

## Practical Applications

- **Hospital design**: Curved walls, biophilic elements, and high-coherence spatial organization are measurably beneficial, not aesthetic luxuries
- **School design**: Fascination-optimized environments may enhance learning by preventing cortical understimulation
- **Workplace design**: Hominess dimension (personalization, comfort) predicts wellbeing beyond mere aesthetic preference
