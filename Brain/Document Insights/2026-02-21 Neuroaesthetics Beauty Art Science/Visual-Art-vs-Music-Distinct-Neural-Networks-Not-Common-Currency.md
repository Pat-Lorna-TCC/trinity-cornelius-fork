# Visual Art and Music Use Categorically Distinct Neural Networks - No Common Aesthetic Currency

**Source**: Luo, Y., Yu, Q., Wu, S., Luo, Y.-J. "Distinct Neural Bases of Visual Art- and Music-Induced Aesthetic Experiences." *NeuroImage*, Vol. 305, 2025. Epub December 3, 2024. https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1053811924004592

## Core Insight

A landmark meta-analysis of 68 fMRI studies (~1,410 participants) definitively challenges the long-standing hypothesis of a "common neural currency" for aesthetic experience. Visual art and music appreciation activate **categorically distinct brain networks** - not just different regions within the same system.

- **Visual art**: Frontal pole (FP), ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC), inferior frontal gyrus (IFG) - regions associated with introspective and self-referential processing
- **Music**: Bilateral superior temporal gyrus (STG) and striatal areas - regions tied to auditory processing and reward

Visual art appreciation is more **introspective and viewer-driven**; music appreciation is more **stimulus-driven and outward-facing**.

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Neuroimaging meta-analysis using Activation Network Modelling
**Sample Size**: 68 fMRI studies (34 visual art, 34 music); ~1,410 combined participants
**Key Results**: "Quite distinct brain networks" confirmed by Activation Network Modelling
**Replication**: Meta-analytic across 68 studies; robust statistical methodology
**Citation**: *NeuroImage*, Vol. 305, 2025

## Why This Is Significant

The "aesthetic triad" model (sensory-motor, emotion-valuation, meaning-knowledge) may need **modality-specific elaboration**. The aesthetic experience of a Vermeer painting and a Beethoven symphony are more neurologically different than previously believed.

This has practical implications:
- Therapeutic applications of visual art vs. music work through fundamentally different mechanisms
- You cannot generalize findings from music aesthetics to visual art aesthetics
- "Neuroaesthetics" may be studying several distinct phenomena that share phenomenological surface features but different underlying mechanisms

---

## Contrarian Insight: The Aesthetic Triad Needs a Fourth Dimension

Visual art's reliance on self-referential (vmPFC/IFG) processing vs. music's reliance on reward/auditory (STG/striatum) processing suggests that aesthetic experience is better characterized as a **family of related but distinct processes** than as one unified phenomenon. The key variable is whether the dominant mode of engagement is introspective (art) or immersive/stimulus-following (music).

This maps onto the distinction between: the viewer *interpreting* meaning vs. the listener *being carried by* rhythm.
