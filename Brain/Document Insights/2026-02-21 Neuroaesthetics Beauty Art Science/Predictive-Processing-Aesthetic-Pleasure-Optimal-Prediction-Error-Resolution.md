## Core Framework

Aesthetic pleasure is the hedonic reward the brain generates when prediction errors are resolved in an *optimally calibrated* way - neither too predictable (boring) nor too unpredictable (random/overwhelming). Art that generates pleasure works by building up expectations and then violating them in rewarding, ultimately resolvable ways. This is not metaphor - it maps directly onto the brain's predictive processing architecture.

The key mechanism: the transition from **uncertainty** to **increased predictability** generates dopaminergic reward. Musical chills (frisson) represent the somatic signature of a sharp, successful prediction error resolution event.

---

## How This Reframes Art

Under the predictive processing (PP) framework:

- **Artists are expectation engineers**: They build up predictions, then violate them in ways that ultimately resolve
- **Aesthetic complexity is calibrated pedagogy**: Complex art rewards sustained attention because it trains predictive hierarchies over time
- **Harmonic resolution feels pleasurable**: Because it resolves the prediction error created by tension
- **Inverted-U aesthetic (Berlyne/Wundt curve) has a mechanistic basis**: Both maximally predictable and maximally unpredictable stimuli should be aesthetically unsatisfying - confirming the "optimal complexity" observation with a neural mechanism
- **Film schema theory (Gombrich) maps onto predictive hierarchies**: Prior knowledge shapes film aesthetic response through the same mechanism

---

## Contrarian Insight: Aesthetic Experience Is Accelerated Learning

If aesthetic pleasure is the reward for optimal prediction error resolution, then aesthetic experience is a form of *accelerated learning* - it trains predictive hierarchies. This reframes art not as a luxury overlay on survival circuits but as a precision instrument for calibrating the predictive brain.

Art education is not decorative but cognitively constitutive: it trains the very mechanisms by which the brain handles uncertainty, novelty, and pattern completion.
