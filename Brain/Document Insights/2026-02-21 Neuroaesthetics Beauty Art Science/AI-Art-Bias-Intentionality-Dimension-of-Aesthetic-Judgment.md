## Core Insight

When humans know artwork was generated by AI, they systematically rate it lower - even when they cannot visually distinguish it from human-made art. The bias operates before conscious deliberation: EEG studies show knowledge of AI origin modulates neural responses within 150-200ms of seeing the artwork. This reveals that **aesthetic judgment is not purely perceptual** - it contains an irreducible intentionality-attribution dimension processed through social cognition circuits.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Experimental aesthetics studies with AI vs. human attribution conditions (ETH Zurich team)
**Key Results**:
- Systematic rating downgrade when AI origin is disclosed, across multiple studies
- Bias persists even when participants cannot visually distinguish AI from human art
- EEG shows neural modulation within 150-200ms - before conscious deliberation
- Bias appears to reflect assumptions about intentionality, effort, and authentic emotional communication
**Replication**: Cross-study convergence across neuroaesthetics, social cognition, and art history frameworks

---

## The Three Layers of Aesthetic Judgment

This research reveals aesthetic experience has at least three distinct layers:

1. **Perceptual layer** (0-200ms): Basic visual processing; identical for human and AI art when disclosed origin is masked
2. **Intentionality layer** (150-200ms): Social-cognitive attribution of mind, effort, intention; immediately modulated by knowledge of AI origin
3. **Post-hoc layer** (conscious deliberation): Justifications for the already-modified response

The intentionality layer operates at a speed (~150ms) that is automatic rather than deliberate - suggesting the social-cognitive component of aesthetic judgment is hardwired, not learned.

---

## Contrarian Insight: Aesthetic Experience Is Partly a Social Act

Purely perceptual and neurobiological models of aesthetics (beauty = mOFC activation) miss a fundamental component: **aesthetic pleasure is partly constituted by perceiving the work as the expression of a mind**. When that attribution is removed (AI origin), pleasure decreases - not because the perceptual stimulus changed, but because the social encounter changed.

This means:
- Art is not just sensory input - it is a social transaction
- The "meaning-knowledge system" component of the aesthetic triad (vmPFC, DMN) includes attributions of intentionality and effort
- A world with indistinguishable AI art does not simply "democratize beauty" - it alters the social substrate of aesthetic experience in ways that systematically reduce reported pleasure

---

## The Intentionality Question in Broader Context

An artist's perceived *moral character* also affects aesthetic judgments of their paintings (Penn Center for Neuroaesthetics, 2026 advance online). This confirms that aesthetic judgment consistently imports social-cognitive content beyond pure perception.

Artists who are perceived as immoral or unethical produce work judged as less beautiful - even when the work is unchanged. Intentionality attribution is a genuine component of aesthetic processing, not a cognitive error to be corrected.
