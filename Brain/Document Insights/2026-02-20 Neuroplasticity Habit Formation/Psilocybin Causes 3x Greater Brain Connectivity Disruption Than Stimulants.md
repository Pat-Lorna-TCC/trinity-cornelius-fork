# Psilocybin Causes 3x Greater Brain Connectivity Disruption Than Stimulants

**Source**: "Psilocybin Desynchronizes the Human Brain" - Nature (2024)
**Document Type**: Research Paper (Precision Functional Mapping, High-Quality Design)
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)

## Core Insight

Psilocybin causes **more than threefold greater disruption** to functional brain connectivity than methylphenidate (Ritalin), with effects that are both rapid (acute) and persistent (lasting 3 weeks post-dose). The disruption is global - affecting both cortex and subcortex - and appears to create a window of **neural flexibility** for pattern change. Critically, the disruption targets the Default Mode Network, the same system that maintains habitual self-narratives.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Precision functional mapping (individual-level brain tracking)
**Sample**: Multiple participants tracked across ~18 MRI visits each
**Protocol**: Before, during, and 3 weeks after high-dose psilocybin (25mg) vs. methylphenidate (40mg)
**Comparison**: Direct pharmacological comparison, same participants, controlled design

**Key Findings**:
- Psilocybin: >3x greater functional connectivity disruption than methylphenidate
- Effects: Rapid (acute onset) and **persistent** (3 weeks post-dose)
- Scope: Global - cortex AND subcortex affected
- Mechanism: Three converging pathways:
  1. Elevated BDNF expression promoting synaptic plasticity
  2. Dendritic spine density increases in medial PFC and hippocampus (lasting weeks)
  3. Default Mode Network disruption - reducing self-referential processing that maintains habitual patterns

**Clinical outcomes** (2024-2025 trials):
- 58% depression remission rate at 12 months
- 71% of PTSD patients experiencing lasting relief (MDMA-assisted therapy)
- 86% maintaining alcohol abstinence (ketamine therapy)

**Citation**: Nature (2024) - https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07624-5

## The Mechanism: Why Disruption Enables Change

The "disruption = opportunity" model for psilocybin:

1. Functional connectivity encodes habitual patterns (neural routines become connectivity patterns)
2. Psilocybin disrupts these patterns globally - essentially "resetting" default connectivity
3. During the post-disruption period, new connectivity patterns can form more easily
4. The DMN disruption specifically weakens the self-narrative that maintains identity-linked habits

**The persistence paradox**: The disruption is temporary (hours), but the effects last weeks. This is explained by the dendritic spine density increases - psilocybin creates new synaptic infrastructure that outlasts the acute pharmacological effect.

## Non-Hallucinogenic Alternative - Tabernanthalog (TBG)

A key emerging development: **Tabernanthalog (TBG)** - a non-hallucinogenic psychoplastogen from Nature Neuroscience - promotes neuroplasticity through 5-HT2AR activation WITHOUT causing hallucinations or acute glutamate storms. This represents a potential path to the plasticity benefits of psilocybin without the subjective experience requirements.
