# ASD Reframed as Body-Signal Translation Disorder

**Source**: "Interoception in individuals with autism spectrum disorder: a systematic literature review and meta-analysis" - Frontiers in Psychiatry, 2025 - https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2025.1573263/full; "Links between facets of alexithymia, self-rated interoception, and social perception" - Frontiers in Psychology, January 2026 - https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1669056/full

## Core Insight

ASD (Autism Spectrum Disorder) is commonly understood as a social cognition disorder - difficulty reading other people's emotions and intentions. The 2025 meta-analysis suggests a reframe: ASD is primarily a **body-signal translation disorder**. Social difficulties emerge *downstream* from a more fundamental impairment: the inability to connect interoceptive signals to emotional concepts. Approximately 50% of ASD cases have alexithymia mediated specifically by interoceptive deficits.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Systematic literature review and meta-analysis
**Key Statistics**:
- **~50%** of individuals with ASD exhibit comorbid alexithymia mediated by interoceptive deficits
- **74%** of autistic adults report significant interoceptive confusion unless bodily signals are extreme
- "Alexisomia" (difficulty reading somatic signals) correlates strongly with Toronto Alexithymia Scale scores
- The validated "Interoception Sensory Questionnaire" (ISQ) now provides clinical measurement for autistic populations

**Alexithymia Causal Chain** (from second paper):
- Impaired interoceptive self-awareness
- → Difficulty connecting body signals to emotional concepts
- → Emotion regulation difficulties
- → Alexithymia (inability to identify and describe emotions)
- → Social perception deficits

**Critical insight**: Alexithymia in ASD is not the inability to experience emotions, nor the absence of bodily signals. It is the **failure to connect interoceptive signals to emotional concepts**. The signals are present; the translation layer is impaired.

---

## The Translation Layer Concept

Normal emotional processing:
```
Body signal (elevated heart rate, gut tension)
→ Interoceptive awareness ("something is happening in my body")
→ Emotional concept assignment ("I am anxious / excited")
→ Emotional awareness ("I feel anxious")
→ Social communication ("I'm feeling nervous about this")
```

ASD interoceptive deficit:
```
Body signal (elevated heart rate, gut tension)
→ BLOCK: Cannot reliably connect signal to concept
→ Confusion or misidentification ("Something is wrong but I don't know what")
→ Social difficulty: Cannot name emotion, cannot communicate it, appears disconnected
```

This model explains why autistic individuals often seem unaware of emotions in real-time but can retrospectively identify them with coaching, or experience them at extreme intensities (when signals become unmissable).

---

## Why This Reframing Matters

**Previous Model**: ASD = Theory of Mind deficit = difficulty understanding other people's mental states
**Limitation**: Does not explain the 74% interoceptive confusion finding or why interoceptive training helps social outcomes

**New Model**: ASD = Interoceptive translation deficit → downstream alexithymia → downstream social difficulty
**Advantage**:
- Explains interoceptive confusion as primary, not incidental
- Points to interoceptive training as a first-line intervention, not just social skills training
- Explains the heterogeneity: ~50% of ASD cases with alexithymia are those with the interoceptive deficit; the other 50% may have different pathways
- Reduces stigma: the "social deficit" is a downstream consequence, not a core trait

---

## Clinical Implications

**Assessment**: Use ISQ (Interoception Sensory Questionnaire) alongside existing ASD assessments to identify interoceptive subtype
**Intervention Sequence**:
1. Interoceptive training (build body-to-concept translation)
2. Emotion identification from body signals
3. Emotion regulation
4. Social skills
**Expected Outcome**: Addressing the root (interoceptive translation) should improve alexithymia and social outcomes more effectively than directly training social skills
