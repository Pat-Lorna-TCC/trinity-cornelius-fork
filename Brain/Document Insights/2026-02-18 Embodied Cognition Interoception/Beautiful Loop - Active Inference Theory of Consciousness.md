# Beautiful Loop - Active Inference Theory of Consciousness

## Core Framework

Consciousness emerges from a "strange loop" in predictive processing - when a system's world model contains knowledge of its own existence, recursive interoceptive loops turn back upon themselves, generating subjective experience. This is not a metaphor but a formal theoretical account: three specific conditions are necessary and together sufficient for consciousness to arise.

---

## The Three Conditions

**1. Epistemic Field**
A world model that determines what can be known or acted upon. The model generates the space of possible inferences.

**2. Bayesian Binding**
Inferential competition where only coherence-increasing inferences enter consciousness - a "selection for consciousness." Not all predictions reach awareness; only those that increase the coherence of the overall model.

**3. Epistemic Depth**
Recurrent sharing of Bayesian beliefs throughout the system. The system's predictions must turn back upon themselves. When the world model contains knowledge of its own existence, the recursive loop closes and consciousness emerges.

---

## The Interoceptive Role

Crucially, interoception is not subordinate to cognition in this framework - it has **veto power**:

- At the lowest level of the hierarchy, the organism receives inputs from interoceptive, proprioceptive, visceromotor, immune, neuroendocrine, and gustatory systems
- Inputs from **homeostatic interoception can override** attenuation by the executive/metacognitive system
- The body can break through even strong top-down predictions
- This explains why extreme body states (pain, visceral urgency) dominate consciousness regardless of cognitive intent

---

## Explanatory Power for Altered States

The theory accounts for altered states as modifications to precision-weighting of the recursive loop:

| State | Mechanism | Phenomenology |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Deep meditation | Reduced precision on self-referential loops | Reduced sense of self, expanded awareness |
| Psychedelics | Increased weight on sensory/interoceptive signals | Heightened body sensation, reduced narrative self |
| Flow states | Reduced epistemic depth of self-monitoring | Selfless absorption in action |
| Depression | Miscalibrated visceromotor predictions biasing the loop | Persistent negative self-model |
| Sleep | Reduced epistemic field and binding | Loss of coherent self-model |

## Context and Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - proposed by prominent theorists (Friston) but not yet empirically validated
**Supporting Evidence**: Consistent with predictive processing literature; accounts for known phenomenological data
**Alternative Theories**: Integrated Information Theory (Tononi), Global Workspace Theory (Baars), Controlled Hallucination (Seth)
**Distinguishing Feature**: The only theory that formally requires interoception as necessary (not incidental) to consciousness
