## Core Framework

**Allostatic Interoceptive Overload (AIO)** occurs when the mismatch between **anticipated energy expenditure** and **actual energy required** becomes chronic. This creates a cascade: hypervigilance → inflammation → metabolic imbalance → neurotoxicity. AIO is proposed as a transdiagnostic mechanism underlying anxiety, depression, PTSD, Alzheimer's disease, and frontotemporal dementia. Different conditions represent different points of collapse in the same allostatic-interoceptive system.

---

## The Mechanism

**Normal Allostatic Function**:
1. Brain predicts upcoming energy needs
2. Body pre-allocates resources (stress hormones, immune readiness, metabolic changes)
3. Prediction proves accurate - resources used and recovered
4. System returns to baseline

**Allostatic Overload**:
1. Brain generates **chronically inflated predictions** of upcoming demands
2. Body pre-allocates resources that are never needed
3. Chronic resource allocation → chronic physiological activation
4. No recovery → accumulating allostatic load
5. System cannot return to baseline → dysregulation becomes baseline

**The Interoceptive Failure**:
- The inflated predictions are generated from faulty interoceptive inference
- Body signals are misread as indicating higher threat/demand than actually present
- Or: accurate body signals are over-weighted (precision too high), generating false urgency
- Result: body is perpetually preparing for emergencies that don't materialize

## Transdiagnostic Coverage

The Biological Psychiatry review covers how AIO manifests across conditions:

| Condition | AIO Manifestation |
|-----------|------------------|
| Anxiety | Chronic anticipatory activation; body perpetually preparing for threat |
| Depression | Depleted allostatic reserves; system collapsed after prolonged overload |
| PTSD | Allostatic overload triggered by trauma-related interoceptive cues |
| Alzheimer's | Chronic neuroinflammation from sustained allostatic overload; neurotoxicity |
| Frontotemporal Dementia | Visceromotor cortex degeneration (the allostatic command center) |

**Shared Biological Mechanisms Documented**:
- Whole-body cascades of inflammation
- Brain structure changes in allostatic-interoceptive network
- Heart-brain interaction dysregulation
- Respiratory-brain interaction dysregulation
- Gut-brain-microbiota axis disruption
- Peripheral inflammatory and immune processes
- Epigenetic pathway changes

## Why This Is a Paradigm Shift

**Previous approach**: Treat each condition separately with condition-specific interventions
**AIO approach**: Identify the shared upstream mechanism and intervene at that level

If AIO is real, then:
- A shared biomarker (allostatic load measure) should predict multiple conditions
- Interventions that reduce AIO should be transdiagnostically beneficial
- The chronic co-morbidity of anxiety and depression makes sense: same system at different stages of overload
- Neurodegeneration may be a late-stage consequence of decades of AIO beginning in midlife
