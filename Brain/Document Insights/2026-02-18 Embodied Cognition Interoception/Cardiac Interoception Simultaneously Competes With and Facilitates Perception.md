# Cardiac Interoception Simultaneously Competes With and Facilitates Perception

**Source**: "Interoception vs. Exteroception: Cardiac interoception competes with tactile perception, yet also facilitates self-relevance encoding" - PNAS, 2025 - PMID: 41329741 - https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2516229122

## Core Insight

The heart does not simply distract from or simply enhance external perception. It does **both simultaneously**, through **independent mechanisms in distinct brain regions**. This is not a paradox but a resolution: the long-standing debate about whether cardiac interoception helps or hurts external perception was based on the false assumption that it must do one or the other.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Heartbeat-evoked potential (HEP) study measuring how cardiac cycle phase affects external perception
**Measurement**: HEP amplitudes (brain electrical activity evoked by each heartbeat) across different cortical regions
**Key Findings**:

**Competition Effect**:
- Prestimulus HEP amplitudes over **somatosensory cortex** correlated with **slower reaction times** to tactile stimuli
- Interpretation: When the brain is processing cardiac signals in somatosensory cortex, it has fewer resources available for incoming tactile information
- Shared neural substrate creates competition

**Facilitation Effect**:
- HEPs over **integrative sensorimotor regions and default-mode network** facilitated **self-relevance encoding**
- Interpretation: Heartbeat processing in DMN-connected regions enhances the meaning-attribution system, making stimuli seem more personally relevant
- Distinct neural substrate enables a different, simultaneous effect

---

## Why This Resolves a Long-Standing Debate

**Previous Position 1**: Cardiac interoception competes with external perception (evidence: slower reaction times during systole)
**Previous Position 2**: Cardiac interoception enhances external perception (evidence: improved self-relevance attribution)

**Actual Resolution**: Both are true, operating in parallel through different neural pathways. The PLOS Biology 2025 field review confirmed "both accounts of how interoception influences perception, reflecting independent mechanisms in distinct brain regions."

---

## Implications

**For Attention Research**:
- Multi-tasking with internal and external demands is not just about cognitive load - it involves literal shared neural substrates at the level of primary sensory cortex
- But the internal-external interface is not simply competitive: the body simultaneously contextualizes external information

**For Self-Relevance and Meaning**:
- The heart's signal processing in DMN regions is part of how the brain decides "this matters to me"
- Emotion may be partly an interoceptive labeling process: the heartbeat helps determine whether an event is personally significant
- This may explain why people feel more "connected" to experiences during moments of strong bodily arousal

**For Cardiac Timing in Decision-Making**:
- The bioRxiv March 2025 study (somatic marker mechanism) found cardiac systole timing directly shifts decision transition probabilities
- The PNAS facilitation finding provides a complementary mechanism: cardiac activity doesn't just compete with decisions, it contextualizes their self-relevance
- Together: cardiac signals both constrain (competition) and frame (facilitation) how we process and decide
