# Depression Begins in Visceromotor Cortex Not Cognition

## Core Insight

Depression is not primarily a cognitive distortion disorder. It is an **interoceptive misprediction disorder** originating in the agranular visceromotor cortex. The visceromotor cortex generates chronically biased predictions about the body's upcoming autonomic, metabolic, and immunological needs. This corrupts the interoceptive signal chain from the ground up, and negative thinking is downstream - not causal - of this bodily misprediction.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Systematic review + meta-analysis of neuroimaging studies
**Key Finding**: The **mid-dorsal insula** represents the transdiagnostic hub - disrupted connectivity appears across depression, anxiety, eating disorders, and substance use disorders
**Mechanism Identified**: Reduced insula-ACC connectivity in both depressive and anxiety disorders
**Active Inference Account**: "Depressive disorders arise from aberrant interoceptive predictions from the agranular visceromotor cortex" - these abnormal predictions generate false alarms about upcoming autonomic, metabolic, and immunological needs, chronically activating physiological stress responses

---

## The Causal Chain

Traditional understanding (being challenged):
```
Negative thoughts → Emotional suffering → Body symptoms
```

Emerging active inference account:
```
Visceromotor cortex misprediction
→ False alarms about body's upcoming needs
→ Chronic activation of physiological stress response
→ Actual body symptoms (fatigue, anhedonia, inflammation)
→ Cognitive narrative built around body experience ("I am worthless")
```

The implication: Cognitive behavioral therapy addresses the downstream narrative, not the root cause.

---

## Why This Matters Clinically

**Treatment Implication**: Interoceptive interventions should be first-line for depression, not adjunctive. If the root cause is visceromotor misprediction, then:
- Directly training interoceptive precision may be more effective than cognitive reframing
- Body-based therapies (yoga, somatic work, mindfulness of physical sensations) target the cause
- Talk therapy that ignores body may only treat the symptom

**The Inflexible Precision Finding**: Depressed patients cannot adapt interoceptive precision weighting during physiological challenges (e.g., breath-holding). Healthy individuals increase cardiac signal precision when the body changes state; depressed patients do not. This inflexibility prevents accurate perception of changing body states.

**Bidirectional with Metabolism**: A companion Science Advances paper (Mehrhof et al. 2025) proposes that broken interoceptive inference creates the observed bidirectional link between depression and metabolic dysfunction (obesity, diabetes). Same upstream failure, two manifestations.
