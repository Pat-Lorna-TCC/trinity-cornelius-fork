# Genuine LLM Emergence Requires Mechanistic Variables Not Just Performance Jumps

**Source**: Krakauer, D.C., Mitchell, M. et al. (Santa Fe Institute). "Large Language Models and Emergence: A Complex Systems Perspective." arXiv:2506.11135, June 2025. https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11135

## Core Framework

Applying the full complexity science framework to LLMs, Krakauer and Mitchell (Santa Fe Institute) establish a rigorous standard: "emergence" in LLMs should not merely mean surprising or unpredictable task performance. Genuine emergence requires identifying *coarse-grained mechanistic variables* - reduced internal degrees of freedom that form effective mechanisms explaining higher-level behavior. Analogous to how temperature "emerges" from molecular motion.

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - represents the complexity science community's highest-standard response to the LLM emergence debate
**Key Departure**: This framework shifts the question from "does performance jump discontinuously?" to "can we identify the macro-level variables that explain the system?"
**Significance**: Sets a rigorous standard the field must meet - not just showing capability thresholds, but explaining them mechanistically

## The Core Argument

**What Emergence Means in Complexity Science:**
- In thermodynamics, temperature is emergent from molecular motion - a coarse-grained variable that captures collective behavior
- The emergent variable has causal autonomy: it explains phenomena that the micro-level description cannot efficiently capture
- "Less is more": emergence involves reduction of internal degrees of freedom, not just addition of properties

**What This Requires for LLMs:**
- Don't just demonstrate that capabilities appear at scale thresholds
- Identify WHAT the relevant macroscopic variables are that govern those capabilities
- Show these variables have genuine explanatory power at the macro level
- Demonstrate they cannot be reduced to simply "more parameters" descriptions

**Intelligence as Emergent Property:**
The paper treats intelligence itself as "a consummate emergent property, manifesting increasingly efficient uses of emergent capabilities to solve problems" - where "less is more." Intelligence = efficient use of coarse-grained variables, not raw computational power.

---

## The Mirage vs. Reality Debate Resolved

The complexity science framework provides a principled way to move past the Schaeffer et al. "Mirage" critique:
- Schaeffer showed performance jumps can be metric artifacts
- Krakauer/Mitchell's framework says: the right question was never about performance metrics
- The right question is: what macroscopic variables govern LLM behavior? Can we identify them?
- If yes - that is genuine emergence (regardless of metric choices)
- If no - claims of emergence are premature

---

## The Large Reasoning Model Evidence

The 2025 survey (Berti et al.) provides the strongest empirical case for genuine phase transitions:
- OpenAI o1: 83.3% on AIME 2024 mathematical competition vs. GPT-4o's 13.4%
- OpenAI o3: 88% on ARC-AGI benchmark vs. GPT-4o's 5%
- o1: 89.0% on Codeforces vs. GPT-4o's 11.0%
- These jumps occur in competitions specifically designed to resist pattern-matching
- The discontinuity (5% to 88%) is instrumentally significant regardless of metric debates

**What makes o3 different by Krakauer/Mitchell standard**: o3 uses RL + inference-time search - it has a *different architecture* that may constitute different macro-level organization. This is closer to genuine emergence than mere parameter scaling.

---

## Contrarian Implication

The framework is also a warning against LLM hype:
- Claiming LLMs are "emergently intelligent" without identifying the relevant macroscopic variables is premature
- The field tends to attribute surprise-at-scale to emergence when it may be metric artifact
- The appropriate scientific stance: test for mechanistic emergence (coarse-grained variables), not just performance emergence
