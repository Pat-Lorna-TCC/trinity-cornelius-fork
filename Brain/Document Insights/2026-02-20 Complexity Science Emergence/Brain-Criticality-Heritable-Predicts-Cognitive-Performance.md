# Brain Criticality is Heritable and Predicts Cognitive Performance

**Source**: (1) "Genetic Contributions to Brain Criticality and its Relationship with Human Cognitive Functions" (PNAS, 2025) https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2417010122 | (2) "Critical Dynamics Predicts Cognitive Performance and Provides a Common Framework for Heterogeneous Mechanisms Impacting Cognition" (PNAS, 2025) https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2417117122

## Core Insight

Two landmark 2025 PNAS papers establish that brain criticality - the neural system operating at the boundary between order and chaos - is both genetically heritable and directly predicts cognitive performance across multiple domains. This is the strongest empirical evidence to date connecting the "edge of chaos" hypothesis to actual human intelligence.

---

## Evidence

**Study 1 - Genetic Foundations:**
- **Study Design**: Twin study + non-twin subjects via resting-state fMRI
- **Sample Size**: 250 monozygotic twins, 142 dizygotic twins, 437 non-twin subjects
- **Key Results**: Genetic factors substantially influence brain criticality across scales (regions, functional networks, whole brain). Specific gene expression profiles underlie regional critical dynamics.
- **Conclusion**: Brain criticality is a heritable biological phenotype - some individuals are genetically predisposed toward or away from criticality
- **Replication**: Not yet independently replicated (2025 publication)

**Study 2 - Cognitive Performance Prediction:**
- **Study Design**: Comprehensive cognitive testing combined with multiday intracranial EEG
- **Sample**: Persons with epilepsy (unique access to intracranial recording)
- **Key Results**: Proximity to critical dynamics directly predicts cognitive performance across multiple domains. Diverse factors (epileptiform activity, medications, sleep-like episodes) all converge on one endpoint: perturbing the critical state.
- **Three Performance Regimes Identified**:
  - Subcritical: sparse, interrupted activity; short temporal correlations; lower cognitive performance
  - Critical: reverberating dynamics; maximized temporal correlations; optimal cognitive performance
  - Supercritical: dense, chaotic activity; short temporal correlations; limited cognitive performance

---

## Why This Is Significant

The critical state maximizes *simultaneously*: information transmission, integration, storage, dynamic range, and sensitivity to inputs. This explains why criticality predicts performance - it is the neural state that optimizes all information-processing metrics at once, not just one.

The genetic heritability finding opens new dimensions for understanding individual cognitive differences. Distance from the critical state may explain cognitive performance variation previously attributed to effort or trait-based factors.

---

## Important Nuance

Criticality is NOT universally optimal. Spiking neural network research shows that simple tasks actually suffer at criticality - only complex tasks benefit. This aligns with flow state research: flow occurs during tasks at the edge of current skill, not during easy or impossible tasks. The pattern: criticality is adaptive for complex, uncertain, creative cognition but not for simple rote tasks.
