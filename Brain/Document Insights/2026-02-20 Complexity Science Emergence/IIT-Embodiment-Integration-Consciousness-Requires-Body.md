## Core Framework

The 2024 Frontiers paper extends IIT (Integrated Information Theory) to require integration with embodied cognition - the finding that the non-neural body is "structurally and dynamically intertwined with the brain." A complete theory of consciousness cannot treat the brain as isolated from the body, because the body's dynamics are constitutive of, not merely inputs to, conscious experience.

## Why Embodiment Matters for Consciousness Theory

**Standard IIT Position**: Consciousness arises from integrated information (Φ) in neural networks
**IIT + Embodiment Position**: The integrated information includes body-brain dynamics, not just neural dynamics

**The evidence from embodied cognition research (Feb 2026 expansion in knowledge base):**
- The body's physiological processes continuously shape neural processing (interoception)
- Interoceptive signals are not mere inputs - they set the precision weighting of all neural processing
- The boundary of the conscious system cannot be drawn at the skull

**What changes if consciousness requires embodiment:**
- LLMs would have zero consciousness on this view (no body, no interoception, no physical grounding)
- The distinction between conscious and unconscious AI becomes sharper and more defensible
- "Embodied AI" becomes not just a performance enhancement but a potential path to consciousness

## The Two-Level IIT Reading

A complementary 2024 PMC paper proposes a two-level reading that separates the claim:
- **Level 1**: IIT describes autonomous systems that achieve sufficient complexity (this is the defensible claim)
- **Level 2**: IIT claims to describe consciousness specifically (this is the controversial claim)
- **Conclusion**: Sufficient complexity + embodiment may be necessary but not sufficient for consciousness; embodiment adds grounding but doesn't solve the hard problem

---

## The Panpsychism Problem and the Emergentist Solution

IIT's panpsychism implication (even a photodiode has minimal consciousness) continues to alienate mainstream neuroscientists. The "Emergentist IIT" interpretation offers an escape relevant to embodiment:
- Consciousness is not a fundamental property of information processing
- Consciousness *emerges from* the causal powers of physical systems that have reached sufficient embodied complexity
- This makes embodiment load-bearing: the path to consciousness is through physical embodiment and self-referential body-brain integration

**For AI**: Under Emergentist IIT + Embodiment, current AI systems would not be conscious because they lack both the embodied grounding and the specific causal architecture. This is not a statement that AI consciousness is impossible, but that the current architecture is insufficient.
