## Core Insight

The next phase of AI capability comes not from larger single models but from orchestrated multi-agent systems where intelligence emerges from agent interactions. This is the complexity science prediction applied to AI architecture: the transition from Dennard scaling (bigger = better) to multicore architecture (distributed = better) has arrived in AI. Intelligence is achieved through *smarter systems*, not just *bigger models*.

## Why Complexity Science Explains This Transition

**What complexity science predicts:**
- Optimal performance emerges at criticality (edge between order and chaos) in CAS
- A single large model is analogous to a single fully-connected node - too ordered, insufficient adaptability
- Distributed multi-agent systems with appropriate coordination are analogous to systems operating at criticality

**The architectural shift in CAS terms:**
- **Single model** = centralized, hierarchical, subcritical (over-ordered)
- **Uncoordinated agents** = distributed, unconstrained, supercritical (chaotic)
- **Orchestrated multi-agent** = distributed, coordinated, critical (edge of chaos)

The orchestration IS the criticality mechanism. Good orchestration balances autonomy (avoiding subcriticality) and coordination (avoiding supercriticality).

## The Pursuit-Evasion Demonstration

A Scientific Reports (Nature) paper demonstrates emergent strategic coordination:
- 99.9% success rate in 1,000 randomized pursuit-evasion trials
- Strategic coordination emerged from simple rule interaction without explicit programming
- 6 fundamental pursuit actions combining into 21 composite actions
- The emergent composite actions were more effective than any pre-programmed strategy

This is the clearest empirical demonstration: intelligence (strategic effectiveness) emerged from the interaction of simple rules in a multi-agent system. The intelligence was not in any individual rule or agent.

DreamerV3 (2025) implements "emergent policy self-organization" through world-model learning:
- Agents learn internal world models from environment interaction
- Policy emerges from the interaction between world model and goal specification

This is the embodied AI principle in practice: intelligence emerges from agent-environment interaction, not from pre-specified rules.
