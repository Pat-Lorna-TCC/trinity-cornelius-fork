## Core Insight

Combining Unanimous AI's Conversational Swarm Intelligence (CSI) platform with LLM facilitation, groups of humans organized as real-time swarms achieved an effective IQ of 128 (97th percentile). This directly operationalizes the complexity science prediction that collective intelligence can exceed individual intelligence through structured swarm dynamics - and provides the first quantified IQ-equivalent measurement of human swarm performance.

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Comparison of CSI (Thinkscape platform) vs. standard text-chat brainstorming among networked human groups
**Platform**: Unanimous AI's Conversational Swarm Intelligence (CSI) - Thinkscape platform
**Results**:
- Groups using CSI significantly preferred the platform over text-chat brainstorming
- Collaborating groups averaged an effective IQ of **128** (97th percentile)
- This represents a substantial cognitive amplification above any typical individual member
**Replication**: Published study from Carnegie Mellon University Tepper School of Business

## What Makes CSI Different from Standard Group Discussion

Standard group discussion fails to achieve swarm intelligence because:
- Sequential rather than simultaneous input - first speaker influences all others
- Social conformity dynamics suppress minority views
- Cognitive load from tracking conversation prevents optimal individual contribution

CSI addresses these by:
- Real-time parallel input from all participants simultaneously
- Dynamic aggregation of inputs without sequential dominance
- LLM facilitation maintains problem framing and synthesizes convergences

The swarm structure means each participant contributes simultaneously rather than sequentially, preventing the groupthink dynamics documented in the harmful emergence research.

## The 97th Percentile Context

128 IQ = 97th percentile means the collective group performance exceeds 97% of individuals on standard cognitive measures. The average individual is approximately IQ 100. Groups performing at 128 are effectively amplifying collective intelligence by roughly 20-25%.

This demonstrates the complexity science principle empirically: local interactions (individual contributions to the swarm) produce global behavior (128 IQ) that exceeds the sum of local behaviors.

## Why This Matters for AI-Human Collaboration

**Traditional AI-human collaboration assumption**: AI enhances individual human performance
**Swarm intelligence finding**: Structured group dynamics can amplify human collective intelligence even without AI involvement (the AI serves as facilitator/aggregator, not source)

This suggests the highest-value AI applications may be in swarm architecture design and facilitation rather than direct intelligence replacement. The AI's role: maintain the conditions that enable human swarm intelligence to emerge.
