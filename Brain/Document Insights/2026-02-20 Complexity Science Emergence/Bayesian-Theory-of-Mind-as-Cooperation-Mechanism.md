# Bayesian Theory of Mind is the Complexity Mechanism That Enables Generalizable Reciprocity

**Source**: "Evolving General Cooperation with a Bayesian Theory of Mind." PNAS, 2024-2025. https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2400993122
**Document Type**: Research Paper (PNAS)

## Core Insight

The ability to infer what type of agent one is interacting with - theory of mind as Bayesian inference - is the complexity mechanism that enables reciprocity to generalize beyond simple tit-for-tat. Agents that maintain probabilistic beliefs about partners' types can cooperate in both direct and indirect reciprocity contexts, outcompeting all simpler rule-following strategies. Theory of mind IS the complexity upgrade that makes cooperation robust.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Evolutionary game theory - competition between agent types across diverse environments
**Key Methodology**: Built "Bayesian Reciprocator" agents that use Bayesian inference to assess whether interacting partners are "like them"
**Results**:
- Bayesian Reciprocator outcompetes all simpler automata strategies in evolutionary competition
- Enables BOTH direct reciprocity (I'll help you if you help me, repeated games) AND indirect reciprocity (help those who help others, one-shot observable games)
- Sustains cooperation across *larger* environmental parameter ranges than prior approaches
**Replication**: Published in PNAS (peer-reviewed)

---

## The Mechanism

**Simple reciprocity (tit-for-tat)**: Responds to what the partner DID - requires repeated interaction to calibrate
**Bayesian reciprocity**: Infers what the partner IS - builds probabilistic model of partner type from limited observations

**Why Bayesian Inference Wins:**
1. Partners are not observed in every interaction - type inference allows generalization across contexts
2. Allows probing strategies (test cooperativeness across different stakes) to rapidly identify partner type
3. Can shift from cooperation to defection or vice versa as Bayesian update strengthens
4. Handles both repeated games (direct) and single-shot games (indirect) without separate algorithms

**The Complexity Bridge:**
Theory of mind is not just a social skill - it is a complexity mechanism. By maintaining internal models of partner types, agents reduce the dimensionality of the cooperation problem: instead of responding to every observable behavior, they respond to inferred stable properties (trustworthiness, cooperativeness). This is a form of coarse-graining that is computationally more efficient and behaviorally more robust.
