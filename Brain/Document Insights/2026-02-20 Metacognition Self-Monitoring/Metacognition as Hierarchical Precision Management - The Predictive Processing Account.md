## Core Framework

Within predictive processing, **metacognition is the brain estimating the reliability of its own predictions**. The brain does not just form beliefs about the world - it forms second-order beliefs about how trustworthy those beliefs are. This precision management function IS metacognition, not a separate faculty.

---

## The Mechanism in Detail

**Predictive processing hierarchy:**
```
Level N+1: Second-order beliefs (confidence in Level N)
           ↓ Precision weighting
Level N:   First-order predictions (about world states)
           ↓ Prediction error
Level N-1: Sensory/perceptual input
```

**Metacognitive feelings** (confidence, familiarity, fluency) are not epiphenomenal - they ARE second-order precision estimates surfacing into phenomenal awareness. They arise when:
- Precision estimates diverge significantly from expectation
- First-order predictions generate unexpected error patterns
- The system needs to signal reliability for action selection

---

## Three Key Principles

**1. The "Aha" Experience as Bayesian Model Reduction**
The sudden insight ("aha" moment) represents a discrete event: dopaminergic precision-weighting precipitates a rapid restructuring of the generative model. It is literally the system collapsing many low-precision hypotheses into one high-precision explanation. This makes aha experiences not mystical but mechanistically explicable.

**2. Metacognitive Feelings are Directive, Not Just Descriptive**
Unlike classical views where metacognition passively monitors, the PP account positions metacognitive feelings as **action-guiding signals**. Low confidence = increase evidence-seeking. High confidence = commit to action. The feelings are embedded in the decision architecture, not observing from outside.

**3. Unified Account Without Separate Faculty**
A unified PP account explains all metacognitive phenomena (monitoring, evaluation, regulation) without requiring a separate metacognitive module. The same hierarchical prediction machinery handles first-order cognition and second-order monitoring.
