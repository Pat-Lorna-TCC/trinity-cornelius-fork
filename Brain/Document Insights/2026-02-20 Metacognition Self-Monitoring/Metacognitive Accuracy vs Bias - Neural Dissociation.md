# Metacognitive Accuracy vs Bias - Neural Dissociation with Distinct Substrates

**Source**: Mattes, A. & Soutschek, A. - "Brain stimulation over dorsomedial prefrontal cortex causally affects metacognitive bias but not mentalising" - Cognitive, Affective, & Behavioral Neuroscience, 2025; Kapetaniou et al. 2025
**Document Type**: Research Paper (brain stimulation, causal)
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)

## Core Finding

Two distinct metacognitive components have **separable neural substrates**, causally confirmed through brain stimulation:

- **Metacognitive accuracy**: whether confidence tracks correctness → **FPC-DLPFC circuit**
- **Metacognitive bias**: overall confidence level (systematic over/underconfidence) → **dmPFC**

These can be selectively impaired without affecting the other. This double dissociation is one of the most clinically significant recent findings in metacognitive neuroscience.

---

## The Double Dissociation

**Experiment 1 (Kapetaniou et al. 2025 - FPC tACS)**:
- Disrupted: metacognitive ACCURACY
- Unchanged: metacognitive bias, first-order task performance, mentalizing

**Experiment 2 (Mattes & Soutschek 2025 - dmPFC tDCS)**:
- Disrupted: metacognitive BIAS (reduced subjective confidence)
- Unchanged: objective accuracy, reaction times, mentalizing

**Result**: You can have someone who tracks their errors perfectly (high accuracy) but is systematically overconfident (high bias). Or someone who is well-calibrated in relative terms (low bias) but cannot reliably distinguish correct from incorrect responses (low accuracy).

---

## Clinical Translation

**Why this matters:**

| Condition | Likely Component Affected | Targeted Intervention |
|-----------|--------------------------|----------------------|
| Schizophrenia | Metacognitive accuracy (cannot track own errors) + bias (overconfident in delusions) | Both circuits impaired; MCT targets accuracy component |
| Depression | Metacognitive bias (systematic underconfidence) | dmPFC intervention; restore confidence calibration |
| Narcissistic personality | Metacognitive bias (systematic overconfidence) | dmPFC; reduce confidence to match accuracy |
| ADHD | Metacognitive accuracy (poor error detection) | FPC circuit training; improve monitoring |

**This creates a precision psychiatry framework**: Assess WHICH metacognitive component is impaired → target the corresponding circuit → measure improvement in that component specifically.

---

## The Practical Measurement Implication

Most everyday discussions of "overconfidence" conflate accuracy and bias. The distinction matters:

- **"I'm bad at knowing when I'm right"** = accuracy problem (FPC)
- **"I systematically think I'm better than I am"** = bias problem (dmPFC)

Someone could have excellent accuracy (they do correlate confidence with correctness) while still being biased (their baseline confidence is too high for their actual performance level). The current vault notes on overconfidence likely need this distinction added.
