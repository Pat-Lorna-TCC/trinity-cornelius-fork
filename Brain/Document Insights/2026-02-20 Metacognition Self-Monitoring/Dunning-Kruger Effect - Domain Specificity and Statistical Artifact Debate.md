# Dunning-Kruger Effect - Domain Specificity and Statistical Artifact Debate

## Core Finding

The Dunning-Kruger Effect is domain-specific, not universal - **no DKE appears in creativity**, suggesting the effect depends on domains with clear competence hierarchies and feedback mechanisms. Meanwhile, the statistical artifact debate continues: large replications find a small but significant effect, but the effect's heritability implicates metacognition (not just measurement error) as the actual mechanism.

---

## What the Debate Has Resolved

**Large-scale replication finding**: Studies with approximately 4,000 participants per condition still find a **small but statistically significant** DKE. The "pure statistical artifact" dismissal is too strong - there is a real phenomenon, though potentially smaller than originally claimed.

**Domain specificity established (2024)**: Lebuda et al. found NO strong support for DKE in creativity domain, whether analyzing self-assessment in absolute or relative terms. Creativity differs from knowledge domains:
- No clear external competence standard (creativity is partly subjective)
- Self-assessment reflects different metacognitive processes in open-ended vs. closed-answer domains
- Experts in creativity may be MORE uncertain, not overconfident (domain norms differ)

**Heritability implication (2025 twin study)**: Patterned heritability estimates for metacognitive accuracy across IQ quartiles suggests that the heritable component of metacognition partly explains DKE patterns. This SUPPORTS metacognition as the real mechanism - it's not just measurement choice causing the effect, but genuine individual differences in metacognitive accuracy that are partly genetic.

---

## The Rational Model (Princeton)

A rational Bayesian account of DKE offers an alternative framing: DKE emerges naturally when agents use their own performance as noisy evidence about their relative standing. Low-performers receive noisy negative signals but are uncertain how negative; they regress to the prior (population average) which is higher than their actual position. This is not irrationality - it's rational inference under noise.

**Implication**: DKE may be less about "incompetent people don't know they're incompetent" and more about "everyone uses the same rational inference strategy that happens to create systematic upward bias for low performers."

---

## What Remains Uncertain

- Whether DKE effects persist when controlling for random measurement error vs. heritability effects
- Why creativity is exempt (subjective standards? domain culture? feedback differences?)
- Whether the effect is driven more by low performers overestimating vs. high performers underestimating
