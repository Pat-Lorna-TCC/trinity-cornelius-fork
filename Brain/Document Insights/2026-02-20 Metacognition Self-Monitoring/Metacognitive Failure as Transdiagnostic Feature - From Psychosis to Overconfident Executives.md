# Metacognitive Failure as Transdiagnostic Feature - From Psychosis to Overconfident Executives

**Source**: "Insight differences between deficit and non-deficit schizophrenia: systematic review and meta-analysis" - ScienceDirect 2025; "Two decades of metacognitive training for psychosis" - Expert Review of Neurotherapeutics 2025; "Metacognition and Cognitive Flexibility in Autistic and Neurotypically-Developing Populations" - PMC 2025

## Core Finding

Metacognitive failure is not a disease-specific phenomenon but a **transdiagnostic feature** cutting across conditions from schizophrenia (severe: anosognosia) to autism (contested: may be enhanced in some domains) to subclinical overconfidence in healthy executives. The same neural substrate (FPC-DLPFC circuit) failing at different severity levels produces this spectrum. Critically, 20 years of Metacognitive Training (MCT) in psychosis provides a clinical validation framework for what targeted metacognitive intervention can achieve.

## The Transdiagnostic Spectrum

| Population | Metacognitive Failure | Mechanism | Severity |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------|----------|
| Schizophrenia | Anosognosia (up to 80% lack insight) | Overconfident priors override weak sensory evidence + delusion maintenance | Clinical |
| Autism (ASD) | Enhanced metacognition in sensory/reward domains; impaired in social/prior knowledge domains | Failure to predict precision of predictions (second-order) | Variable |
| Depression | Metacognitive accuracy intact; bias toward negative self-assessment | dmPFC-mediated confidence bias | Clinical |
| Healthy overconfidence | Domain-specific miscalibration | Architectural constraint + insufficient feedback | Subclinical |
| AI-assisted overconfidence | Feedback loop disruption | Metacognitive circuit short-circuited by AI assistance | Induced |

## Schizophrenia: The Severe End

**Key statistics**:
- Up to **80% of schizophrenia patients** exhibit insight impairment, present from early stages
- Global metacognitive deficit: **g = -0.57** across meta-analyses (though inflated by not controlling for lower task performance; true deficit smaller)
- Deficit schizophrenia shows significantly poorer insight than non-deficit schizophrenia

**The mechanism**: The same overconfidence mechanism implicated in delusion maintenance (hyper-precise priors that override sensory evidence) operates in the metacognitive domain. Patients cannot accurately assess the reliability of their own beliefs - precisely because the system that would assess this (FPC-DLPFC circuit) is impaired.

**20 years of Metacognitive Training (MCT)**: MCT for psychosis shows efficacy in:
- Reducing cognitive biases underlying psychosis symptoms
- Enhancing awareness of cognitive distortions
- Reducing overconfidence in schizophrenia patients

This is the strongest clinical evidence that metacognitive awareness can be trained in a population with structural metacognitive deficits.

---

## Autism: The Interesting Inversion

Recent work challenges the "metacognitive deficit" view of ASD. More precise finding:
- ASD shows **enhanced metacognition** in tasks relying on sensory information and reward evaluation
- ASD shows **impaired metacognition** in tasks requiring integration with prior knowledge

Within predictive processing: autism may involve not a failure of prediction but a **failure to accurately predict the precision of predictions** - metacognitive failure at the level of second-order beliefs about one's own certainty.

**This mirrors the FPC architecture**: If the second-order monitoring system (FPC) has impaired access to contextual/prior knowledge, it will over-rely on sensory evidence (appearing "enhanced" on sensory tasks) while failing on tasks that require prior integration.

## The Practical Implication for Organizations

The transdiagnostic view suggests that **the mechanisms causing psychotic delusions and executive overconfidence are the same mechanism at different severity levels**:
- Hyper-precise priors that resist updating
- Impaired monitoring of belief reliability
- Absence of error feedback signals

Organizations that create environments with poor feedback loops (protect leaders from negative feedback, attribute outcomes to skill not luck, reward confidence over calibration) may inadvertently create the subclinical version of the psychosis metacognitive failure pattern.
