## Core Finding

A landmark meta-analysis (Nature Human Behaviour, 2025) establishes that most cognitive biases are educationally reducible (g = 0.26), but with an important constraint: the **representativeness heuristic** resists intervention. More importantly, a complementary 2025 organizational framework distinguishes two fundamentally different debiasing pathways that are not interchangeable - they're suited to different decision contexts.

---

## The Meta-Analytic Foundation

**Nature Human Behaviour, 2025** (54 RCTs, 383 effect sizes, 10,941 participants):
- Overall debiasing effect: **g = 0.26** (small but significant)
- Most biases are reducible with targeted instruction
- **Exception**: Representativeness heuristic resists educational intervention
- Questions remain about transfer beyond training context

**This updates the vault's debiasing knowledge**: The existing Debiasing Shows Only 19% Transfer Beyond Training Conditions finding from 2021 focused on transfer failure. The 2025 meta-analysis shows that within-context effects are real (g = 0.26) - the limitation is transfer, not in-context reduction.

## The Two-Pathway Framework

**Pathway 1: Debiasing (Individual Training)**
- Mechanism: Equips decision-makers with bias awareness and metacognitive tools
- When effective: High-uncertainty environments with complex decisions
- Advantage: Develops generalizable metacognitive skills
- Limitation: Requires motivated learners; poor transfer across domains
- Evidence base: Most extensively tested approach

**Pathway 2: Choice Architecture (Environmental Redesign)**
- Mechanism: Changes the structure of decision problems to reduce bias opportunities
- When effective: Stable, predictable, routine environments where optimal choices can be identified
- Advantage: Works without requiring cognitive effort; persistent
- Limitation: Requires advance identification of optimal choice; rigid; can undermine autonomy
- Evidence base: Nudge literature

**Critical insight**: These are **complementary, not substitutes**. Choosing between them based on the decision environment is itself a metacognitive judgment.

## The Adaptive Toolbox Model (Medical Context)

A 2024 paper on clinical debiasing introduces the "adaptive toolbox" metaphor: metacognition creates the SPACE to notice when cognitive biases are operating, making it a **prerequisite for using any specific debiasing tool**.

This extends the Mental Models Taxonomy framework:
- **Lenses** = perspectives for analysis (first-order cognition)
- **Biases** = errors to avoid (requires metacognitive awareness to apply)
- **Tools** = calculative methods (requires metacognitive space to deploy)

Without metacognitive monitoring, the tools in the toolbox are inaccessible - you can't select the right tool if you don't notice you're in a biased state.

## The Representativeness Heuristic Exception

The meta-analysis finding that **representativeness resists educational intervention** is significant. Representativeness involves pattern-matching to prototypes ("does this look like an X?") - it operates pre-consciously and rapidly. Training in metacognitive awareness can't easily interrupt a pre-conscious process.

**Implication**: Some biases require choice architecture (changing stimuli before they're processed) rather than post-processing metacognitive intervention. This sets clear limits on what training can achieve.
