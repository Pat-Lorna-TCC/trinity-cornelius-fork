## Core Finding

Polarization can emerge from **biased reward processing that favors choice-confirmatory evidence** at the individual level—**independent of social interactions or network architecture**. Confirmation bias in individual learning (reward prediction error favoring confirmatory evidence) is sufficient to create group polarization.

**Critical Implication**: You don't need echo chambers, social networks, or tribal dynamics. Polarization emerges from how individual brains process rewards.

## The Reward Processing Hypothesis

### Traditional View (Challenged)
- Polarization = echo chambers + social media + tribal identity
- Caused by selective exposure to like-minded others
- Network architecture creates information bubbles

### New View (This Research)
- Polarization = individual confirmation bias + reward processing
- Dopamine system rewards confirmatory evidence more than disconfirming
- Can occur in **isolated individuals** without social component
- Social media amplifies pre-existing individual mechanism

**Analogy**: Social media didn't create polarization mechanism—it discovered and exploited an individual-level vulnerability.

---

## Mechanism: How Reward Processing Creates Polarization

1. **Initial belief formation** (random or weak evidence)
2. **Confirmation bias in learning**: Brain prioritizes evidence confirming belief
3. **Reward prediction error**: Dopamine spike when belief confirmed
4. **Reinforcement learning**: Confirmatory evidence weighted more heavily
5. **Belief strengthening**: Each confirmation makes belief more extreme
6. **Polarization emerges**: Different initial beliefs → divergent belief trajectories

**Key Insight**: Same evidence presented to two people with slightly different priors → dramatically divergent conclusions over time due to differential reward processing.
