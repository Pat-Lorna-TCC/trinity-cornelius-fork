# Higher Cognitive Ability Reduces Bias (Contradicts Earlier Findings)

## Core Finding

Recent research shows **higher cognitive ability reduces bias** (especially confirmation bias and misinformation susceptibility)—**contradicting earlier Kahan et al. (2017) findings** that suggested cognitive sophistication could worsen motivated reasoning.

**Critical Reversal**: The "smarter people are more biased" claim has been challenged and largely refuted.

---

## Evidence

**Previous View** (Kahan et al., 2017):
- Higher cognitive ability → better at motivated reasoning
- Smarter people better at defending pre-existing beliefs
- Cognitive sophistication enables more sophisticated bias

**New View** (2023-2025 research):
- Higher cognitive ability → **reduced** bias overall
- Reflection helps overcome identity-protective cognition (for most)
- Exception: Entrenches beliefs only when identity **severely threatened**

**Key Distinction**: Context and identity threat level determine whether cognition helps or hurts.

---

## The Identity Threat Interaction

### Low-to-Moderate Identity Threat
**Higher cognitive ability**:
- Reduces confirmation bias
- Improves evidence evaluation
- Enables perspective-taking
- Reduces misinformation susceptibility

### Severe Identity Threat
**Higher cognitive ability**:
- May entrench beliefs (defensive reasoning)
- Better at generating supporting arguments
- More sophisticated rationalization
- **Protective mechanism**: High-stakes identity defense

**Implication**: Cognitive ability is **generally beneficial** for bias reduction, except in extreme identity-defense scenarios.

---

## Confirmation Bias Awareness Interventions

**Research Finding**:
Confirmation bias awareness interventions **reduce misinformation susceptibility**, especially among initially negative populations (those most skeptical).

**Mechanism**:
1. Learn about confirmation bias tendency
2. Recognize it in own thinking
3. Actively seek disconfirming evidence
4. Reduce polarization

**Effectiveness**: Higher among those with:
- Higher cognitive ability (can apply meta-cognitive awareness)
- Initial skepticism (recognize they might be biased)

---

## Why the Reversal?

**Kahan et al. Critique**:
- May have measured **motivated reasoning capacity** (can you argue your side?)
- Not **bias reduction** (do you seek truth regardless of identity?)
- Context: Politically charged topics → extreme identity threat
- Sample: May not generalize to non-political domains

**New Research**:
- Broader contexts beyond politics
- Meta-cognitive interventions (awareness of bias)
- Identity threat measured and controlled
- Finds cognitive ability generally **protective**

---

## Practical Implications

**For Reducing Bias**:
1. **Cognitive training helps** (contrary to pessimistic view)
2. **Meta-cognitive awareness** particularly effective
3. **Identity safety** required for cognition to reduce bias
4. **Context matters**: Political/tribal topics different from neutral domains

**For Organizations**:
- Higher-ability individuals **can** overcome bias (not hopeless)
- Create **identity-safe environments** for evidence evaluation
- Bias awareness training **works** (especially for reflective individuals)
- Avoid framing disagreements as identity threats
