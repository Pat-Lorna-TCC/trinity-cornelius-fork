# Debiasing Shows Only 19% Transfer Beyond Training Conditions

**Source**: Frontiers in Psychology (2021), "Retention and Transfer of Cognitive Bias Mitigation Interventions: A Systematic Literature Study"

## Core Finding

A systematic review of debiasing interventions found that **only one study** demonstrated transfer involving real behavior beyond training conditions, with just **19% of participants** showing transfer effects. This suggests bias mitigation is often a **"learned trick"** while fundamental decision-making competences remain unchanged.

**Critical Implication**: Knowing about biases ≠ avoiding them in real-world decisions.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Systematic literature review of debiasing intervention studies
**Transfer Definition**: Ability to apply bias mitigation to novel situations beyond training context
**Key Result**: Minimal transfer to real-world behavior
**Conclusion**: Most debiasing effects are context-specific, not generalizable skill development

**Citation**: Frontiers in Psychology (2021)

---

## What This Means

### "Learned Trick" vs. "Fundamental Competence Change"

**Learned Trick**:
- "When I see [specific scenario from training], apply [specific technique]"
- Context-dependent pattern matching
- Doesn't generalize to new situations
- Surface-level behavioral compliance

**Fundamental Competence Change**:
- Deep understanding of bias mechanism
- Automatic recognition across contexts
- Generalizable skill application
- Genuine cognitive restructuring

**Reality**: Most debiasing training produces learned tricks, not competence change.

---

## Implications for Training Programs

**What Doesn't Work** (or works only narrowly):
- One-time bias awareness workshops
- Context-specific examples without principle extraction
- Teaching bias names without mechanisms
- Relying on conscious application during decisions

**What Might Work Better** (not yet validated):
- Extensive practice across varied contexts
- Mechanism-focused training (why bias occurs, not just what it is)
- Habit formation around bias-checking (automaticity)
- Environmental restructuring > individual training
- Choice architecture > debiasing
