## Core Insight

The Annual Review of Psychology 2025 review synthesizes a two-layer biological architecture governing attention capacity: (1) the 24-hour circadian cycle modulating arousal through the suprachiasmatic nucleus, and (2) the ~90-120 minute Basic Rest-Activity Cycle (BRAC) governing ultradian attention oscillations during wakefulness. Together, these create personalized "attention windows" that can be optimized through chronotype-aligned scheduling. The 90-minute work block is not a productivity hack - it reflects actual neurobiology.

---

## Evidence

**The Two-Layer Architecture**:

**Circadian Process (24-hour)**:
- Suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) modulates arousal through ascending arousal systems
- Attention components improve during the day, peaking in afternoon/evening
- Risk of errors highest during night and early morning
- Chronotype (morning vs. evening person) creates personalized attention peaks
- "Cognitive synchrony effect": Performance peaks when testing time matches chronotype
- Cortical attention responses show extensive circadian rhythms with region-varying phases

**Ultradian BRAC (90-120 minutes)**:
- Basic Rest-Activity Cycle (Kleitman) - 90-120 minute cycles during wakefulness
- Cognitively demanding work (programming, writing, analysis) tracks most closely to 90-minute cycles
- EEG shows approximate 90-minute arousal rhythms
- Night shift workers experience BRAC-circadian conflicts requiring extra fatigue management

**Homeostatic Process (Process S)**:
- Accumulates sleep pressure from wake time
- Creates non-linear interaction with circadian drive
- Explains why late afternoon feels like a "second wind" after the post-lunch dip

**Study**: Annual Review of Psychology 2025 synthesis + Scientific Reports 2024 (n=nationally representative sample, older adults)
**Replication**: Multiple independent lines of evidence (chronobiology, EEG, sleep science)
