## Core Framework

The classic dichotomy between voluntary (top-down, goal-driven) and involuntary (bottom-up, salience-driven) attention is being dissolved in 2025 research. Three developments converge: a tripartite model adds "selection history" as a distinct third factor; a unified framework proposes top-down and bottom-up as "different temporal expressions of the same underlying mechanism"; and PLOS Biology reveals the neural implementation through nested oscillations across frequency bands.

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging consensus in cognitive neuroscience - challenges the foundational top-down/bottom-up dichotomy

---

## The Tripartite Framework (Annual Review of Psychology, 2025)

Three distinct influences on attention, not two:
1. **Top-down goals**: Voluntary, conscious attention deployment
2. **Bottom-up salience**: Involuntary capture by novel/threatening stimuli
3. **Selection history**: Implicit statistical learning from past attentional acts

The third factor (selection history) is particularly important:
- Attentional capture can be controlled proactively through implicit statistical learning
- This learning occurs below conscious awareness
- It represents the accumulated "attentional habits" shaped by environment and experience
- **Can be deliberately reprogrammed** through sustained changes in attentional environment

---

## The Unified Framework (Acta Psychologica, 2025)

Top-down and bottom-up are not competing systems but "different temporal expressions of the same underlying mechanism" - guided by overlapping priorities across three timescales:
1. **Evolutionary imperatives** (ancient sensory priorities - milliseconds to respond)
2. **Learned experiences** (statistical regularities - seconds to minutes of training)
3. **Immediate task demands** (current goals - seconds to minutes of setup)

---

## The Neural Implementation - Nested Oscillations (PLOS Biology, 2025)

Trajkovic et al. revealed the neural mechanism:
- Top-down predictions from **frontal eye fields (FEF)**: communicate via **oscillatory coherence at <25 Hz** (theta/alpha bands) to posterior cortex
- These modulate **high-frequency (>40 Hz) gamma-band** sensory information flow
- "Nested oscillations" framework: slow rhythms (top-down) gate fast rhythms (bottom-up sensory)

This explains why top-down attention feels "effort-ful" (requires sustained slow-frequency coordination) while bottom-up capture is "automatic" (fast gamma responses dominate).

## Why This Matters

1. **Attention training**: If selection history is a distinct, plastic factor, deliberate practice shapes future attentional priorities - habit formation and mindfulness both target this layer
2. **Digital design**: Social media exploits bottom-up salience and trains selection history simultaneously - doubly capturing attention
3. **Recovery strategy**: Detoxing from dopamine-rich environments rebuilds selection history toward deeper engagement
