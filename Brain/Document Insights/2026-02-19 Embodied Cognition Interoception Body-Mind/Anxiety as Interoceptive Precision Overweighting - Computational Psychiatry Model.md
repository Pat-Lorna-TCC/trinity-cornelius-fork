## Core Framework

Anxiety disorders arise from **hyperprecise interoceptive threat priors** - the brain overestimates confidence in internal bodily signals as indicators of threat. This creates rigid belief states where benign bodily sensations (elevated heart rate, rapid breathing) are misinterpreted as dangerous. The computational anomaly is not perception itself but the **precision weighting** assigned to interoceptive prediction errors.

## Computational Anomalies in Anxiety

**1. Hyperprecise Threat Priors**:
- Overestimation of threat probability during interoceptive inference
- Exaggerated belief states from normal bodily signals
- High confidence assigned to threat interpretations

**2. Context Rigidity**:
- Failure to flexibly adjust expectations when conditions change
- Threat priors persist even in safe environments
- Bayesian updating impaired for interoceptive beliefs

## Empirical Evidence

### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
**Neural Biomarker**: Elevated heart-evoked potential (HEP) amplitudes
- **HEP**: Neural correlate of interoceptive prediction error
- **Finding**: Elevation occurs even for neutral stimuli (e.g., saline injections)
- **Mechanism**: Heightened anterior insula reactivity to threat-related respiratory predictions

**Interpretation**: GAD patients assign excessive precision to internal signals, treating neutral bodily sensations as significant prediction errors requiring attention.

### Panic Disorder / Agoraphobia
**Pattern**: Heightened interoceptive sensitivity to internal bodily signals
**Mechanism**: Benign sensations misinterpreted as threatening
**Example**: Normal heart rate increase → catastrophic interpretation → panic attack

## Treatment Implications

**Computational Target**: Recalibrate precision weighting of interoceptive signals

**Evidence-Based Interventions**:

1. **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)**:
   - **Mechanism**: Exposure recalibrates precision weighting
   - **Effect**: Reduces confidence in threat interpretations
   - **Computational**: Updates Bayesian priors

2. **Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR)**:
   - **Mechanism**: Body scan practices update interoceptive beliefs
   - **Effect**: Increases tolerance for bodily sensations
   - **Computational**: Improves precision arbitration

3. **Interoceptive Awareness Training**:
   - **Mechanism**: Direct manipulation of belief updating
   - **Effect**: Enhances metacognitive awareness of interoception
   - **Computational**: Trains flexible precision adjustment
