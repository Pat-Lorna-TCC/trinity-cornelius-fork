## Core Framework

The brain treats interoceptive and exteroceptive inference as parallel hierarchical systems that continuously exchange **precision-weighted prediction errors**. Precision weighting determines how much influence each information stream (internal body signals vs. external sensory signals) has on perception, action, and cognition. The anterior insula and ACC serve as arbitration zones where precision weights are dynamically adjusted.

## Computational Mechanism

**Key Components**:
1. **Hierarchical prediction**: Brain generates predictions about both internal (body) and external (world) states
2. **Prediction errors**: Mismatch between predicted and actual signals
3. **Precision weighting**: Confidence assigned to each information stream
4. **Dynamic arbitration**: Anterior insula/ACC adjust precision weights based on context

**Mathematical Formulation**:
- Prediction errors from interoceptive hierarchy compete with exteroceptive hierarchy
- Precision weights determine which stream dominates perception
- System reaches equilibrium through Bayesian updating

## Psychopathology Through Precision Dysregulation

**Anxiety Disorders**:
- **Mechanism**: Interoceptive overweighting
- **Result**: Internal body signals given excessive confidence
- **Manifestation**: Benign sensations misinterpreted as threatening
- **System Dynamics**: Rigid, self-sustaining imbalances

**PTSD**:
- **Mechanism**: Interoceptive underweighting
- **Result**: Internal signals given insufficient confidence
- **Manifestation**: Slowed recalibration after exposure therapy
- **System Dynamics**: Delayed belief updating
