# Aphantasia - Mental Imagery Requires Interoceptive Grounding

## Core Insight

Aphantasia (inability to generate mental imagery) arises from **suboptimal interoceptive precision processing in the insula**. Mental imagery is not a purely visual cortical process but requires interoceptive grounding - imagined content must be anchored in bodily self-awareness to be experienced as vivid and voluntary. Low interoceptive precision prevents the generation of high-gain top-down predictions necessary for vivid mental imagery.

## Mechanism

**Interoception's Dual Role in Imagery**:

1. **Integration Function**:
   - Merges interoceptive input with sensory information
   - Anchors imagined content in bodily self
   - Provides phenomenological substrate for "seeing" in mind's eye

2. **Agency Function**:
   - Enables sense of voluntary control over mental content
   - Distinguishes self-generated imagery from perception
   - Supports metacognitive awareness of imagination

**Precision Weighting Failure**:
- **Low interoceptive precision** → Weak top-down predictions
- Weak predictions → Insufficient vividness to reach conscious awareness
- Result: Absence of visual mental imagery

## Implications for Embodied Cognition

**Mental Imagery is Embodied**:
- NOT disembodied visual processing
- Requires bodily self-awareness as foundation
- Interoceptive signals provide grounding substrate
- Further evidence against Cartesian dualism

**Conscious Experience Hierarchy**:
- Interoceptive precision enables conscious imagery
- Visual cortex alone insufficient
- Phenomenology requires body-mind integration
