## Core Finding

The predictive coding framework provides a **unified computational account** of psychiatric disorders as precision miscalibration - not as qualitatively distinct diseases, but as different points on a continuum of hypo-prior to hyper-prior balance. The ASD-schizophrenia continuum hypothesis has moved from theoretical to empirically validated using Hierarchical Gaussian Filters (HGF) and EEG biomarkers.

---

## The Predictive Continuum

| Disorder | Predictive Style | Mechanism | Experience |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|------------|
| **Autism (ASD)** | Hypo-prior | Under-reliance on priors; over-sensitivity to sensory input | Fragmented but detailed perception; sensory overload; pattern consistency (rules are absolute) |
| **Neurotypical** | Balanced prior | Calibrated precision on priors vs. sensory input | Normal perception integrating expectation and sensation |
| **Schizophrenia (SSD)** | Hyper-prior | Over-reliance on priors; reduced sensory precision | Hallucinations and delusions (hyper-precise prior beliefs override weak sensory evidence) |

**Empirical Validation (2025)**: Using the Hierarchical Gaussian Filter (HGF), higher positive schizotypal traits correlated with greater reliance on prior beliefs and reduced flexibility in prediction error updating. This provides computational biomarkers for targeted interventions.

## Clinical Significance

**This is the most advanced computational psychiatry application of predictive processing to date.** The HGF provides computational biomarkers that could:
- Differentiate individuals along the ASD-SSD continuum
- Guide targeted pharmaceutical interventions (precision recalibration vs. symptom management)
- Enable precision-medicine psychiatry: match intervention to specific precision dysregulation profile

**Honest caveat**: The 2025 transdiagnostic systematic review found translation to clinical biomarkers and therapies has been uneven. Most rigorous work remains computational (modeling) rather than clinical intervention.

---

## Unified Therapeutic Target

If all disorders represent precision miscalibration, the unified therapeutic target is **precision calibration**:

Interventions that work across disorders:
- **CBT** - Explicitly challenges hyper-precise negative priors; creates conditions for prediction error
- **Meditation** - Systematic precision attenuation training; reduces lock-in on self-model and trauma priors
- **Psychedelics** - Temporary disruption of hierarchical precision architecture; "reset" to lower-precision priors
- **rTMS/ECT** - Directly modulates neural precision-encoding circuits

This reframes the mechanisms of EXISTING treatments and suggests precision calibration as the unified target for novel interventions.

## Aphantasia Discovery (2025)

A particularly striking 2025 finding: Reduced interoceptive precision disrupts the brain's ability to generate high-gain top-down predictions needed for mental imagery. This demonstrates interoception's role beyond body regulation - interoceptive precision enables cognitive functions including imagination.
