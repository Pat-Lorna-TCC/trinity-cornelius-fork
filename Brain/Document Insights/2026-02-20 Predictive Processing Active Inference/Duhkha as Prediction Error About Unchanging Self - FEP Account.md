## Speculative Synthesis

Under FEP, the Buddhist concept of Duhkha (suffering/unsatisfactoriness) can be precisely formalized:

**Duhkha = the accumulated prediction error generated by maintaining a high-precision prediction of a permanent, unchanging self in an impermanent, constantly-changing world**

The brain generates predictions about what "I" am - a stable, coherent entity persisting through time. But:
- The world changes constantly (impermanence)
- The "self" changes constantly (moment-to-moment neurological flux)
- Reality repeatedly disconfirms the prediction of an unchanging self
- Each disconfirmation = prediction error
- Prediction error = Duhkha (the felt sense of wrongness, incompleteness, suffering)

Craving (tanha) = the self-model's attempt to achieve prediction accuracy through action: "if I get X, the prediction of a satisfied self will be confirmed." But the model is structurally miscalibrated - no amount of external acquisition resolves the fundamental precision mismatch between "permanent self" prior and impermanent reality.

## The Buddhist Solution as FEP Solution

Buddhism's Four Noble Truths map onto FEP:

| Buddhist Teaching | FEP Formalization |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Duhkha exists | Prediction errors from permanent-self prior are inevitable |
| Duhkha has a cause (tanha/craving) | Hyper-precise prior on self-permanence generates constant prediction errors |
| Duhkha can cease | Precision of self-model can be reduced through training |
| There is a path | Meditation = systematic precision attenuation training for self-model |

**The Eighth-fold Path as Precision Calibration Practice:**
Each element of Buddhist practice can be reinterpreted as a precision recalibration technique - training the mind to hold self-related predictions with lower confidence, making them less costly to update.
