## Core Framework

The Free Energy Principle provides the first mechanistic account of WHY confirmation bias is so hard to overcome. Under FEP:

**Confirmation bias = appropriate precision weighting of established priors, problematic only when the world has changed and priors are stale**

This is not a design flaw - it is the correct Bayesian behavior for a system that has built up high-confidence prior beliefs through repeated experience. The problem arises when precision is locked to stale priors in a changed environment.

---

## The Mechanism

Under FEP, all cognitive biases can be re-described in precision terms:

| Cognitive Bias | FEP Precision Account |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **Confirmation bias** | High-precision maintenance of established priors; incoming disconfirming evidence assigned low precision = low influence |
| **Anchoring** | Strong priors resist updating because low-precision incoming evidence cannot override them |
| **Attribute substitution** | Brain uses available precision-weighted signals as proxies for unavailable ones |
| **Status quo bias** | Existing priors have accumulated precision through repeated confirmation; change requires massive prediction error to overcome |

**Why biases are hard to change:** To overcome a high-precision prior, incoming evidence must generate a prediction error large enough to overcome the prior's precision weight. If the prior is very high-precision (deeply held belief), only overwhelming evidence creates sufficient prediction error to update it.

This is why Dopamine Explains Why Changing Beliefs Is Neurologically Painful - the dopamine precision-stabilization system is actively fighting to maintain confident priors against disconfirming evidence.

## FEP Reframes Kahneman's System 1/System 2

Active Inference subsumes the dual-process distinction into precision dynamics:

- **"System 1"** = high-precision habitual priors dominate (fast, low-computational cost, automatic)
- **"System 2"** = precision shifted to explicit reasoning (slow, high-computational cost, allows prior updating)

This reframes Kahneman's framework mechanistically: what we call "slow deliberate thinking" is literally shifting precision from habitual priors to current evidence. The effort of System 2 thinking is the effort of overcoming the brain's tendency to trust its high-precision established models.

---

## Superforecasters as Precision Calibrators

Expected Free Energy minimization is Bayes-optimal exploration - mechanistically identical to what Tetlock calls "good forecasting":

- **Calibrated uncertainty** = accurately weighting precision of priors vs. incoming evidence
- **Epistemic humility** = not over-assigning precision to established beliefs
- **Updating on evidence** = allowing prediction errors to adjust precision weights

**Superforecasters perform better because they have better-calibrated precision weighting** - neither hypo- nor hyper-prior. They resist both overconfidence (hyper-precision) and excessive uncertainty (hypo-precision).

This connects to Superforecasting and provides the neural mechanism for why forecasting skill is learnable: it is precision calibration training.

## Therapeutic Implications

If biases = precision miscalibration, effective debiasing requires precision recalibration, not just information provision:

- Simply providing contradictory information doesn't work if precision is locked on existing priors (explains Debiasing Shows Only 19% Transfer Beyond Training Conditions)
- Effective debiasing must target the precision mechanism: creating conditions where prediction errors are large enough to overcome prior precision
- **Meditation** as debiasing tool: systematic precision attenuation training reduces lock-in on established priors
- **Pre-mortem/red team exercises** as precision tools: force the brain to simulate high-precision disconfirmation scenarios
