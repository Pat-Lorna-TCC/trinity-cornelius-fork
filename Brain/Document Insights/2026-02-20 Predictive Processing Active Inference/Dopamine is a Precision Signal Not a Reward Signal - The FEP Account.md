## Core Framework

Under the Free Energy Principle (FEP), dopamine does not primarily encode reward prediction error - it encodes **precision**: the reliability or confidence weight assigned to prediction error signals. This is a formally distinct claim from classical dopamine theory.

- **High dopamine** = increased precision weight on prediction errors = more weight given to new sensory information over prior beliefs
- **Low dopamine** = precision collapses toward priors = perseveration, habit maintenance, delusion-maintenance
- **Dopamine burst from belief confirmation** = brain signaling "my model is well-calibrated here - increase precision on these prior beliefs"

The distinction is subtle but profound: dopamine is not signaling that something good happened - it is signaling that the brain's predictions are reliable and deserve increased confidence.

## Mechanistic Detail

From the 2025 Bayesian Brain Theory review:
- **NMDA receptor signaling** = top-down predictions
- **AMPA receptor signaling** = bottom-up prediction errors
- **Monoaminergic neuromodulators** (dopamine, serotonin) = encode precision weights across the cortical hierarchy

This gives a direct neurobiological substrate for precision-weighting: the neuromodulator systems are the brain's confidence calibration machinery.

## Psychiatric Implications

| Disorder | Precision Dysregulation via Dopamine |
|----------|-------------------------------------|
| Depression | Over-certainty of negative outcomes = trapped in low-precision updated bad priors |
| Anxiety | Precision dysregulation in motor control hierarchies |
| Schizophrenia | Excess dopamine → excess precision on prior beliefs → hallucinations as hyper-confident priors |
| Autism | Reduced dopamine-precision on priors → over-weighting of sensory signals |
| Addiction | Distorted precision toward drug-related predictions |
