## Core Contrarian Argument

The Free Energy Principle faces a fundamental epistemological problem that remains **unresolved as of 2025**: it may be unfalsifiable as a general theory. A theory that can explain any finding is not a scientific theory in the Popperian sense - it is a sophisticated post-hoc rationalization machine.

**The critics' strongest point**: The "remarkable flexibility in accommodating diverse findings" (cited in Myth of the Bayesian Brain, 2025) is not a feature of a good theory - it is the signature of an unfalsifiable one.

---

## The Four Critiques

### 1. Unfalsifiability at the Theoretical Level

**Friston's response**: FEP is a mathematical principle like calculus. You cannot falsify calculus. Specific generative models ARE falsifiable.

**Critics' counter**: This is a category error. The claim that "all biological systems minimize free energy" IS an empirical claim, not a mathematical tautology. If this claim cannot be falsified, it is not science - it is philosophy dressed as neuroscience.

**Current status**: The debate remains genuinely open. Philosophers of science are divided.

### 2. Biological Implausibility

Exact Bayesian inference is computationally intractable for neural circuits. The brain cannot literally compute Bayesian posteriors.

**FEP defense**: The brain implements variational approximations - approximate Bayesian inference, not exact.

**Problem**: "Approximate Bayesian inference" describes almost any neural process. This weakens the theory's specificity to near-zero.

### 3. The Tautology Charge

Specific formal critiques (2025):
1. Conflation of thermodynamic and information-theoretical "free energy" creates **category errors** - these are formally different quantities combined under one term
2. Causal relationships asserted across levels of description are **explanatorily circular**: the model explains X by assuming X is minimized
3. **Ontological confusion** between descriptive and prescriptive levels

### 4. The AIF vs. RL Evidence Gap

Despite theoretical arguments that AIF subsumes reinforcement learning, empirical head-to-head comparisons remain limited (as of 2025):
- Limited benchmark comparisons on standard RL environments
- Requirement to specify prior preferences = analogous to reward specification (possibly no easier)
- The eLife 2025 EEG study is a notable exception but one study does not settle the debate

## The Instrumentalism Resolution

A 2025 British Journal for Philosophy of Science paper proposes an exit: the **Literalist Fallacy** - the mistake of thinking FEP realism requires organisms to literally embody FEP mathematics.

Under instrumentalism: FEP is a useful toolkit for modeling cognitive systems - it generates good predictions and insights - without requiring the brain to "literally compute" variational free energy.

**This resolution has practical implications**: Applied researchers can use AIF architectures productively without committing to the grand claim that all self-organizing systems minimize free energy.
