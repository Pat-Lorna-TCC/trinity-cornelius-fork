## Core Finding

A Harvard/MGH research program is systematically formalizing Buddhist meditative states within Active Inference and computational phenomenology frameworks. This is not metaphor-mapping - it is formal mathematical modeling of:
- Jhana (absorptive concentrative states)
- Cessation events (temporary cessation of all experience)
- Non-dual awareness
- Deconstructive meditation trajectories

The research places three meditation styles (focused attention, open monitoring, non-dual) on a **single computational continuum** where each technique relinquishes increasingly engrained habits of prediction, including the predicted self.

---

## The Computational Account of Deconstructive Meditation

**The Laukkonen-Slagter Framework (highly cited 2024-2025):**

The brain makes predictions based on past experience. Deconstructive meditation brings one closer to the "here and now" by:
1. Disengaging anticipatory processes
2. Gradually reducing counterfactual temporally-deep cognition
3. Until all conceptual processing falls away
4. Unveiling a state of pure awareness

**In FEP terms**: Meditation is systematic training in precision attenuation across progressively deeper levels of the generative model hierarchy:
- Level 1: Attenuation of moment-to-moment sensory predictions (focused attention)
- Level 2: Attenuation of narrative/conceptual predictions (open monitoring)
- Level 3: Attenuation of the predicted self itself (non-dual / deconstructive)

---

## Cessation Events - A Computational Account

**Cessation** (the most advanced meditative state - temporary complete cessation of experience) now has a computational model:

- Cessation = generative model temporarily suspended
- Followed by reset to high-precision present-moment prediction
- Phenomenologically: absence of experience → profound clarity → insight into "emptiness of phenomena"

**FEP interpretation**: The generative model that normally runs continuously (generating the stream of consciousness through predictive processing) briefly stops. On restart, the system resets to a low-prior, high-sensory-precision state - which is experienced as unusual clarity and directness of perception.

This is the most computationally precise account of what Buddhist practitioners have described for millennia as the most significant meditative experience.

## Meditation Styles as Precision Strategies

| Style | FEP Mechanism | Phenomenology |
|-------|--------------|---------------|
| Focused Attention | High precision on chosen object; attenuation of distractor predictions | Concentration; reduced mind-wandering |
| Open Monitoring | Reduce precision on any single object; balanced global monitoring | Non-reactive awareness; equanimity |
| Non-Dual / Deconstructive | Attenuation of self-model precision itself | No-self experience; "just awareness" |
| Jhana | Near-total precision allocated to absorptive object | Bliss; complete absorption; ego dissolution |

## Why This Matters

This is a **paradigm shift** in contemplative science:
- Buddhism has described meditative states experientially for 2,500 years
- Neuroscience has correlated states with brain activity
- Computational phenomenology now provides the **mechanism**: what is actually being computed, optimized, and recalibrated during practice

This enables:
1. **Precision-targeted meditation protocols** - prescribe specific techniques for specific precision dysregulations
2. **Meditation as clinical intervention** - formal models allow RCTs with specific hypotheses
3. **AI meditation systems** - could model and guide precision recalibration in real-time
