# Cerebellum as Surprise Social Brain Hub

**Source**: Three landmark 2024 studies - Nature Reviews Neuroscience (October 2024, Van Overwalle, VUB); Current Biology (2024); Cognitive Cerebellum Mapping (November 2024)

## Core Insight

The cerebellum - previously understood exclusively as a motor coordination center - has been repositioned as an active social brain hub by three independent 2024 studies. The cerebellum contributes to theory of mind, emotion regulation, and "social smoothness" (the ability to navigate relationships and avoid social missteps). The posterior cerebellum (Crus I and Crus II) shows causal functional connectivity to the posterior superior temporal sulcus (pSTS) - a core social brain hub. This discovery extends the principle that the brain is "more social than we knew" to the oldest, most evolutionarily conserved structures.

## Evidence

**Study 1 - Nature Reviews Neuroscience (October 2024):**
- **Researcher**: Prof. Frank Van Overwalle (VUB)
- **Finding**: Cerebellum contributes to emotion regulation, theory of mind, and "social smoothness"
- **Social smoothness**: The ability to navigate social relationships and avoid social missteps
- **Mechanism**: Cerebellar prediction mechanisms extended to social prediction (timing social sequences)

**Study 2 - Current Biology (2024):**
- **Finding**: Causal involvement of POSTERIOR cerebellum in emotion perception
- **Connectivity**: Functional connectivity to posterior superior temporal sulcus (pSTS) - a core social brain hub
- **Method**: Causal manipulation (not just correlation)

**Study 3 - Cognitive Cerebellum Mapping (November 2024):**
- **Finding**: Adjacent but DISTINCT cerebellar regions specialized for higher-order cognition vs. social intelligence
- **Implication**: Social intelligence has dedicated cerebellar real estate

**Clinical Translation:**
- TMS stimulation of Crus I and Crus II may improve social cognition in autism, social anxiety disorder
- Initial evidence: improvements in cognitive sequencing in autistic individuals through magnetic stimulation
- **"Chronometry of social perception"** - timing accuracy as a hidden social skill

## Theoretical Significance

The cerebellum has long been associated with timing, prediction, and coordination of motor sequences. The 2024 research extends this "prediction machine" function to social prediction - anticipating others' responses, timing social interventions, and coordinating social sequences. This adds cerebellar prediction mechanisms to the Bayesian social brain model.

**The "social smoothness" concept is novel**: It describes the ability to execute social behavior in a coordinated, well-timed manner - a function that requires the same predictive timing capacities the cerebellum applies to motor movements.
