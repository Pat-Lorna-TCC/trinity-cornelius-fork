## Core Framework

The Cascading-Resonance Model (CRM) provides a unified three-layer framework for how psychological states spread through social systems - from the neural level (mirror neurons) through interpersonal synchrony up to group dynamics and online platforms. The key insight: emotional contagion does NOT spread randomly but follows engineered rules of amplification online. Once high-arousal content gains momentum, platforms PERSONALIZE AND REINFORCE its reach, converting individual emotional vulnerabilities into collective risks.

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - synthesizes established neuroscience with social media dynamics

---

## The Three-Layer Model

**Layer 1: Individual Resonance**
- Mirror neuron systems and emotional mimicry facilitate rapid spread of affective states
- Automatic, unconscious, somatic
- Nine identified mechanisms instantiate this layer

**Layer 2: Interpersonal Synchronization**
- Dyadic attunement processes
- Physiological co-regulation (skin conductance, cardiac, respiratory)
- Neural synchrony (hyperscanning evidence)

**Layer 3: Group-Level Dynamics**
- Social identification
- Emotion amplification
- Reinforcement of shared identity
- At scale: engineered amplification via platform algorithms

**Critical Mechanism**: The cascade only amplifies through Layer 3 when:
1. High-arousal content (not neutral/positive content)
2. Platform personalization locks in the reach
3. Identity resonance triggers sharing behavior

---

## The Online Amplification Finding

Emotional contagion online does NOT spread randomly - it follows ENGINEERED RULES:
- Once high-arousal content gains momentum → platforms personalize to maximize reach
- Individual emotional vulnerability → becomes COLLECTIVE risk through algorithmic amplification
- This converts Layer 1 (individual resonance) directly into Layer 3 (group dynamics) bypassing normal Layer 2 social regulation

**The crucial gap**: In face-to-face contexts, Layer 2 (interpersonal synchrony) moderates and regulates emotional spread. Online, Layer 2 is absent, so high-arousal content jumps directly from individual resonance to algorithmic group amplification.
