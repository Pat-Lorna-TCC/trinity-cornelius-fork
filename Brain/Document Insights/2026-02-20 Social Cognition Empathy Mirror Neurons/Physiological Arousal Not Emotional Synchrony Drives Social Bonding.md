# Physiological Arousal Not Emotional Synchrony Drives Social Bonding

**Source**: Chung V, Mennella R, Pacherie E, Grezes J. "Social Bonding Through Shared Experiences: The Role of Emotional Intensity" - *Royal Society Open Science* - October 30, 2024 - 11(10):240048

## Core Insight

Counter-intuitive finding: social bonding between strangers is driven by shared PHYSIOLOGICAL AROUSAL INTENSITY - not by feeling the same emotions. Chung et al. (2024) tested 56 stranger dyads and found that dyadic physiological arousal (skin conductance, cardiac, respiratory) under joint attention predicted mutual connectedness, but emotional convergence did NOT occur. You bond with others not because you feel what they feel, but because your bodies are equally activated in each other's presence.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: 56 stranger dyads (N=112); simultaneous physiological recording
**Measures**: Skin conductance, cardiac, respiratory arousal; subjective emotional reports; bonding ratings
**Key Result**:
- Shared physiological arousal INTENSITY predicted mutual connectedness (bonding)
- Emotional convergence (feeling the same emotion) did NOT occur
- Joint attention was the trigger condition for arousal synchronization

**Mechanism**: Social bonding between strangers depends on shared INTENSITY of activation in each other's presence, not on feeling the same emotions. The body is the primary social bridge; emotion labeling is secondary.

---

## Why This Is Counterintuitive

The intuitive model: "We bond because we feel the same thing" (emotional synchrony)
The actual mechanism: "We bond because our bodies are equally activated" (arousal synchrony)

This distinction matters enormously for:
- **Meditation retreats/sangha**: Buddhist community practice may create social bonding through shared somatic arousal (breath, body focus, collective quiet) rather than emotional contagion
- **Collective effervescence**: The "energy" of crowds at concerts, rituals, sports events is fundamentally a SOMATIC phenomenon
- **Digital interaction limitation**: Screen-based interaction cannot create the physiological co-presence needed for genuine bonding
