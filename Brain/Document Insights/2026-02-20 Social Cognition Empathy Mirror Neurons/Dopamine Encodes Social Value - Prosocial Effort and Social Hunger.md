# Dopamine Encodes Social Value - Prosocial Effort and Social Hunger

**Source**: Multiple - Chong et al. *Journal of Neuroscience* (January 2025); Molas et al. *Nature Communications* (April 2024); Tye KM et al. MIT "Social Hunger" research; *Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews* (2024)

## Core Insight

Three 2024-2025 findings collectively rewrite the dopamine story: (1) dopamine augmentation SPECIFICALLY increases motivation to exert effort FOR OTHERS, not just for self-reward; (2) images of socializing activate the same substantia nigra/VTA circuits as food images in hungry people - social connection is a biological drive like hunger; (3) oxytocin enhances dopamine release in the nucleus accumbens during social interactions, creating a dual-neuropeptide bonding engine. The mesolimbic dopamine system encodes SOCIAL VALUE broadly, not just personal gain.

---

## Evidence

### Finding 1: Dopamine Increases Prosocial Effort
**Study**: Chong et al., *Journal of Neuroscience*, January 2025 (JNEUROSCI.1593-24.2024)
**Method**: Parkinson's disease patients (dopaminergic deficiency) ON vs. OFF dopaminergic medication
**Key Finding**:
- PD patients normally more willing to work for themselves than for others (consistent with controls)
- When ON dopaminergic medication: INCREASED willingness to exert effort for OTHERS relative to self
- Dopamine augmentation specifically shifted the prosocial-to-self effort ratio
- **Interpretation**: Dopamine doesn't just motivate personal gain - it motivates prosocial agency

### Finding 2: Social Hunger Uses Hunger Circuits
**Researcher**: Kay Tye, MIT
**Method**: fMRI; images of socializing vs. food in hungry/socially isolated participants
**Key Finding**: Images of socializing activate the **substantia nigra pars compacta and ventral tegmental region** - the same regions activated by food images in hungry participants
**Interpretation**: Social connection is literally encoded as a biological drive (like hunger) in the dopamine system, not as a learned preference

### Finding 3: Social Novelty Circuit Constraint
**Study**: Molas et al., *Nature Communications*, April 2024 (15:2891)
**Key Finding**: Mesolimbic DA is strongly activated by SOCIAL NOVELTY, controlling interaction bout length. An IPN→LDTg GABAergic circuit constrains this - activated during FAMILIAR social encounters to REDUCE dopamine, shortening interactions with familiar stimuli
**Interpretation**: The dopamine system actively habituates social reward - explaining why novelty in relationships maintains dopamine engagement and familiarity reduces it

### Finding 4: Oxytocin-Dopamine Axis
**Study**: *Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews* (2024) 161:105675
**Key Finding**: Oxytocin enhances dopamine release in the nucleus accumbens during social interactions
**Interpretation**: OXT and DA are a dual-neuropeptide bonding system; the "warm feeling" of connection (OXT) is also a dopamine reward event

## Integration - The Revised Dopamine Picture

The dopamine system now appears to encode:
- Self-directed rewards (classic)
- Prosocial effort - working for others (new)
- Social novelty as primary reward
- Social hunger - baseline drive for connection like food
- Loneliness as dopaminergic deprivation state
