# Broken Mirror Theory Dismantled - Autism as Different Social Cognition

## Core Insight

The "Broken Mirror Hypothesis" - the dominant autism theory proposing that ASD results from mirror neuron system dysfunction - has been decisively dismantled by 2024-2025 evidence. Children with ASD show intact motor imagery AND intact facial mimicry. The emerging replacement framework positions autism social difficulties as arising from a multi-component failure across three systems (cognitive + emotional + behavioral), not broken mirroring hardware. Crucially, autistic individuals show GREATER neural synchrony when paired with similar-trait others, suggesting their social cognition is optimized for different contexts, not broken.

## Evidence

**Study 1 - Motor Imagery Intact:**
- **Publication**: *Frontiers in Neurology*, January 2025
- **Finding**: Children with ASD Level 1 "can imagine actions in a manner similar to their healthy peers"
- **Implication**: Motor imagery (core MNS function) unimpaired in ASD

**Study 2 - Facial Mimicry Intact:**
- **Publication**: British Psychological Society Research Digest, 2024
- **Finding**: Automatic facial mimicry - supposedly MNS-dependent - is intact in autism
- **Key Nuance**: Controls with higher self-rated empathy showed faster responses; autistic individuals showed no correlation, but this is a dissociation in the empathy-mimicry link, NOT a mimicry deficit

**Study 3 - Shared Neural Mechanisms:**
- **Publication**: *MDPI Applied Sciences*, June 2025
- **Finding**: Mu rhythm suppression (EEG proxy for MNS activation) reveals SHARED mechanisms across ASD and schizophrenia - suggesting common socio-cognitive pathways, not broken mirrors

**Study 4 - Greater Synchrony in Matched Dyads:**
- **Publication**: *Psychoradiology*, 2025 systematic review
- **Finding**: Dyads where BOTH individuals are high in autistic traits show GREATER neural synchrony than mismatched dyads
- **Implication**: Autistic individuals have their own coherent social communication style

**Emerging Multi-Component Framework:**
The "broken mirror" metaphor is replaced by "different mirroring patterns" or "contextual dysregulation" - social difficulties arise from failures across:
1. Cognitive processing of social rules/norms
2. Emotional regulation capacity
3. Behavioral execution in dynamic social contexts
