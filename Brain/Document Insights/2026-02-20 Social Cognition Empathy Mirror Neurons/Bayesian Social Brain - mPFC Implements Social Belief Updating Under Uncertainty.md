# Bayesian Social Brain - mPFC Implements Social Belief Updating Under Uncertainty

**Source**: Hofmans & van den Bos. "Neural Correlates of Bayesian Social Belief Updating in the Medial Prefrontal Cortex" - *Cerebral Cortex* - 2025; Mangalam et al. "The Myth of the Bayesian Brain" - *European Journal of Applied Physiology* - 2025

## Core Insight

The mPFC (medial prefrontal cortex) implements a Bayesian-like social belief updating process: participants use MORE social information when they are uncertain AND when others report being confident. Peer confidence serves as a "precision modulation signal" - the brain uses others' confidence as metadata about how much to weight their input. However, a 2025 critique argues the Bayesian brain hypothesis occupies an "ambiguous territory between useful metaphor and testable mechanistic explanation" - unfalsifiability being the central problem.

---

## Evidence

**mPFC Bayesian Social Updating (Hofmans & van den Bos, Cerebral Cortex, 2025)**:
- Neural correlates of social belief updating mapped in the medial prefrontal cortex
- Participants use MORE social information when:
  1. They are uncertain themselves (lower prior confidence)
  2. Others report being confident (higher source reliability)
- Peer confidence reports serve as **precision modulation signals**
- The mPFC implements the computational process of weighting social information by source reliability

**The Bayesian Brain Challenged (Mangalam et al., European J Applied Physiology, 2025)**:
- The hypothesis occupies "ambiguous territory between useful metaphor and testable mechanistic explanation"
- **Unfalsifiability**: Bayesian models can be adjusted post-hoc to fit virtually any data pattern
- Alternatives: Dynamic systems theory, ecological psychology, embodied cognition may better explain prediction and adaptive behavior
- These alternatives don't require probabilistic inference machinery

**Practical Resolution**:
- Social prediction errors (the gap between expected and actual social responses) drive belief updating in the mPFC
- This IS the neural basis of social surprise, trust calibration, and social learning
- The Bayesian critique doesn't eliminate the prediction error concept, but suggests the full probabilistic formalism may be unnecessary

## Social Prediction Errors = Dopaminergic Events

This finding directly connects the social belief updating system to the dopamine system:
- Social prediction errors are dopaminergic events
- Unexpected social rewards drive DA release
- When someone surprises you with unexpected generosity or hostility → dopamine prediction error signal → belief update
- The mPFC is implementing what the dopamine system processes: social surprise → learning
