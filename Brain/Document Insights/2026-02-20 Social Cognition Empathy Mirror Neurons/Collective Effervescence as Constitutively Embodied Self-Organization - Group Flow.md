## Core Framework

Collective effervescence (CE) - Durkheim's concept of the heightened energy felt in collective rituals - is NOT epiphenomenal or merely psychological. Kronsted (2025) using 4E cognition theory (enactive, embodied, embedded, extended) shows that CE is constitutively part of the embodied experience: it IS the self-organization, not a product of it. When high degrees of self-organizing bodily activity occur in a human crowd under the right boundary conditions, CE emerges as a genuine collective phenomenon. Critically, this maps precisely onto the neuroscience of individual flow states - making collective effervescence the GROUP-LEVEL EQUIVALENT of individual flow.

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - 4E cognition framework widely accepted; CE-as-self-organization is a recent theoretical advance

## Evidence

**Theoretical Grounding (Kronsted 2025 - Journal of Social Ontology)**:
- Integrates 4E cognition (enactive, embodied, embedded, extended) with Durkheim's CE theory
- CE = "undergoing strong self-organization with other agents"
- High degrees of self-organizing bodily activity + right boundary conditions → CE emergence
- CE is NOT epiphenomenal: it is constitutively part of the embodied experience (not just caused by it)

**Empirical Support (Koefler et al. 2024 - PSYB, N=789)**:
- CE at live music events predicts HAPPINESS ONE WEEK LATER
- Mechanisms: deep song immersion, parasocial artist bonds, social co-presence
- Effect persists well beyond the event itself

**Physiological Mechanism (Chung et al. 2024)**:
- Shared physiological arousal (not emotional synchrony) drives social bonding
- CE's "energy" effect is SOMATIC, not merely psychological
- The body is the primary site of collective experience

**Flow Neuroscience Parallel (Neuroscience of Consciousness, 2025)**:
- Individual flow: intense focus → inhibition of deep counterfactual planning → elimination of reflective self-awareness → temporally shallow, highly precise present-moment engagement
- This maps precisely onto Buddhist meditative absorption (jhana) AND onto collective effervescence
