# AI Theory of Mind - Prompted vs Spontaneous and the Projection Problem

**Source**: "Spontaneous Theory of Mind for Artificial Intelligence" - *arXiv* (2024); Strachan et al. (2024) LLM ToM testing; Zhang et al. "Theory of Mind in Human-AI Interaction" - *IBM Research / CHI 2024*; "Mindful Machines" - *Psychology & Marketing* (2025)

## Core Insight

LLMs achieve human-level performance on classic Theory of Mind tasks - but trivial alterations to those same tasks completely undermine LLM performance (Ullman 2023). This reveals the fundamental distinction: current AI has "prompted ToM" (mental state inference when explicitly instructed) but lacks "spontaneous ToM" (emerging from unintentional cognitive processes). More profoundly: humans systematically apply ToM to AI through anthropomorphism regardless of actual AI capability - attributing mental states AI doesn't possess. This suggests ToM is fundamentally a PROJECTION SYSTEM, not a detection system. The implications for human-AI interaction design are significant.

---

## Evidence

**LLM Performance on ToM Tasks**:
- Strachan et al. (2024): LLMs achieve human-level performance on classic ToM tasks in AT LEAST SOME cases
- Ullman (2023): TRIVIAL alterations to the same tasks completely undermine LLM performance
- **Interpretation**: LLMs learned the TEST PATTERNS, not the underlying ToM capacity

**Prompted vs. Spontaneous ToM Distinction (arXiv 2024)**:
- **Prompted ToM**: Mental state inference only when EXPLICITLY INSTRUCTED - what current AI does
- **Spontaneous ToM**: Emerging from unintentional cognitive processes, NOT requiring explicit task demands - what humans do automatically
- True artificial social intelligence requires spontaneous ToM; current AI lacks this

**Mutual ToM Paradox (Zhang et al. 2024 - IBM Research / CHI)**:
- Framework: both human AND AI possess ToM → bidirectional social simulation
- Unexpected finding: AI's independent ToM didn't improve team performance
- Enhancing the HUMAN's understanding of the agent mattered more
- Bidirectional communication SOMETIMES DECREASED performance
- **Interpretation**: Too much AI social modeling creates interference rather than synergy

**Human Attribution of Mind to AI (Multiple Sources)**:
- Humans systematically apply ToM to AI through anthropomorphism
- This attribution happens REGARDLESS of actual AI capability
- Shapes all interaction with AI whether or not AI has inner states
- **Profound implication**: Social cognition is fundamentally about MODELING minds, not detecting them

**AI ToM and Consumer Trust (Psychology & Marketing, 2025)**:
- AI's ToM capability (defined as ability to infer human thoughts/feelings empathetically while responding proactively) measurably influences consumer trust and recommendation acceptance
- Even the APPEARANCE of ToM is functionally real in social context

## The Projection System Insight

The finding that humans anthropomorphize AI regardless of actual inner states reveals something fundamental about human ToM:

**ToM is not a detection system** (passively identifying others' mental states)
**ToM is a projection system** (actively constructing models of others' minds, then treating those models as real)

This has always been true in human-human interaction too - we don't "detect" others' minds, we project models onto them. The AI case makes this visible because the target CANNOT have the inner states we attribute to it, yet the attribution happens anyway and shapes behavior.
