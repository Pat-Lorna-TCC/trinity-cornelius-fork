# Predictive Processing Is More Cognitive Than Sensory - PFC Generates Its Own Prediction Errors

## Core Insight

Classical predictive coding assumed a strict hierarchical flow: higher areas generate predictions sent *down* to sensory areas; mismatches generate prediction errors *at sensory stages*. A 2025 *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* review challenges this: genuine prediction errors emerge in the prefrontal cortex itself. This reframes predictive processing as "predictive routing" - a cognitive control mechanism, not primarily a sensory error-correction mechanism.

---

## Evidence

**Research Status**: High-impact review in *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* (2025)
**Evidence Type**: Synthesis of fMRI and electrophysiology studies showing PFC prediction error signals
**Key Claim**: Genuine prediction errors (not just relayed signals) are computed in PFC
**Alternative Framework Proposed**: "Predictive routing" - flexible routing of predictions and errors through cognitive control circuits

---

## Classical vs. New Predictive Processing

**Classical predictive coding**:
- Higher areas → predictions → lower sensory areas
- Mismatches → prediction errors → propagated up hierarchy
- PFC role: generate predictions only, not process errors
- Primarily sensory/perceptual mechanism

**New "predictive routing" framework**:
- PFC is an ACTIVE PARTICIPANT in constructing expected states
- PFC computes its own prediction errors, not just receives them from below
- Routing of predictions and errors is flexible - not strictly hierarchical
- Primarily COGNITIVE mechanism with sensory components

---

## Why This Matters for Imagination Science

**Direct implication for endogenous learning from imagination**:

If prediction errors arise in PFC (not just sensory cortex), then imagined scenarios can generate genuine learning signals WITHOUT sensory grounding. This is mechanistically consistent with the CU Boulder finding (Dabas & Benoit, 2025) that imagination generates endogenous dopamine prediction errors.

**The chain**:
1. PFC runs imagination (mental simulation / rollouts)
2. PFC generates prediction errors from the imagined outcomes
3. Dopamine mediates these prediction errors (precision signal)
4. Neural representations update based on imagined experience
5. Learning from pure imagination becomes neurologically possible

This unifies three independent findings from this research report.

## Reframing Confirmation Bias

**Existing vault note**: Finding a confirmation of the belief creates a spike of Dopamine

Under the new predictive routing framework:
- Beliefs are high-precision priors in the PFC
- Confirming information → PFC generates small/positive prediction error → dopamine stability signal
- Disconfirming information → PFC generates large negative prediction error → dopamine dip → aversive
- The cognitive pain of belief change is PFC-generated prediction error, not just emotional resistance

This gives a computational mechanism for why belief change is cognitively exhausting (not just emotionally threatening): it requires the PFC to continuously generate large prediction errors until the prior updates.
