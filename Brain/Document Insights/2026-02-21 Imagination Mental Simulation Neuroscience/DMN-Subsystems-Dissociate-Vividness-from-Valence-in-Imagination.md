## Core Insight

The DMN is not a unified "imagination network" - its two primary subsystems serve distinct functions in imagined future events. The ventral DMN responds to how *vivid* an imagined event is; the dorsal/core DMN responds to the *emotional valence* (positive/negative) of the imagined event. These are separate functions running in parallel within the same network.

---

## Evidence

**Ventral DMN (medial temporal lobe subsystem)**:
- Responds to VIVIDNESS of imagined events
- Indifferent to whether the event is positive or negative
- Essentially the hippocampal-scene-construction output evaluator

**Dorsal/Core DMN**:
- Responds to VALENCE (positive/negative) of imagined events
- Indifferent to how vivid the imagery is
- Essentially the emotional significance evaluator

**Temporal Distance Update (Casadio et al. 2024)**:
- fMRI study: participants imagined themselves at different time points (past, present, near future, far future)
- When events were perceived as temporally *closer*, brain regions for self-focused internal thinking were MORE active
- Temporal distance modulates which DMN subsystems are recruited

**7T MRI Mapping (Paquola et al. 2025)**:
- Most comprehensive DMN subregion map to date
- Reveals previously undescribed subregions with distinct cytoarchitectural profiles
- Replaces "DMN = imagination" with a nuanced functional map

---

## Why This Refines the Existing DMN Understanding

**Current vault note** Default Mode Network (DMN) describes DMN as the self-narrative network suppressed by flow/meditation. This finding adds precision:

- The vividness subsystem (ventral DMN) is most relevant to pure imagination quality
- The valence subsystem (dorsal DMN) is most relevant to emotional motivation and self-narrative
- Suppressing DMN in flow states may differentially affect these: task absorption may suppress valence processing while preserving some vividness capacity

This explains why athletes in flow can still form vivid mental images of their actions (vividness subsystem partially active) while not engaging self-narrative (valence/self subsystem suppressed).

---

## Temporal Distance Effect on Imagination

The closer in time an imagined future event, the MORE active the self-focused internal thinking regions become.

**Implication**: When imagining tomorrow vs. imagining in 5 years:
- Tomorrow activates stronger self-referential DMN processing
- 5-years-from-now activates more abstract, less self-referential processing
- This explains why near-future scenarios feel more real and emotionally charged

This connects to EFT research: interventions that make future events feel closer (vivid, specific, realistic) work by pulling near-future-like DMN activation for distant events.
