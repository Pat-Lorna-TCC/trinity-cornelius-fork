## The Consilience Zone

Three independent scientific disciplines converge on a single principle: **imagination generates real learning signals that reshape neural representations, preferences, and behavior**.

| Domain | Finding | Mechanism |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Cognitive Neuroscience** | Imagined events generate dopamine prediction errors (Dabas & Benoit 2025) | Ventral striatum + dopamine |
| **Motor Neuroscience** | Motor imagery improves performance on non-imagined linked movements (Gippert 2025) | Alpha/beta band synchronization + motor pathways |
| **Behavioral Science** | Episodic future thinking reduces temporal discounting (Tang 2024, g=0.52) | Temporal window expansion + motivation |
| **Computational Neuroscience** | PFC-hippocampal planning rollouts produce behavioral change equivalent to physical practice | Neural simulation = neural practice |

These are not four descriptions of the same study. They are four independent research traditions finding the same truth from different angles.

## The Unified Principle

**Imagination is a learning modality, not just a content generation system.**

The brain's learning machinery - dopamine prediction errors, motor adaptation circuits, temporal motivation systems, and hippocampal replay - all respond to imagined events with the same algorithms they use for real events.

**Implication**: "Practice makes perfect" needs updating. "Practice (real or imagined) makes perfect" is the neurologically accurate version.
