## Core Insight

Episodic Future Thinking (imagining vivid personal future events) reduces temporal discounting - the tendency to over-value immediate rewards. A 2025 study shows that adding narrative structure (NEFT) - requiring imagined events to form a thematically coherent story linked to a broader personal goal - amplifies this effect significantly. Storytelling structure makes the future more motivationally real.

---

## Evidence

**Key Data Points**:
- EFT effect on delay discounting: g=0.52 (positive events, Tang et al. 2024) - moderate-large effect
- Meta-analysis of 12 studies (951 adults with overweight/obesity): EFT reduces both delay discounting AND food choice impulsivity (Colton et al. 2024)
- E-cigarette users (2024): monetary delay discounting showed significantly greater decrease following EFT vs. standardized episodic thinking (p=.006)
- NEFT outperforms EFT by requiring thematic linkage to personal goals

**Why NEFT Outperforms EFT**:
1. Humans are natural storytellers - narrative coherence makes imagined futures more meaningful and memorable
2. Thematic linkage to goals increases the salience of future rewards
3. Creates a stronger "temporal window expansion" effect

**Citation**: NEFT feasibility study (2025), *Health Psychology and Behavioral Medicine*

---

## Mechanism: Temporal Window Expansion

Standard EFT works by making the future feel more vivid and present-like, reducing the "psychological distance" that drives discounting. The brain evaluates future rewards as closer in time when it can simulate them in rich detail.

NEFT adds a second mechanism: narrative coherence links individual imagined events into a causal chain connecting present choices to future states. This doesn't just make the future vivid - it makes the *path* to the future feel traversable.

---

## Cross-Domain: Imagination-Dopamine-Learning Triangle

This finding completes a three-part consilience:

1. Imagination generates real dopamine prediction errors (Dabas & Benoit, 2025)
2. NEFT reduces delay discounting by making future rewards salient via imagination
3. **Combined mechanism**: Narrative future imagination generates dopamine signals for future states, which reduces the motivational asymmetry between now and later

The dopamine system, optimized for near-term rewards, gets partially "updated" by imagining future states with the same machinery it uses for real experiences.
