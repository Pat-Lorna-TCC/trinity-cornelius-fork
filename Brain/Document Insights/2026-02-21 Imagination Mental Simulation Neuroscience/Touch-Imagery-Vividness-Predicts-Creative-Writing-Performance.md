# Touch Imagery Vividness Predicts Creative Writing Performance via Semantic Integration

## Core Insight

Touch (haptic/tactile) imagery vividness - not visual imagery - is the strongest predictor of creative writing performance. The mechanism is semantic integration and reorganization: rich tactile imagery enables writers to restructure semantic relationships between concepts, creating the unexpected associations that make writing feel vivid and original. Multisensory imagination matters for creativity more than the visually-dominated model of creative imagination.

---

## Evidence

**Key Results**:
- Positive correlation between touch imagery vividness and creative writing performance
- Mechanism: touch imagery facilitates creative writing via *semantic integration and reorganization* - not just visual simulation
- Suggests multisensory imagery is more creativity-relevant than visual imagery alone

**Significance**: Most creativity research and mental imagery research focuses on visual imagery. This finding challenges the visual-centric model.

**Citation**: *Communications Biology* (2025)

---

## Why Touch Over Vision?

**Possible mechanisms**:
1. Touch imagery is more embodied and less abstract - it grounds semantic concepts in physical sensation, enabling unexpected cross-domain connections
2. Tactile metaphors ("grasping a concept," "rough idea," "smooth argument") are among the most common in language - tactile imagery may directly activate semantic networks differently than visual imagery
3. Touch imagery is more cognitively demanding for most people (less practiced than visual) - the effort required for rich tactile imagery may drive deeper semantic processing

**Connection to embodied cognition**: This finding aligns with extended mind / embodied cognition research showing that conceptual thought is grounded in bodily experience. Touch is perhaps the most body-near of the senses.
