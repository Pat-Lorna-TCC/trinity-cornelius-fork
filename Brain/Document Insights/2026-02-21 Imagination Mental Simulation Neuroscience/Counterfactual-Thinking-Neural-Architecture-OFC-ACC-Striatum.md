**Source**: Meta-analysis synthesizing 20 fMRI studies. (2025). *Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews*. (Also: Ramadan, Tang, Watters, Jazayeri. "Computational basis of hierarchical and counterfactual information processing." *Nature Human Behaviour*, 2025.)

## Core Insight

Counterfactual cognition ("what if?") recruits a specific multi-region network distinct from general episodic simulation. The bilateral orbitofrontal cortex evaluates counterfactual value and regret, the anterior cingulate monitors the contradiction between factual and counterfactual worlds, and the hippocampus provides the episodic simulation engine. The unique feature of counterfactual thinking is the cognitive tension it generates - simultaneously holding two contradictory realities.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Meta-analysis of 20 fMRI studies using ALE (Activation Likelihood Estimation)
**Core network identified**:
- **Bilateral orbitofrontal cortex (OFC)** - Evaluating counterfactual value and regret/relief
- **Right anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)** - Conflict detection and error monitoring
- **Left caudate (striatum)** - Reward-based counterfactual learning
- **Left amygdala** - Emotional processing (regret, relief)
- **Left hippocampus/parahippocampal gyrus** - Episodic simulation of alternatives
- **Left insula** - Interoceptive emotional signaling

**What distinguishes counterfactual thinking from other episodic simulation**:
- Additionally activates bilateral inferior parietal lobe (integration of alternative scenarios)
- Additionally activates posterior medial frontal cortex (conflict between factual and counterfactual)
- The *inherent contradiction* between counterfactual and factual drives conflict detection uniquely

**Two distinct processes**:
1. *Counterfactual generation* - voluntarily imagining alternative self-referential scenarios
2. *Counterfactual evaluation* - assessing externally provided unchosen outcomes during decision-making

**Citation**: Meta-analysis (2025), *Neuroscience and Biobehavioral Reviews*

## What Makes Counterfactual Thinking Unique

Standard episodic imagination simulates ONE scenario. Counterfactual thinking requires the brain to simultaneously maintain TWO states:
- The world as it is/was (factual)
- The world as it could have been (counterfactual)

The ACC activates precisely because of this contradiction - its standard function is conflict detection. Counterfactual thinking is cognitively expensive because it demands maintaining a contradiction.
