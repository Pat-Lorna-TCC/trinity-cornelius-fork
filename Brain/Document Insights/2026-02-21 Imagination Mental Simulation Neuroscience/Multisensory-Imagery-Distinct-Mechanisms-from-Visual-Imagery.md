## Core Insight

Spatial attention improves performance in BOTH mental imagery and visual perception, but through distinct neural mechanisms: mental imagery engages frontal alpha activity; visual perception modulates posterior alpha. This challenges the simple "imagery = weak perception" equivalence. Imagery and perception share representations but use different attentional mechanisms. Additionally, a meta-analysis reveals three distinct brain networks during mental imagery: one supramodal (shared across imagery types) and two modality-specific (auditory vs. visual).

## Evidence

**Key 2025 finding (Journal of Neuroscience)**:
- Spatial attention shortens response times in both mental imagery and visual perception
- But through *distinct mechanisms*:
  - Imagery: spatial attention engages FRONTAL alpha activity
  - Perception: spatial attention modulates POSTERIOR alpha activity
- Same behavioral benefit, different neural pathway

**Multisensory ALE meta-analysis (2024)**:
- Three distinct networks identified during mental imagery:
  1. **Supramodal network**: Shared across imagery types - attention, memory retrieval, motor preparation
  2. **Auditory-specific network**: Active specifically during auditory imagery
  3. **Visual-specific network**: Active specifically during visual imagery

**YOTO Dataset (2025, Scientific Data)**:
- New EEG dataset for studying concurrent visual, auditory, and tactile imagery
- Enables research into how the brain coordinates multiple simultaneous internal simulations

## The "Imagery = Weak Perception" Myth

The classic view: imagery is just perception with the eyes closed - weak sensory activation trickling down from memory. This finding shows this is wrong in an important way:

- The visual cortex activations are similar (shared representations - true)
- But the **control mechanism** - how attention is directed within those representations - is completely different
- Imagery uses FRONTAL alpha to direct attention into memory-space
- Perception uses POSTERIOR alpha to direct attention into sensory-space

These are different operating systems running on similar hardware.
