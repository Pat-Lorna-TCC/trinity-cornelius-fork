# Fusiform Gyrus Tracks Signal Strength Not Source for Reality Monitoring

**Source**: Dijkstra, N., von Rein, T., Kok, P., & Fleming, S. (2025). "A neural basis for distinguishing imagination from reality." *Neuron*. Published June 5, 2025. UCL. https://www.cell.com/neuron/fulltext/S0896-6273(25)00362-9

**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)

## Core Insight

The brain does not distinguish imagination from reality by tracking the *source* of sensory signals. Instead, it tracks the *combined strength* of sensory activity in the fusiform gyrus - from either imagery or perception. When imagination is vivid enough to exceed the fusiform signal threshold, the brain classifies it as real. Reality monitoring is a threshold mechanism, not a source-tagging system.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: 26 participants looked for a faint target pattern within noisy background while simultaneously imagining a pattern (same or different to target). A real pattern was present only half the time.
**Key Results**:
- When imagination was particularly vivid, participants frequently mistook mental images for external reality
- Activity fluctuations in the fusiform gyrus predicted reality confusions on a trial-by-trial basis
- Anterior insula (prefrontal cortex) collaborates with fusiform in reality judgment and metacognitive processes
- The brain uses *signal strength* - not signal source - as the primary criterion for reality judgment

**Citation**: Dijkstra, Fleming, Kok, von Rein (2025), *Neuron*

---

## Why This Matters

**Solves a longstanding puzzle**: If imagination and perception share neural substrates, how does the brain avoid constant confusion between the two? The answer: it doesn't always succeed - it relies on signal strength, which is a probabilistic threshold, not a categorical distinction.

**Explains multiple phenomena**:
- Why vivid imagination can "feel real" - it literally exceeds the reality threshold
- Why psychosis involves disrupted reality monitoring - dopamine dysregulation (see FEP account) inflates precision weights on internally generated signals, making them appear more real
- Why aphantasics rarely confuse imagination for reality - their imagery signals are too weak to cross the threshold
- Why certain meditation states blur boundaries between experience and imagination
