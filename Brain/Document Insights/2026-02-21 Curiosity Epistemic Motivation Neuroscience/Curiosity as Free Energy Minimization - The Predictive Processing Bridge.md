## Core Framework

The 2024-2025 curiosity neuroscience literature is converging with the Free Energy Principle / Active Inference framework from a different direction. When integrated, they produce a unified account: **curiosity is the motivated, behaviorally expressed face of the brain's fundamental drive to minimize free energy** (prediction error / uncertainty). Information-seeking is active inference.

---

## The Convergence

**From Curiosity Neuroscience (2025)**:
- Dopamine encodes prediction errors across ALL domains (value-free teaching signal)
- Curiosity = the motivated response to high prediction error states
- Information seeking = active resolution of prediction errors
- The brain builds predictive models; curiosity is what drives that model-building

**From Predictive Processing / Active Inference**:
- The brain is a prediction machine minimizing free energy (surprisal / prediction error)
- Action is taken to bring the world into alignment with predictions (active inference)
- Perception is bringing predictions into alignment with sensory data
- Curiosity = motivated free energy minimization = the subjective experience of high-priority prediction error

**The Unification**:
```
High Prediction Error (surprisal)
        ↓
Dopaminergic prediction error signal (value-free)
        ↓
Appraisal via vmPFC (coping potential high?)
        ↓ YES
Subjective Curiosity (phenomenological experience)
        ↓
Information-seeking behavior (active inference / active free energy minimization)
        ↓
New sensory data / experience
        ↓
Updated generative model (reduced surprisal)
        ↓
Dopamine "completion" signal
        ↓
Memory consolidation (hippocampal surge = aha moment)
```

## Information Gap Theory as Active Inference

Loewenstein's classical Information Gap Theory (1994) - curiosity as awareness of knowledge gap driving fill - is now formally derivable from active inference:
- Knowledge gap = high free energy region in the generative model
- Curiosity = the phenomenological marker of high free energy
- Information-seeking = active inference to reduce that region's free energy
- Satiation = free energy reduced, new model stable, curiosity extinguishes

**Why people avoid uncomfortable information (Ostrich Effect)**:
- Information may increase rather than decrease free energy (increase uncertainty)
- Active inference then predicts avoidance: don't seek information that increases surprisal
- Horn et al. (2024, *Social Science and Medicine*): curiosity can override this when framed to make information acquisition feel resolution-directed rather than uncertainty-increasing
