## The Gap

The 2024-2025 curiosity neuroscience literature does NOT yet contain a direct fMRI study measuring curiosity induction effects on confirmation bias reduction. Despite strong theoretical groundwork and supporting indirect evidence, the specific question "does inducing a curious state causally reduce confirmation bias and improve belief updating?" remains empirically unaddressed.

---

## Why This Matters

This gap is significant because it sits at the intersection of the vault's three most important hubs:
- **Dopamine hub**: Curiosity uses the same dopaminergic prediction-error system that drives confirmation bias
- **Identity hub**: Confirmation bias is identity-protective cognition; curiosity may temporarily decouple beliefs from identity
- **Decision-making hub**: Confirmation bias is one of the most persistent cognitive biases affecting judgment quality

If curiosity induction directly reduces confirmation bias, it becomes a practical intervention for better thinking - not just a pleasant mental state.

---

## What We Know (Supporting Indirect Evidence)

1. **Epistemic curiosity → intellectual humility** (well-established personality correlation)
2. **High intellectual humility → better attention to evidence quality** - people scoring high on epistemic curiosity show superior attention to evidence regardless of its alignment with their priors
3. **Curiosity → engagement with disconfirming information** - curious states force engagement with information that might challenge current beliefs (the mechanism of confirmation bias reversal)
4. **vmPFC pathway (Cohanpour et al., 2024)**: vmPFC encodes uncertainty AND generates curiosity; vmPFC is also involved in belief updating and error correction (same region potentially serving both functions)
5. **IAMEC model (Erdemli, 2025)**: Curiosity decouples uncertainty from threat (high coping potential); this decoupling is mechanistically similar to what would be needed to disengage identity-protective cognition
6. **Curiosity state may reduce identity-protective cognition** by temporarily reducing the precision on self-model priors (beginner's mind mechanism)

---

## The Theoretical Mechanism (Hypothesis-Level)

**Proposed pathway**:
Curiosity induction
→ Increased vmPFC sensitivity to uncertainty (not threat)
→ Reduced amygdala threat-processing of belief-challenging information
→ Temporarily reduced precision on self/identity priors (self-model less defended)
→ Information from disconfirming sources processed with less prior filtering
→ Belief updating enhanced
→ Confirmation bias reduced

This mechanism integrates the IAMEC model (coping potential gate) with the vmPFC precision-weighting pathway and the identity-as-high-precision-self-model framework.

---

## What Research Is Needed

**Critical Study Design**:
- Curiosity induction (e.g., information gap creation about a neutral topic)
- Followed by exposure to belief-challenging information on a separate political/identity topic
- Measure belief updating accuracy vs. control condition (no curiosity induction)
- fMRI to track vmPFC, amygdala, and ACC responses to belief-challenging information during curious vs. neutral state
- Between-subjects: high vs. low trait curiosity
- Measure if curiosity induction transfers (does curiosity in domain A reduce confirmation bias in domain B?)
