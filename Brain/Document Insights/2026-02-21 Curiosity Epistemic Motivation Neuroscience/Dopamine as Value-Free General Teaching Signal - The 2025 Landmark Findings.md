## Core Insight

Two landmark 2025 papers establish that dopamine is not primarily a reward signal - it is a **general-purpose prediction error encoder** that fires for ANY departure from expectation, whether reward, action, sensory, or informationally neutral. This is the most significant revision to dopamine neuroscience in decades and directly explains why curiosity (the motivated drive to resolve prediction errors) is built into the architecture of learning itself.

---

## Evidence

**Study 1 - Value-Free Action Prediction Errors (Nature, 2025)**:
- **Design**: Dopamine neuron activity recording during behavioral tasks designed to separate value from predictability
- **Key Result**: Dopamine neurons in the tail of the striatum encode "action prediction errors" (APEs) - the difference between action taken and how predictable it was - **completely independent of reward value**
- **Implication**: A value-free dopaminergic teaching signal exists alongside the reward-based system, supporting habitual/repetitive learning

**Study 2 - Cross-Domain Value-Neutral Prediction Errors (Science Advances, 2025)**:
- **Design**: Animals learned associations between stimuli with zero biological relevance (value-neutral latent learning)
- **Key Result**: Striatal dopamine (both NAcc and DMS) signaled prediction errors for completely neutral stimuli - dopamine appeared, disappeared when cues became predictable, and returned when unpredicted
- **Dynamics**: Identical to reward prediction error dynamics, but for zero-value stimuli
- **Replication**: Two independent labs converging on same conclusion in same year

**Combined Implication**: Dopamine operates as a "general predictability-dependent teaching signal" conveying information about departures from ANY prediction - value, action, or sensory.

## Why This Massively Expands the Existing Dopamine Framework

The vault already has an extensive dopamine framework centered on reward prediction error and the uncertainty-dopamine-belief loop. These 2025 findings extend the framework in a crucial direction:

| Previous Understanding | New Understanding |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Dopamine = reward signal | Dopamine = general prediction signal |
| Fires for reward surprises | Fires for ANY unpredicted event |
| Curiosity "uses" dopamine | Curiosity IS dopamine doing its core job |
| Learning motivated by reward | All learning driven by prediction error |

**The profound implication for curiosity**: Curiosity is not a "bonus" motivational overlay on top of learning - it is the phenomenological face of the brain's prediction error system operating exactly as designed. When you feel curious, dopamine is doing its fundamental job.
