## Core Insight

Intolerance of uncertainty (IU) and curiosity both drive information seeking, but they have **opposite affective profiles and produce opposite behavioral outcomes**. When IU drives information seeking instead of curiosity, the result is compulsive hoarding of digital information, chronic anxiety, and impaired learning. This is the maladaptive pole of the curiosity-anxiety uncertainty spectrum.

---

## The Curiosity vs. IU Contrast

| Dimension | Curiosity | Intolerance of Uncertainty (IU) |
|-----------|-----------|--------------------------------|
| Affective response to uncertainty | Positive (appetitive, interesting) | Negative (threatening, distressing) |
| Information sought | Arbitrary/interesting information | Reward outcome / safety information |
| Effect on learning | Enhances learning | Impairs learning (anxiety interferes) |
| Patch-leaving behavior | Appropriate (leaves when gains drop) | Stays too long (hoards) |
| Information management | Selective and purposeful | Indiscriminate and compulsive |

**Key connection to IAMEC**: IU = chronically low coping potential → all uncertainty routes to anxiety (not curiosity) → safety-seeking information behavior

---

## The Information Hoarding Mechanism (2025)

**Chain mediation pathway**:
IU → Information Overload → Rumination → Anxiety → Information Hoarding

- IU predicts saving excessive digital content (articles, screenshots, bookmarks)
- IU predicts fear of deleting information
- The chain: feeling unable to handle uncertainty → overloading on information → ruminating about what you might miss → anxiety → hoarding more to prevent anxiety

**This is digital compulsive behavior driven by anxiety masquerading as curiosity** - the person feels "interested" but is actually managing anxiety about missing something critical.

---

## The Information Foraging Pathology

Optimal foraging theory applied to information:
- Adaptive (curiosity-driven): Leave information "patch" when marginal gain drops below average
- Maladaptive (IU-driven): Stay in patch indefinitely out of fear of missing crucial reward information
- Digital environment enablers: Infinite scroll, push notifications, algorithmic feeds - all designed to prevent patch-leaving, exploiting IU

**Why modern digital environments are particularly dangerous for high-IU individuals**:
- Near-infinite information = patch never depleted = hoarding never limited
- Algorithmic optimization = feeds preferentially surface safety/reward/threat information = IU activated constantly
- Social media's intermittent variable reinforcement = unpredictable reward information = IU management nightmare

## The Children's Study Findings (SAGE Open, 2025)

- Curiosity → POSITIVE affective responses to uncertainty + improved learning
- IU → NEGATIVE affective responses + anxiety + impaired learning
- Moderate POSITIVE correlation: IU and deprivation-type curiosity (wanting to fill gaps) can co-exist
- Moderate NEGATIVE correlation: IU and interest-type curiosity (joyous exploration) are inversely related

**The developmental implication**: Cultivating genuine curiosity in children (rather than just information-seeking behavior) requires building coping potential alongside information gap creation. Presenting knowledge gaps to high-IU children without coping support may INCREASE anxiety, not curiosity.
