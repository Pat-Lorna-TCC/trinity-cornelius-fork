# vmPFC as Generator of Perceptual Curiosity from Sensory Uncertainty

## Core Insight

The ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC) mediates the conversion of sensory uncertainty into the subjective feeling of curiosity. This is the first direct neural evidence pinpointing WHERE in the brain perceptual curiosity is generated - not in sensory cortex (which detects uncertainty) nor in ACC (which recruits cognitive resources to resolve it), but in the vmPFC which sits between them.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: fMRI; 32 participants (17F, 15M); novel "texforms" paradigm - algorithmically distorted images of animals/objects that are ambiguous but contain recognizable structure
**Key Measure**: OTC Certainty - certainty conveyed by occipital-temporal cortex multivoxel patterns, validated with machine learning classifiers

**Key Findings**:
- Perceptual curiosity peaked at LOW confidence (subjective uncertainty) - mirroring the inverted-U function seen in epistemic curiosity
- OTC Certainty negatively correlated with curiosity - more sensory uncertainty = more curiosity
- **vmPFC mediated the pathway from sensory uncertainty to curiosity** (the critical finding)
- ACC tracked confidence and curiosity but did NOT mediate the uncertainty-curiosity link
- ACC function: recruits cognitive resources to satisfy curiosity AFTER vmPFC generates it

**Direct Quote from Dr. Gottlieb (Columbia)**: "This is really the first time we can link the subjective feeling of curiosity about information to the way your brain represents that information."

---

## Neural Pathway Clarified

```
Sensory input (ambiguous)
    ↓
OTC (occipital-temporal cortex) - encodes sensory uncertainty
    ↓
vmPFC - converts uncertainty → subjective curiosity signal [KEY STEP]
    ↓
ACC - recruits cognitive resources to resolve curiosity
    ↓
Dopaminergic system - drives information-seeking behavior
```

## Significance for Depression/Anxiety Research

The vmPFC is also critically involved in:
- Belief updating and error correction
- Value assignment
- Self-referential processing

Clinical connection: Depression and anxiety impair curiosity. The 2024 Hilmerich et al. mini-review suggests this may relate to **impaired vmPFC encoding of sensory uncertainty** - the uncertainty-curiosity conversion pathway breaks down, so ambiguous stimuli no longer generate curiosity but instead generate anxiety (when coping potential is low) or nothing (anhedonia/apathy).

This creates a diagnostic and therapeutic implication: vmPFC responses to ambiguous stimuli could serve as a biomarker of curiosity capacity - useful for tracking depression treatment response.
