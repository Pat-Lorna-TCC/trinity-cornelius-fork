## Core Framework

Anxiety and curiosity are **not opposites** - they are two responses to the SAME underlying state (uncertainty/prediction error), differentiated by a single variable: **coping potential**. The same knowledge gap, the same uncertainty, the same ambiguous situation can generate either curiosity or anxiety depending on whether the person believes they can handle what they find.

**The Formula**:
- Uncertainty + High Coping Potential → Curiosity (dopaminergic processes dominate; engage and explore)
- Uncertainty + Low Coping Potential → Anxiety (amygdalar processes dominate; avoid and defend)

---

## The IAMEC Model (Erdemli, 2025)

The Integrative Appraisal Model of Epistemic Curiosity specifies the appraisal profile that generates curiosity:

**Curiosity = HIGH relevance + HIGH coping potential** applied to a knowledge gap

When coping potential drops:
- Same high relevance + LOW coping potential = anxiety (not curiosity)
- This explains why the same unknown can be **exciting to one person and terrifying to another** - it is literally the same neural uncertainty signal processed differently by the appraisal system

---

## Neural Mechanism

**ACC (anterior cingulate cortex)**: Signals cognitive conflict in BOTH anxiety and curiosity - it detects the uncertainty but does not determine the response

**The Gate**:
- Amygdala: Appraises uncertainty with low coping potential → anxiety, avoidance
- Dopaminergic system: Appraises uncertainty with high coping potential → curiosity, approach

**Individual Differences in Curiosity-Memory Benefits** (Hilmerich et al., 2024):
- ACC-hippocampus bilateral functional connectivity strength predicts how much individuals benefit from curiosity-driven memory enhancement
- Those with stronger intrinsic ACC-hippocampus connectivity show LARGER curiosity-memory benefits
- Connectivity in left hippocampus specifically predictive

## Key Implications

**1. Curiosity is a skill, not just a trait**:
- Training coping potential (psychological safety, competence building, self-efficacy) directly cultivates curiosity
- This explains the link between psychological safety and intellectual risk-taking

**2. The same curriculum creates curiosity in confident students and anxiety in anxious ones**:
- Both groups face the same knowledge gaps; different appraisal → different response
- Educational design must build coping potential BEFORE introducing challenging gaps

**3. Depression and anhedonia**:
- Chronic low coping potential may chronically route all uncertainty to anxiety pathway
- The vmPFC uncertainty-curiosity pathway may become blocked when coping potential is consistently evaluated as insufficient

**4. Connection to "Beginner's Mind" (Buddhist shoshin)**:
- Beginner's mind is fundamentally an act of raising coping potential: "whatever I find is fine; I have no expectation to defend"
- By releasing the need to be "right," coping potential becomes infinite → pure curiosity toward any discovery
