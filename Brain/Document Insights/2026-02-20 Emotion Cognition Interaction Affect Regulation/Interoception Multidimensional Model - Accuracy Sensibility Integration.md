## Core Framework

Interoception - the sensing of internal bodily signals (heartbeat, gut state, respiratory patterns, visceral tension) - is the fundamental bridge between body and emotion, and must be understood as a multidimensional construct with three distinct components that do not necessarily correlate with each other.

---

## The Three Dimensions

| Dimension | Definition | Measurement |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Interoceptive Accuracy** | Objective ability to detect internal signals | Heartbeat detection tasks (behavioral) |
| **Interoceptive Sensibility** | Subjective beliefs about one's internal awareness | Self-report questionnaires |
| **Interoceptive Integration** | How accurately internal signals are incorporated into decision-making | Behavioral economic tasks |

**Critical distinction**: These three dimensions can dissociate from each other - someone can have high accuracy but poor integration, or high sensibility (believing they're aware) with low accuracy. This explains why "listening to your body" doesn't always lead to better decisions.

---

## Neural Architecture

The interoceptive network:

- **Anterior insular cortex**: Primary cortical representation of interoceptive signals; activity correlates with both emotional intensity AND interoceptive accuracy
- **Ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC)**: Integrates interoceptive representations into value-based decisions (directly validates Damasio's somatic marker hypothesis)
- **Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)**: Processes conflicts between interoceptive signals and contextual expectations
- **Amygdala**: Modulates interoceptive signal salience based on threat/reward context

---

## Counter-Intuitive Finding

**Increased interoceptive accuracy does not always improve emotional regulation.**

In hypervigilant anxiety states, more accurate body awareness can AMPLIFY distress rather than regulate it. The context and interpretation of interoceptive signals matters as much as accuracy itself.

This means:
- Body scanning practices are not universally beneficial
- Anxious individuals may need to reduce interoceptive focus, not increase it
- Interoceptive training must account for precision weighting, not just accuracy

---

## Clinical Profiles

Different clinical conditions show distinct interoceptive signatures:

| Condition | Interoceptive Profile |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Anxiety disorders | Heightened interoceptive sensibility; over-precise weighting |
| Depression | Blunted interoceptive accuracy; prediction inertia |
| PTSD | Disrupted accuracy; dissociation from body signals |
| Autism | Reduced interoceptive awareness; may explain alexithymia |
| Emotionally unstable personality | Hyperactive integration causing dysregulation |

These distinct profiles suggest interoceptive training is a targetable therapeutic intervention - but one that must be personalized to the clinical profile.
