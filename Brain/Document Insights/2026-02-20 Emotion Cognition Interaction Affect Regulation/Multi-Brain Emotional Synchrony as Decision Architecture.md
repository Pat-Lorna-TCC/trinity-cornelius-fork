## Core Framework

Emotions are not only intra-individual states - they are dynamically synchronized patterns across interacting brains. When people meet, their neural systems begin to co-evolve, with emotional states in one brain triggering resonant states in adjacent brains through a feedback loop. Group emotional tone is not a "soft" organizational factor - it directly modulates the neural states of all participants, with measurable downstream consequences for individual and collective judgment quality.

## Key Mechanisms

**Neural Resonance**:
- Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) representations of emotional states can be triggered by observing others experiencing similar emotions
- This is not metaphorical - ACC shows measurable co-activation across observer-observed pairs
- The system is literally designed for emotional resonance

**Emotional Contagion Feedback Loops**:
- Emotional states spread through behavioral cues (facial expression, prosody, body language)
- These cues trigger corresponding neural activations in observers
- Observer's activated state then generates additional behavioral cues
- Creating amplifying OR attenuating loops depending on group context

**Co-Evolving Neural Dynamics**:
- Interacting brains operate as an integrated system
- Coordination happens through both explicit (verbal) and implicit (emotional) channels
- Emotional synchrony precedes and predicts behavioral synchrony

## Implications for Decision-Making in Groups

**Meeting-room emotional climate** directly modulates:
- Individual attention and information processing (through neural state changes)
- Confirmation bias strength (threat states amplify; positive states reduce)
- Risk tolerance (fear states shift toward loss aversion)
- Creative exploration vs. threat-focused thinking

**Leader emotional expression has amplified effects**:
- Leaders' emotional states propagate with greater fidelity through the group
- This is likely due to power hierarchy effects on attention allocation
- A leader who models threat responses generates a room of threat-appraising individuals

**What "soft" organizational culture actually is**:
- Synchronized neural states across team members
- A shared attentional and regulatory environment
- Emotionally "safe" cultures are neurologically distinct from emotionally "unsafe" ones - not just psychologically
