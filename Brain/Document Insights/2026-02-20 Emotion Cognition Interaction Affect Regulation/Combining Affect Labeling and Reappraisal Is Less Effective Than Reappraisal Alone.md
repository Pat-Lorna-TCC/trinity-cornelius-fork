# Combining Affect Labeling and Reappraisal Is Less Effective Than Reappraisal Alone

**Source**: Yoshimura, S., Shimomura, K., & Onoda, K. (2024). "Diminished negative emotion regulation through affect labeling and reappraisal: insights from functional near infrared spectroscopy on lateral prefrontal cortex activation." *BMC Psychology*. Published October 31, 2024. https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40359-024-02103-y
**Document Type**: Empirical study (fNIRS neuroimaging)
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-20

## Core Insight

A counter-intuitive finding: combining affect labeling ("name it to tame it") with cognitive reappraisal simultaneously produces WORSE emotional regulation outcomes than reappraisal alone. The neural explanation is that the two tasks compete for prefrontal resources, creating cognitive interference that undermines both strategies. Emotion regulation skill sequencing matters - these are distinct steps, not a combined technique.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**:
- **Sample**: 35 adults (mean age 28.2 years)
- **Protocol**: 12 vignettes inducing fear, anger, and sadness
- **Neuroimaging**: First fNIRS study to directly compare affect labeling (AL), cognitive reappraisal (CR), and AL+CR combined
- **Measurement**: Subjective negative emotion ratings + lateral prefrontal cortex activation

**Key Results**:
- **AL+CR combined was LESS effective** at reducing subjective negative emotion than CR alone
- Difference: Δ = 3.15 (p < 0.01); Hedge's g = 1.71 (large effect size)
- The combined condition showed increased bilateral dorsolateral AND right ventral PFC activation
- Interpretation: Doing both tasks simultaneously creates cognitive resource competition

**Neural Explanation**:
- Affect labeling engages right ventrolateral PFC (rvlPFC) → medial PFC → amygdala inhibition pathway
- Cognitive reappraisal engages dlPFC, vlPFC, dmPFC for meaning reframing
- When combined: both systems activate simultaneously, exhausting the shared prefrontal resource pool
- The competition for resources UNDERMINES both strategies

---

## Background: The Individual Strategies

**Affect labeling alone** (Lieberman et al., 2007, foundational):
- Simply labeling an emotional stimulus ("fear," "angry") activates rvlPFC
- rvlPFC → medial PFC → amygdala inhibition
- "Naming it tames it" - but only when done alone

**Cognitive reappraisal alone** (established literature):
- Changing the meaning/appraisal of a situation
- More effective for long-term outcomes than labeling
- Requires full prefrontal engagement

---

## Practical Implication

**When teaching emotion regulation skills, sequencing matters**:
1. First: label the emotion with precision ("I'm feeling anxious about rejection")
2. Pause
3. Then: reappraise the meaning ("this is my threat system activating, which means I care about this")

Do NOT ask people to label AND reappraise simultaneously. These are distinct sequential steps, not a combined technique.

**Therapeutic implication**: Treatment protocols that package "name it to tame it" with immediate reappraisal may be inadvertently reducing treatment efficacy. The strategies should be practiced separately and sequenced with deliberate spacing.
