## Core Insight

Humans are systematically poor predictors of their future emotional states, and these biases are not random - they follow predictable patterns that directly maintain anxiety disorders and depression. Negative forecast bias (overestimating how bad future experiences will feel) functions as a maintaining mechanism for avoidance behavior, creating a self-perpetuating cycle that standard cognitive models have underemphasized.

---

## Evidence

**Scoping Review (ScienceDirect, 2024)**:
- First comprehensive examination of affective forecasting in psychopathology contexts
- Social anxiety: Negatively biased forecasts serve as a maintaining mechanism for avoidance behaviors
- Depression: Negative forecast bias predicts treatment dropout and behavioral activation failure

**fMRI Study (Brain Imaging and Behavior, 2025)**:
- Neural correlates of affective forecasting in high trait-anxiety individuals via resting-state fMRI
- Specific connectivity patterns in vmPFC and amygdala predict the magnitude of forecasting bias
- Links to interoceptive-somatic marker circuitry

---

## The Three Core Biases

**1. Impact Bias**:
- We overestimate both the INTENSITY and DURATION of future emotional responses
- Applies to both positive (how happy will I be if...) and negative (how bad will it feel if...) events
- Recent methodological challenge: some overestimation may be measurement artifactual; true impact bias may be smaller than classic literature suggests

**2. Focalism**:
- We focus narrowly on the target event, ignoring how other life events will continue to occupy attention and moderate emotional impact
- When imagining a job loss, we focus on the loss itself - not on how Netflix, friends, and projects will continue to absorb attention
- East Asians show LESS impact bias than Euro-Canadians: attributed to holistic thinking that naturally considers multiple life events (reducing focalism)

**3. Immune Neglect**:
- We fail to anticipate our own psychological immune system's ability to reframe negative outcomes
- Our "psychological immune system" (Damasio/Gilbert) automatically generates positive reframes, rationalizations, and meaning-making
- We predict how we'd feel without the immune system, but the immune system always activates
