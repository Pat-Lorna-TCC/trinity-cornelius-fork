# Anxiety-as-Excitement Reappraisal - Meta-Analytic Evidence

**Sources**:

## Core Insight

Pre-performance anxiety, rather than being suppressed, can be strategically redirected to improve outcomes by reappraising it as excitement. This works because anxiety and excitement share the same high physiological arousal profile - they are "arousal-congruent." Reappraising anxiety as excitement requires only a shift in valence (negative to positive), not a shift in arousal level (high to low), making it cognitively cheap and powerfully effective. The meta-analytic evidence across 5,638 participants confirms this as one of the most accessible performance interventions available.

---

## Evidence

**Meta-Analysis (PMC, 2024)**:
- **Sample**: N=5,638 across 35 RCTs
- **Main effect**: d = 0.23 (p < 0.001) - significant improvement in task performance
- **Mixed interventions (SAR + additional content)**: d = 0.45
- **Public performance tasks**: d = 0.34 (stronger than cognitive tasks d = 0.20)
- **After publication bias correction**: d = 0.14 (remains significant)

**Foundational study (Brooks, 2014)**:
- Participants told to say "I am excited" before karaoke, math test, or public speech
- Outperformed those told to "calm down"
- The mechanism: arousal-congruent reappraisal is cognitively cheaper than down-regulation

**Esports RCT (2024)**:
- Single-session arousal reappraisal in competitive esports
- Shifted appraisals from threat to challenge
- Reduced cognitive and somatic anxiety
- Increased quiet eye duration (attentional focus marker)
- Improved shooting accuracy and task completion times

**Neuro-priming combination (2025)**:
- Combining arousal reappraisal with anodal tDCS to prefrontal cortex
- Showed additive effects on esports performance
- Suggests reappraisal efficacy can be enhanced by concurrent neural priming

---

## Key Boundary Condition

**Arousal reappraisal works best when arousal is already high** (pre-performance anxiety context).

It does NOT work as well for:
- Low-arousal states
- Intense, sustained chronic stress
- Post-event processing

**It is a state-transition tool, not a general stress management strategy.** The physiological arousal itself becomes the resource; only the cognitive label changes.

---

## Practical Application

The simplest version: Before a high-stakes performance, say (aloud if possible): "I am excited."

This reframes the physiological state from threat (arousal = danger) to challenge (arousal = readiness), mobilizing the same arousal as a resource rather than fighting it as an obstacle.

Contrast with common advice to "calm down" - this requires an actual arousal reduction, which is physiologically slower and cognitively demanding. Reappraisal uses the existing state more efficiently.
