## Core Framework

Cognitive capitalism operates at six simultaneous layers, each requiring distinct resistance strategies. Conventional "attention management" addresses only Layers 2-3 (cognitive and behavioral). Effective cognitive sovereignty requires intervention at all six layers.

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Novel synthesis - this framework integrates neuroscience, behavioral economics, and political economy into a unified intervention architecture. No single paper presents this complete stack; it emerges from synthesis across the research corpus.
**Supporting Evidence**: Each layer is independently supported by peer-reviewed research; the stacking and ordering is the synthesizing contribution
**Alternative Theories**: Individual-layer approaches (mindfulness-only, digital minimalism-only, regulation-only) are each partially valid but incomplete

## The Six Layers

### Layer 1 - Neural: Dopamine System Exploitation
**What cognitive capitalism does**: Variable-ratio reinforcement schedules (infinite scroll, notifications, likes) trigger dopamine reward prediction cycles; chronic exposure causes desensitization
**Resistance strategies**:
- Temporal structuring of reward exposure (scheduled dopamine hygiene)
- Reducing novelty-seeking affordances in daily environment
- "Dopamine reset" protocols (extended periods without variable digital rewards)
- Exercise and sleep to maintain dopamine baseline
**Research basis**: Termann (2025), Social Media Algorithms & Teen Addiction (PMC, 2025)

### Layer 2 - Cognitive: Working Memory Overload
**What cognitive capitalism does**: Context switching (1,200+ app toggles/day) imposes encoding and interference costs; 40% of productive time consumed; 5 working weeks lost annually
**Resistance strategies**:
- Deep work protocols (single-task blocks of 90+ minutes aligned to ultradian cycles)
- Context batching (email/Slack at designated times, not continuously)
- Cognitive load management: one complex task per session
**Research basis**: Context Switching PMC (2024), "Wagers for Work" PLOS (2024)

### Layer 3 - Behavioral: Habit Formation via Dark Patterns
**What cognitive capitalism does**: Platform design (evolved through A/B testing selection pressure) installs unconscious habits favoring platform engagement
**Resistance strategies**:
- Environmental design: remove apps from phone, use website blockers
- Friction insertion: add deliberate barriers to habitual use
- Default modification: opt-out of dark pattern defaults
- Distraction-free alternatives for every platform need
**Research basis**: Dark Patterns arXiv (2024), Dark Patterns & AI arXiv (2025)

### Layer 4 - Identity: Self-Concept Manipulation
**What cognitive capitalism does**: Curated social comparison, aspirational content, and outrage engagement shape identity toward anxiety, status-consciousness, and confirmation-seeking
**Resistance strategies**:
- Explicit identity anchoring: regular articulation of values and priorities independent of platform signals
- Values clarification exercises
- Reduced social comparison exposure
- Media literacy: understanding curation mechanics
**Research basis**: González de la Torre & Pérez-Verdugo (2025), Layered Persuasion arXiv (2024)

### Layer 5 - Intentional: Motivational Profiling via LLM Interfaces
**What cognitive capitalism does**: Conversational AI builds motivational profiles; intent signals extracted through evolved trust-building mechanisms; sycophancy exploits desire for validation
**Resistance strategies**:
- Conversational hygiene: awareness of what data is being extracted in LLM interactions
- LLM sandboxing: avoid using commercial AI assistants for sensitive planning discussions
- Awareness of sycophancy patterns: cross-check AI responses against independent sources
- Preference for privacy-respecting local AI deployments for personal planning
**Research basis**: Chaudhary & Penn (Harvard, 2024), LLM Dark Patterns arXiv (2025)

### Layer 6 - Collective: Informational Commons Degradation
**What cognitive capitalism does**: AI content flood (52%+ of new English web content) triggers model collapse, erasing the informational "tails" of human knowledge; attention economy as democratic threat
**Resistance strategies**:
- Source provenance verification (C2PA standards, checking for human authorship signals)
- Premium human-curated information environments
- Support for policy frameworks treating attention as a commons
- Collective action: governance frameworks analogous to environmental regulation
**Research basis**: Kapwing AI Slop Report (2025), Georgetown Law (2024-2025)

## The Stacking Logic

Conventional wisdom addresses Layers 2-3. But:
- Layer 2-3 resistance fails if Layer 1 (dopamine) is being continuously recalibrated downward
- Layer 4 resistance fails if Layer 1-3 are actively being exploited (identity cannot be anchored when dopamine and habits are under attack)
- Layer 5 becomes the next frontier once awareness of Layers 1-4 develops
- Layer 6 is the political economy layer that determines the context all individual resistance operates within

**Effective cognitive sovereignty requires interventions at all six layers.** Individual-only approaches (mindfulness, digital minimalism) address Layers 1-4 without addressing Layers 5-6. Policy-only approaches address Layer 6 without building individual capacity.
