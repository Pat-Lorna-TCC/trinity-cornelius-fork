## Core Framework

Digital platforms exploit three ancient neural systems that evolved for fundamentally different environments, creating an "evolutionary mismatch" - the brain's fitness algorithms running on mis-calibrated inputs:

1. **Novelty-seeking / threat detection** (evolved for predator detection): Notification pings activate the same vigilance circuits that evolved to detect movement in tall grass
2. **Social signal detection** (evolved for face-to-face status tracking): Likes, follows, comments are proxies for ancient social validation signals, activating circuits that evolved for small-group status management
3. **Variable reward sensitivity** (the most addictive reinforcement pattern): Platform reward schedules match the natural variability of ancestral foraging environments, triggering identical dopamine patterns

The combination of these three simultaneously-stimulated ancient systems creates an **exploitation gradient no prior technology has achieved**.

## Why Digital Work Creates Unique Mismatch

**What ancestral environments had** that digital environments lack:
- Natural cues signaling work transitions (colleagues leaving, sundown, physical location changes)
- Absence of 24/7 social signal monitoring demands
- Face-to-face social validation with embodied cues (microexpressions, tone, context)
- Natural attention rhythms synchronized with daylight/activity cycles

**What digital environments have** that ancestral environments lacked:
- Continuous notifications (artificial predator-detection triggers at ~1,200/day for heavy users)
- Infinite social signal streams (artificial status-tracking demands without social closure)
- Engineered variable reward schedules (artificial foraging optimization)
- Work/social/entertainment blended in single device (elimination of environmental transition cues)

**Result**: The brain's evolved fitness algorithms are running constantly in a context they weren't calibrated for. Higher IQ and executive functioning provide partial but incomplete protection.

## The Three-System Exploitation Gradient

Research on short-form video (TikTok, Instagram Reels) provides the clearest data:

- 20% decrease in sustained task focus for users averaging >2 hours daily of short-form video (Nature Neuroscience, 2023, cited in 2025 review)
- High immersion + temporal disorientation + "flow" states that intensify dopamine loops
- The combination of novelty-seeking + social signals + variable rewards in a single infinite-scroll product achieves maximum exploitation of all three ancient systems simultaneously
