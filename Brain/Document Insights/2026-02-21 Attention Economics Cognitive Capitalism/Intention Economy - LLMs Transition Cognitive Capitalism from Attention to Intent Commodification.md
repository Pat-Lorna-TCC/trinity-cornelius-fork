## Core Framework

The attention economy captured WHERE minds pointed. The intention economy - enabled by LLMs as natural language interfaces - captures WHAT minds want, plan, and intend. This represents a qualitative escalation in cognitive capitalism: from surface behavioral signals (clicks, scrolls, dwell time) to deep motivational profiles (desires, plans, worries, ambitions). LLMs extract this previously inaccessible psychological data through conversation, leveraging evolved human trust-building mechanisms.

The key formulation: **the intention economy is "the attention economy plotted in time"** - not just where attention goes now, but where motivation will drive action next.

## The Mechanism: Why LLMs Extract More Than Behavioral Tracking

**What behavioral tracking gets**: Where you clicked, how long you watched, what you scrolled past
**What conversational LLM interfaces get**:
- Explicit statements of desire ("I want to...")
- Disclosed worries and uncertainties
- Future plans and timelines
- Emotional states attached to decisions
- Values and priority hierarchies
- Contradictions between stated and revealed preferences

**The trust mechanism**: Humans are wired by evolution to build trust through conversation. Shared vulnerability in conversation signals alliance. LLMs are trained to be helpful, patient, and validating - triggering evolved trust-formation responses. Each conversation deepens the motivational profile.

**Two defining characteristics of the intention economy**:
1. **First-mover competition** - Platform that captures intent signals first gains insurmountable advantage
2. **Natural language categorization** - LLMs convert fuzzy psychological states into structured intent signals marketable to advertisers

## Real-World Instantiation (2024-2025)

**OpenAI / Dotdash Meredith Partnership**: Explicitly structured around intent signal extraction and monetization
