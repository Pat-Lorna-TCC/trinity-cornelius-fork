## Synthesis

Buddhist meditation practices - specifically mindfulness (sati) and focused attention (samatha) - are the most empirically validated countermeasure to cognitive capitalism's core mechanism. The mechanism is neurological, not mystical:

**Cognitive capitalism's attack vector**: Platform design exploits **bottom-up attention** (sensory salience, novelty, social signals) to override **top-down attention** (goal-directed focus). This is not depletion of a resource - it is hijacking of a competition.

**Buddhist training's defense**: Meditation systematically strengthens the **prefrontal cortex circuits** that regulate top-down attentional competition. Specifically: sustained attention practice (breath focus) trains the capacity to detect when bottom-up capture has occurred and voluntarily return to the chosen object. This is literal neural training for resisting platform hijacking.

**The ancient insight**: Buddhist teachers 2,500 years ago recognized that undirected, hijacked attention (the "monkey mind") is the root of suffering (dukkha) - identical to the mechanism of the attention economy from a phenomenological perspective.

## Rationale

**Why Buddhist Attention Training Is Uniquely Suited**:

1. **Mechanism match**: Meditation trains precisely the neural architecture that cognitive capitalism attacks (top-down PFC regulation)
2. **Empirical support**: Mindfulness research shows measurable PFC thickening, improved sustained attention, and reduced bottom-up reactivity with regular practice
3. **Training for noticing, not suppressing**: Buddhist practice develops meta-awareness of when attention has been captured - "noticing the hijack" without reactive engagement is exactly the skill needed
4. **Non-reactivity to variable rewards**: Advanced practice cultivates equanimity toward variable reward stimuli - directly countering the dopamine exploitation architecture
5. **Present-moment anchoring**: Attention anchored in direct experience (breath, body) is less susceptible to narrative-based manipulation than attention operating in conceptual space

**Confidence Level**: High - meditation neuroscience is well-established; the "countermeasure to cognitive capitalism" framing is the synthesis
