## Core Framework

Herbert Simon's foundational 1971 claim: "A wealth of information creates a poverty of attention." This framing treats attention scarcity as an inevitable consequence of information abundance - a natural economic equilibrium.

**The 2025 paradigm shift**: Attention scarcity is "not the cause, but the lived effect of the attention economy." Scarcity is engineered, not natural.

This is a radical reframe. The question shifts from "how do we cope with scarcity?" to "who benefits from engineering the experience of scarcity - and how do they do it?"

## Why the Distinction Matters

**If scarcity is natural** (Simon's view):
- The problem is individual coping and self-management
- Solution: personal productivity techniques, distraction resistance
- Attention is a private resource management challenge
- Frame: "Why can't I focus?"

**If scarcity is manufactured** (González de la Torre & Pérez-Verdugo):
- The problem is political economy and environmental design
- Solution: collective governance, ecological redesign, structural regulation
- Attention is a commons being enclosed by private interests
- Frame: "What is deliberately designed to prevent my focus?"

---

## The Manufacturing Mechanism

How attention scarcity is produced:

1. **Hebbian learning exploitation**: Platforms use repetition to fix habitual patterns of neural activation toward platform use. Repeated engagement literally rewires neural pathways toward platform-seeking
2. **Intermittent reinforcement modulation**: Variable rewards (likes, comments, notification surprises) stabilize specific neural interaction patterns - the most addictive conditioning architecture known
3. **Habit disaggregation**: AI and data analytics "disaggregate habits away from the individual, into an AI-managed collection of behavioural patterns" - effectively externalizing and commodifying the self
4. **Affordance competition manipulation**: Platform design makes digital affordances dominate the competitive landscape of bottom-up attention, not by depleting attention but by winning the competition

---

## The Implications: Attention as Commons

If scarcity is manufactured, the appropriate response is **ecological** not individualistic. González de la Torre and Pérez-Verdugo call for an "ecology of attention" - treating attention as a common resource requiring collective protection, governance frameworks, and design regulation.

This parallels:
- Environmental commons theory (clean air as public good requiring collective protection)
- Information commons (Wikipedia, open-source as protected alternatives to enclosed commons)
- The concept of "cognitive commons" requiring regulation analogous to environmental regulation
