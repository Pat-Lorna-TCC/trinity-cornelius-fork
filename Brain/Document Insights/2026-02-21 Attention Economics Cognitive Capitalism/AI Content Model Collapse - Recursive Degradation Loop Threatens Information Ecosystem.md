## Core Insight

The attention economy has triggered a self-destructive recursive loop: AI-generated content (optimized for engagement, not accuracy) now constitutes 52%+ of new English web content. Future AI models are trained on this AI-generated content. Each recursive generation loses the "tails" of human knowledge distribution - the rare, specific, original details that make content valuable. The result is progressive homogenization and degradation of the informational commons.

Simultaneously, AI systems trained on engagement-optimized social media content show "lasting cognitive decline" analogous to human effects. **The attention economy's cognitive costs are not limited to biological neural systems.**

## Evidence

**Scale of AI Content Flood (December 2025)**:
- 52%+ of newly published English-language web articles are AI-generated
- 74.2% of newly created webpages contain AI-generated text (April 2025)
- AI "news" sites: grew from 49 to 1,271 between May 2023 and May 2025
- 21% of short-form video recommendations are AI-generated

**Model Collapse Mechanism (Academic Research)**:
- AI models trained on AI-generated data lose variability with each recursive generation
- Rare details disappear; outputs become repetitive
- Models lose the statistical "tails" of human-generated content distribution
- Engagement-optimized AI content trains models to internalize attention-capture patterns

**AI "Brain Rot" Findings (Texas A&M / UT Austin / Purdue, 2025)**:
- Continual exposure to short, viral social media posts induces "lasting cognitive decline" in LLMs
- Degradation in model reasoning quality persists through retraining

## The Gresham's Law of Content

The attention economy implements a version of Gresham's Law: bad money drives out good. Engagement-optimized content (designed for attention capture) drives out insight-rich content (designed for accuracy or depth), because:
1. Engagement-optimized content gets amplified by platform algorithms
2. Higher traffic = more training data signals
3. AI systems trained on this data generate more engagement-optimized content
4. This content floods the training data for next-generation models
5. Repeat

The authentic human engagement signals the attention economy depends on are being increasingly replaced by synthetic signals generated by AI systems trained on the same degraded environment.
