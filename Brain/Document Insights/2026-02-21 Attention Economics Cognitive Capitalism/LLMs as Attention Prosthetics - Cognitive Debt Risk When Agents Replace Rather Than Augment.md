## Core Insight

LLMs provide "relatively cheap synthetic competent attention" (Metzger, 2025) - partially solving the attention bottleneck in knowledge work. But the MIT study reveals a critical asymmetry: using LLMs for cognitively demanding tasks (specifically writing) produces the lowest brain engagement across 32 neural regions and the weakest learning outcomes. **Outsourcing effortful cognitive processes in the short term incurs long-term cognitive capacity costs - "cognitive debt."**

AI agents are simultaneously attention prosthetics (solving scarcity) and potential cognitive atrophy accelerators (through disuse of the capacities they replace).

---

## Evidence

**MIT Study (Preprint, 2025) - Study Design**:
- n=54 participants over 4 months
- Three conditions: brain-only, internet search, LLM-assisted
- EEG measurement across 32 brain regions

**Key Findings**:
- LLM users showed **lowest brain engagement** of all three conditions
- Brain-only condition: **widest neural network engagement** + strongest learning outcomes
- LLM use specifically reduces engagement in areas related to: idea generation, semantic integration, critical evaluation
- Neural pathways for these skills show signs of degradation under disuse
- **"Use it or lose it" applies to higher cognition**

**Metzger (2025) - "Synthetic Competent Attention"**:
- In complex decision-making contexts, the limiting factor is no longer information access but cognitive bandwidth to process, synthesize, and act
- LLMs partially solve this bottleneck - they are "cheap synthetic competent attention"
- Risks: naturalizing technical/political boundaries, hallucination, algorithmic bias in value-laden decisions
- The prosthetic metaphor: useful when the limb is damaged, potentially atrophying when the limb is healthy

**Study Caveats**: MIT study is a preprint (not yet peer-reviewed); study design details not fully vetted publicly. Treat cognitive debt finding as preliminary.

## The Augmentation vs. Replacement Distinction

**Augmentation pattern** (preserves cognitive capacity):
- LLM handles information retrieval while human performs synthesis
- LLM generates options while human evaluates and selects
- LLM manages routine tasks while human focuses on novel problems
- Neural pathways for critical thinking remain engaged

**Replacement pattern** (creates cognitive debt):
- LLM generates the essay / analysis / synthesis
- Human reviews and accepts
- Neural pathways for idea generation, semantic integration are bypassed
- Cognitive atrophy risk increases with each outsourced operation

The distinction maps onto the "extended mind" framework: tools that genuinely extend cognition (notebook, map, calculator) vs. tools that substitute cognition (auto-calculate disables arithmetic capacity with use).
