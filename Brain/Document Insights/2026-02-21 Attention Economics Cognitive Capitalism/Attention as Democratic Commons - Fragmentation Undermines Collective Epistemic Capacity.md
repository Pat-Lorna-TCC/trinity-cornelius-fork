## Core Framework

Individual-level cognitive harms aggregate into collective outcomes. The attention economy is not merely a personal health issue or consumer protection concern - it is a **democratic threat**. When millions of citizens simultaneously have their attention fragmented, captured, and directed by competing commercial interests, the shared epistemic environment required for democratic deliberation degrades.

**The core claim**: Collective cognitive autonomy is a public good. The attention economy is destroying it.

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging - attention economics increasingly framed as political economy issue rather than consumer protection issue
**Supporting Evidence**: Correlational evidence linking social media engagement with political polarization, declining institutional trust, and epistemic fragmentation
**Alternative Theories**: Platform accountability approaches that treat this as a consumer protection issue rather than a commons/democratic issue

---

## Why This Is a Commons Problem

**Attention as Individual Resource** (conventional framing): Each person manages their own limited attention. Harms are personal productivity losses.

**Attention as Democratic Commons** (Georgetown Law framing): Democratic deliberation requires:
1. A shared informational substrate (sufficient common epistemic ground to have meaningful disagreements)
2. Citizens capable of sustained, complex reasoning across issues
3. Attention allocation not fully captured by commercial interests with specific political valences

When the attention economy fragments citizens' cognitive capacity, these three requirements degrade simultaneously. The result is not just individual harm but **degraded collective intelligence**.

## The Scale Numbers

- Global digital advertising revenue: $567B (2022), projected $700B+ (2025)
- Social media: ~35% of total digital ad spend
- The commercial model explicitly depends on maximum attention extraction
- This is not incidental: it is the business model itself that constitutes the democratic threat

---

## The Ecology of Attention Response

González de la Torre and Pérez-Verdugo (2025) propose the most developed positive response: **attention as a commons requiring an ecology of governance**. This means:

- Treating attention as a shared resource requiring collective protection (like clean air, shared water)
- Governance frameworks for "attentional pollution" analogous to environmental regulation
- Information environment design standards analogous to building codes or product safety standards
- "Attentional commons" as a political and legal category

**The Parallel**: Environmental commons were treated as free inputs to economic production until they became visibly degraded. Cognitive commons are following the same trajectory.
