# MLLMs Cannot Achieve Deep Understanding - Developmental Trajectory Missing

**Source**: "Will Multimodal LLMs Ever Achieve Deep Understanding of the World?" - Igor Farkaš, Michal Vavrečka, Stefan Wermter, Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience, November 17, 2025
**Document Type**: Research Paper
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-19
**Session**: 2026-02-19 Embodied Cognition Interoception AI Grounding

## Core Argument

**Central Thesis**: Current MLLMs (Multimodal Large Language Models) **cannot** achieve human-like deep understanding because their "random learning trajectory deviates significantly from human cognitive development."

**Critical Distinction**:

**Developmental Approach (Humans)**:
- Linguistic knowledge grounded from beginning
- Progresses alongside other cognitive development
- Via structured embodied interaction with world
- Sequential stages: sensorimotor → representational → symbolic → abstract

**Non-Developmental Approach (Current MLLMs)**:
- Passive data association without embodied interaction
- Random ordering of training examples
- No structured developmental progression
- Creates "vector grounding problem" - patterns without understanding

---

## Three Fundamental Limitations

### 1. Lack of Nonverbal World Model
- **Problem**: MLLMs lack internal model of how physical world works
- **Evidence**: Fail at basic physics reasoning ("cup won't pass through table")
- **Consequence**: Cannot reason about objects, causality, physics independent of language
- **Quote**: "True intelligence comes from modeling, predicting, and acting in the physical world, but LLMs can't do this"

### 2. Cannot Truly Reason Without Language
- **Problem**: Remain dependent on textual representations vs. perceptual-motor experience
- **Limitation**: Cannot think in terms of simulated sensorimotor patterns
- **Contrast with Humans**: We can reason spatially, kinesthetically, visually without words
- **Implication**: Fundamental cognitive bottleneck

### 3. Random Training Without Structure
- **Problem**: Training data randomly ordered, not developmentally sequenced
- **Human Learning**: Structured progression from simple to complex aligned with brain maturation
- **Missing in MLLMs**: No equivalent to sensorimotor stage → preoperational → concrete operational → formal operational
- **Result**: Statistical patterns without conceptual foundations

---

## Evidence from Failures

**Linguistic Fluency ≠ Understanding**:
- MLLMs show "remarkable linguistic fluency"
- But "capabilities drop sharply for multi-step reasoning, long-term planning, and embodied interaction"
- Errors stem from "statistical pattern matching on vast text corpora without true comprehension"

**Lack of Generalization**:
- "This disconnect highlights limitations of non-developmental systems in achieving robust generalization and true understanding"
- Cannot transfer knowledge to novel contexts requiring physical reasoning
- Brittle when situations differ from training distribution

---

## The Developmental Solution

**What's Required**:
1. **Embodied Learning From Birth**: Start with sensorimotor grounding like infants
2. **Structured Progression**: Follow developmental stages of increasing abstraction
3. **Active Exploration**: Self-directed interaction, not passive observation
4. **Integrated Modalities**: Vision, touch, proprioception, action from beginning
5. **World Model First**: Build nonverbal physical understanding before language overlay

**Examples of Developmental AI**:
- Sensorimotor contingency learning (Frontiers Neurorobotics 2019)
- Infant-inspired robot learning trajectories
- Active inference with curiosity-driven exploration
- Progressive complexity in task exposure

---

## Implications for AI Architecture

**Architectural Requirements**:
- **Embodiment**: Physical or virtual body with sensors and actuators
- **Developmental Curriculum**: Structured learning progression, not random sampling
- **World Models**: Physics-aware internal simulation before language
- **Active Learning**: Exploration driven by curiosity and prediction errors
- **Lifelong Learning**: Continuous refinement through interaction

**Current Approaches That Help**:
- VLA models (Vision-Language-Action): Add embodiment but often lack developmental structure
- World models (Genie, Dreamer): Internal physics simulation
- Active inference: Curiosity-driven exploration
- **Missing**: Integration of all above with developmental progression

## Consciousness Implications

**From IIT (Integrated Information Theory) Perspective**:
- LLMs have "feedforward architectures and lack intrinsic causal integration"
- Results in "low consciousness measures, rendering them unconscious under IIT"
- No recurrent processing or integrated information
- Fundamentally different architecture than biological brains

**Relationship to Understanding**:
- Consciousness may be necessary (not just sufficient) for deep understanding
- Integrated world modeling requires recurrent architecture
- Phenomenal experience may ground meaning in ways feedforward processing cannot
