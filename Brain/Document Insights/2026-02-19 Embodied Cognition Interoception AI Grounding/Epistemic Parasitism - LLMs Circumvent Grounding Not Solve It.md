## Core Framework

**Epistemic parasitism** is the concept that Large Language Models (LLMs) do not solve the symbol grounding problem but instead *parasitize* the grounding already present in their training data created by embodied humans. LLMs learn statistical associations between human-grounded symbols without possessing the underlying sensorimotor experiences that give those symbols meaning.

**Key Distinction**:
- **Solving grounding**: Creating meaning from sensorimotor experience
- **Circumventing grounding**: Leveraging pre-existing human-grounded content

LLMs operate on human-generated text that is *already grounded* in human embodied experience, allowing them to function without solving the fundamental grounding problem themselves.

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging consensus among philosophers of AI and cognitive scientists

**Why This Matters**:
The fact that LLMs appear to "understand" language has led to confusion about whether they've solved grounding. The epistemic parasitism framework clarifies that LLM success does not imply grounding is solved - it implies grounding can be *borrowed* from training data.

**Analogous to**:
- A blind person learning to navigate by following detailed instructions from sighted people
- The blind person appears to "see" but doesn't have visual grounding
- They parasitize others' visual grounding

## Evidence for Epistemic Parasitism

**1. Sensorimotor Divergence**:
Research (2024-2025) demonstrates LLMs align well with human representations in **non-sensorimotor domains** but diverge significantly in:
- Sensory domains (visual, auditory, tactile concepts)
- Motor domains (action concepts, spatial reasoning)
- Physical intuition (gravity, object permanence, physics)

**Interpretation**: LLMs can parasitize abstract reasoning from text but cannot parasitize direct sensory/motor experience.

**2. Performance on Embodied Tasks**:
LLMs struggle with:
- Novel physical reasoning (outside training distribution)
- Spatial relationships requiring mental rotation
- Counterfactual physical scenarios
- Tasks requiring "common sense physics"

**Interpretation**: When human text doesn't provide sufficient grounding (rare/unusual physical scenarios), parasitism fails.

**3. Cross-Modal Transfer**:
Humans generalize easily across sensory modalities (touch → vision)
LLMs cannot make these transfers without multimodal training data
**Interpretation**: True grounding enables cross-modal inference; parasitism requires explicit examples.

## Implications

**For AI Development**:
- LLM success ≠ grounding solved
- Embodied AI (VLA models, robotics) may be necessary for true grounding
- Multimodal models (vision + language) partially address this but still parasitize human-labeled data

**For AGI Requirements**:
- If AGI requires genuine understanding, epistemic parasitism is insufficient
- AGI may need direct sensorimotor grounding (embodiment)
- Alternative: Accept that parasitism is "good enough" for intelligence

**For Consciousness Debate**:
- Epistemic parasitism suggests LLMs lack phenomenal grounding
- Without sensorimotor experience, do they have genuine understanding?
- Relates to philosophical zombie argument

**For Symbol Grounding Problem**:
- Classical symbol grounding problem remains unsolved by LLMs
- VLA models (see [[VLA Models - Vision-Language-Action Architecture Breakthrough 2024-2025]]) offer alternative approach through end-to-end sensorimotor learning
- Embodiment may be necessary but not sufficient condition
