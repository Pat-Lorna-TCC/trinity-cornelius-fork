# Symbol Grounding Problem - LLMs Circumvent Rather Than Solve

## Core Framework

**Central Thesis**: LLMs don't solve the symbol grounding problem - they **circumvent** it through **"epistemic parasitism"**. LLMs operate exclusively on human-grounded content that already has meaning from embodied human experience, causal interaction with the world, and socio-cultural practices. They lack "unmediated access to possible worlds" and instead exploit pre-grounded semantic content.

**Key Distinction**:
- **Humans**: Ground symbols through direct sensorimotor interaction with world
- **LLMs**: Parasitize human-grounded content without independent grounding mechanism

---

## Epistemic Parasitism Explained

**What It Means**:
- LLMs function by processing content already imbued with meaning by embodied agents
- No direct perceptual or causal connection to referents
- Semantic grounding is borrowed, not generated
- Works because training data contains human-grounded associations

**Why It Works (For Now)**:
- Massive scale of human-grounded text
- Statistical patterns reflect real-world semantic relationships
- Pragmatic success without genuine understanding
- "Good enough" for text-based tasks

**Critical Limitation**:
- Cannot ground novel concepts without human mediation
- Vulnerable to distributional shift (AI-generated content training AI)
- No independent verification mechanism for truth
- Fundamentally derivative intelligence

---

## Formal Framework (Category Theory)

**Key Contribution**: Uses category theory to formalize how LLMs vs. humans transform content into truth-evaluated propositions:

- **Humans**: Objects (experiences) → Morphisms (causal interactions) → Propositions (grounded)
- **LLMs**: Objects (text patterns) → Morphisms (statistical associations) → Outputs (ungrounded)

**Critical Difference**: Humans have functorial mappings to possible worlds; LLMs only have internal structural relationships without world-reference.

---

## Implications for AI Development

**Fundamental Constraint**:
- Scaling LLMs alone cannot solve grounding problem
- No amount of text-only training creates direct world access
- Multimodal training (vision, action) may help but still mediated through human-labeled data
- True grounding requires embodied interaction, not passive observation

**Sustainability Question**:
- As AI-generated content dominates web, what happens to grounding?
- Parasitism requires host (human-grounded content)
- Risk of "semantic collapse" as models train on model outputs
- May necessitate return to embodied AI architectures
