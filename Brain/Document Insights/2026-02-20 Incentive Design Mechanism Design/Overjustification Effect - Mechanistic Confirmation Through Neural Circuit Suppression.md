## Core Finding

The overjustification effect (extrinsic rewards destroying intrinsic motivation) is now mechanistically confirmed, not just statistically observed. The mechanism operates through two parallel pathways, with neurological grounding:

**Two Causal Pathways**:
1. **Impaired self-determination**: Locus of control shifts from internal to external - the person no longer feels they are the author of their behavior
2. **Impaired self-esteem**: The reward signals that one's intrinsic engagement wasn't valued in the first place

**Neural Mechanism**: Extrinsic rewards co-opt ventral striatum dopamine circuits, which then suppress the dorsal striatal circuits supporting intrinsic engagement (See: [[D1 vs D2 Dopamine Receptors - Overturning the Dopamine Equals Reward Simplification]])

## New 2024-2025 Nuances (More Important Than the Classic Finding)

**Counter-intuitive: Penalties can crowd IN motivation**
- Lab experiments find high monetary penalties actually increase intrinsic motivation in low-engagement individuals
- This reverses the simple "rewards good, penalties bad" framing

**Public revelation crowds OUT**
- Public disclosure of behavior crowds out intrinsic motivation, primarily in conditional cooperators
- Making private virtuous behavior public converts it to performed virtue (social pressure replaces genuine motivation)

**The removal effect is asymmetric**
- Taking away expected payment causes performance to drop BELOW the "never paid" baseline
- Not just back to baseline - but below it (Krupka et al., 2020, confirmed in replications)
- Once you introduce extrinsic payment and remove it, you're worse off than never having paid

**Perceived salience matters MORE than contingency structure** (Saini et al., 2025)
- How salient the incentive is perceived to be - not just whether it's contingent - determines the undermining effect
- Implication: even non-contingent rewards can crowd out intrinsic motivation if they're highly salient

**The classic daycare case** (Gneezy & Rustichini, 2000 - confirmed repeatedly across cultures):
- Daycare fines for late pickups increased late arrivals
- Mechanism: converted moral obligation (internal) into market transaction (external)
- Recent replications confirm this generalizes cross-culturally

## Design Implication

Task-contingent rewards (based on quantity/quality) produce the strongest crowding-out. Performance-contingent rewards produce the least - because they can serve as competence signals that reinforce rather than replace intrinsic motivation.

The safest extrinsic reward: unexpected, post-hoc, framed as recognition rather than contingent payment.
