# Progressive Abstraction: Exemplar to Prototype Shift During Learning

## Core Insight

Category learning exhibits **progressive abstraction**: learners begin with exemplar-based representations (storing specific instances) and gradually shift toward prototype-based representations (generalized category centers) as expertise develops. This is a **dynamic process**, not a static choice.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Computational modeling of 5/5 category learning task
**Methodology**: Compared prototype vs exemplar model fits across learning stages
**Key Finding**:
- **Early learning**: Exemplar representations dominate
- **Late learning**: Prototype representations dominate
- **Overall best fit**: Prototype model across full learning trajectory

**Interpretation**: Learning involves progressive compression from specific instances to abstract summaries

**Citation**: "Prototype or Exemplar Representations in the 5/5 Category Learning Task," *Journal of Intelligence* 14(6):470 (June 2024). MDPI, PMC11200643

---

## Mechanism: Why the Shift?

**Cognitive Efficiency Pressure**:
1. **Early learning**: Uncertainty high → store specific examples for safety
2. **Middle learning**: Patterns emerge → begin extracting commonalities
3. **Late learning**: Confidence high → compress to generalized prototypes
4. **Result**: Reduce memory load while preserving discrimination ability

**Parallel to Compression**: Lossy compression once signal is well-understood
