# Embodied Categorization Requires Sensorimotor Grounding

## Core Framework

Concepts and categories are **heavily body-dependent**, grounded in sensorimotor systems rather than abstract symbolic representations. Categorization is both grounded in AND capable of reactivating bodily experience. Concepts contain facts about the sensorimotor particularities of their possessors.

**Key Claim**: You cannot fully understand "chair" without simulating sitting. You cannot categorize "running" without motor system activation.

---

## Evidence

**Field Acceptance**: Widely accepted in cognitive science (2024)

**Supporting Evidence**:
- Psychological studies: Concept use correlates with sensorimotor activity
- Neurological studies: Category recognition reactivates sensorimotor regions
- **Bidirectional relationship**:
  - **Forward**: Sensorimotor experience → concept formation
  - **Backward**: Concept activation → sensorimotor reactivation

**Citation**: "Embodied Cognition," *Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy* (Spring 2024). plato.stanford.edu/archives/spr2024/entries/embodied-cognition/

## Mechanism

**How Categories Ground in Body**:

1. **Perception**: See/hear/feel exemplars → sensorimotor patterns encoded
2. **Abstraction**: Extract commonalities across sensorimotor experiences
3. **Category formation**: Cluster of sensorimotor features = concept
4. **Retrieval**: Activating concept → partial reactivation of original sensorimotor patterns

**Example - "Cup" category**:
- Visual: Shape, size, texture patterns
- Motor: Grasping action, lifting motion
- Tactile: Surface feel, weight
- Functional: Drinking action simulation
- **Category = Pattern across all modalities**
