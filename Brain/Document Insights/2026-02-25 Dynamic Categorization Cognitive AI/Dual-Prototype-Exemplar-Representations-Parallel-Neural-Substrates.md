# Dual Prototype-Exemplar Representations in Parallel Neural Substrates

## Core Insight

The brain doesn't choose between prototype or exemplar categorization strategies - it maintains **both simultaneously in different neural substrates**. Neuroimaging evidence shows parallel formation of:

- **Prototypes**: Ventromedial prefrontal cortex + anterior hippocampus
- **Exemplars**: Inferior frontal gyrus + lateral parietal cortex

Representations form at multiple levels of specificity to promote flexibility. This resolves the longstanding prototype-vs-exemplar debate: the answer is "both, in parallel."

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Neuroimaging study tracking category learning
**Key Results**:
- Distinct brain regions encode prototype (generalized) vs exemplar (specific instance) representations
- Both representation types develop simultaneously during learning
- Multiple levels of specificity maintained in parallel

**Replication**: Highly cited 2024-2025, foundational finding
**Citation**: Davis et al., "Tracking prototype and exemplar representations in the brain across learning," *eLife* (2020). PMC7746231

## Mechanism

**Why Parallel Systems?**
Maximum cognitive flexibility - the brain can:
- Use prototypes for fast generalization (low cognitive load)
- Use exemplars for handling edge cases and high-variance categories
- Dynamically select strategy based on task demands

**Adaptive Selection**:
- Simple, well-separated categories → prototype-based
- Complex, fuzzy boundaries → exemplar-based
- System switches fluidly between strategies
