# NAD+ Precursors (NR/NMN) Show Stronger Evidence for Metabolic Aging Than Cognitive Benefits

**Source 1**: Wu et al. (January 2025). "Cognitive and Alzheimer's disease biomarker effects of oral nicotinamide riboside (NR) supplementation in older adults with subjective cognitive decline and MCI." *Alzheimer's & Dementia*. https://alz-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/trc2.70023

## Core Insight

NAD+ precursors (nicotinamide riboside [NR] and nicotinamide mononucleotide [NMN]) present a nuanced evidence picture in 2024-2025: **stronger evidence for metabolic and muscle aging effects** (gait speed improvement SMD 0.34m/s, muscle function, insulin resistance reduction) but **modest and not yet robustly established** cognitive benefits in humans. The key limitation is that healthy, fit individuals with adequate NAD+ levels may not benefit from supplementation - and brain-specific NAD+ effects are harder to demonstrate than peripheral effects.

---

## Evidence

**NR for MCI/AD Biomarkers (Alzheimer's & Dementia, January 2025)**:
- NR well-tolerated in adults with MCI
- In AD mouse models: NR improves hippocampal synaptic plasticity, attenuates Aβ and p-tau pathologies
- Human early-phase studies: potential disease modification but larger trials needed
- Status: promising signal, not confirmed efficacy

**NR for Long-COVID Cognitive Symptoms (Lancet eClinicalMedicine 2025)**:
- Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT of NR 2000 mg/day
- Targets: mitochondrial dysfunction and neuroinflammation underlying long-COVID cognitive impairment
- Published evidence: evaluated NAD+ levels, cognitive symptoms, symptom clusters
- Status: results published but not yet widely replicated

**NMN for Older Adults (GeroScience, May 2024)**:
- 250 mg/day NMN, healthy adults aged 65-75, 12 weeks
- Results:
  - Significantly shorter 4-meter walking time
  - Higher blood NAD+ and metabolites
  - **Significantly improved sleep quality**
  - Gait speed improvement in meta-analysis: SMD 0.34 m/s (412 participants across 9 studies)

**NMN vs. placebo meta-analysis (August 2024, 9 studies, 412 participants)**:
- Significant effects: muscle mass and function
- Better effect: liver enzyme ALT (liver health marker)
- Enhanced: muscle function, reduced insulin resistance
- **NOT shown**: significant cognitive function improvement in healthy individuals

---

## The Key Limitation: Who Benefits?

NAD+ supplementation appears most beneficial when:
1. **Baseline NAD+ is depleted**: Elderly individuals (NAD+ declines 50% between 40-60 years), metabolically compromised individuals, long-COVID patients
2. **Target tissue is accessible**: Muscle and liver show robust effects; brain penetration and brain-specific NAD+ elevation are harder to achieve and measure
3. **Accompanying metabolic dysfunction**: Insulin resistance, mitochondrial dysfunction - conditions where NAD+ restoration is genuinely therapeutic

For **healthy, fit individuals with adequate NAD+ levels**, the supplementation may do little beyond providing the raw metabolite - the bottleneck may be downstream enzymatic capacity, not NAD+ availability.

---

## The Sleep Quality Finding Is Underappreciated

The significant sleep quality improvement in the NMN trial is noteworthy:
- NAD+ is a cofactor for SIRT1, which regulates circadian clock genes (BMAL1, CLOCK, PER)
- NAD+ supplementation may improve sleep by supporting the circadian clock gene expression that requires NAD+-dependent SIRT1 activity
- Given sleep's primary role in glymphatic clearance and memory consolidation, sleep quality improvement may be the indirect cognitive pathway
