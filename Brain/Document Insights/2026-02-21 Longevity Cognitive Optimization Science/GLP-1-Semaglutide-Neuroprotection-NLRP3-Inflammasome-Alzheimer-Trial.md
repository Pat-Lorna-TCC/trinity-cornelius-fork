## Core Insight

Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) - originally a diabetes/obesity drug - has emerged as one of the most compelling AD treatment candidates of the decade. Its neuroprotective effects operate through at least six parallel mechanisms including direct NLRP3 inflammasome suppression, amyloid burden reduction, and blood-brain barrier strengthening. The EVOKE/EVOKE+ Phase 3 trials enrolled ~1,800 early AD patients; results expected late 2025 will be one of the most watched moments in neurology.

**Key caveat**: Semaglutide showed no effect in 5XFAD and APP/PS1 mouse models, indicating model-specific variability - human trial results are essential.

---

## Evidence

**Cell Reports Medicine meta-analysis (2025)**:
- Synthesized 278 preclinical + 96 clinical studies on GLP-1 RAs in brain conditions
- Large propensity-matched analyses: semaglutide-treated T2D patients demonstrate significantly reduced cognitive deficit risk vs. other antidiabetic agents
- GLP-1 RAs significantly decrease dementia risk across multiple retrospective analyses

**EVOKE / EVOKE+ Trial Design (PMC 2025)**:
- Largest-ever clinical trials of a GLP-1 drug in AD
- ~1,800 people with early symptomatic AD enrolled
- Started 2021; results expected late 2025
- Primary endpoint: Clinical Dementia Rating - Sum of Boxes (CDR-SB) change at 104 weeks

**Semaglutide EU approval (2025)**: Received EU approval for stroke management - indicating confirmed cardiovascular/cerebrovascular benefit

---

## Six Neuroprotective Mechanisms

1. **NLRP3 inflammasome blockade**: Directly suppresses the central neuroinflammation hub (see NLRP3 note)
2. **Amyloid burden reduction**: Reduces Aβ plaque burden and tau tangles in preclinical models
3. **Cerebral glucose uptake improvement**: Addresses age-related metabolic decline (the Metabolic-Cognitive Bridge)
4. **Blood-brain barrier strengthening**: Reduces neuroinflammatory spillover and peripheral immune activation
5. **Cardiovascular risk reduction**: Reduces systemic factors that compound brain aging (atherosclerosis, hypertension)
6. **Neuroinflammation via direct GLP-1R activation**: GLP-1 receptors are expressed throughout memory and cognition brain regions

## The Drug Repurposing Pattern

Semaglutide exemplifies the 2024-2025 drug repurposing trend:
- Already FDA-approved (rapid deployment if AD trial succeeds)
- Affordable relative to novel biologics
- Well-characterized safety profile after millions of patient-years
- Multi-mechanism action (vs. single-target novel drugs)

If EVOKE succeeds, semaglutide would be the most rapidly accessible, affordable disease-modifying AD treatment in history.
