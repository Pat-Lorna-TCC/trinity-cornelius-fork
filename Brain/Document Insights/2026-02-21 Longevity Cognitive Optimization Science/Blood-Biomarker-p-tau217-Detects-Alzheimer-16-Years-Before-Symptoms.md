## Core Insight

On May 16, 2025, the FDA approved the first blood-based in vitro diagnostic device for Alzheimer's disease - the Lumipulse G pTau217/beta-Amyloid 1-42 Plasma Ratio. This single blood test can detect Alzheimer's pathology up to **16 years before symptom onset**, replacing expensive and invasive spinal taps. It marks the beginning of a era where Alzheimer's can be monitored and potentially intercepted before any memory symptoms appear.

## Evidence

**FDA Approval**: May 16, 2025 - first clinical AD blood diagnostic
**Nature Medicine Cohort**: 2,148 subjects followed for 16 years; p-tau217 predicts dementia onset up to 16 years in advance
**Pan et al. 2025 (Alzheimer's & Dementia)**:
- p-tau217 distinguishes AD from frontotemporal dementia (bvFTD) with **96% accuracy**
- Distinguishes AD from psychiatric disorders with **93% accuracy**
- Predicts hippocampal atrophy and global cognitive decline in amyloid-positive individuals

**GFAP (glial fibrillary acidic protein)** adds complementary value:
- Drives amyloid-induced tau spreading across Braak stages
- Predicts language, visuospatial, and executive function decline
- Independent of age, phenotype, and APOE status

**NfL (neurofilament light chain)**: broader neurodegeneration marker, particularly useful for distinguishing neurodegenerative from non-neurodegenerative conditions
