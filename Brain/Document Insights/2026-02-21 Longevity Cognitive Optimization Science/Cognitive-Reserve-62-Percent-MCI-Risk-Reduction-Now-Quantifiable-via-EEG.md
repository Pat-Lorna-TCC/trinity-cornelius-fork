## Core Insight

Cognitive reserve has transitioned from a vague descriptive concept to a **quantifiable, operationalizable, and actively buildable construct**. High cognitive reserve is associated with a 62.6% reduction in MCI/AD risk even in the presence of elevated amyloid burden - meaning reserve protects against Alzheimer's pathology that is already present. EEG neural markers now provide objective measurement (lower theta/delta power = higher efficiency = higher reserve). The greatest protection comes from combining multiple modalities - their effects are multiplicative, not additive.

---

## Evidence

**Risk reduction magnitude (Alzheimer's & Dementia 2024)**:
- High cognitive reserve: **62.6% reduction in risk of MCI/AD** vs. high amyloid risk alone
- This protective effect holds even in individuals at high genetic risk (APOE4 carriers with matched amyloid burden)
- Cognitive reserve can therefore partially compensate for genetic predisposition and existing pathology

**EEG neural correlates (Frontiers in Aging Neuroscience 2025)**:
- High CR individuals show consistently **lower spectral power in theta and delta frequency bands**
  - This is a marker of more efficient neural processing
- High CR associated with: increased dorsal attention network activity
- High CR associated with: decreased ventral attention network activity
- The ratio = **more efficient attentional allocation** - better signal-to-noise in attention circuitry

**MTT24.5 Cognitive Stimulation RCT (PLOS ONE 2025)**:
- 76 healthy middle-aged adults
- Intervention: program targeting "direct stimulation of neuroplasticity and cognitive reserve through new, systematized, structured learning"
- Design: randomized controlled, open-label
- Programs designed to maximize neuroplasticity through **sustained novel challenge**

## What Builds Cognitive Reserve Most Effectively

Based on the 2024-2025 evidence base:
1. **Sustained novel challenge** - genuinely new, cognitively demanding skills (not just repetitive practice)
2. **Physical-cognitive dual tasks** - martial arts, dance, instrument (multiple neural pathway engagement)
3. **Social connections with intellectual engagement** - not just social contact but cognitively stimulating interaction
4. **Formal education and continued learning** - structural effects on white matter connectivity
5. **Bilingualism** - persistent executive function advantage (confirmed long-term)

**The MTT24.5 insight**: The program works through "systematized, structured learning" - not casual exposure but deliberate, progressive challenge. This mirrors the hormesis principle applied to cognition.
