# Senolytics (Dasatinib + Quercetin) Show First Human Evidence for Cognitive Improvement - STAMINA Pilot Trial

**Source 1**: "A pilot study of senolytics to improve cognition and mobility in older adults at risk for Alzheimer's disease (STAMINA)." *eBioMedicine* (Lancet) (February 2025). https://www.thelancet.com/journals/ebiom/article/PIIS2352-3964(25)00056-8/fulltext

## Core Insight

The STAMINA pilot trial provided the first direct human evidence that **senolytic therapy** - drugs that selectively eliminate senescent ("zombie") cells - can improve both cognitive and physical function in older adults with mild cognitive impairment and mobility issues. Crucially, the brain-penetrance of dasatinib was confirmed (found in CSF), and the intermittent dosing protocol (2 consecutive days every 2 weeks) was safe in this vulnerable population. Senescent cell burden in the brain is now a validated therapeutic target.

---

## Evidence

**STAMINA Pilot (eBioMedicine/Lancet, February 2025)**:
- **Population**: 12 older adults aged 65+ with:
  - Slow gait speed (marker of physical aging)
  - Mild cognitive impairment
- **Protocol**: Dasatinib 100 mg + Quercetin 1250 mg orally, 2 consecutive days every 2 weeks, 6 cycles over 12 weeks
- **Results**:
  - **No serious adverse events** related to the intervention
  - Improvements in both physical AND cognitive function domains
  - Safety, feasibility, and preliminary efficacy established

**Prior AD trial (5 patients, early Alzheimer's, 12 weeks)**:
- **Dasatinib confirmed to penetrate CSF** (direct brain access - critical for CNS senolysis)
- Favorable safety profile
- Increased CSF IL-6 and GFAP (acute inflammatory response to senescent cell clearance)
- Non-significant decreases in senescence-related cytokines/chemokines
- Non-significant increases in Abeta42 levels (potential amyloid redistribution signal)

**DQ Epigenetic Aging Study (Aging journal 2024)**:
- 19 participants received DQ for 6 months; DNA methylation assessed at 3 and 6 months
- Significant increases in epigenetic age acceleration in first-generation clocks at 3 and 6 months
- No significant differences in second/third generation clocks (more biologically validated clocks)

**DQF Triple Combo**: Subsequent study combined Dasatinib + Quercetin + Fisetin (DQF) in 19 participants for 6 months - ongoing analysis of synergistic effects

## Why Senescent Cells Matter for Brain Aging

**What senescent cells are**: Cells that have permanently exited the cell cycle (from DNA damage, oxidative stress, telomere shortening) but remain metabolically active and secrete a Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype (SASP):
- Pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, IL-8, TNF-alpha)
- Matrix metalloproteinases
- Growth factors

**How SASP drives cognitive aging**:
1. SASP → chronic low-grade neuroinflammation
2. SASP → NLRP3 inflammasome activation
3. SASP → impairs clearance of amyloid and tau
4. Senescent astrocytes and microglia lose their neuroprotective functions
5. Accumulation over decades → progressive cognitive decline

**The intermittent dosing innovation**: "Hit and run" protocol clears senescent cells without chronic immunosuppression (senolytics require only brief exposure to trigger apoptosis in senescent cells). This solves the safety challenge of continuous senolytic administration.
