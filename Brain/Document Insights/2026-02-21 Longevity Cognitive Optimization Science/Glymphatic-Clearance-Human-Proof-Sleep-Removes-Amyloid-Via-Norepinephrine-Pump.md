## Core Insight

For the first time, a 2025 human randomized crossover trial directly demonstrated that **the glymphatic system physically transports amyloid-beta and tau from brain tissue to blood plasma during sleep**. The mechanism is now fully mapped: infraslow norepinephrine oscillations during NREM sleep drive rhythmic arterial constrictions that pump cerebrospinal fluid through brain tissue, flushing toxic proteins into circulation where they can be cleared peripherally. Sleep deprivation reverses this - instead of clearance, biomarker *accumulation* dominates.

---

## Evidence

**Human Randomized Crossover Trial (Nature Communications 2025)**:
- N = 39 participants
- Normal sleep condition: morning plasma levels of AD biomarkers (Aβ and tau) significantly elevated vs. baseline - confirming successful overnight brain-to-plasma clearance
- Sleep deprivation condition: biomarker *release* (not clearance) dominates - proteins accumulate in brain rather than clearing
- Sensitivity analysis: confirmed glymphatic clearance is dominant influence during sleep vs. synaptic/metabolic release during wakefulness

**Mechanistic Discovery (Cell 2024)**:
- Infraslow NE oscillations during NREM sleep create slow, rhythmic arterial constrictions and dilations
- This vasomotion physically propels CSF through perivascular spaces - the "brain lymphatic pump"
- The norepinephrine oscillation pattern is the molecular clock driving brain waste clearance

**Clinical correlate (PMC 2025)**:
- Glymphatic activity (DTI-ALPS index) mediates the relationship between sleep quality and brain network coupling - but ONLY in good sleepers
- In poor sleepers, this glymphatic-brain relationship is completely disrupted, explaining their faster cognitive decline

**Quantified degradation statistics**:
- Sleep deprivation reduces glymphatic clearance rate approximately 30%
- Interstitial space expands up to 60% during slow-wave sleep, enhancing fluid flow
