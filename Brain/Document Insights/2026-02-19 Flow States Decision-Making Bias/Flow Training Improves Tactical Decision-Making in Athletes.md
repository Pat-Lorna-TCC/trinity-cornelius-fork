# Flow Training Improves Tactical Decision-Making in Athletes

**Source**: Martiny, L., Dias, G., Ferreira, J.P., Mendes, R., & Mendes, R. (2024). Flow training program: Mindfulness, decision making, and mental well-being of young and adult elite handball athletes. *Sports*, 12(6), 160. DOI: 10.3390/sports12060160

## Core Insight

**Mindfulness-based flow training significantly improves tactical decision-making in high-performance athletes.** A 9-week intervention with 105 elite handball athletes demonstrated medium-to-large effects (η²p = 0.086) on decision-making accuracy, with particularly strong effects for female athletes.

**Counterintuitive Finding**: Inverse relationship between decision-making quality and sense of control - **excessive control-seeking impaired performance**. This suggests "letting go" of deliberate control may be mechanistically necessary for optimal intuitive decision-making.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Quasi-experimental study
**Sample Size**: N=105 elite handball athletes (ages 13-40)
**Intervention**: 9-week mindfulness-based flow training program
**Control Group**: Yes

**Key Results**:
- **Decision-making accuracy**: Significant improvement in experimental vs. control group (η²p = 0.086, medium-to-large effect)
- **Dispositional flow state**: η²p = 0.103
- **Mental well-being**: η²p = 0.045
- **Non-judgment (mindfulness)**: η²p = 0.040
- **Refocusing**: η²p = 0.052

**Unexpected Finding**: Inverse control-performance relationship - athletes who tried to maintain explicit control showed worse decision-making

**Replication**: N/A (study from 2024)

**Citation**: Martiny et al. (2024), *Sports*, DOI: 10.3390/sports12060160
