## Core Finding

**First direct demonstration of dopamine-serotonin interaction** in nucleus accumbens (emotion/motivation/reward hub):

| Neurotransmitter | Function | Behavioral Effect |
|------------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Dopamine** | "Go" signal | Encourages reward-seeking when outcomes exceed predictions |
| **Serotonin** | "Stop/wait" signal | Puts brakes on impulsive behavior, promotes patience and long-term thinking |
| **Interaction Site** | Both converge in nucleus accumbens | Opposition creates behavioral balance |

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Direct measurement of dopamine-serotonin interaction in nucleus accumbens

**Key Results**:
- Dopamine promotes approach when reward prediction error is positive
- Serotonin inhibits impulsive action, enables waiting for better outcomes
- Both systems converge in same neural hub (nucleus accumbens)

**Additional Finding**: Policy complexity suppresses dopamine responses - **simpler behavioral strategies elicit stronger dopamine signals**, consistent with reinforcement learning models of policy compression.
