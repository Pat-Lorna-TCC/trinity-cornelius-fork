## Core Insight

**Buddhist meditation practices alter self-referential processing through distinct neural pathways, providing empirical validation for 2,500-year-old contemplative insights.** Different meditation types target different neural mechanisms:

- **Vipassana (insight meditation)**: Reduces gamma activity in PFC, "undermines the narrative ego"
- **Samatha (focused attention)**: Increases frontal midline theta, correlates with attentional stability
- **Metta (loving-kindness)**: Reduces self-other P300 amplitude differences, balances emotional processing

**Neural correlates of anattā (no-self)**: Buddhist practitioners show weakened VMPFC encoding of self-relatedness but enhanced DMPFC evaluative monitoring - they **detect self-referential thoughts but don't identify with them**.
