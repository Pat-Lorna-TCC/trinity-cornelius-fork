# Hostile Emotional Content - Not Ideological Sorting - Drives Polarization

**Source**: Jia et al. "Reranking partisan animosity in algorithmic social media feeds alters affective polarization." *Science*, 2025. https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adu5584
**Document Type**: Randomized controlled experiment with platform manipulation
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-20
**Session**: 2026-02-20 Network Science Belief Propagation

## Core Insight

A landmark 2025 *Science* paper delivers the strongest causal evidence yet on what actually drives affective polarization. The key finding: reducing **antidemocratic attitudes and partisan animosity (AAPA) content** in X (Twitter) feeds shifted out-party feelings by more than 2 points on a 100-point thermometer within 10 days. This effect is described as equivalent to what "normally would be seen over three years" of natural change - produced in a single week. Critically, this was achieved not by exposing users to more diverse ideological content, but simply by downranking emotionally hostile and antidemocratic content regardless of its ideological source.

This finding reframes the entire polarization debate: **the mechanism is emotional tone, not ideological exposure.**

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Platform-independent browser extension method (no corporate cooperation required)
- **Intervention**: Reranking algorithm that demoted AAPA content in X feeds
- **Sample**: Platform users in controlled experiment
- **Duration**: 10 days
- **Outcome measure**: Affective polarization (feeling thermometer toward out-party)

**Key Results**:
- 2+ point shift on 100-point out-party feeling thermometer in 10 days
- Effect equivalent to 3 years of natural affective polarization change
- Achieved by removing hostile content, NOT by adding cross-ideological content

**Methodological significance**: Browser extension approach enables platform-independent causal research without requiring corporate cooperation - likely to become a major methodological tool for the field

---

## What AAPA Content Includes

Antidemocratic attitudes and partisan animosity content includes:
- Content expressing antidemocratic attitudes (support for authoritarian measures against political opponents)
- Content expressing intense partisan animosity (dehumanizing outgroup rhetoric)
- Content promoting partisan conflict and division over cooperative frames

**Note**: This is distinct from ideologically extreme content. Left-wing extreme content and right-wing extreme content can both be low in AAPA; partisan hostility can come from moderates too.

---

## Counter-Intuitive Implication for Content Strategy

Standard polarization reduction strategies focus on:
- Exposing people to ideologically diverse content (filter bubble bursting)
- Showing people "the other side"
- Increasing cross-partisan contact

This research suggests the correct intervention is much simpler and more tractable:
**Reduce hostile emotional framing, regardless of ideological source.**

You do not need to burst someone's filter bubble to reduce their polarization. You need to reduce their exposure to emotionally charged, antidemocratic, partisan hostility content. The ideological composition of their feed matters far less than the emotional tone.
