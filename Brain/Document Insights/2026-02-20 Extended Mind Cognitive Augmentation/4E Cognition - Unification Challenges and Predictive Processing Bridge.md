## Core Framework

The **4E framework** (Embodied, Embedded, Extended, Enacted) unifies multiple approaches to cognition that reject pure computationalism. A 2025 review provides the most rigorous contemporary account of the field's coherence and tensions.

**Family Resemblance Unity** (Wittgenstein-inspired):
Rather than forcing a single definition, the four approaches are unified by overlapping dimensions:
- Rejection of computational/representational accounts of mind
- Phenomenological methods (Husserl, Merleau-Ponty)
- Embodiment as constitutive (not merely causal) for cognition
- Dynamical systems perspective
- Evolutionary perspective on mind
- Non-dualist commitments

---

## Internal Tensions Identified

**Embodied vs. Extended**:
- Embodied cognition places the body as central to cognition
- Extended cognition places the environment as cognitive (Clark-Chalmers)
- These conflict: if mind extends into environment, the body loses its privileged status

**Both vs. Enactivism**:
- Enactivism (Varela, Thompson, Maturana) grounds cognition in the body-environment distinction
- Both embodied and extended approaches blur the body/environment distinction in different ways

**The Predictive Processing Tension**:
- The Free Energy Principle (Friston) offers one possible unification through embodied cognitive neuroscience
- But FEP formalizes 4E claims within a **computational framework** - at the cost of abandoning anti-computationalist commitments
- The field must choose: empirical coherence (via FEP) or philosophical consistency (anti-computationalism)

## The Predictive Processing Bridge

The 2025 landscape: 4E cognition can be made empirically tractable through predictive processing/FEP, but this requires:
1. Accepting that "computation" is a broad enough term to include biological processes
2. Giving up strong anti-representationalism
3. Treating the boundary between organism and environment as dynamically constructed, not fixed

This connects directly to existing vault notes on predictive processing and interoception: the Anil Seth "controlled hallucination" framework is a predictive processing implementation of embodied cognition.
