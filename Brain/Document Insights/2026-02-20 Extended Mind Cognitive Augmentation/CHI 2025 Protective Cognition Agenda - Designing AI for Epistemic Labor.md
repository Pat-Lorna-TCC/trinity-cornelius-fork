## Core Insight

The CHI 2025 "Tools for Thought" workshop marks a **field-level inflection point**: the HCI community has formally shifted from "AI will augment us" to "how do we design AI so it augments rather than replaces human thinking?" This represents an institutional formalization of the protective cognition research agenda.

---

## The Epistemic Labor Argument (Self-Determination Theory)

**Key theoretical lens applied to AI**: Self-Determination Theory (Deci and Ryan)

**Epistemic labor** = the active work of acquiring, processing, evaluating knowledge

**SDT claims**: Ongoing epistemic labor is vital for:
- Intrinsic motivation (knowing is rewarding when self-generated)
- Psychological well-being (sense of competence and agency)
- Autonomy (beliefs and knowledge feel genuinely one's own)
- Competence development (skills develop through exercise)

**AI risk**: When AI substitutes for rather than supports epistemic labor, it threatens all four basic human needs simultaneously.

The strongest version of this argument: AI that replaces thinking doesn't just reduce skill - it reduces the psychological experience of being a competent autonomous agent. The hollowing is not merely cognitive; it is motivational and existential.

## Bloom's Taxonomy Applied to AI

Singh et al. apply Krathwohl's Revised Bloom's Taxonomy to examine how GenAI affects cognitive ability development:

**Bloom's hierarchy** (lower to higher):
Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze → Evaluate → Create

**AI threat pattern**: AI is most capable at lower-order tasks (remember, understand, apply) and increasingly useful for higher-order tasks (create). But humans need to develop through the lower orders to access higher orders.

**The illusion of comprehensive understanding**: Synthesized AI responses create the appearance of "understand" without genuine engagement at that level. Users feel they understand (and AI has convincingly explained) without having done the cognitive work that builds genuine understanding.

**Novices are most vulnerable**: Lacking domain schemas, they cannot tell the difference between AI-mediated comprehension and genuine understanding.

## Design Patterns Emerging from CHI 2025

**"From Answer Machines to Ignorant Co-Learners"**:
- AI that expresses genuine uncertainty rather than confident answers
- AI that asks questions rather than providing responses
- AI that invites the user to form their own view before providing the AI view
- AI designed to be useful without being satisfying (productive discomfort)

**"Distributed Cognition for AI-Supported Remote Operations"**:
- AI as member of distributed cognitive system, not oracle
- Human maintains meta-knowledge of AI capabilities
- Explicit division of cognitive labor based on comparative advantage

## Central Tension Formalized by the Workshop

AI simultaneously:
- Radically expands automation of cognitive work (risk of atrophy)
- Enables entirely new forms of thought impossible without AI (genuine augmentation)

Resolution is not "use AI" or "avoid AI" but **task-specific design**:
- Tasks where AI automation is purely positive (eliminates grunt work, no developmental value)
- Tasks where AI should support but not replace (skill-building domains)
- Tasks where AI creates new possibilities (genuinely novel cognitive territory)
