# Human-AI Synergy is Rare and Task-Dependent - Meta-Analysis of 106 Experiments

**Source**: Vaccaro, M., Almaatouq, A., & Malone, T. (December 2024). "When Combinations of Humans and AI Are Useful: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis." Nature Human Behaviour. DOI: 10.1038/s41562-024-02024-1. arXiv:2405.06087.
**Document Type**: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
**Sample**: 370 results from 106 experiments, published January 2020 - June 2023
**Institution**: MIT Sloan
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-20
**Session**: 2026-02-20 Extended Mind Cognitive Augmentation

## Core Finding

Human-AI combination on average performs **significantly worse** than the best of humans or AI alone (Hedges' g = -0.23; 95% CI: -0.39 to -0.07). True synergy - where human-AI pairs outperform both components individually - is **rare and task-specific**. It appears robustly only in creative content generation tasks.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Meta-analysis of controlled experiments comparing human-alone, AI-alone, and human-AI collaboration
**Sample**: 370 results from 106 experiments (Jan 2020 - Jun 2023)
**Primary Result**: Negative synergy overall (Hedges' g = -0.23)

**Task-Type Breakdown**:

| Task Type | Human-AI Outcome |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Decision-making | Performance losses in combinations |
| Creative content generation | Significant synergy gains - outperforms both alone |
| Analytical/reasoning | Mixed, generally no synergy |

**Key Moderator - Relative Expertise**:
- When humans outperformed AI alone: combinations showed gains
- When AI outperformed humans: combinations showed losses
- **Expertise is the master variable**

**Notable Null Result**:
- AI explanations did NOT significantly improve human-AI performance
- AI confidence levels did NOT significantly improve human-AI performance
- Neither factor that practitioners commonly use to "calibrate trust" actually works at scale

---

## Why Creative Tasks Produce Synergy

Creative tasks require complementary strengths:
- Human contribution: creativity, insight, contextual judgment, aesthetic sense
- AI contribution: repetitive execution, broad reference, variation generation, consistency

The dual nature creates genuine complementarity. Most tasks don't have this structure - they require one type of strength dominantly, not two complementary types.

---

## Implication for Extended Mind Theory

The meta-analysis empirically refines the extended mind thesis: cognitive extension through AI produces genuine enhancement *specifically* when:
1. The human partner contributes superior domain knowledge, OR
2. The task involves creative recombination of elements (dual complementarity)

Mere augmentation (human outperforms baseline) is common. Genuine synergy (human+AI outperforms both alone) is rare.

---

## Why This Is Counterintuitive

The popular narrative is that "human + AI > human or AI alone" is generally true. This meta-analysis shows the opposite is typical. The conditions for genuine synergy are narrow. Most practitioners are experiencing augmentation (they're better than baseline) while assuming they're experiencing synergy (best of both worlds).

This has profound implications for how organizations should be designing human-AI workflows.
