# The AI Efficiency-Capacity Trade-Off - A Cross-Domain Pattern

**STATUS: SPECULATIVE SYNTHESIS - Original pattern identified across multiple research findings. Supported by converging evidence but not tested as a unified principle.**

**Source**: Synthesized from multiple papers in the Extended Mind research report (2026-02-20) and existing vault notes.

## The Pattern

Across multiple domains, AI assistance produces a consistent asymmetric trade-off:

**Short-term**: Efficiency gain (output quality/quantity improves, effort reduces)
**Long-term**: Capacity cost (the underlying capability for independent performance deteriorates)

The efficiency gain is visible and immediate. The capacity cost is invisible and delayed. This makes the trade-off systematically underweighted by individuals, organizations, and policy.

---

## Evidence Across Domains

**Domain 1 - Written Reasoning**:
- Efficiency gain: LLM-assisted essays are longer, use more references (2-3x named entities)
- Capacity cost: 55% reduced brain connectivity, 83% recall failure, statistical homogeneity (less original thinking) - Kosmyna et al. 2025

**Domain 2 - Critical Thinking**:
- Efficiency gain: Users get answers to complex questions without effortful analysis
- Capacity cost: Significant negative correlation between AI tool usage and critical thinking (r = -0.68, N=666) - Gerlich 2025

**Domain 3 - Emotional Coping**:
- Efficiency gain: AI coping tools alleviate present emotional/cognitive burdens
- Capacity cost: Potentially undermine development of long-term resilience strategies - Frontiers Psychology 2025

**Domain 4 - Navigation (analogue)**:
- Efficiency gain: GPS provides optimal routes
- Capacity cost: Hippocampal spatial memory atrophies without navigation practice

**Domain 5 - Team Coordination**:
- Efficiency gain: AI handles information retrieval and synthesis for team members
- Capacity cost: Human team members lose meta-knowledge of their own and others' capabilities; transactive memory system degrades

---

## The Mechanism

The mechanism is consistent across domains: **capacity develops only through effortful exercise**. When AI provides the output of a cognitive process without the human performing that process, the neural or cognitive substrate that would be strengthened by performing it is not activated. Use it or lose it.

**Deep connection to dopamine-learning mechanism**:
Effortful cognitive work → reward signal (dopamine) → long-term potentiation → skill development
AI-provided output → no effort → no reward signal → no skill development

The AI efficiency-capacity trade-off is therefore rooted in the same mechanism as the dopamine-learning architecture: you only build what you actually do.

---

## When the Trade-Off Does Not Apply

The trade-off does NOT apply when:
1. The task has no developmental value (routine retrieval, arithmetic for non-specialists)
2. The human maintains independent practice in the domain
3. The human uses AI as scaffolding (support + challenge) rather than replacement
4. The task represents genuinely new cognitive territory AI opens up (new categories of synthesis impossible without AI)

---

## Testable Predictions

If this pattern is a genuine unified principle, we would expect:
1. Any domain where AI reduces cognitive effort would show capacity decline with sustained use
2. Intermittent AI use (preserving practice) would not produce capacity decline
3. The magnitude of capacity decline would correlate with the proportion of cognitive effort displaced
4. Expert users would show smaller capacity declines (they have more reserve and more off-AI practice)

---

## Significance

If confirmed as a general principle, the AI efficiency-capacity trade-off would require a fundamental reframing of AI adoption decisions:
- Current framing: "What efficiency gains does AI provide?"
- Required framing: "What efficiency gains does AI provide AND what capacity costs does it impose?"

Organizations, educators, and individuals are currently evaluating only the numerator of this ratio.
