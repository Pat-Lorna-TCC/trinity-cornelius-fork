## Core Finding

AI coping support tools (chatbots for emotional support, AI therapists, stress management AI) operate asymmetrically across time:
- **Short-term**: Effectively alleviates present cognitive and emotional burdens
- **Long-term**: May undermine the development of durable, internal resilience strategies

The efficiency of AI coping support comes at the potential cost of never developing robust internal coping architectures.

---

## The Cognitive Load Theory Application

**Cognitive Load Theory distinguishes three types of load**:
1. **Intrinsic load**: Inherent complexity of the material/challenge
2. **Extraneous load**: Unnecessary difficulty from poor design or format
3. **Germane load**: Effortful processing that builds schemas and adaptive capacity (the "good" load)

**Critical finding**:
- Reducing *extraneous* cognitive load is beneficial (e.g., therapy that clarifies thinking)
- Technology that reduces *germane* load (the effortful processing that builds coping schemas) negatively impacts long-term cognitive development

AI coping tools risk reducing germane load: when AI resolves emotional and cognitive problems efficiently, it removes the productive struggle through which resilience is built.

---

## Unlike Static Tools and Human Communities

A key distinction from prior forms of support:
- **Static tools** (journals, books): Passive, require the human to do all integrative work
- **Human communities**: Support through presence and co-regulation, but do not replace the person's processing
- **AI adaptive tools**: Actively reshape the cognitive and emotional environment in which coping occurs

AI is uniquely risky because it is *adaptive and personalized* - it fits so perfectly to the user's immediate needs that the user never has to develop their own fitting strategies.

---

## The Resilience-Efficiency Trade-Off

This is an instance of a broader pattern: short-term efficiency at the cost of long-term capability. The same trade-off appears in:
- Cognitive offloading: AI efficiency at cost of critical thinking development
- The Hollowed Mind: AI answers at cost of knowledge architecture
- GPS navigation: Route efficiency at cost of spatial memory

The pattern is consistent enough to suggest a general principle: **AI efficiency in any domain tends to trade short-term output for long-term capacity** when the domain involves skills that develop only through effortful practice.
