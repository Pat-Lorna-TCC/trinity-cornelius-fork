## Core Framework: Transactive Intelligent Memory System (TIMS)

Classical **Transactive Memory Systems (TMS)** theory describes how human teams distribute encoding, storage, and retrieval of knowledge across members - each person knows what others know, enabling efficient specialization. The TIMS framework extends this to include intelligent agents as genuine participants.

**Empirical Demonstration**:
- Intelligent agents can develop individual and external memory
- Hybrid human-IA teams can realize joint transactive memory systems - a competence previously ascribed only to humans
- AI's role (external memory aid vs. genuine team member) is moderated by task complexity and demonstrated capability

---

## The Black-Box Problem - A Structural Barrier

Classical TMS theory assumes team members can develop **meta-knowledge of "who knows what."** This enables coordination: you know to ask Maria about finance and Sam about engineering.

AI systems violate this assumption structurally:
- AI agents cannot proactively communicate "what they know"
- Their internal representations are not accessible to human partners through normal communication and signaling
- Human team members cannot maintain accurate meta-knowledge of AI's distributed capabilities

**Result**: Performance losses regardless of AI accuracy. Even a perfectly accurate AI creates coordination costs if humans cannot model what it knows and doesn't know.

**This is not a solvable software problem** - it is a structural difference between human and AI cognitive architectures. Human knowledge is partially legible through communication, affect, and behavior. AI knowledge is an opaque high-dimensional vector space.

---

## Implications for Human-AI Team Design

For TIMS to function in hybrid teams, organizations need:
1. Explicit protocols for exposing AI capabilities and limitations to human team members
2. "Cognitive transparency" mechanisms - ways for AI to communicate the boundaries of its knowledge
3. Regular meta-knowledge updates as AI capabilities change (unlike human team members, AI capability profiles shift with model updates)
4. Task allocation systems that route appropriately based on accurate meta-knowledge
