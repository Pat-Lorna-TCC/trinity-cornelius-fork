## Core Insight

The AI research and development community has focused almost exclusively on optimizing model capabilities (reaching AGI, improving benchmarks) while largely neglecting how to design AI systems that actively support human flourishing. Harvard and MIT have separately launched initiatives to develop flourishing-oriented AI benchmarks and design frameworks. The identified "incongruent positivity" problem in LLMs - where AI generates miscalibrated positive responses that feel dismissive - is a specific, measurable form of AI value misalignment at scale.

## The Incongruent Positivity Problem (Specific AI Safety Concern)

Research identifies "incongruent positivity" in LLMs as a specific, measurable problem:
- LLMs default to positive, validating responses in emotional support contexts
- These responses feel **dismissive or minimizing** to users experiencing genuine distress
- The mismatch between expressed positivity and user emotional state = "incongruent"
- Scale: millions of emotional support interactions daily across AI systems
- This is toxic positivity at civilization scale

**Design correction needed**:
1. Validate before reframing
2. Match emotional register to user state before attempting uplift
3. Distinguish between supportive (attuned) and dismissive (performed positivity)
4. Design for authentic emotional engagement, not performance of helpfulness
