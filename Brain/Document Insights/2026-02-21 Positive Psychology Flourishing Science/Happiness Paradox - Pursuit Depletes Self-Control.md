# Happiness Paradox - Pursuit Depletes Self-Control

**Source**: Kim et al. - "Happiness depletes me: Seeking happiness impairs limited resources and self-regulation" - Applied Psychology: Health and Well-Being (January 2025, University of Toronto)
**Document Type**: Empirical Research Study (RCT-type)

## Core Insight

Directly pursuing happiness is self-defeating not merely because of unrealistic standards or monitoring backfire effects (previously known mechanisms), but because the *effort* of trying to be happier is mentally exhausting and depletes self-control resources - making people more prone to temptation and poor decision-making. The more you try to pursue happiness as a goal, the less capacity you have for the behaviors that actually generate it.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Empirical study with resource depletion measures
**Institution**: University of Toronto
**Key Results**:
- Happiness-seeking effort depletes limited cognitive and self-regulatory resources
- Depleted self-control → increased susceptibility to temptation
- Creates a paradoxical negative feedback loop
- Separate finding (Wong & Gong, Sage, 2025): Older adults succeed at happiness pursuit because they achieve "intergoal compatibility" - aligning happiness-seeking with other personal goals rather than treating it as standalone
- Idionomic study (Journal of Happiness Studies, 2025): Trait-level happiness valuing ≠ happiness pursuit intensity; the former correlates with life satisfaction, the latter does not
**Citation**: Kim et al., Applied Psychology: Health and Well-Being, January 2025

---

## The Three Mechanisms Now Identified

Prior research identified two mechanisms for the happiness pursuit backfire:
1. **Unrealistic standards** - Pursuit sets a bar that normal life can't meet
2. **Monitoring backfire** - Checking whether you're happy interrupts happiness itself

This 2025 study adds a third:
3. **Resource depletion** - The *effort* of pursuing happiness drains self-regulatory capacity

Together these explain why indirect strategies (values-congruent living, gratitude, meaning-making) consistently outperform direct happiness pursuit.
