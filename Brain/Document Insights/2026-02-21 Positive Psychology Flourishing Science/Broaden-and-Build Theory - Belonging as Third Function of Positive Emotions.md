# Broaden-and-Build Theory - Belonging as Third Function of Positive Emotions

**Source**: Seehagen et al. - "Belong, Broaden, and Build: The Role of Positive Emotions in Early Human Development" - Child Development Perspectives (2025); Roth et al. - "Testing the validity of the broaden-and-build theory of positive emotions: a network analytic approach" - Frontiers in Psychology (2024)

## Core Insight

The Broaden-and-Build theory (Fredrickson, 1998) proposes that positive emotions broaden thought-action repertoires and build enduring resources. A 2025 extension proposes a third function: **belonging**. Positive emotions satisfy infants' fundamental need to belong, and this belonging function may chronologically *precede* and *enable* the broaden-and-build functions - suggesting that social connection is not a product of positive emotions but rather their foundational prerequisite.

---

## The Original Theory (1998)

**Broaden function**: Positive emotions expand the range of thoughts and actions that come to mind (vs. negative emotions which narrow focus)
**Build function**: Over time, this broadening builds enduring psychological resources (knowledge, social bonds, emotional resilience)

**Network analytic validation (Frontiers in Psychology, 2024)**:
- Used network analytic approaches to test BBT validity
- Confirmed that positive emotions initiate an upward movement by broadening thought-action repertoires
- Provided methodological innovation beyond traditional statistical approaches

---

## The 2025 Extension: Belonging as Third Function

**The argument (Child Development Perspectives, 2025)**:
- Positive emotions are "inextricably linked to social interactions in early development"
- Infants' first positive emotions occur in social contexts (caregiver-infant interactions)
- These positive emotions satisfy infants' fundamental need to belong
- Belonging may chronologically precede broadening and building in development
- Proposed sequence: Belong → Broaden → Build (not Broaden → Build → Belong)

**Why this matters**:
1. It repositions social connection from *outcome* to *prerequisite* of positive emotion benefits
2. It explains why isolated positive experiences have weaker effects than socially embedded ones
3. It integrates evolutionary biology (humans survived through group membership) with positive psychology
4. It bridges the individual positive psychology literature with the social connectedness literature

**Current status**: Proposed theoretical extension (2025) - sparse empirical support in infancy/early childhood; adult evidence stronger

---

## The Loneliness-Flourishing Link

If belonging is a foundational function of positive emotions, then loneliness (absence of belonging) doesn't merely cause suffering - it *prevents the positive emotion system from functioning properly*. This would explain:
- Why social connection interventions reduce depression (belonging enables positive emotion → upward spiral)
- Why loneliness interventions don't reduce loneliness directly (the cause is absence of belonging-generating positive emotions, not just absence of people)
- Why GFS data shows relational flourishing as separable from (and partly prerequisite for) other flourishing dimensions
