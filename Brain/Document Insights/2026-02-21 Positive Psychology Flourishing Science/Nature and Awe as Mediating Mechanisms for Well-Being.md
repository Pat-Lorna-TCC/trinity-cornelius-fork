# Nature and Awe as Mediating Mechanisms for Well-Being

**Source**: Zhou et al. - "Nature Exposure and Subjective Well-Being: The Mediating Roles of Connectedness to Nature and Awe" - Sustainability (2025, Wuhan University); VR/EEG/HRV study (2024); Scientific Reports EEG study (2024)

## Core Insight

Nature exposure does not improve well-being directly - it works through two specific mediating mechanisms: (1) connectedness to nature and (2) awe experiences. This mechanistic clarity matters because it explains both why nature works AND why virtual nature representations (VR, photos, videos) also generate well-being benefits - they can trigger the same mediating pathways. The first EEG-based evidence (vs. self-report) for nature's cognitive benefits has now been obtained.

---

## Evidence

**Mediation pathway confirmed (Wuhan University, Sustainability, 2025)**:
- Nature exposure → connectedness to nature + awe → subjective well-being
- Pathway applies to *both* direct nature contact AND virtual representations
- Both mediators are necessary components of the mechanism

**Extraordinary vs. ordinary nature (VR/EEG/HRV study, 2024)**:
- Extraordinary landscapes (Arctic, underwater, unique ecosystems) produce significantly greater emotional recovery than ordinary nature
- Five dimensions of extraordinariness identified: awe, remoteness, mystery, complexity, uniqueness
- Uniqueness is the primary influencing factor
- Excessive exposure shows diminishing returns (hedonic adaptation applies here too)

**EEG validation (Scientific Reports, 2024)** - critical finding:
- Nature walkers showed improved executive control (EEG-measured)
- Urban walkers showed no such improvement (controlling for exercise benefits)
- First EEG-based (not self-report) evidence for nature's specific cognitive benefits
- The mechanism is not just exercise - it is nature-specific

**Natural soundscapes (PLOS ONE, 2024)**:
- Birdsong specifically lowers blood pressure, heart rate, and respiratory rates
- Natural soundscapes reduce anxiety/stress and enhance mood recovery after stressors

**Loneliness reduction**:
- 2024 nationally representative longitudinal study: nature contact may *reduce* loneliness
- Connects nature and social connectedness research streams

## The Awe Mechanism

Awe appears to work through a specific psychological mechanism:
- **Self-diminishment**: Awe produces a "small self" experience (reduced self-referential thinking)
- **Expanded time perception**: Awe creates sense of having more time (reduces time pressure)
- **Prosocial motivation**: Awe increases desire for connection and cooperative behavior
- **Cognitive openness**: Awe temporarily disables rigid schemas (accommodation need)

This connects directly to the Buddhist small-self and no-self literature: awe may be a secular pathway to the same neurological state (reduced DMN activity, reduced self-referential processing) that meditation achieves through training.
