## Core Insight

The most comprehensive meta-analysis of well-being interventions to date (183 RCTs, Nature Human Behaviour, 2025) finds that yoga, mindfulness, compassion practice, exercise, and positive psychology all improve adult well-being vs. inactive controls. The largest effects come from **combined exercise + psychological approaches**. Effect sizes are moderate but clinically meaningful, and they hold for populations ranging from general adults to cardiovascular disease patients.

---

## Evidence

**Network meta-analysis (Nature Human Behaviour, 2025)**:
- 183 randomized controlled trials of well-being interventions
- All major approaches (yoga, mindfulness, compassion, exercise, PPIs) outperformed inactive controls

**Multi-component PPIs (50 studies)**:
- Subjective well-being: g = 0.34
- Psychological well-being: g = 0.39
- Depression reduction: g = 0.32

**PPIs broadly across all types**:
- Well-being: g = 0.39
- Strengths: g = 0.46
- Quality of life: g = 0.48
- Depression reduction: g = -0.39
- Anxiety reduction: g = -0.62
- Stress reduction: g = -0.58

**Key finding**: Combined exercise + psychological approaches showed the **largest effect sizes** of all tested combinations

**CVD patients (separate analysis)**:
- Mental well-being: β = 0.33 at post-intervention
- Distress reduction: β = 0.34
- Effects remain significant at follow-up

**Ecological momentary interventions (Applied Psychology: Health and Well-Being, 2025)**:
- Positive EMIs create positive emotion spirals consistent with BBT predictions
- Short-term affect gains are more robust than long-term well-being changes
- Long-term stability of EMI effects needs further research

## The Simplicity Principle Applied to Well-Being

The finding that effect sizes are comparable across intensive clinical interventions and brief digital self-administered PPIs is consistent with the knowledge base's emerging "simplicity principle" framework (from AI architecture research, now extending to well-being):

- Brief scales (single item) achieve 77-85% accuracy vs. comprehensive measures
- Taking the VIA survey alone = positive intervention
- Digital PPIs for youth show comparable effects to clinical interventions

**Implications**: The "dose" of intervention required for meaningful well-being improvement may be much smaller than assumed. Complex, expensive programs may not outperform well-designed simple ones.

## The Exercise Integration Finding

The **combined exercise + psychology** superiority deserves particular attention. Possible mechanisms:
1. **Neurobiological synergy**: Exercise generates BDNF, dopamine, serotonin - providing biological substrate for psychological change
2. **Self-efficacy**: Physical achievement creates confidence that generalizes to psychological work
3. **Embodiment**: Psychological well-being improvements may require body-level changes, not just cognitive shifts
4. **Interoceptive base**: Improved interoceptive awareness from exercise may enhance receptivity to psychological interventions

This connects to the embodied cognition research already in the knowledge base.
