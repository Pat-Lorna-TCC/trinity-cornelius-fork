## Core Insight

Ketamine achieves >80% meaningful improvement rate in PTSD patients in real-world settings - higher than any approved PTSD treatment. Its mechanism is distinct from all existing PTSD interventions: it induces a "controlled dissociation" that allows trauma processing without triggering full re-experiencing. Unlike SSRIs (which target serotonin, the wrong pathway for PTSD), ketamine targets glutamatergic dysregulation - a core PTSD pathway. The dissociative state allows patients to "look at the trauma without reexperiencing it," acting on fear extinction and memory reconsolidation pathways simultaneously.

---

## Evidence

**Real-World Outcomes (University of Florida 2025)**:
- Sample: 98 survey respondents with PTSD diagnoses
- **>80%** reported: better sleep, less anxiety, fewer trauma triggers, improved quality of life
- Baseline: PTSD patients started with more severe depression, anxiety, and sleep disruption than non-PTSD comparison groups
- Improvement rates: **equivalent** to non-PTSD group despite more severe starting point

**Meta-Analysis (Frontiers in Psychiatry 2024)**:
- Ketamine significantly reduces PTSD symptoms in veterans including treatment-resistant populations

**Standard Protocol**: 4-8 infusions over ~28 days (6 infusions most common), followed by maintenance

**Evidence Limitation**: Larger RCTs still needed; real-world studies face selection bias and lack control groups. Current evidence is promising but not yet Phase 3 RCT quality.

## The Mechanism - Why Ketamine Is Different

**The Dissociation Mechanism (key distinction)**:
Ketamine induces a dissociative state described by clinicians as: "Medical ketamine induces a controlled dissociation that separates the patient from trauma without being drawn into the fear. It allows the patient to look at the trauma without reexperiencing it."

This is mechanistically distinct from:
- **Exposure therapy**: Patient is in full contact with trauma content, experiencing activation
- **SSRIs**: Blunt emotional response generally, don't target trauma-specific circuits
- **MDMA**: Increases emotional openness and trust, but not dissociative

**The Glutamate Pathway**:
- PTSD involves glutamatergic dysregulation (not serotonergic - hence SSRIs are not optimal)
- Ketamine is an NMDA glutamate receptor antagonist
- This targets the actual dysregulated circuit in PTSD rather than a tangentially related one
- Also attenuates pro-inflammatory cytokines elevated by chronic stress (anti-inflammatory mechanism)

**Memory Reconsolidation Enhancement**:
- Ketamine enhances extinction recall
- Reduces fear renewal (the return of fear after successful extinction)
- These are reconsolidation-pathway effects - creating more durable fear reduction than simple extinction

## The Ketamine + Psychotherapy Synergy Opportunity

**Current evidence**:
- Ketamine + Written Exposure Therapy: "large and durable symptom reductions" for severe chronic PTSD
- Yale Medicine protocol: Intensive 7-day combined ketamine + prolonged exposure - compressing months of treatment into one week

**The mechanism for combination therapy**:
1. Ketamine opens a neuroplasticity window (BDNF elevation, glutamate modulation)
2. During this window, psychotherapy can modify fear memories through reconsolidation with lower emotional activation
3. The dissociative state reduces the retraumatization risk of trauma processing
4. Reconsolidated memories are restabilized during the post-infusion period

This is analogous to the psilocybin neuroplasticity window - but ketamine operates through dissociation rather than psychedelic expansion, making it more accessible for trauma work.

---

## The Regulatory Context

Unlike MDMA (which went through and failed FDA review), ketamine is already FDA-approved for anesthesia and has an FDA-approved nasal spray (esketamine/Spravato) for treatment-resistant depression. This creates a different regulatory pathway - ketamine for PTSD is used off-label through existing clinical infrastructure rather than requiring new drug approval.

**The risk**: Unregulated ketamine clinics are proliferating without proper protocols, raising misuse and access inequality concerns.
