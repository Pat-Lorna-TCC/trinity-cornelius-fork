## Core Framework

Complex PTSD (cPTSD) is now formally recognized in ICD-11 as distinct from PTSD, defined by PTSD's six symptom clusters PLUS three Disturbances of Self-Organisation (DSO): emotional dysregulation, interpersonal difficulties, and negative self-concept. cPTSD typically arises from prolonged, inescapable interpersonal trauma (repeated childhood abuse, domestic violence, captivity, war). Critically, 2025 research shows that treating cPTSD with STAIR Narrative Therapy not only reduces emotional symptoms but measurably improves **neurocognitive function** (immediate memory, global cognition) - suggesting cPTSD treatment can reverse cognitive damage, not just emotional distress.

---

## Evidence

**ICD-11 Validation (2025)**:
- Meta-analytic confirmatory factor analysis supports the PTSD/cPTSD distinction
- Two-factor second-order model (PTSD + DSO) best fits clinical samples
- cPTSD is associated with: more severe functional impairment, greater comorbidity, lower quality of life than standard PTSD

**The DSO Triad** (Disturbances of Self-Organisation):
1. **Emotional dysregulation** - Persistent difficulty modulating emotional responses
2. **Interpersonal difficulties** - Sustained problems in relationships (trust, boundaries, attachment)
3. **Negative self-concept** - Persistent negative beliefs about self as damaged, worthless, permanently altered

**STAIR Narrative Therapy Cognitive Outcomes (2025)**:
- Skills Training in Affective and Interpersonal Regulation + trauma narrative processing
- Showed significant improvements in **immediate memory** at 3 months post-treatment
- Improved **global cognitive functioning** - not just symptom reduction
- Implication: cPTSD treatment can reverse neurocognitive dysfunction, adding it to the list of treatable cognitive symptoms

**Dissociative Subtype**:
- Dissociative PTSD/cPTSD shows **diminished response** to standard psychotherapy
- Requires phase-based treatment: address dissociation before trauma processing
- Standard trauma protocols retraumatize dissociative presentations

## Why DSO Changes the Treatment Model

Standard PTSD treatments (PE, CPT, EMDR) target the fear-memory network and intrusion/avoidance symptoms. DSO represents something structurally different:

**DSO is damage to the self-system** - not just fear circuit dysregulation, but:
- The **emotional regulation system** (limbic modulation by PFC)
- The **relational system** (attachment, trust, social engagement)
- The **identity system** (self-concept, self-worth, continuity of self)

This maps directly onto the vault's identity framework: cPTSD creates identity fragmentation and negative identity content simultaneously - making it doubly challenging because both the identity structure AND the content are damaged.

**For treatment**: You cannot do trauma narrative processing when the self doing the processing is fragmented and self-negating. Phase-based treatment (stabilization → disturbance reduction → trauma processing) is not just clinically conservative - it is mechanistically necessary.

---

## The Neurocognitive Finding

The discovery that STAIR treatment improves immediate memory and global cognition is underappreciated:

**Prior understanding**: Trauma impairs cognitive function (attention, memory, executive function)
**New finding**: Treating cPTSD with appropriate therapy **reverses these cognitive deficits**

This means cPTSD's neurocognitive impairment is not just a consequence to be lived with - it is a treatable component of the disorder. The brain's cognitive systems damaged by the same mechanisms (HPA hyperactivation, hippocampal compromise, PFC dysregulation) that create cPTSD symptoms can recover when the trauma is processed.

**Wider implication**: Cognitive rehabilitation for cPTSD may be achievable through trauma-specific therapy rather than separate cognitive training - addressing the root cause rather than the symptom.

## cPTSD and the Identity Framework

cPTSD is essentially the trauma version of identity fragmentation:

- PTSD: Fear-memory circuit dysregulation; identity broadly intact but overwhelmed by trauma
- cPTSD: DSO adds fragmented/negative identity as a core feature

From Identity is a set of Beliefs: Identity is the organizing narrative that gives coherent meaning to experience. Prolonged interpersonal trauma systematically attacks the belief-clusters that make up identity:
- "The world is safe" → destroyed by repeated violation
- "I am worthy of protection" → destroyed by being repeatedly unprotected
- "Relationships are trust-capable" → destroyed by repeated betrayal

The result is not just traumatic memories (PTSD) but a damaged identity infrastructure (cPTSD).
