## Core Framework

Post-traumatic growth (when genuine) requires a specific prerequisite: the trauma must have **shattered core beliefs** - fundamental assumptions about the world, self, and future. Mild trauma that does not challenge core beliefs produces no PTG. The relationship is paradoxical: more shattering = more PTG potential, but also more PTSD risk. Identity disruption is not an obstacle to PTG but its gateway - you cannot build a more complex, capacious self without the existing structure being disrupted. A "metamorphic model" of narrative identity describes how personal narratives transform across mental illness and recovery.

## Evidence

**Framework Acceptance**: Widely accepted in PTG research (Tedeschi & Calhoun's foundational model); the 2025 research adds the narrative identity mechanism.

**2025 Narrative Identity Framework**:
- "Metamorphic model" of how personal narratives transform across mental illness and recovery
- Narrative coherence (integration of self-knowledge) positively associated with both self-efficacy and mental health
- Narrative therapy's re-authoring process - creating alternative, values-aligned stories about traumatic experience - validated as genuine growth mechanism (not mere coping)
- The re-authoring process requires a disrupted narrative to begin with - an intact narrative resists re-authoring

**PTG Research (Tedeschi & Calhoun + 2025 systematic review)**:
- PTG requires that trauma challenges the person's assumptive world (core beliefs)
- Mild trauma that confirms or is assimilated into existing beliefs produces no PTG
- "Seismic" trauma (that ruptures core beliefs) creates the opening for PTG - but also for PTSD
- The same event that creates PTSD potential also creates PTG potential: they share a prerequisite

**The PTG-PTSD Paradox Explained** (from illusory PTG note):
- PTG is highest at moderate PTSD precisely because this is the seismic zone: enough core belief disruption to create PTG potential, not so much as to overwhelm processing capacity

## The Mechanism - Core Belief Shattering as Gateway

**Before trauma (typical)**:
- Assumptive world: "The world is largely safe/just/predictable"
- Core beliefs: "I am generally competent/loveable/protected"
- Narrative coherence: Life story integrates experiences into a stable identity

**Trauma disrupts**:
- Assumptive world: "Safety was an illusion"
- Core beliefs: "I am vulnerable/unlucky/permanently altered"
- Narrative coherence: Life story is fractured; pre-trauma self and post-trauma self cannot be integrated

**Without adequate processing (PTSD)**:
- Disrupted beliefs remain as intrusions, avoidance, hypervigilance
- Identity fracture maintained; new narrative cannot be built
- Core beliefs remain fragmented, cannot be updated or replaced

**With adequate processing (genuine PTG)**:
- Disrupted beliefs become available for reconstruction
- New assumptive world built that is more complex and realistic (acknowledges both safety and danger)
- Core beliefs rebuilt at higher capacity: not "I am protected" (naive) but "I can survive loss" (earned)
- Narrative integration: pre-trauma self, traumatized self, and post-trauma self form a coherent story

**Key insight**: PTG is not the restoration of pre-trauma beliefs but the **construction of qualitatively superior beliefs** that could not have been built without the trauma-forced disruption.
