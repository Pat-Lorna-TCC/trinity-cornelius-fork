## Core Insight

Interoception is the perception of signals from inside the body - the "hidden sixth sense" that monitors internal organs, blood chemistry, immune status, and overall physiological state. Unlike the five external senses that perceive the world, interoception perceives the body itself, forming the foundation for consciousness, emotion, and sense of self.

---

## Context & Evidence

**NIH Recognition (2025)**: $14.2 million initiative to map interoceptive pathways, calling it "the body's hidden sixth sense" - comparable in significance to the Human Genome Project.

**Neural Pathways**: Interoceptive signals travel via:
- Vagus nerve (75% of parasympathetic fibers)
- Lamina I spinal cord pathways
- Brainstem nuclei (parabrachial, nucleus tractus solitarius)
- To cortical hubs: posterior insula, anterior insula, anterior cingulate cortex

**Functional Role**: Interoception underlies:
- Emotional experience (how we feel)
- Sense of self (embodied awareness)
- Decision-making (gut feelings, somatic markers)
- Allostatic regulation (predictive body maintenance)
- Consciousness itself (subjective feeling states)

**7 Tesla fMRI Validation (2025)**: First comprehensive mapping of the allostatic-interoceptive system in humans at submillimeter resolution, validating 96% of connections documented in animal studies.
