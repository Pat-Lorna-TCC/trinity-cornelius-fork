## Core Insight

**HYPOTHESIS**: Dopamine may serve as a **precision modulator** for interoceptive signals, determining how much attention and weight the brain assigns to body states. High dopamine states increase precision weighting on reward-relevant interoceptive signals (hunger when food available, arousal when mate nearby), while low dopamine states decrease interoceptive precision (anhedonia as inability to feel body-based pleasure). This would explain the intersection of motivation (dopamine), body states (interoception), and attention (precision weighting).

## Context & Evidence

**What We Know**:

**From Dopamine Research**:
- Dopamine encodes reward prediction errors
- Drives motivation and wanting (not liking)
- Signals salience and attention-worthiness
- Modulates learning rates
- Low dopamine → anhedonia (inability to feel pleasure)

**From Interoception Research (2024-2025)**:
- Precision weighting determines influence of interoceptive signals
- Clinical disorders show dysregulated precision
- Attention to body modulated by precision weights
- Prediction errors drive interoceptive learning

**Gap in Current Research**: Minimal explicit exploration of dopamine-interoception interaction, despite theoretical overlap.
