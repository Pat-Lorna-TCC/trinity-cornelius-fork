# Analytic-Holistic Cognitive Style Binary Is Empirically Untenable

**Source**: Lacko, D., et al. "Investigating the geography of thought across 11 countries: Cross-cultural differences in analytic and holistic cognitive styles using simple perceptual tasks and reaction time modeling." *Journal of Experimental Psychology: General*, Vol. 154(2), pp. 325-346, February 2025. ## Core Insight

The foundational East-West cognitive style paradigm - which holds that Westerners think analytically (object-focused, rule-based, context-independent) while East Asians think holistically (context-sensitive, relational, field-dependent) - has failed its most rigorous cross-national empirical test. An 11-country study using multilevel modeling found that cultural dimensions like individualism and long-term orientation showed only *some* association with cognitive style, while "many cultural predictors had no significant impact." Crucially, exploratory latent profile analysis found no "rigidity metastyle" - there are no profiles showing strong preference for one cognitive dimension with low preference for the other.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Multi-country empirical study using simple perceptual tasks and reaction time modeling
**Sample**: 11 countries with multilevel modeling
**Method**: Tested Hofstede's and Schwartz's cultural dimensions as predictors of analytic vs. holistic cognitive style
**Key Results**:
- Individualism and long-term orientation: some association with cognitive styles
- Majority of cultural predictors: no significant impact
- No "rigidity metastyle" found (i.e., no profiles strongly preferring one cognitive style exclusively)
- Failures to replicate earlier results "have grabbed headlines" across classification, attention, change/causation, and reasoning domains

**Neural basis partially identified** (NeuroImage, 2024): Brain regions distinguishing holistic from analytic thinking have been mapped (bilateral frontal lobes, bilateral parietal lobes, bilateral fusiform, bilateral insula, angular gyrus) - connecting culture-related brain regions with language function. This confirms there are neural correlates, but they don't map cleanly onto a simple East-West binary.

---

## Why This Matters

The analytic-holistic framework has been cross-cultural psychology's most cited and applied model for decades. Its empirical destabilization has cascading implications:

1. **For AI training**: Models trained on "cultural" datasets that assume the East-West binary may encode a false construct
2. **For cross-cultural research**: Studies comparing "Western" vs. "East Asian" samples may have been comparing intra-cultural variance with cultural labels attached
3. **For organizational design**: Global team management frameworks built on cognitive style assumptions need re-examination
4. **For the WEIRD problem**: WEIRD bias corrections that have simply added East Asian samples (creating a WEIRD-Confucian binary) may not actually represent genuine cognitive diversity

The field is moving toward more granular, within-culture analysis and recognition that cognitive styles vary along multiple independent dimensions rather than a single axis.
