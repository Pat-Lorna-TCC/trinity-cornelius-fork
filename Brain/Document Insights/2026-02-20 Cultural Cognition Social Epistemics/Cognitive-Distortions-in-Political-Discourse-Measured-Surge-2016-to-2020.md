# Cognitive Distortions in Political Discourse Show Measurable Surge Between 2016 and 2020 Elections

**Source**: "Cognitive distortions are associated with increasing political polarization." *Communications Psychology* (Nature), 2025. https://www.nature.com/articles/s44271-025-00289-4

## Core Insight

Thought patterns that clinical psychology identifies as **cognitive distortions** - including black-and-white thinking (splitting), exaggeration (catastrophizing), and absolutism - are measurably increasing in political discourse. A 2025 *Communications Psychology* (Nature) study finds that these markers "bear a striking similarity" to those characterizing political polarization, and shows a "sharp increase" in these cognitive distortion markers between the 2016 and 2020 U.S. elections.

This is significant because it bridges clinical psychology (cognitive distortions as individual pathology) with political psychology (polarization as social phenomenon), suggesting the mechanisms are the same.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Longitudinal analysis of political discourse markers across 2016 and 2020 U.S. election periods
**Published In**: *Communications Psychology* (Nature publishing group), 2025
**Evidence Level**: Moderate - measurement of discourse markers; causal mechanisms not definitively established
**Key Results**:
- Cognitive distortion markers measurably increase in political discourse 2016 → 2020
- Pattern similarity between clinical cognitive distortions and political polarization markers:
  - **Black-and-white thinking (splitting)**: Zero-sum framing, no nuance, absolute positions
  - **Exaggeration (catastrophizing)**: Opponents' policies described in apocalyptic terms
  - **Absolutism**: "Always," "never," "everyone," "no one" - eliminating conditional reasoning

---

## The Clinical-Political Bridge

This finding is conceptually important because cognitive distortions in clinical psychology are understood as:
- Patterns that persist despite contradicting evidence
- Self-reinforcing (the distorted interpretation generates emotions that reinforce the distorted framework)
- Responsive to structured intervention (CBT)
- Identity-linked when severe (in personality disorders)

If political discourse is exhibiting the same patterns at scale, then:
1. Polarization may respond to CBT-derived interventions (already being tested as "cognitive bias modification")
2. Political polarization is partly a population-level mental health phenomenon, not purely a structural or informational problem
3. The surge between 2016 and 2020 suggests environmental triggers that activated existing cognitive distortion tendencies
4. Like individual cognitive distortions, political distortions may be resistant to evidence-based counter-argument while being responsive to relationship-based interventions
