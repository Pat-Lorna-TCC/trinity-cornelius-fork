## Core Insight

AI access produces a systematic **divergence between individual and collective outcomes**. While individuals with AI assistance produce more creative outputs, the population of AI-assisted outputs is more *similar to each other* than independently-produced outputs. Stories written with AI assistance converge on shared patterns despite each individual story being more sophisticated. This is the creativity equivalent of the biodiversity paradox: a technology that makes each individual "better" while reducing the ecosystem-level diversity that protects against collective failure.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Controlled empirical study with and without AI access conditions
**Published In**: *Science* - highest-tier peer review
**Key Results**:
- AI access increases individual creativity metrics
- But AI-assisted outputs are more similar to each other than independently-produced outputs
- Collective diversity of outputs *decreases* with AI assistance despite individual quality increasing
- Mechanism: AI systems trained on overlapping data drive users toward similar creative solutions

## The Homogenization Mechanism

AI systems are trained on a finite dataset representing the distribution of existing human creative work. When individuals use these systems for creative assistance:

1. The AI suggests patterns consistent with its training distribution
2. Users accept or are influenced by these suggestions
3. Independent creative exploration is partially replaced by AI-guided pattern-following
4. Population-level output shifts toward the center of AI's training distribution
5. Creative outliers - which provide diversity and occasionally breakthrough insights - are suppressed

This is essentially **regression to the mean at the population level** driven by a shared statistical oracle.
