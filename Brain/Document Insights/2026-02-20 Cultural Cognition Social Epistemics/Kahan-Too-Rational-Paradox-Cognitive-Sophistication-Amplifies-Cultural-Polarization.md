# Kahan's Too-Rational Paradox - Higher Cognitive Sophistication Amplifies Cultural Polarization

## Core Framework

The conventional wisdom holds that cognitive sophistication reduces bias - smarter, more educated people should be better at updating beliefs based on evidence. Kahan's Politically Motivated Reasoning Paradigm (PMRP) overturns this. The most cognitively sophisticated people are the *most* culturally polarized on contested identity-linked issues. The reason: higher cognitive sophistication provides more powerful tools for **identity-protective cognition** - the selective processing of evidence to protect cultural identity commitments.

This is rationality in service of social belonging, not epistemic truth.

---

## The Mechanism

Identity-protective cognition (IPC) operates in full System 2 mode. It is not a failure of deliberate reasoning but deliberate reasoning deployed for a social goal:

1. **Identify which conclusion aligns with your cultural group**
2. **Selectively search** for evidence supporting that conclusion
3. **Evaluate evidence quality** using culturally-biased credibility heuristics
4. **Generate sophisticated rationalizations** for rejecting contrary evidence
5. **The more cognitively capable you are, the better you execute all five steps**

The result: IQ, education, and numerical literacy increase polarization on identity-linked empirical questions (climate risk, gun safety, nuclear waste) rather than resolving it.

---

## The Epistemic Defense (2025 Revision)

A 2025 paper in *Episteme* offers a surprising finding that complicates the standard critique of IPC: identity-protective reasoning may actually **support collective truth-seeking under certain conditions**:

- Under deliberative conditions (structured dialogue with representatives of competing identity groups), even like-minded partisan groups become *less* extreme
- IPC signals the social importance of an issue - issues that don't trigger IPC may not receive enough distributed epistemic attention
- Collective identity-linked scrutiny of evidence may be more thorough than individual dispassionate analysis

**The nuance**: IPC is epistemically harmful when groups are isolated (confirming their own conclusions) but may be epistemically useful when engaged in genuine adversarial collaboration.

This is a direct challenge to the standard "rationality good, motivated reasoning bad" framework.
