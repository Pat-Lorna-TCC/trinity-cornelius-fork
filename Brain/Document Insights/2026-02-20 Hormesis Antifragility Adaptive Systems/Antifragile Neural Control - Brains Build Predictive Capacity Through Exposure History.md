## Core Framework

Neural systems do not merely become *stronger* in response to environmental uncertainty - they become *smarter about anticipating it.* The Axenie 2024 computational framework demonstrates that neural antifragility operates by building predictive models of future environmental states through exposure history, enabling pre-emptive rather than reactive adaptation.

**Key Insight**: Neural systems "build the capacity to **anticipate changes** through their exposure history - they become predictively better, not just reactively stronger."

This extends the hormesis principle: biological hormesis produces reactive resilience (stronger response to stressors). Neural antifragility produces *predictive* resilience (better anticipation of future stressors before they arrive).

## Three Canonical Neural Circuits Analyzed

| Circuit | Function | Antifragility Mechanism |
|---------|----------|-------------------------|
| Homeostatic Activity Regulation | Maintain stable firing rates despite perturbation | Gains from perturbation history to set better homeostatic targets |
| Winner-Take-All | Competitive selection and decision-making | Exposure history improves discrimination threshold calibration |
| Hebbian Temporal Correlation Learning | Associative learning, pattern detection | Uncertainty exposure improves future correlation detection |

**Multi-timescale antifragility:**
- Single-neuron level: synaptic weight adjustment from perturbation exposure
- Within-network level: connectivity pattern optimization
- Between-network level: inter-circuit coordination improvement

## Implications for Understanding Wisdom

The framework provides a mechanistic model for how *wisdom* (not just knowledge or skill) accumulates through experience:

**Skill**: Reactive capability (faster, more accurate response to known challenges)
**Wisdom**: Predictive capability (better anticipation of *new* challenges based on pattern recognition from diverse prior exposures)

Neural antifragility is the computational substrate of wisdom: each novel challenge encountered enriches the predictive models that prepare the system for future novelty. The brain is not just learning specific responses; it is learning *how to adapt more efficiently* to whatever comes next.

This explains why diverse experience (not just practice in one domain) builds wisdom: heterogeneous challenge histories build richer predictive models than narrow expertise.
