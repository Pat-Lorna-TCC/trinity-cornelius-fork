## Core Framework

The Catabolic-Anabolic Cycling Hormesis (CACH) model proposes that cells and organ systems are evolutionarily designed for repeated oscillation between two complementary phases - and that it is the *cycling itself* (not merely the stressor or the rest) that generates adaptive gain. Neither continuous stress nor continuous rest produces optimal resilience; the transition between phases does.

**Catabolic phase**: Adaptive stress responses, resource conservation, autophagy activation, cellular repair
**Anabolic phase**: Growth, neuroplasticity, protein synthesis, regeneration

The key molecular cascade: catabolic phase activates **AMPK** and **SIRT1** → triggers **autophagy** → recycles amino acids → fuels **mTOR**-mediated protein synthesis in the anabolic phase → net adaptive gain beyond pre-stress baseline.

## The Paradigm Shift

**Old framing**: Stress is an obstacle to overcome. Recovery is the absence of stress. Goal is stability.

**CACH framing**: Stress IS the signal. Recovery is the productive phase where gains are consolidated. The rhythm of challenge-and-recovery is not incidental to health - **it IS health.** Chronic comfort (absence of catabolic phases) causes progressive fragility.

The refeeding phase following intermittent fasting may be as important as the fast itself - the anabolic rebound is where stem cells regenerate and protein synthesis rebuilds to above-baseline levels.

## Implications for Practice

- **Exercise**: The workout is the catabolic signal; sleep is where the anabolic phase consolidates the gains. Skipping sleep after training destroys the cycle.
- **Intermittent fasting**: The fasting window (catabolic) activates autophagy and cellular repair; the eating window (anabolic) drives regeneration and stem cell activation.
- **Mental performance**: Learning is a catabolic process (neural disruption); sleep consolidation is anabolic. Continuous studying without sleep disrupts the cycle.
- **Organizational design**: Deliberate disruption (catabolic) followed by consolidation periods (anabolic) builds institutional capacity.
