## Core Insight

The 2025 *Journal of Physiology* study is a milestone in longevity research: it provides the **first direct demonstration of intermittent fasting (IF)-induced autophagic flux in humans.** Previous evidence for IF-induced autophagy relied on animal models. The human confirmation closes a critical translational gap and establishes IF as a validated hormetic intervention operating through the cellular self-cleaning mechanism.

## The Refeeding Phase Is Equally Critical

Consistent with the CACH model's emphasis on cycling: **the refeeding phase following fasting may be as important as the fast itself.**

- Fasting (catabolic): activates autophagy (cellular cleaning), AMPK, SIRT1, epigenetic remodeling
- Refeeding (anabolic): activates stem/progenitor cell regeneration, mTOR-mediated protein synthesis, replaces what autophagy cleared

**Practical implication**: Continuous caloric restriction (no refeeding cycle) misses the regenerative anabolic phase. Intermittent fasting with proper refeeding exploits the full CACH cycle.

## Why Autophagy Matters for Aging

Autophagy is the cellular garbage-disposal system: it identifies and eliminates damaged proteins, dysfunctional organelles, and intracellular pathogens. Age-related decline in autophagic efficiency is a core driver of:
- Accumulation of damaged mitochondria (mitochondrial dysfunction in aging)
- Protein aggregation diseases (Alzheimer's tau and amyloid partly result from clearance failure)
- Chronic low-grade inflammation (inflammaging) from degradation products
- Reduced cellular function from crowded, inefficient cellular environment

IF-induced restoration of autophagic flux represents a mechanism for slowing this accumulation - and the human demonstration makes this clinically actionable.
