## Core Framework

The critical distinction in organizational design is not fragile vs. resilient but **preventive vs. preparatory** disruption strategy. Preventive strategies require accurately predicting specific disruptions (impossible in complex environments). Preparatory strategies build systemic strength that works regardless of *which* disruption arrives.

**Antifragile organizations**: Don't invest in predicting specific threats. Invest in structural properties that generate strength from *any* unexpected disruption.

## Six Design Elements of Organizational Antifragility

| Element | Description | Function in Disruption |
|---------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Redundancy** | Multiple pathways for critical functions | Buffer capacity; enables experimentation with failure absorption |
| **Loose coupling** | Components can fail without cascading collapse | Local learning; limits contagion |
| **Modularity** | Contained experimentation zones | Limits damage; allows stress-induced adaptation in subsystems |
| **Scalability** | Capacity to expand stress-response systems under load | Surge capability without structural breakdown |
| **Decentralized governance** | Decision-making distributed where stress information exists | Speed and accuracy under uncertainty |
| **Self-organization** | Emergent adaptation without central coordination | Novel response to genuinely novel stressors |

**These elements work together**: Redundancy gives modularity room to fail. Loose coupling prevents local failures from becoming system failures. Decentralized governance enables rapid adaptive response. Self-organization generates genuinely novel solutions.

## The Preventive vs. Preparatory Shift

**Preventive Strategy** (fragile in complex environments):
- "Identify all possible disruptions"
- "Build specific defenses against each"
- "Optimize against the expected threat landscape"
- **Problem**: Complex environments are fundamentally non-predictable; each defense is also a brittleness point

**Preparatory Strategy** (antifragile):
- "Build structural properties that generate strength from any disruption"
- "Create capacity for rapid adaptation regardless of disruption type"
- "Maintain productive volatility internally to keep adaptive muscles active"
- **Advantage**: Works against novel disruptions by design; doesn't require prediction

## CACH Model Applied to Organizations

The CACH (Catabolic-Anabolic Cycling) model applies to organizations:
- **Catabolic phase**: Deliberate disruption, experimentation, challenge, market turbulence
- **Anabolic phase**: Consolidation, learning, capacity integration, culture-building

Organizations that *only* have catabolic phases (constant disruption) burn out and collapse.
Organizations that *only* have anabolic phases (pure optimization/efficiency) become progressively brittle.

The most resilient organizations cycle deliberately: periods of productive challenge followed by consolidation.
