# Implementation Intentions - Daily WOOP Outperforms Single-Session Training

**Source**: "Daily Mental Contrasting with Implementation Intentions and Goal Motives in Reducing Bedtime Procrastination: A Registered Report", April 2025, Psychology & Health. DOI: 10.1080/08870446.2025.2491593
**Document Type**: Preregistered Replication (Registered Report - highest methodological rigor)

## Core Insight

Daily application of WOOP (Wish-Outcome-Obstacle-Plan) / MCII (Mental Contrasting with Implementation Intentions) outperforms a single-session training in supporting goal pursuit - but only for participants with specific goal motive profiles. This means implementation intention effectiveness is not universal but contingent on motivational context, suggesting personalization of self-regulation interventions based on what motivates the individual.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Preregistered registered report (highest rigor), N=297 participants (Prolific platform), 7-day diary study
**Condition 1**: Daily MCII application (7 evenings)
**Condition 2**: Single MCII session
**Outcome**: Bedtime procrastination (a high-prevalence, real-world self-regulation failure)
**Key Finding**: Daily MCII led to more efficient pursuit of bedtime goals, particularly for participants with specific goal motive profiles

**Mechanism of MCII (Two-Stage Process)**:
1. **Mental contrasting** creates an implicit energy-reality association: the brain associates the desired future with the current obstacle, triggering goal-directed behavior when the obstacle is encountered (automatic, non-deliberate)
2. **Implementation intentions** automatize the obstacle-action link: "When X obstacle occurs, I will do Y" - reducing reliance on willpower by pre-loading the behavioral response

**Why MCII Works Better Than Either Component Alone**:
- Mental contrasting alone: motivates but doesn't specify the action
- Implementation intentions alone: specifies action but doesn't create motivational urgency
- Combined: creates both the motivation trigger AND the behavioral response - uniquely combining motivation regulation with action planning in a single procedure

## Why This Matters

### The "Dose" Question for Psychological Interventions

Parallel to the ego depletion finding (intensity matters), this research shows that **frequency and repetition of implementation intention practice also matter**. A single MCII training session provides benefit, but daily practice over a week provides more durable, goal-aligned behavior.

This raises a broader question about psychological interventions: most are delivered as one-time treatments but may require repeated "dosing" for durable effects.

### Personalization of Self-Regulation Interventions

The finding that daily MCII was specifically beneficial for certain goal motive profiles (not universally) suggests that self-regulation is not one-size-fits-all. The "right" intervention depends on:
- The type of motivation involved (intrinsic vs. extrinsic, approach vs. avoidance)
- The nature of the obstacle (external situational vs. internal emotional)
- The individual's trait self-control strategies (consistent with Process Model)
