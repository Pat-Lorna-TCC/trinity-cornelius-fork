# GABAergic Hippocampal Circuits Implement PFC-Directed Thought Suppression

**Source**: "Brain Mechanisms Underlying the Inhibitory Control of Thought", Anderson, Crespo-Garcia & Subbulakshmi, May 2025 (published July 2025), Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 26(7): 415-437. DOI: 10.1038/s41583-025-00929-y
**Document Type**: Comprehensive Review (20+ years of research synthesis)

## Core Insight

The brain stops unwanted thoughts not through cortical activity alone, but through a specific two-stage circuit: the right dorsolateral PFC detects and recruits a fronto-temporal pathway, which then activates parvalbumin-expressing (PV+) GABAergic interneurons in the hippocampus via the entorhinal cortex. These hippocampal GABA circuits are the actual *effectors* of thought suppression. This is the first mechanistic explanation of how a top-down intention (PFC) translates into actual memory suppression - and failure of this GABA system is proposed as a transdiagnostic mechanism underlying intrusive thoughts in PTSD, OCD, depression, and anxiety.

---

## Evidence

**Review Scope**: 20+ years of research, both action stopping and thought stopping literature
**Key Mechanisms Identified**:

1. **Domain-general right PFC inhibitory hub**: Right dorsolateral and ventrolateral PFC supports suppression of both motor actions and memory retrieval - same cortical machinery, domain-general function

2. **The fronto-temporal thought-stopping pathway**: Thought stopping additionally recruits a unique pathway beyond action stopping. The right DLPFC negatively couples with the left hippocampus during successful directed forgetting.

3. **GABAergic hippocampal suppression as the effector**: PFC recruits PV+ interneurons in the hippocampus via entorhinal cortex to downregulate hippocampal output. This is the mechanistic bridge between intention and execution.

4. **Beta-oscillatory signaling**: When intrusive thoughts occur, ACC detects them and signals right DLPFC, which upregulates hippocampal suppression via beta-oscillatory signals.

5. **Transdiagnostic clinical application**: Hippocampal disinhibition (failure of this GABA system) is a proposed transdiagnostic factor underlying intrusive thinking across PTSD, depression, OCD, and anxiety disorders.

## Why This Matters

### Hippocampus as Executive Function Node

This fundamentally repositions the hippocampus. Previously understood primarily as a **memory formation and retrieval** structure, the hippocampus is now also understood as a critical **executive function** component - the final effector in the brain's thought-control system. Memory research and executive function research converge here.

### The Two-Stage Control Architecture

The mechanism is architecturally elegant: PFC sets the intention, sends the signal, but the hippocampus does the implementation work. This is analogous to how the PFC doesn't store working memory but controls its allocation (Finding 3). The brain consistently implements a **controller/executor split** rather than centralizing all functions in PFC.

### Transdiagnostic Mental Health Implications

If hippocampal GABA disinhibition underlies intrusive thinking across multiple disorders, then:
- PTSD flashbacks, OCD intrusions, depressive ruminations, and anxious worry share a common neural mechanism
- Fear extinction therapy may work partly by strengthening the PFC-hippocampal-GABA pathway
- GABA-targeting interventions (benzodiazepines, but also mindfulness/exercise that increase GABA) may work across multiple intrusive thought disorders
- This unifies what seemed like separate clinical phenomena
