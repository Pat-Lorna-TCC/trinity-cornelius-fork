# Ego Depletion Is Real But Only Under Sustained Cognitive Demand

**Source**: "Revisiting Ego Depletion: Evidence from Multi-Lab Collaborations", Dang, Xiao, Mao, Liu, Baumert et al. (30+ co-authors), October 2025, Journal of Pacific Rim Psychology. DOI: 10.1177/18344909251386084
**Document Type**: Multi-lab Preregistered Replication Study
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-20
**Session**: 2026-02-20 Self-Regulation Executive Function Neuroscience

## Core Insight

Ego depletion - the effect that prior self-regulatory effort impairs subsequent self-control - is a real, robust phenomenon, but only manifests when the initial depletion task is genuinely demanding (30-40 minutes of sustained antisaccade performance). The prior multi-lab null results (Hagger et al. 2016, Vohs et al. 2021) were not evidence against ego depletion; they were artifacts of insufficiently demanding depletion tasks.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Multi-lab preregistered replication, 14 international labs, fully computerized and in-person conditions
**Sample Size**: N = 2,078 participants
**Depletion Task**: 30-40 minute antisaccade task (demand > inhibitory control threshold)
**Outcome Task**: Go/NoGo performance
**Key Results**:
- Effect size: d = 0.31 to 0.35 (small-to-medium)
- Heterogeneity: I² = 0 (unusually low - minimal variance across labs)
- Bayesian evidence: BF₁₀ > 700 (decisive evidence for the effect)
- Consistent across age groups, gender, in-person and online formats

**Why Prior Replications Failed**:
- Hagger et al. (2016), 23 labs: d = 0.04 (null) - used weak 5-10 minute letter-crossing task
- Vohs et al. (2021), 36 labs: d = 0.06 (null) - similar methodological limitations
- Conclusion: Both prior "refutations" used tasks below the intensity threshold required to produce detectable depletion

**Replication Status**: Represents landmark resolution after decade-long controversy. Key limitation: 14 labs were predominantly recruited through original authors' networks, raising potential allegiance effects.

---

## Why This Matters

### The Replication Crisis Reframe

This finding has major methodological implications beyond ego depletion itself. **The replication crisis in psychology may be partly a manipulation-intensity crisis** - many null results reflect underpowered manipulations rather than false-positive original findings. Researchers systematically underestimate the dose required to produce psychological effects.

### Practical Implications

If ego depletion requires 30+ minutes of demanding inhibitory control before manifesting, then:
- Routine daily decisions likely do NOT meaningfully deplete self-regulatory resources
- Self-control "fatigue" is real but confined to genuinely demanding, sustained cognitive work
- Self-control training analogy holds: depleting tasks may build capacity if followed by recovery, similar to muscle-building
