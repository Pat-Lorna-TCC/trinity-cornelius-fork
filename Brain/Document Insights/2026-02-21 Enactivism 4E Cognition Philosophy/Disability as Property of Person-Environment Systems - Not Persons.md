# Disability as Property of Person-Environment Systems - Not Persons

**Source**: Toro, Rietveld & Kiverstein, "Disabling Scaffolds: The Lived Embodiment of Disability in an Oppressive Sociomaterial Environment," *Topoi* 44(2), 529-543, 2025 [https://erikrietveld.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/s11245-024-10132-w.pdf]; Van Grunsven, "Disabled Body-Minds in Hostile Environments," *Topoi* 44(2), 505-515, 2025; Albright et al., *Journal of Multicultural Counseling and Development*, 2025

## Core Insight

Disability is not primarily a property of a person (their brain, their body, or their genetics). It is a property of a **person-environment system**. Change the environment and disability changes or disappears. This goes beyond the standard social model of disability (which says disability is socially caused while impairment remains biological). The 4E/enactivist claim is stronger: because the environment is cognitively constitutive (not just constraining), a disabling environment does not merely prevent actions - it **shapes the cognitive capacities of the persons within it**. Oppressive scaffolding does not just block; it cognitively forms.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The standard medical model**: Disability is a property of individuals. The disabled person has an impairment (biological) that causes disability (functional limitation). Treatment is medical intervention to reduce impairment.

**The standard social model**: Disability is socially constructed through barriers. Impairment may be biological but disability is caused by inaccessible environments. Change the environment, remove the disability.

**The 4E/enactivist model (stronger claim)**: Because cognition is organism-environment coupling, and environments are cognitively constitutive, a systematically inaccessible environment does not just prevent action - it reduces cognitive capacity. The concept of **material oppression** (Toro, Rietveld & Kiverstein, 2025): ableism structures the sociomaterial environment to fit able-bodied persons and exclude others. This creates:
- Pervasive "I-cannot" experiences (failure of affordance perception)
- **Pathological embodiment**: Inability to enact activist affordances (resistance, reorganization, advocacy) due to perceived risk of catastrophic reaction from the environment
- Reduced cognitive agency across multiple domains

**Empirical application** (Albright et al., 2025): Black individuals with disabilities face intersecting forms of material oppression (racism + ableism). They see fewer affordances - not because they lack cognitive capacity per se, but because the system of affordances available to them is systematically reduced by oppressive social structuring. The reduced affordance field is real, not perceived.

**Van Grunsven's quasi-Cartesian critique**: A Cartesian picture of the body (as a biological machine separate from mind and world) shapes our sociotechnical niches in an ableist manner. The built environment assumes a normative body. This is not merely inconvenient for those who deviate from the norm - it is a **threat to human and planetary flourishing at large**, because it maintains cognitive niches that exclude the perspectives needed for adaptive collective response to complex challenges.

---

## Paradigm Shift

This reframing implies:
- Treatment: **Environmental redesign** is primary therapy, not accommodation
- Research: Studying "disability" in oppressive environments generates misleading data about persons, not about person-environment systems
- Ethics: The distribution of affordances is a justice issue, not a medical issue
- Design: Creating accessible environments is cognitively beneficial to everyone, not just accommodation for the minority
