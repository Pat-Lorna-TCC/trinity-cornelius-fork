# Enactive Psychiatry - Mental Illness as Person-Environment System Dysfunction

## Core Insight

Enactive psychiatry reconceptualizes mental illness not as a brain disorder but as **a disturbance of the ecology of the brain** - a disruption of the patient's lived body, lived space, and relationships. If cognition is organism-environment coupling, then pathological cognition is a disturbance at the coupling level, not primarily at the neural level. This reframing has direct treatment implications: environmental redesign becomes therapy, not merely accommodation.

---

## Context & Evidence

**Three accounts of enactive psychopathology** (De Prado-Gordillo & López-Silva, 2025):
1. **Maiese**: Disruptions in autonomy and agency - illness is loss of the organism's capacity for adaptive self-regulation
2. **Nielsen**: Behaviors conflicting with individual functional norms - illness as mismatch between organism's patterns and its own normative structure
3. **De Haan**: Patterns of disordered sense-making transformed through existential dimension - illness as disruption of the organism's capacity to make meaning

**Depression through an enactive lens** (2024 paper): In melancholic depression, "time becomes explicit to such an extent that it turns into a constant burden." Normal human temporality is implicit, embedded in activity. Depression makes time a present object of experience rather than a flowing medium of engagement. Treatment implication: **material engagement** with malleable substances (clay, pottery, textiles) can counter this temporal distortion by restoring the organism's absorbed engagement with material process. The controlled malleability of clay enacts a different relationship to time.

**Social integration extension** (2024): Enactive psychiatry has been extended beyond dyadic (therapist-patient) interactions using Canguilhem's distinction between health and pathology. Health is the capacity to create new norms in response to new conditions. Pathology is the loss of normative flexibility. This provides a principled basis for assessing social environments as health-promoting or pathological - which previous enactive psychiatry frameworks lacked.

**Autism application**: The Skilled Intentionality framework (combining ecological-enactive views with Free Energy and predictive processing) applied to autism shows that autistic differences stem from **aberrant precision estimation** - not from defective internal representations but from different ways of weighting reliability of sensory signals versus prior expectations. The environment's unpredictability becomes more cognitively costly for people with different precision architectures.

## Field Acceptance

**Emerging in academic psychiatry**: The 2024-2025 papers represent systematic development from theoretical proposals to applicable frameworks.

**Not yet clinical mainstream**: Mainstream psychiatry remains predominantly brain-disorder focused (DSM model). Enactive psychiatry is an academic research program with growing clinical implications.

**Significant clinical potential**: Environmental redesign and material engagement therapies are receiving empirical investigation.
