# Radical Enactivism - Extensive vs Autonomous Enactivism Distinction

## Core Insight

Within enactivism, a crucial but underappreciated distinction exists between two positions that are easily conflated: **Extensive Enactivism** (Radical Enactivism/REC) and **Autonomous Enactivism** (autopoietic enactivism). Extensive Enactivism holds that cognition is dynamically unfolding, situated, informationally sensitive engagement - but does NOT require that all information processing be replaced by sense-making. Autonomous Enactivism holds that basic cognition IS sense-making and that information processing concepts should be eliminated entirely. This distinction matters enormously for what enactivism claims about AI and representation.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The three-way distinction** (Sganzerla, Hutto & Kirchhoff, 2025):

1. **Extended Mind Thesis (Clark & Chalmers, 1998)**: Cognition sometimes constituted by external resources. Still accepts functionalism and representationalism. External resources extend mental content.

2. **Extensive Enactivism (REC - Radical Enactivism)**: Basic cognition is dynamically unfolding, situated interaction with informationally sensitive engagements. Anti-representationalist about BASIC cognition. Higher cognition (language, reasoning) arises through sociocultural symbolic practices but is explained naturalistically.

3. **Autonomous Enactivism (Autopoietic)**: Basic cognition IS sense-making activity. No information processing at all in the relevant sense. Meaning arises from the organism's autonomous organizational activity.

**The identity crisis at REC's 10-year anniversary** (Antwerp workshop, 2024): One presentation proposed that Hutto and Myin should remove the word "enactive" from REC entirely. The claim: REC disagrees with Varela/Thompson/Rosch enactivism on so many fundamental points (REC rejects sense-making ontology, rejects life-mind continuity thesis, accepts information processing in some form) that calling the disputes "sibling disagreements" stretches the metaphor to breaking.

**Mental Fictionalism challenge**: The 2025 paper engages Mental Fictionalism's claim that mental states are fictional (not real entities). If mental states are fictional, what does that mean for claims about cognitive extension? Extensive Enactivism's answer: cognition is dynamically unfolding interaction (not a state that can be in or out of the skull), so the question of extension dissolves rather than requires resolution.

**The 26-year retrospective**: The paper situates itself within 26 years of developments since Clark and Chalmers (1998), showing how the metaphysics of mind has shifted. The key shift: from "where is cognition?" (extended mind) to "what kind of thing is cognition such that location makes sense?" (enactivism).

---

## Why This Distinction Matters

For the AI question: Extensive Enactivism is more open to sophisticated AI systems potentially achieving functional cognition through dynamically unfolding interaction. Autonomous Enactivism requires biological sense-making (autopoiesis) that AI cannot achieve. These lead to different conclusions about AI's cognitive status.

For the representation question: Extensive Enactivism allows information-sensitive engagement without full representationalism. This potentially bridges computationalism and enactivism more than Autonomous Enactivism does.
