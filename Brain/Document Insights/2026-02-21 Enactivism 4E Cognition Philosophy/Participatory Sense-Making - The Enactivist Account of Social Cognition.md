# Participatory Sense-Making - The Enactivist Account of Social Cognition

## Core Insight

Participatory Sense-Making (PSM) is the enactivist account of social cognition: social understanding arises not from one brain simulating another's mental states (the standard "theory of mind" model) but from the **emergent coordination process of two or more organisms dynamically coupling with each other**. Social interaction has its own autonomy - it is not reducible to the individual organisms involved. This has been extended in 2024 to social institutions, which are characterized as higher-level PSM systems that achieve their own form of autonomous existence.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The standard theory-of-mind model**: We understand others by internally simulating their mental states (simulation theory) or by applying folk-psychological rules (theory theory). Social cognition is fundamentally about reading internal mental states.

**PSM's alternative**: Social understanding emerges from the dynamics of coordinated interaction itself. Two organisms coupling dynamically generate an interaction process that has properties neither organism has alone. Social understanding is not about mind-reading but about **coordination quality** - how well the interaction process achieves mutual sense-making.

**The 2024 institutional extension**: Enacted institutions are characterized as problem-domains that patterns of PSM have brought forth. Institutions are not just collections of individuals or sets of rules. They achieve a form of higher-level autonomy - they have their own normative standards, their own health and pathology, their own self-maintenance processes. This is PSM "scaling up" from dyadic interaction to collective institutional dynamics.

**The normative power explanation**: PSM explains why social norms motivate without external enforcement - because norms are constitutive of the sense-making practices through which agents understand their situations. This is a deeper explanation than standard social psychology (which explains norm compliance through identity, reputation, or internalized reward) - it grounds normative motivation in the structure of sense-making itself.

**Clinical applications**: Enactive psychiatry extends PSM to understand mental illness as disruption of participatory sense-making. Depression, schizophrenia, and autism are being analyzed not as individual brain disorders but as disturbances in the quality of organism-environment and organism-organism coupling.

## Field Acceptance

**Accepted within enactivism**: PSM is a cornerstone of the enactivist approach to social cognition.

**Debated against standard theory of mind approaches**: The claim that social understanding arises from coordination dynamics rather than internal simulation is contested in empirical social neuroscience.

**Growing applied development**: 2024-2025 applications in psychiatry, institutional theory, and social philosophy.
