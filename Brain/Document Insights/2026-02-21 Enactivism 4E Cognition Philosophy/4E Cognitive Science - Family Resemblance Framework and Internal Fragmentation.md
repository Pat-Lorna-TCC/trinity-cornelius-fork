# 4E Cognitive Science - Family Resemblance Framework and Internal Fragmentation

## Core Insight

"4E cognitive science" (Embodied, Embedded, Extended, Enacted) is not a unified paradigm with a single defining feature. It is best understood as a **family resemblance** in Wittgenstein's sense - a set of overlapping similarities with no single feature shared by all members. This framework resolves long-running definitional debates by showing they were asking the wrong question. More provocatively: only autopoietic enactivism constitutes a genuine theoretical break from mainstream cognitive science. The other three E's challenge some but not all of traditional cognitive science's assumptions, making much "4E research" less radical than it appears.

---

## Context & Evidence

**The definitional failure**: Previous attempts to define 4E cognitive science failed because they either captured the wrong extension (included non-4E work) or generated incoherence (excluded paradigmatic 4E work). This is because they tried to find a single essential feature.

**Alexander's family resemblance solution** (2025): 4E cognitive science is constrained by historical influence (Varela's foundational work) and exhibits overlapping similarities across six dimensions:
1. **Embodiment** - cognitive processes involve the whole body
2. **Non-dualism** - rejection of mind-body split
3. **Non-representationalism** - skepticism about internal representations
4. **Phenomenological methods** - first-person experiential data
5. **Evolutionary frameworks** - cognition shaped by evolutionary history
6. **Dynamical systems perspectives** - cognition as dynamical process

No single dimension is essential; Varela's foundational work includes all six, but no later 4E work needs all six to count as genuinely 4E.

**Why the field feels fragmented**: It IS fragmented by design. Connected only by family resemblance, different 4E researchers share different subsets of commitments. This is philosophically healthy (pluralism) but practically confusing.

**The exceptionality of enactivism**: The companion 2025 paper evaluates each 4E strand against five traditional cognitive science assumptions (computationalism, representationalism, functionalism, internalism, realism):
- Embodied cognition: challenges internalism, weakly challenges representationalism
- Embedded cognition: challenges internalism, remains broadly realist
- Extended cognition: challenges internalism, still accepts functionalism and representationalism
- **Enactivism (autopoietic)**: challenges ALL FIVE, including resistant to realist commitments about cognitive posits

**Implication**: The "4E" label creates false equivalences. If you want a genuine alternative to computationalism, you must go all the way to enactivism's autopoietic core. Extended mind (Clark & Chalmers) is a reformist position within computational cognitive science, not a revolution.

## Field Acceptance

**Widely accepted within field**: That 4E cognitive science is a family resemblance rather than a unified paradigm is now the mainstream view within the field itself.

**The exceptionality claim is more contested**: That only enactivism genuinely departs from computational assumptions is argued by the 2025 paper but disputed by proponents of other 4E approaches.
