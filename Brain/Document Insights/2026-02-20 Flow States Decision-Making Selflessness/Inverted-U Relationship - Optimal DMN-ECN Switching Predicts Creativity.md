# Inverted-U Relationship - Optimal DMN-ECN Switching Predicts Creativity

**Source**: Chen, Q., Kenett, Y. N., et al. (2025). Dynamic switching between brain networks predicts creative ability. *Communications Biology*, 8, Article 54.
**Document Type**: Large-scale neuroimaging study
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-20
**Session**: 2026-02-20 Flow States Decision-Making Selflessness

---

## Core Finding

Creativity is predicted by **number of DMN-ECN switches**, not static network activity, and follows an **inverted-U relationship**: optimal performance requires balanced switching frequency.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Multi-site neuroimaging analysis
**Sample Size**: N = 2,433 participants across 10 independent samples
**Geographic Diversity**: Austria, Canada, China, Japan, United States
**Significance**: **Largest and most ethnically diverse creativity neuroscience study to date**

**Key Results**:
- Creativity (but NOT general intelligence) reliably predicted by DMN-ECN switches
- Inverted-U relationship confirmed across all samples
- Effect independent of IQ

**Citation**: Chen et al. (2025), *Communications Biology*
**Replication**: 10 independent samples = robust finding

---

## The Inverted-U Pattern

**Too Few Switches**:
- Insufficient spontaneity
- Rigid thinking
- Low creativity

**Optimal Switching**:
- Balanced network dynamics
- Spontaneous thought (DMN) ↔ Cognitive control (ECN)
- Peak creativity

**Too Many Switches**:
- Cognitive instability
- Unable to sustain ideas
- Reduced performance

---

## Mechanism

**Creativity arises from**:
- Mental state balancing spontaneous thought (DMN) and cognitive control (ECN)
- Dynamic flexibility, not static configuration
- Ability to switch AT THE RIGHT RATE

**Critical Insight**: It's not how much of each network, but how flexibly you switch between them.

---

## Implications for Flow States

**Flow may represent the "sweet spot"**:
- Optimal switching frequency
- Neither stuck in one network nor chaotically switching
- Explains why flow is creative AND controlled

**Why this matters**:
- Flow isn't just "DMN off"
- Flow isn't just "ECN on"
- Flow is **optimal dynamic switching**
