# The Paradox of Ego Dissolution - Reduces Personal Bias While Increasing False Belief Susceptibility

**Source**: An Integrated theory of false insights and beliefs under psychedelics, Communications Psychology, 2024

## Core Insight

Ego dissolution states (induced by psychedelics, meditation, or flow) create a **double-edged cognitive effect**: they reduce self-referential biases (self-serving bias, identity-protective cognition, confirmation of existing beliefs) while simultaneously **increasing susceptibility to false insights, suggestion effects, and formation of new false beliefs**.

This paradox reveals that ego dissolution creates a **highly malleable cognitive state** where bias reduction is possible but not guaranteed - the quality of the information environment during ego dissolution becomes critically important.

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Theoretical framework with neuroimaging support
**Key Mechanism**: DMN decoupling and reduced precision of prior beliefs
**Research Base**: Psychedelic neuroimaging studies, predictive processing framework

### Neurobiological Mechanism

"The neurobiological action of psychedelics on the brain may increase the intensity of the experience of insight, leading to flexible updating of beliefs, critically **including the adoption of false beliefs**"

**DMN Reduction**: "Neuroimaging studies have shown that psychedelics reduce connectivity of the default mode network (DMN), a group of brain regions involved in self-referential processing, which is hypothesized to underlie the 'ego dissolution' often reported"

**Reduced Precision Model**: "The reduced precision of prior beliefs under psychedelics increases the range of hypotheses that can be entertained, with greater attentional resources allocated to alternate hypotheses"

### The Two Opposing Forces

**Force 1: Personal Bias Reduction**
- ↓ Self-serving bias
- ↓ Identity-protective cognition
- ↓ Confirmation of existing beliefs
- ↑ Openness to new perspectives

**Force 2: Susceptibility Increase**
- ↑ False insights
- ↑ Suggestion effects
- ↑ Formation of new false beliefs
- ↑ Uncritical acceptance

### Therapeutic Implications

"A key challenge for the field involves understanding how suggestion effects can both act as a significant confound in research on psychedelics but also improve outcomes in therapeutic interventions"

**Critical Factor**: "Set and setting" become paramount - ego dissolution creates cognitive flexibility where biases can either be therapeutically reduced (with proper guidance) or replaced with new biases or false beliefs (without guidance).
