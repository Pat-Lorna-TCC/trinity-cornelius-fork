## Core Framework

Self-narrative is not a description of a pre-existing self - it is a **performative act** that constitutes the self being described. The self is not discovered through introspection and then narrated; it is continuously created through the act of narration.

This is the linguistic parallel to the vault's core insight that "self is an illusion created by conceptualization" - the conceptualization IS the linguistic narrative. Language does not express identity; it constructs it.

**Field Acceptance**: Performativity theory is well-established in philosophy of language (Austin) and gender studies (Butler). Its application to identity formation broadly is emerging as a coherent framework in cognitive science, narrative psychology, and organizational studies.

---

## The Mechanism

**Dominant cultural narratives and institutional discourses** do not just influence how people talk about themselves - they provide the raw materials from which selves are constructed. This has three implications:

1. **Therapeutic**: Narrative therapy works by offering alternative linguistic framings that constitute alternative self-experiences - not by "changing how someone talks about themselves" but by changing the self through changing the narrative
2. **Organizational**: Being a "good professional" in AI-integrated environments is linguistically constructed through organizational language, not just demonstrated through behavior (Springer AI & Society, 2025)
3. **Dopamine**: The finding that familiar linguistic frames activate dopamine-linked confirmation responses means that challenging someone's linguistic self-narrative is not just intellectually uncomfortable - it is neurologically experienced as threat

---

## Why Reframing Meets Dopamine Resistance

If:
- Identity is constructed through self-narrative (this framework)
- Confirmation of identity-linked beliefs triggers dopamine reward (The Uncertainty-Dopamine-Belief Loop)
- Linguistic frames activating at 250ms below conscious awareness ([[Dead Metaphors Are Neurologically Alive - 250ms Preconscious Activation]])

Then: **The resistance to narrative reframing is neurological, not just intellectual.** Challenging someone's self-narrative creates uncertainty (dopamine withdrawal from loss of certainty), activates threat responses, and disrupts the ongoing reward stream from identity confirmation.

This explains why:
- Therapy requires trust, time, and repeated exposure
- Organizational change fails when it reframes professional identity
- Political identity change is rare even in the face of contradictory evidence
