## Core Insight

Three independent strands of 2024-2025 research reveal a striking convergence: Buddhist philosophical insights about language and emptiness (sunyata) are structurally isomorphic with contemporary cognitive linguistics and predictive coding neuroscience.

The convergence: **concepts have no intrinsic nature** (sunyata) maps directly onto CMT's discovery that **conceptual metaphors are conventional mappings without intrinsic semantic necessity** - ARGUMENT IS BATTLE is cultural, not logical, yet once established, shapes cognition as if it were natural.

## The Three Convergences

### 1. Buddhist Linguistic Constructivism (2,500 years before Lakoff)

**Divino (2025)**: Early Buddhist thought in the Pali Canon presents language as not describing a pre-given world but *constituting* the phenomenological world of experience. Buddhist analysis anticipates Saussure's structural linguistics and contemporary philosophy of perception.

**Cognitive Linguistics parallel**: CMT demonstrates that abstract concepts are constituted through metaphorical mappings - they do not exist as pre-formed mental objects waiting to be labeled.

**Predictive Coding parallel**: The brain does not receive reality and label it - it generates a model (including linguistic model) that constitutes experienced reality.

### 2. Abhidhamma and the Symbol Grounding Problem

**Cheng & Sandamadulle (2025)**: Buddhist emptiness (*sunyata*) does not negate cognitive functionality but reframes it as emerging from interdependent processes (*paticca-samuppada*) rather than intrinsic essences. Conventional symbols (*sammuti sacca*) function like Language of Thought representations with syntactic roles, but **without intrinsic meaning** - meaning arises from causal networks, not static semantic structures.

This is exactly the symbol grounding problem in AI: symbols gain meaning not from intrinsic properties but from their causal relationships to experience and other symbols.

**Buddhist resolution** (2,000+ years old, independently converged upon by contemporary AI research): meaning arises from causal networks, not intrinsic essences.

### 3. Ba Linguistics and Preconscious Meaning Generation

**Kawahara (2024) on Kukai and ba linguistics**: Kukai's view - "existence is language" - all existence is fundamentally linguistic. "Ba linguistics" proposes that meaning in dialogue emerges from a "deeper primordial field" not recognized by surface consciousness. Izutsu's *segmentation theory of meaning*: "reality" as experienced is created by consciousness through linguistic segmentation.

**Cognitive science parallel**: Dead metaphors activate at 250ms (below conscious awareness) - meaning is generated before and below conscious appraisal. The "primordial field" below surface consciousness is what neuroscience now calls preconscious linguistic processing.
