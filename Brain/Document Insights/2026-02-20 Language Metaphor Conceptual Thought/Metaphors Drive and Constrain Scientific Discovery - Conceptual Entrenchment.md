# Metaphors Drive and Constrain Scientific Discovery - Conceptual Entrenchment

## Core Insight

Scientific metaphors do not just illuminate - they create **cognitive commitments that define which experiments get done and which anomalies get explained away**. The metaphors used to understand a phenomenon shape the entire research program: what counts as evidence, what counts as an explanation, what counts as an anomaly worth pursuing.

**Conceptual entrenchment**: once a metaphor is established in a scientific field, it becomes cognitively invisible - it feels like description rather than interpretation. This is when metaphors constrain discovery most powerfully.

---

## The Mechanism

When a field adopts a core metaphor:
1. The source domain's logic gets imported as constraints on the target domain
2. Research designs are built to test predictions within the source domain's structure
3. Anomalies that don't fit the source domain's logic are classified as noise or experimental error
4. The metaphor becomes the background assumption against which results are interpreted

**Example from the report**: The metaphor of "friction" in economics (transaction costs) shaped an entire research program, including:
- What counts as evidence (measurable transaction costs)
- What counts as an explanation (friction-reducing mechanisms)
- What counts as a problem to solve (reducing friction)
- What does NOT get investigated (transaction benefits, friction as signal)

**The SCIENCE IS EXPLORATION metaphor itself** (arXiv:2410.08991): This paper uses the exploration metaphor to examine CMT's computational tractability - demonstrating how a chosen metaphor determines which aspects of a problem appear important.

---

## Conceptual Entrenchment vs. Conceptual Innovation

Scientific progress requires both:
- **Exploitation**: using established metaphors to generate predictable research (most science)
- **Exploration**: questioning the metaphor and proposing alternatives (paradigm shifts)

The danger of conceptual entrenchment is that exploration becomes cognitively invisible. Scientists who question the root metaphor appear to be questioning the data itself.

**Historical examples** (implied by the framework):
- Newtonian mechanics (UNIVERSE IS A MACHINE) → limited quantum and relativistic thinking
- Information theory (MIND IS A COMPUTER) → limited embodied/situated cognition research
- Evolution (COMPETITION IS SURVIVAL) → limited cooperation/niche-construction research
