## Core Framework

AI adoption resistance is often framed as fear of job loss. The deeper mechanism is **fear of losing the cognitive activities that constitute meaningful work**. This is a **linguistically-mediated identity threat**: the meaning of cognitive labor is constructed through language, and AI challenges that construction.

Being a "good professional" in AI-integrated environments is **linguistically constructed** through organizational language, not just demonstrated through behavior. When an organization introduces AI and frames certain cognitive tasks as automatable, it is redefining what competence means - and therefore what identity-constituting activities remain.

---

## The Mechanism

**Step 1**: Cognitive activities become identity-constituting when:
- They require training/expertise (scarcity creates identity value)
- They are visible to others (social recognition)
- They are linguistically labeled ("strategic thinking," "creative problem-solving")

**Step 2**: AI can perform some of these cognitive activities.

**Step 3**: Organizations frame AI deployment in language that implicitly or explicitly redefines which cognitive activities are "human value-add" vs. "automatable."

**Step 4**: This reframing is an identity threat - not "my job is at risk" but "what makes my work meaningful and defines my professional identity is being redefined."

**Step 5**: Resistance to AI adoption is partly resistance to this identity redefinition.

---

## The Linguistic Dimension

The key insight from AI adoption metaphor research: the language through which people encounter AI shapes psychological orientation more than technical understanding does. When AI is framed as:
- **"AI as efficiency optimizer"**: Cognitive labor is framed as a resource to be optimized → identity threat (cognitive labor is now "inefficiency")
- **"AI as assistant"**: Cognitive labor is augmented → lower identity threat
- **"AI as colleague"**: Professional identity expands to include AI collaboration → identity opportunity

**Organizational leaders who don't audit their AI framing are making the adoption battle harder without realizing it.**
