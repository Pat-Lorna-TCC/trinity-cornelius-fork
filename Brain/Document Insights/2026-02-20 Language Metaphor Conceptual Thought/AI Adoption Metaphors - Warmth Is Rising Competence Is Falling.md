# AI Adoption Metaphors - Warmth Is Rising Competence Is Falling

**Source**: "Metaphors of AI indicate that people increasingly perceive AI as warm and human-like." *Communications Psychology* (Nature portfolio), 2025. DOI available at nature.com/articles/s44271-025-00376-6
**Document Type**: Research Finding (longitudinal study, Nature portfolio)
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-20
**Session**: 2026-02-20 Language Metaphor Conceptual Thought

## Core Insight

A year-long longitudinal study collecting 12,000+ metaphors of AI from a nationally representative U.S. sample found that anthropomorphic and warm metaphors are *increasing* over time while competence-framing metaphors are *decreasing*. The metaphors people use to describe AI significantly predict their AI trust and adoption willingness - and these metaphors are shifting toward warmer, more human-like framings.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Longitudinal, nationally representative U.S. sample, 12 months
**Corpus**: 12,000+ metaphors collected and analyzed
**Dominant metaphors identified**: tool, brain, thief, teacher, friend, search engine, library, god, genie, animal, assistant

**Key Quantitative Findings**:
- Warmth-anthropomorphism correlation: Pearson's r = 0.45
- Warmth and anthropomorphism in metaphor use significantly predict AI trust and adoption willingness
- Trend: anthropomorphic/warm metaphors increasing; competence-framing metaphors decreasing

**The Paradox**:
- Human-like metaphors do not automatically generate positive perceptions
- "Thief" is anthropomorphic but low in both warmth and competence
- "Library" is non-anthropomorphic but high in both warmth and competence
- Anthropomorphism and warmth are correlated but not identical

---

## What This Means for AI Adoption

The language through which people encounter AI shapes their psychological orientation toward it **more than their technical understanding does**. Organizations deploying AI are implicitly choosing metaphorical frames when they name, describe, and position their AI tools.

**Adoption Dynamics by Frame**:
- "AI as assistant" / "AI as colleague" → trust through warmth → higher adoption engagement
- "AI as optimizer" / "AI as system" → trust through competence → lower adoption engagement, higher resistance
- "AI as replacement" → anthropomorphic but low warmth → adoption resistance through identity threat

**The Shifting Trend**: Americans are increasingly perceiving AI as warm and human-like rather than as supremely competent. This is counter to the "AI as powerful optimizer" narrative pushed by technology companies. The public metaphors are moving in a different direction than the marketing.
