# Language Experience Reshapes the Brain's Anticipatory System - Predictive Coding Evidence

## Core Insight

Lifelong experience with a spoken language **shapes the brain's automatic anticipatory (predictive) mechanism** at a fundamental neural level - not just at the level of meaning processing, but at the level of preconscious auditory prediction. Language does not just use the brain's predictive system - it calibrates and reshapes it.

Furthermore, language comprehension *is* Bayesian inference: the brain is constantly predicting upcoming words and computing prediction error, and that error signal drives learning and comprehension simultaneously.

---

## Evidence

**eLife 2024**: The mismatch negativity (MMN) - a neural response to violations of expected auditory patterns - is measurable even during sleep and coma. Language-shaped prediction operates below conscious awareness. This means language experience has restructured the brain's anticipatory machinery below the level of conscious language processing.

**Cognition 2024 (Noureddine & Kuperberg)**: A predictive coding model of the N400 (the brain's semantic surprise signal) shows that lexico-semantic prediction error mirrors the N400's sensitivity to:
- Lexical variables (word frequency, predictability)
- Priming effects
- Contextual interactions

The N400 is the brain's "you said something unexpected" signal. It now has a computational model: it is the output of a Bayesian prediction error computation.

---

## The Implication: Language IS Bayesian Inference

Language comprehension is not:
1. Hear word
2. Retrieve meaning
3. Build sentence meaning

Language comprehension IS:
1. Predict what comes next (based on prior context, world knowledge, activated schemas)
2. Process incoming signal
3. Compute prediction error (N400 if high error)
4. Update model
5. Generate new prediction

**The brain is not receiving language and interpreting it. It is constantly making predictions, and perception is the process of testing those predictions.**

---

## What This Means for Linguistic Framing

If language comprehension is Bayesian inference:
- The frame activated at the beginning of a communication sets the predictions for everything that follows
- Information matching the frame is processed easily (low prediction error)
- Information violating the frame generates N400-level error (attention, surprise, resistance)
- The choice of linguistic frame is a literal intervention in the brain's prediction system

**The frame is not a persuasion technique - it is a pre-configuration of the brain's processing architecture for incoming information.**
