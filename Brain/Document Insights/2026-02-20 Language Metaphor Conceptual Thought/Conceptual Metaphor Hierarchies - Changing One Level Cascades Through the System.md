## Core Framework

The conceptual system has **hierarchical levels of cognitive complexity and schematicity**, with metaphors operating at different levels of abstraction. This is the multilevel approach to Conceptual Metaphor Theory (CMT).

Generic metaphors (CAUSES ARE FORCES) contain more specific variants (ANGER IS A HEATED FLUID IN A CONTAINER) in hierarchical relationships. Changing one level of metaphorical framing does not just change a label - it **cascades into related structures across the cognitive system**.

**Field Acceptance**: Extension of widely-accepted CMT framework (Lakoff & Johnson foundational); Kövecses is a leading CMT researcher; published in dedicated cognitive linguistics journal. Accepted by specialists; mainstream psychology less familiar with this specific development.

---

## The Hierarchy

Metaphors are not flat mappings but **nested structures**:

| Level | Example | Schematicity |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Generic | CAUSES ARE FORCES | Highly abstract |
| Mid-level | SOCIAL INFLUENCE IS PHYSICAL FORCE | Intermediate |
| Specific | PERSUASION IS PUSHING | Concrete |
| Most specific | "He pushed his agenda through" | Instantiated |

More generic metaphors have more entailments and affect more conceptual territory when activated or changed.

## Why Belief Change Is So Cognitively Disrupting

Previous understanding: changing a belief is difficult because of dopamine reward cycles (The Uncertainty-Dopamine-Belief Loop).

**This framework adds**: A conceptual frame change is not a single-node update. It requires restructuring across multiple levels of the metaphorical hierarchy simultaneously. The higher the level of the changed metaphor, the more cascading updates are required.

Changing "capitalism IS A GAME" (where some winners and losers are acceptable) requires cascading updates to:
- What counts as success/failure
- Who is responsible for outcomes
- What interventions are legitimate
- What emotions are appropriate
- What goals are worth pursuing

This is why paradigm shifts (scientific and personal) are so rare and cognitively costly.

---

## Implications

**For persuasion**: Changing specific-level metaphors (nicknames, descriptions) is easier but has limited cognitive impact. Changing generic-level metaphors (SOCIETY IS A MACHINE vs. SOCIETY IS AN ORGANISM) is rare but transformative.

**For scientific discovery**: Scientific metaphors constrain which experiments get done and which anomalies get explained (MIT "paths of abstraction" research). The metaphor of "friction" in economics shaped an entire research program. Changing the metaphor requires restructuring the entire framework.

**For AI adoption**: The generic-level metaphors for AI (TOOL, ORGANISM, COLLEAGUE, THREAT) predict adoption trajectories more than specific-level descriptors. Organizations that change AI's generic-level frame change everything downstream.
