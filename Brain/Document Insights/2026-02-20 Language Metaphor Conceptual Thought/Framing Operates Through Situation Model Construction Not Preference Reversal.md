# Framing Operates Through Situation Model Construction Not Preference Reversal

**Source**: Flusberg, S.J., Holmes, K.J., Thibodeau, P.H., Nabi, R., & Matlock, T. (2024). "The Psychology of Framing: How Everyday Language Shapes the Way We Think, Feel, and Act." *Psychological Science in the Public Interest*, 25(3). DOI: 10.1177/15291006241246966.

## Core Insight

The standard account of framing effects (Tversky-Kahneman) treats them as **preference reversals** - the same information presented differently flips choices. The 2024 landmark synthesis provides a more fundamental mechanism: different frames lead people to construct different **situation models** (mental representations of scenarios). It is not that preferences flip - it is that people are constructing entirely different representations of reality.

Framing effects are not a glitch in reasoning - they are a feature of how meaning is constructed.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Comprehensive review synthesizing decades of framing research across health, politics, business, journalism, and law
**Meta-Analytic Effect Sizes** (all substantial and replicable):
- Risky framing: d = 0.437
- Attribute framing: d = 0.260
- Goal framing: d = 0.444

**Key Domain Findings**:
- War metaphors increase urgency and support for aggressive policy solutions
- Cancer framing: "battle" vs. "journey" framing altered fatalism about diagnosis
- DeKay & Dou (2024, *Psychological Science*): framing effects amplified when option descriptions are **mismatched** across gain/loss frames

**Moderation Finding**: Framing effects are **reduced** when decisions are made in a second language - because foreign language processing reduces the emotional resonance that frames exploit (Jiao et al., 2024-2025, *Bilingualism: Language and Cognition*)

## The Situation Model Mechanism

When you describe a problem as a "battle," you do not merely change the label - you activate the **battle schema**:
- Adversaries exist
- There are tactics
- There are victory conditions
- There are casualties
- Urgency is high

Incoming information is then processed through these activated expectations. Non-matching information generates prediction error; matching information is assimilated smoothly. This is why frames are so cognitively efficient and so cognitively dangerous simultaneously.

**Commentary (James Walsh, same issue)**: Frames function not just as shortcuts but as **constitutive cognitive structures** - they do not merely influence judgment, they constitute the situation being judged.

## Distinction From Existing Knowledge

The existing note on Framing and Pre-Suasion - Context Before Content (Hassan/Cialdini) focuses on persuasion and pre-suasion. This insight specifies the **mechanism**: situation model construction. The distinction matters because:
- Pre-suasion focuses on sequence (frame before message)
- Situation model construction explains *why* the sequence works: the frame has already built the mental model through which the message will be received
