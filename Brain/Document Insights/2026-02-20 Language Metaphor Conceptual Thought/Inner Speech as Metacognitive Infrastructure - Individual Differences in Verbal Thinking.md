## Core Insight

Inner speech (verbal thinking) functions as **context-resistant, modality-transcending cognitive scaffolding** - a unique form of metacognitive infrastructure that allows thoughts to be examined, manipulated, and reported on in ways that other mental representations cannot. Individual differences in inner speech propensity matter for object recognition, abstract reasoning, and metacognition. The controversial claim that some people lack inner speech entirely is almost certainly an overclaim.

---

## Evidence

**Andy Clark's Framework**: Inner speech provides "context-resistant" and "modality-transcending" cognitive scaffolding. Sentences in inner speech can be:
- Examined (thought-about)
- Manipulated (modified, extended)
- Reported on (verbalized)

This makes inner speech uniquely suited for metacognition in a way that purely visual or motor representations are not.

**Borges & Mueller (2024)**: Significant variation in inner speech propensity within the general population, measured with new tools for German-speaking populations. Individual differences affect:
- Object recognition
- Abstract reasoning
- Metacognitive monitoring

**The "no inner voice" controversy**: Nedergaard & Lupyan (2024) suggested measuring people without inner speech is possible. Lind (2025, *Psychological Science*) critique: no participant in a 1,037-person sample actually reported completely lacking inner speech - raising methodological questions. The popular narrative about "people with no inner voice" is likely an overclaim.

**Social Metacognition**: Inner speech enables "inner social metacognition" - people interact with themselves dialogically to negotiate meaning, using the same cognitive structures as external social dialogue.

## The Urgent Open Question

Researchers are explicitly asking:

> "Has human inner speech changed with the use of interactive social media, and how will it change with new communicative systems made possible by LLMs?"

This is a question with profound implications:
- If inner speech is partly constituted by public language norms
- And LLMs are reshaping public language patterns (vocabulary colonization)
- Then LLMs may be reshaping the architecture of individual human thought

The relationship is not just "LLMs answer questions" - it is potentially "LLMs are modifying the cognitive scaffolding through which humans conduct metacognition."

---

## Individual Differences Have Consequences

If inner speech propensity varies significantly across individuals, and inner speech is the primary metacognitive infrastructure, then:
- People with lower inner speech propensity have less metacognitive access to their own reasoning
- They may be MORE susceptible to framing effects (less ability to monitor and revise their own thinking)
- They may rely MORE heavily on external scaffolding (AI, social validation, institutional narratives)

This connects inner speech research to broader questions about cognitive autonomy and AI-assisted reasoning.
