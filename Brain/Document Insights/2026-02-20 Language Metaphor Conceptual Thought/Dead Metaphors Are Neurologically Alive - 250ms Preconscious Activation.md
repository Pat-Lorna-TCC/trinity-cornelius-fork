# Dead Metaphors Are Neurologically Alive - 250ms Preconscious Activation

**Source**: Harris et al. (2024). EEG study on conventionalized metaphor neural activation. Cited in Wang & Sun (2024), "Research Front and Future Prospect: A Bibliometric Study of Metaphor Comprehension." SAGE Open.
**Document Type**: Research Finding (EEG empirical study)

## Core Insight

Conventionalized "dead" metaphors - expressions like "breakthrough" or "grasping an idea" - activate sensorimotor areas of the brain within **250 milliseconds** of exposure. This is below the threshold for conscious appraisal, meaning metaphorical cognition operates at a preconscious, automatic level. The "death" of a metaphor is a linguistic phenomenon, not a cognitive one.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: EEG measurement of neural activation in sensorimotor cortex during dead metaphor processing
**Timing**: 250ms window - below deliberate semantic processing threshold
**Key Result**: Sensorimotor areas activate automatically for conventionalized metaphors that are no longer felt as metaphorical
**Implication**: Embodied mappings (UNDERSTANDING IS GRASPING, THEORIES ARE BUILDINGS) remain neurologically encoded even after surface-level conventionalization

---

## Why This Is Surprising

The common assumption: dead metaphors are cognitively inert - linguistic fossils with no active processing role.

The finding: every dead metaphor is potentially a live cognitive frame operating below awareness. When someone says "grasping an idea," the motor cortex simulates grasping even though no one consciously experiences this as a metaphor.

This means:
- Language is saturated with invisible sensorimotor activations
- We are constantly "simulating" physical experiences we don't consciously register
- The body is embedded in language even in the most abstract discourse
