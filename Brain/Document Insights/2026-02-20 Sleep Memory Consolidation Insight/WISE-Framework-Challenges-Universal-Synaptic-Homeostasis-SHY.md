## Core Framework

The Synaptic Homeostasis Hypothesis (SHY, Tononi & Cirelli) has been the dominant model of sleep function for 20+ years: during waking, synapses strengthen via learning; during sleep, synapses globally downscale to baseline. The WISE framework (Wake Inhibition, Sleep Excitation) from PLOS Biology 2025 demonstrates that under standard Hebbian/STDP plasticity rules, the OPPOSITE occurs - wake-like firing DECREASES synaptic weights and sleep-like patterns INCREASE them.

**Field Acceptance**: SHY is widely accepted; WISE framework is emerging and challenging the consensus (2025)
**Alternative Theory Being Challenged**: SHY (Synaptic Homeostasis Hypothesis, Tononi & Cirelli)

---

## The WISE Framework

**Under Hebbian/STDP rules**:
- Wake-like firing patterns → DECREASE in synaptic weights (Wake Inhibition)
- Sleep-like firing patterns → INCREASE in synaptic weights (Sleep Excitation)
- This is OPPOSITE to what SHY predicts

**Under Anti-Hebbian/Anti-STDP rules**:
- Depression during NREM sleep - consistent with original SHY
- This is circuit-specific

**Heterogeneous Synaptic Homeostasis (PLOS Comp Bio 2025)**:
- Uniformly increasing synaptic strength does NOT replicate enhanced signal propagation seen in wakefulness
- Instead: selectively strengthening LONG-RANGE excitatory connections (linking distant regions) boosts signal spread more effectively
- Synaptic upscaling should FAVOR INTER- over INTRA-excitatory connections

---

## The Resolution: It Depends on Which Rule, Which Circuit, Which Stage

SHY was probably overgeneralized from specific circuits. The updated picture:
- Synaptic changes during sleep are NOT uniform but SPATIALLY HETEROGENEOUS
- Which direction (up or down) depends on: which plasticity rule is operating, which neural circuit, which NREM substate, and which memory type is being processed
- Long-range connections may be potentiated during sleep; local connections may be downscaled

**Additional finding (Science, 2024)**: Synaptic potentiation in PFC is BOTH sufficient and necessary for enhancing delta power activity during NREM sleep in mice - directly linking synapse strength to the sleep oscillation pattern, not just the reverse

---

## Why This Matters

1. **SHY's "sleep for forgetting capacity" narrative is incomplete**: If some synapses are potentiated during sleep, not just downscaled, then sleep is not simply "resetting the slate" but actively building selected connections while pruning others
2. **Circuit-specific sleep functions**: Different brain regions may do opposite things during the same NREM stage
3. **Memory selectivity mechanism**: The heterogeneous model provides a candidate mechanism for HOW sleep selects which memories to strengthen (long-range connections = cross-domain links) vs. weaken (redundant local connections)
