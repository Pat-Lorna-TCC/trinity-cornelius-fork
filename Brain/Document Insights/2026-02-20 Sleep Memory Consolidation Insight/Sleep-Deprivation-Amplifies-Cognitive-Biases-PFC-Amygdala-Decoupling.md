# Sleep Deprivation Amplifies Cognitive Biases Through PFC-Amygdala Decoupling

**Source**: "Examining the Effects of Sleep Deprivation on Decision-Making: A Scoping Review." *Behavioral Sciences* (MDPI), 15(6):823. 2025. https://www.mdpi.com/2076-328X/15/6/823

## Core Insight

Sleep deprivation systematically amplifies cognitive biases through a specific neurological mechanism: the prefrontal cortex (PFC) loses inhibitory control over the amygdala, creating "hyperlimbic reactivity." The sleep-deprived brain becomes more risk-seeking, more ambiguity-averse, and more emotionally reactive in its judgments. This is not just tiredness - it is a measurable neurological state that degrades the quality of every cognitive bias pattern relevant to decision-making.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Scoping review of 9+ quantitative studies using validated decision-making paradigms (balloon analogue risk task, gambling paradigms, moral judgment tasks)
**Quantified Findings**:
- **Risk-seeking bias**: Increased frequency of risky decisions confirmed in **9 separate studies**
- **Ambiguity aversion increase**: Impaired decision-making under uncertainty in **7 studies**
- **Moral judgment impairment**: Longer response latencies, difficulty integrating cognition and emotion
- **Amygdala hyperlimbic reaction**: Increased when mPFC functional connectivity is lost during sleep deprivation
- **Primary neurological mechanism**: Prefrontal-amygdala circuit disruption

---

## The Neurological Mechanism

The PFC normally provides inhibitory "top-down" control over amygdala reactivity. During sleep deprivation:
1. PFC metabolic activity decreases (insufficient slow-wave sleep impairs PFC restoration)
2. Inhibitory mPFC → amygdala connectivity is disrupted
3. Amygdala operates with reduced PFC oversight
4. Result: emotional reactivity is amplified, risk assessment is distorted, moral reasoning is impaired

**The circuit specifics**:
- Medial PFC (mPFC) is the primary inhibitory regulator of amygdala
- Sleep deprivation selectively impairs mPFC-amygdala coupling
- The amygdala itself may be HYPERACTIVE (not just mildly impaired) - creating a "hyperlimbic" state
