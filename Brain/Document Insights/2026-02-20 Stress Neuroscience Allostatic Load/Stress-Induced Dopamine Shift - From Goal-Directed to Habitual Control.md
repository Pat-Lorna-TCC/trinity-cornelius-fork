# Stress-Induced Dopamine Shift - From Goal-Directed to Habitual Control

## Core Insight

Stress does not merely impair cognition - it **structurally shifts which neural system controls behavior**. Stress enhances habitual responding (dorsolateral striatum, DLS) at the expense of goal-directed control (PFC + dorsomedial striatum). Dopamine in the DLS is now understood as a "strategy-dependent teaching signal" - it encodes stimulus-choice associations that anticipate future transitions between goal-directed and habitual control, not merely reward prediction errors.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design (Sci Rep 2024)**: Pavlovian-to-Instrumental Transfer (PIT) paradigm + Trier Social Stress Test (TSST)
**Context**: Gaming behavior population (individuals with risky gaming behavior)
**Key Results**:
- Stress enhances habitual responding, particularly in participants UNAWARE of stimulus-outcome associations
- Gaming-related reward cues triggered habitual responses **resistant to devaluation** (key experimental marker of habitual vs. goal-directed control)
- Stress + unawareness = strongest habitual bias

**Mechanistic Review (Nature 2025)**:
- Dopamine release in dorsolateral striatum (DLS) encodes **strategy-dependent stimulus-choice associations**
- DLS dopamine signals anticipate future transitions between goal-directed and habitual control
- This reframes dopamine as a "strategy arbiter" - not just a reward signal but a mechanism that decides WHICH system (PFC deliberative vs. striatum habitual) runs behavior
**Replication**: PIT paradigm is validated behavioral measure; multiple prior studies support stress-habit connection
**Citation**: *Scientific Reports* 2024; *Signal Transduction and Targeted Therapy* (Nature) 2025
