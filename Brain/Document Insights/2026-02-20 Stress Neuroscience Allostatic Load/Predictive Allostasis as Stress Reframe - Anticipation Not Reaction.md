## Core Framework

The most significant conceptual shift in stress neuroscience 2024-2025: **stress is no longer a reactive homeostatic system** responding to threats after they occur. It is a **predictive allostatic system** that constantly anticipates future physiological demands and adjusts behavior prospectively. Allostatic load - cumulative stress damage - is now understood as accumulated prediction errors when this anticipatory system chronically misfires.

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging consensus - two major 2024 reviews (*Neuron*, *Physiological Reviews*) adopt predictive framing; actively applied in clinical frameworks (PAIO model in *Biological Psychiatry*)

## The Conceptual Revolution

**Homeostatic model (old):**
- Stress = external threat
- Body responds reactively to stress after it occurs
- Allostatic load = cumulative wear-and-tear from repeated reactions
- Intervention: reduce stressors or manage response

**Predictive allostasis model (new):**
- Stress = prediction error (reality deviates from predicted future states)
- Body ANTICIPATES future demands and pre-adjusts physiological systems
- Allostatic load = accumulated prediction errors from chronically inaccurate anticipation
- Intervention: improve the accuracy of anticipatory models OR redesign niches to match predictive capacity (niche construction)

**The key insight:** What we experience as "stress" is not just the response to a threat - it is the firing of prediction errors. The suffering of stress is the experience of the world repeatedly not matching what we expected.

---

## The PAIO Framework (Predictive Allostatic Interoceptive Overload)

Santamaria-Garcia et al. (2025) propose that dysregulated allostasis + interoception underlie:
- Anxiety and depression (psychiatric)
- Alzheimer's disease and frontotemporal dementia (neurodegenerative)

**Key neural network**: Allostatic-Interoceptive Network (AIN):
- Anterior mid-cingulate cortex (aMCC)
- Pregenual ACC (pACC)
- Subgenual ACC (sgACC)
- Dorsal amygdala
- Agranular insula + dorsal mid-insula + dorsal posterior insula

**AIN function**: Both anticipatory allostatic regulation AND interoceptive processing - a transdiagnostic substrate

This transdiagnostic framework suggests a single mechanistic framework may explain cognitive/affective dysregulation across both psychiatric and neurodegenerative conditions through predictive failure.
