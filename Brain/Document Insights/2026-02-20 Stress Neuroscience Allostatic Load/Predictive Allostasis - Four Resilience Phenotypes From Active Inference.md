## Core Framework

Building on the Free Energy Principle, this framework proposes that resilience is determined by an organism's ability to use **predictive processing to actively engage with, adapt to, and proactively shape its environment** (its "enactive niche") to reduce future surprise. Stress is formalized as accumulated prediction errors; resilience is the capacity for active niche construction to minimize future prediction errors.

---

## The Four Resilience Phenotypes

| Phenotype | Description | Niche Behavior | Allostatic Pattern |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Fragile** | High allostatic load; "slave to its senses" | Minimal niche influence | Overwhelmed by environmental demands |
| **Durable** | Well-defined but narrow niche | Accurate predictions within restricted range | Allostatic strain when niche expands |
| **Resilient** | Broad, diverse niche | Wide prediction range; accommodates surprise | Actively shapes environment |
| **Pro-entropic** | All Resilient + proactive | Context-sensitive prediction even in novel niches | Allostatic growth from challenge |

## The Key Conceptual Shift

**Old view:** Resilience = ability to "bounce back" (passive recovery, trait-based)
**New view:** Resilience = capacity for "niche construction" (active prediction management, process-based)

**Niche construction** is the process by which organisms actively modify their environments to reduce future prediction errors:
- A Fragile person: accepts circumstances as given; reactions to world they cannot shape
- A Resilient person: actively arranges conditions to minimize future surprises (reducing social conflict, maintaining health routines, building financial buffers)
- A Pro-entropic person: proactively seeks novel niches and builds predictive capacity for them before entering

**This reframes "stress management" as "niche engineering"** - the goal is not managing stress after it arrives but constructing environments where stress prediction errors are minimized.
