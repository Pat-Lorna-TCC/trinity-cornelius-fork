## The Gap

While the vault has extensive coverage of cognitive biases (anchoring, availability heuristic, confirmation bias, noise) and stress neuroscience separately, there is no systematic account of **HOW specifically stress amplifies each known cognitive bias through PFC suppression mechanisms**. The 2024-2025 research provides the neural substrate (Ca-cAMP-K+ cascade, ~33% synapse loss) but does not directly map this onto specific decision biases.

---

## Why This Matters

The combined insight would be: **stress is a cognitive bias amplifier** - it does not create new biases but intensifies ALL existing biases simultaneously through a single mechanism (PFC suppression). This would be a high-value synthesis for the knowledge base because:

1. The vault has deep coverage of both domains (dopamine/decision biases + stress neuroscience is now added)
2. The connection would unify two major knowledge clusters
3. The practical implication is powerful: stress management is simultaneously a bias management strategy

---

## What We Know (Related Research)

**From stress neuroscience (this session):**
- Acute stress + time pressure: catastrophically impairs decision quality (Doroc 2025)
- Ca-cAMP-K+ cascade: weakens dlPFC layer III pyramidal circuits (Arnsten 2025)
- ~33% synaptic loss in mPFC under chronic stress (Algaidi 2025)
- Stress shifts dopamine from PFC goal-directed to DLS habitual control

**From decision science (existing vault):**
- Anchoring, availability heuristic, confirmation bias, planning fallacy, noise
- PFC deactivation under scarcity impairs decision-making (Scarcity note)
- All biases can be categorized as Lenses (useful), Biases (systematic errors), Tools (corrections)

**Missing: Direct mechanism linking**
- Which PFC circuits (layer III) correspond to which bias corrections?
- Does stress amplify ALL biases equally or preferentially certain ones?
- Does the DLS habitual shift specifically amplify "status quo bias" and "availability heuristic" (habitual patterns as available examples)?

---

## Hypothesis to Test

**Stress preferentially amplifies:**
- **Availability heuristic**: DLS habitual dopamine strengthens recently rehearsed patterns → most recently activated associations become overly available
- **Status quo bias**: Habitual system preserves existing choices → change feels disproportionately costly under stress
- **Confirmation bias**: DLS habitual responding reinforces identity-confirming S-R patterns → dopamine spike from confirming existing beliefs larger under stress
- **Anchoring**: Depleted PFC executive function cannot perform the effortful adjustment from anchors that normally corrects anchoring errors

---

## Research Needed

1. Study manipulating stress (TSST) while measuring multiple specific cognitive biases simultaneously
2. Measure: cortisol + gaze tracking + decision bias scores across anchoring, availability, confirmation bias tasks
3. Include neuroimaging: identify which PFC circuits are active during bias correction and how stress impairs them
4. Longitudinal: does chronic stress (allostatic load) create permanent bias amplification vs. acute reversible effects?
