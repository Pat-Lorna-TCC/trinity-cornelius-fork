# Stress Plus Time Pressure is the Catastrophic Decision-Making Combination

**Source**: Doroc, Yadav & Murawski (2025). "Acute stress impairs decision-making at varying levels of decision complexity." *Communications Psychology* (Nature Portfolio). https://www.nature.com/articles/s44271-025-00355-x
**Document Type**: Laboratory Experiment with Gaze Tracking
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-20
**Session**: 2026-02-20 Stress Neuroscience Allostatic Load

---

## Core Insight

Acute stress alone has a "surprisingly limited" effect on decision quality - it is the **combination of stress WITH time pressure** that catastrophically impairs decision-making. The mechanism appears to be attentional: gaze-tracking revealed scattered, unfocused attention under the combined stressor, not under stress alone.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Within-participants laboratory experiment; Trier Social Stress Test (TSST) paradigm
**Sample Size**: N=42; 72 decisions per participant
**Measurement**: Salivary cortisol + skin conductance + gaze-tracking
**Key Results**:
- Acute stress alone: "surprisingly limited" effect on decision quality regardless of computational complexity
- Stress + time pressure: significant deterioration in decision quality
- Cortisol responders: impaired decision quality even at low computational hardness
- Gaze-tracking: attention becomes more **scattered** under the combined condition
**Replication**: Single study; within-participants design strengthens internal validity; replication needed
**Citation**: Doroc, Yadav & Murawski, *Communications Psychology* (Nature), 2025

---

## Mechanism Proposed

**Attention as the mediator:**
1. Stress alone does not meaningfully disrupt attentional deployment
2. Time pressure alone creates urgency but not scattering
3. Stress + time pressure together causes attention to become diffuse - gaze "scattered" across irrelevant information
4. Scattered attention prevents proper evidence integration for decisions
5. This operates on top of (not instead of) the PFC suppression from cortisol

**Compounding factors:**
- Cortisol responders (those who show measurable cortisol increases to stress) are MORE vulnerable
- The finding is independent of decision cognitive complexity - even "easy" decisions impaired when both stressors present

---

## Practical Implications

**For high-stakes professional performance:**
- The key danger zone is NOT stress alone - it is stress under time pressure
- Boards, negotiations, investor calls, crisis decisions should be structured to eliminate artificial time pressure
- Buffer time between stressful events and critical decisions is a structural performance intervention
- "Sleep on it" advice has neurological backing: waiting for cortisol to clear before deciding

**For design of decision environments:**
- Real-time financial trading, emergency medicine, military decisions: the time pressure component is often avoidable with protocol design
- Time pressure in high-stress situations may be the single most preventable source of catastrophic decision failure
