# Chronic Stress Structurally Asymmetric - PFC Shrinks Amygdala Grows

## Core Insight

Chronic stress causes an estimated **one-third loss (~33%) of all axospinous synapses** on apical dendrites of pyramidal neurons in the medial prefrontal cortex. Simultaneously, amygdala dendrites **expand** under chronic stress. This anatomical asymmetry - PFC shrinks while amygdala grows - is the neural structural correlate of stress-driven irrationality and emotional reactivity.

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Review integrating structural, functional, and molecular evidence from development through aging in PFC; structural neuroimaging, cellular morphology studies
**Quantitative Key Finding**: ~33% of axospinous synapses on apical dendrites lost in mPFC after repeated stress exposure
**Region Specificity**:
- mPFC (medial): loses dendrites and spines
- Orbital PFC: relatively spared
- Amygdala: dendrites EXPAND under chronic stress
**Molecular cascade identified**:
1. BDNF-mTORC1 disruption
2. Calcium dysregulation
3. Actin dynamics disruption
4. Mitochondrial changes
5. Glial-mediated inflammatory spine removal
**Replication**: Review synthesizing multiple independent studies; high-evidence level
**Citation**: Algaidi 2025, *Brain Research*; Ren et al. 2025, *Molecular Neurobiology*

## The Irrationality Structure

**Normal brain:** PFC moderates amygdala responses - rational deliberation can override emotional reactivity

**Chronically stressed brain:**
- PFC loses 33% of synaptic connections - fewer computational resources for deliberation
- Amygdala gains dendritic mass - more sensitive, more reactive emotional processing
- Net effect: the structural ratio shifts toward reactivity over reflection

**This explains why chronic stress causes:**
- Difficulty overriding emotional responses with rational analysis
- Increased impulsivity and reactivity
- Reduced capacity for complex planning
- Default to habitual behaviors (striatum) over goal-directed ones (PFC)
