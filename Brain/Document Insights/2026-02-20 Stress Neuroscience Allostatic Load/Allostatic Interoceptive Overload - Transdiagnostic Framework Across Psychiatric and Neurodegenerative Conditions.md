## Core Framework

Dysregulated allostasis and interoception underlie a shared neural network abnormality across **anxiety, depression, Alzheimer's disease, and frontotemporal dementia** - disorders that are typically treated as entirely separate. The **Predictive Allostatic Interoceptive Overload (PAIO)** model proposes that failure of intrinsic neural timescales (INT) in the allostatic-interoceptive network is the common substrate of cognitive and affective dysregulation across these conditions.

## The Allostatic-Interoceptive Network (AIN)

**Key regions:**
| Region | Role |
|--------|------|
| Anterior mid-cingulate cortex (aMCC) | Anticipatory allostatic control |
| Pregenual ACC (pACC) | Affective allostatic regulation |
| Subgenual ACC (sgACC) | Depression-critical; amygdala-HPA interface |
| Dorsal amygdala | Threat prediction and emotional allostasis |
| Agranular insula | Primary interoceptive processing |
| Dorsal mid-insula | Interoceptive integration |
| Dorsal posterior insula | Raw interoceptive signal |

**AIN function**: Both anticipatory allostatic regulation AND interoceptive processing - these are the same fundamental activity

---

## Why These Four Conditions Share a Mechanism

**Anxiety**: AIN hyperactivation - over-predicting threat, generating excessive anticipatory allostatic responses
**Depression**: AIN dysregulation - failure to generate appropriate allostatic responses to aversive states; interoceptive inertia
**Alzheimer's disease**: AIN degradation - early AD pathology damages insula and ACC; interoceptive/allostatic processing fails before memory fails
**Frontotemporal dementia**: AIN atrophy - FTD directly targets anterior insula and ACC; first symptoms are social/emotional dysregulation (allostatic failure)

**The PAIO account**: All four disorders involve **intrinsic neural timescale (INT) failure** - the AIN normally operates at specific temporal scales that allow anticipatory regulation; in all four conditions, INT becomes dysregulated, preventing adaptive allostatic prediction
