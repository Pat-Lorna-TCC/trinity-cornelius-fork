## Core Framework

Trust betrayal triggers the same neural threat-detection systems evolved for physical danger. The critical pivot point is **intent detection**: the TPJ-IFG system distinguishes whether harm was accidental or deliberate, and this distinction determines whether the full amygdala threat response is regulated or unleashed. Trust repair requires active engagement of higher-order social cognition to override amygdala-driven threat responses - which is why forgiveness is effortful and some betrayals leave lasting neural traces.

---

## The Betrayal Neural Cascade

### Stage 1: Betrayal Detection
- The amygdala fires a "betrayal alarm" - triggering threat response even when danger is social rather than physical
- The ancient threat-detection system cannot distinguish social harm from physical harm at this stage

### Stage 2: HPA Activation
- Cortisol release follows amygdala activation
- **Chronic** betrayal/distrust: alters both hippocampal volume and amygdala reactivity over time
- The prefrontal cortex - responsible for regulation and reasoning - effectively "goes offline" during acute betrayal
- Default mode network shifts toward rumination and negative self-referential processing via medial PFC

### Stage 3: Intent Detection - The Critical Pivot
**TPJ-IFG System distinguishes accidental from deliberate harm**:
- **Accidental harm**: Activates right TPJ and IFG; **IFG sends regulatory signals to the amygdala to dampen emotional response**
- **Intentional betrayal**: Bypasses this regulation, allowing FULL amygdala threat response
- The vmPFC integrates intention and harm information into a unified value signal driving punishment decisions

**This is why "it was an accident" actually works neurologically**: if believed, it engages IFG regulation of amygdala

### Stage 4: Forgiveness and Repair
- Forgiveness reduces amygdala activity associated with negative emotions
- Forgiving thoughts increase ventral striatum activation (positive emotion/reward)
- TPJ's role in empathy facilitates forgiveness - activating perspective-taking networks makes forgiveness neurologically easier
- Three distinct recovery pathways for **deliberative, instinctual, and procedural trust** - requiring different repair strategies for different trust layers (2025 arXiv computational model)

## The Three Trust Layers and Their Repair Paths

Based on the 2025 arXiv computational model:

| Trust Layer | Description | Repair Strategy |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Deliberative | Reflective, slow, reasoned | Explanation, evidence, narrative |
| Instinctual | Fast, emotional, automatic | Exposure, gradual re-engagement, emotional processing |
| Procedural | Habit-based, automatic | Repeated positive interactions, new behavioral patterns |

## Key Insight for Organizations and Relationships

**Trust repair is not "forgetting" betrayal** - it requires active engagement of TPJ/mPFC to override amygdala-driven threat responses. This explains:
- Why cognitive reassurance ("it was a mistake") is necessary but insufficient - the body maintains the threat state
- Why forgiveness is effortful - it requires sustained higher-order cognitive engagement
- Why some betrayals leave lasting neural traces - chronic cortisol alters hippocampal and amygdala structure
- Why trust rebuilds slowly but breaks fast - the negative prediction error from betrayal is processed with high salience
