## Core Insight

The 2025 World Economic Forum Global Risks Report identifies misinformation/disinformation as a top short-term risk for the second consecutive year, and societal polarization as a leading long-term risk. Research establishes a **direct causal link between perceived polarization and social trust erosion** - not just correlation. The feedback loop is self-reinforcing: misinformation spreads → institutions appear untrustworthy → alternative (often misinformation) sources gain credibility → trust in legitimate institutions further erodes. Social media algorithms accelerate this loop through engagement optimization.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: WEF Global Risks Report 2025 (policy synthesis) + causal research on polarization-trust link

**Key Results**:
- Misinformation/disinformation: top short-term global risk for 2nd consecutive year (WEF 2025)
- Societal polarization: leading long-term global risk (WEF 2025)
- **Causal finding**: Perceived polarization directly CAUSES social trust erosion (not merely correlated)
- Perceived polarization discourages cooperation in public goods situations
- Affective polarization (hostility toward opposing groups) reduces interpersonal trust across partisan lines
- Social media shapes institutional trust along partisan lines - creating polarized trust patterns

---

## The Self-Reinforcing Loop

```
Misinformation spreads
        ↓
Institutions appear untrustworthy/contradictory
        ↓
Alternative (misinformation) sources gain relative credibility
        ↓
Social media algorithms amplify high-engagement (often polarizing) content
        ↓
Echo chambers form; affective polarization increases
        ↓
Perceived polarization increases → social trust erodes causally
        ↓
Public goods cooperation decreases
        ↓
Institutional performance degrades (underfunding, non-compliance, political interference)
        ↓
Institutions become MORE untrustworthy → loop accelerates
```

## Political Science Context (Putnam/Fukuyama)

Social capital (including trust) is essential for well-functioning democracy:
- Putnam's research: Social trust enables civic participation, institutional function, and collective action
- Fukuyama's analysis: Trust reduces transaction costs in economic and political systems
- The erosion of social trust creates conditions for autocratic tendencies - populations that don't trust institutions become vulnerable to strongmen who promise simple solutions

**Current trajectory**: The WEF identifies this as a 10-year horizon risk - suggesting the consequences are still accumulating

---

## Proposed Countermeasures (2025)

1. **Alternative recommendation algorithms** designed for democratic discourse rather than engagement
2. **Fact-based journalism** standards and support
3. **Algorithmic transparency** requirements
4. **Media literacy education** at scale
5. Researchers: "Designing social media for societal good" (PMC 2025) - provides specific algorithmic design alternatives
