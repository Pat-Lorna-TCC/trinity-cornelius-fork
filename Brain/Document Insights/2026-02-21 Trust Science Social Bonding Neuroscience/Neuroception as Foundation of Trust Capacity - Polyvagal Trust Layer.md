## Core Framework

Polyvagal Theory provides a neurophysiological foundation layer *beneath* the trust circuits studied in social neuroscience: before the amygdala can evaluate trustworthiness, before the TPJ can mentalize about intentions, the **autonomic nervous system must first classify the environment as safe enough for social engagement**. This neuroceptive safety assessment - operating via the ventral vagal complex (VVC) - is the biological prerequisite for trust formation. Trauma dysregulates this baseline, creating persistent distrust even in consciously safe environments.

---

## The Polyvagal Trust Foundation

### Layer 0: Neuroceptive Safety Assessment (Below Trust Circuits)

Before any of the trust neural circuits can activate, neuroception determines:
- **VVC active (safe)**: Social engagement system online → trust circuits can function → trust formation is possible
- **Sympathetic dominant (danger)**: Fight/flight → trust circuits suppressed → trust formation is blocked regardless of conscious intent
- **Dorsal vagal (life-threat)**: Freeze/shutdown → complete social disengagement → trust circuits completely unavailable

**This operates faster than the amygdala's 100ms trustworthiness response** - the ANS state is determined by whole-body sensory integration, not just facial cues.

### What Neuroception Assesses for Trust Safety
- **Vocal prosody**: Melodic, variable-pitch voice = safety signal (VVC activates); monotone or harsh = danger
- **Facial expression**: Engaged, animated face with raised eyebrows = safety
- **Heart rate rhythms**: RSA synchrony between interaction partners = safety
- **Visceral signals**: Internal body state contributes to "safety confidence"

### The NPSS - First Measurement Tool (2025)
The Neuroception of Psychological Safety Scale validated in 2025:
- 3-factor structure: Social Engagement, Compassion, Bodily Sensations
- Enables clinical measurement of neuroceptive safety capacity
- Emerging as tool for assessing trauma dysregulation and treatment progress

## Trauma and Dysregulated Trust Capacity

**The critical implication**: Trauma history can create **chronically dysregulated neuroception** - the ANS remains in heightened alert even in objectively safe environments.

This explains why:
- Trauma survivors struggle to form trust even when they consciously want to - neuroception overrides deliberate social cognition
- "Telling someone to trust you" fails in trauma contexts - cognitive reassurance cannot override subcortical autonomic state
- Trust-building interventions in trauma populations must address the ANS FIRST
- Some people experience "trust freeze" - even when intellectually they recognize trustworthiness, their body cannot enter the state that allows trust to form

**Autonomic nervous system regulation is not optional for trust formation** - it is the prerequisite platform.

---

## The VVC as Social Engagement Gateway

The Ventral Vagal Complex coordinates:
- Facial expression (affect display)
- Vocal prosody (safety signaling to others)
- Cardiac regulation (RSA synchrony for co-regulation)
- Auditory sensitivity (middle ear tuning to human voice frequencies)

When the VVC is active, it simultaneously:
1. Signals safety to others (enabling THEM to form trust with you)
2. Enables YOUR trust formation (creating the physiological platform)

This is why genuine trust is bidirectional and physiological, not just cognitive.
