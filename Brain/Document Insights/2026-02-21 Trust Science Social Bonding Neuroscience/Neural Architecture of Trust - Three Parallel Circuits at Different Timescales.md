## Core Framework

Trust is a distributed computation across three parallel neural circuits operating at different timescales - not a single process. Understanding which circuit is active determines which interventions work, how trust fails, and why repair requires specific approaches.

---

## The Three-Circuit Architecture

### Circuit 1: Rapid Appraisal Layer (100-200ms) - Amygdala-Driven

**Function**: Automatic, pre-conscious trustworthiness assessment
**Speed**: ~100ms - before conscious processing
**Key Properties**:
- Amygdala automatically encodes facial trustworthiness within ~100ms of exposure
- Response tracks *consensus* trustworthiness ratings, not individual judgments - suggesting automatic social categorization
- **Hemispheric asymmetry**: Left amygdala shows quadratic response (strongest to faces at both extremes of trustworthiness); right amygdala shows linear response (inversely proportional to trustworthiness)
- Evaluation appears **mandatory** - occurs regardless of task instructions
- 2025 *Frontiers in Psychology* study: hemispheric asymmetry in centroparietal EEG phase activity after ~800ms in dynamic (not static image) trust judgments

**Vulnerability**: Exploitable by appearance manipulation; AI faces can trigger this circuit

### Circuit 2: Mentalizing Layer - TPJ and mPFC Network

**Function**: Deliberate inference about intentions
**Speed**: Seconds to minutes
**Key Properties**:
- Temporoparietal junction (TPJ) = central hub for mentalizing about others' intentions
- Right TPJ activates selectively for HUMAN (vs. artificial) agents; left TPJ handles second-order inferences in strategic interaction
- **IFG-TPJ connectivity** elevated during strategic social decisions
- Anterior insula-angular gyrus connectivity modulated by trait reward sensitivity (2024 *Biological Psychology*)
- TPJ engagement **decreases** as partner behavior becomes predictable - mentalizing most active when intentional uncertainty is high
- The IFG sends regulatory signals to the amygdala to dampen emotional response when harm is accidental

**Vulnerability**: Cognitive load impairs this circuit, reverting to amygdala-based assessment

### Circuit 3: Reputation Updating Layer - vmPFC-pTPJ and Caudate

**Function**: Slow reputation learning and belief updating about partner reliability
**Speed**: Minutes to days/months
**Key Properties**:
- vmPFC-pTPJ circuit integrates honesty signals into trustworthiness beliefs
- Enhanced vmPFC-pTPJ connectivity associated with higher trust and better reputation-based updating
- Dorsal striatum (caudate nucleus) activates consistently during feedback stages of trust games - reflecting belief updating
- **Unconditional trust** selectively activates the **septal area** (social attachment circuits)
- **Conditional/calculated trust** activates the **ventral tegmental area** (reward evaluation)
- ACC monitors for trust violations and social norm deviations

**Vulnerability**: Requires time and repeated interactions to build; single strong betrayal can override accumulated positive updating

## Why This Framework Matters

This multi-circuit architecture explains seemingly paradoxical trust observations:
1. **Why gut reactions and reasoned judgments conflict** - Circuit 1 and Circuit 2 operate independently and can reach different conclusions
2. **Why "smart" people are still exploited** - Circuit 1 bypasses deliberate reasoning
3. **Why trust repair is slow** - Circuit 3 requires repeated experiences to update reputation priors
4. **Why trust breaks fast** - A single betrayal creates strong negative prediction error in the caudate, overriding accumulated positive signal
5. **Why AI faces can generate trust** - Circuit 1 responds to facial features regardless of the substrate being biological
