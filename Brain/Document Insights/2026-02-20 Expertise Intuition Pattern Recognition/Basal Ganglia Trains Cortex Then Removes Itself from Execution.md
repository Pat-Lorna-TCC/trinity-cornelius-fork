# Basal Ganglia Trains Cortex Then Removes Itself from Execution

## Core Insight

The basal ganglia's primary function in expertise development is not to run automatic behavior forever - it is to **train the purely cortical representations that will eventually run the behavior without subcortical involvement**. The basal ganglia teaches the cortex, then steps aside. This explains why experts cannot articulate their own skilled movements: the knowledge is no longer accessible through the conscious systems.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Neurobiological computational model + review of automaticity research
**Key Finding**: Ashby, Ennis & Spiering's model shows that expertise transitions from subcortical-dependent to cortical-only execution
**2025 Extension**: Soni & Frank (*eLife*, 2025) demonstrate adaptive chunking in the prefrontal cortex-basal ganglia circuit is learned through reinforcement and adapts to task demands. Working memory limits in expertise domains reflect **learning constraints, not anatomical limits**.
**Replication**: Consistent with procedural memory research across motor learning, habit formation, and perceptual categorization

---

## The Four-Stage Mechanism

1. **Early learning**: Explicit declarative knowledge in prefrontal-hippocampal circuits (conscious, effortful)
2. **Repeated practice**: Dopamine-mediated reinforcement learning at cortical-striatal synapses
3. **Automaticity**: Control gradually transfers from striatum to direct cortical-cortical projections (sensory to premotor)
4. **Expert state**: Behavior executes through cortical-cortical projections **without basal ganglia involvement**

---

## Dopamine's Dual Role in Expertise

- **During training**: Dopamine bursts/dips signal prediction errors that drive learning at striatal synapses
- **Optimal state**: A "healthy balance of relatively symmetrical DA bursts and dips" is needed for optimal chunking and working memory performance
- **Disruption mechanism**: This explains why extreme reward pressure or stress disrupts otherwise automatic expert performance - it alters dopamine signaling at the critical striatal level that shouldn't need to be engaged anymore

---

## Counter-Intuitive Implications

1. **Expertise-induced amnesia is a feature, not a bug**: Experts cannot articulate their skills because the knowledge has been offloaded from conscious (PFC-hippocampal) to purely cortical systems
2. **Working memory limits are learned, not fixed**: WM limitations in expertise reflect learning constraints - meaning they can in principle be pushed through appropriately designed training
3. **Asking experts to explain themselves disrupts their performance**: Forcing articulable declarative access reactivates the slower, effortful system
