## Core Framework

Choking under pressure is not a concentration failure - it is the expert's prefrontal cortex interfering with movements that have been automatized. Pressure causes experts to revert from implicit procedural memory (fast, fluent, unconscious) to explicit declarative control (slow, step-by-step, conscious) - disrupting the automaticity that is the definition of expertise. Novices don't choke the same way because they haven't yet automatized the skill.

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Widely accepted - robust experimental and neuroimaging evidence across motor skills (golf, cricket, soccer, music)
**Supporting Evidence**: Functional connectivity studies, behavioral experiments, reinvestment theory validation across cultures and domains
**2023 Extension**: Predictive processing framework unifies previously competing accounts

---

## Two Competing Theories (Both Partially Right)

- **Distraction theory**: Pressure directs resources away from the task toward threat-monitoring
- **Explicit monitoring theory** (Reinvestment): Pressure causes experts to "reinvest" step-by-step declarative knowledge into automated processes, disrupting fluency

**2023 Unification**: A new paper integrates both accounts under **predictive processing theory**: anxiety increases the weight given to proprioceptive prediction errors, disrupting normally smooth motor execution. The PFC doesn't fail - it becomes too active, treating automatic movements as if they need conscious supervision.

---

## Neural Correlate

**Functional connectivity between prefrontal and motor cortex is highest right before choking** - the overactivated executive system interfering with automatized performance. This is the neural signature of choking: not under-activation but over-activation of the wrong system.

---

## Reinvestment Trait: Choking Is Partly Predictable

Individuals who rate high in "self-conscious regulation of movement" choke more often. This trait (measurable before performance) predicts choking vulnerability, making it:
- Identifiable in advance
- Targetable with specific interventions
- A selection factor for high-stakes roles

---

## Validated Countermeasures

1. **Distraction tasks during performance**: Parallel cognitive load prevents monitoring (counting backward while performing)
2. **Pre-performance routines that anchor attention externally**: Sensory focus (feel of hands, sound of breath) rather than movement analysis
3. **Implicit learning protocols during training** (Masters' errorless learning): Build automatic responses without strong declarative encoding - the skill encodes without verbal/conscious representation that pressure can trigger
4. **Identify high reinvestment tendency early**: Screen for this trait and target implicit learning strategies specifically for these individuals

---

## The Paradox

Choking is a product of success: you can only choke on an automatized skill. Novices don't choke because they have no automaticity to disrupt. The more thoroughly you have mastered a skill, the more vulnerable you are to this specific failure mode under pressure.

This connects to the broader expertise paradox: the same mechanisms that enable expert fluency (automaticity, reduced conscious processing) generate the most dangerous failure modes (choking, Einstellung, entrenchment).
