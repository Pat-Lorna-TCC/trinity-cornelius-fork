# Human Plus AI Often Worse Than AI Alone - Vaccaro 2024

**Source**: Vaccaro, M., Almaatouq, A., & Malone, T. (2024). When combinations of humans and AI are useful: A systematic review and meta-analysis. *Nature Human Behaviour*. https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39468277/

## Core Insight

The dominant assumption in AI deployment - that human + AI collaboration produces better outcomes than either alone - is empirically wrong as a general claim. On average, human-AI combinations perform significantly **worse** than the best of humans or AI operating alone. The "human in the loop" doctrine requires domain-specific validation, not default assumption.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Systematic review and meta-analysis of human-AI collaboration research
**Sample Size**: Large-scale meta-analysis (Nature Human Behaviour)
**Key Result**: Human-AI combinations performed significantly worse than best of humans or AI alone
- **Effect Size**: Hedges' g = -0.23 (95% CI: -0.39 to -0.07)
- The negative effect is statistically significant and not trivial
**Replication**: Consistent with radiology literature showing AI assistance improves some clinicians but worsens others

---

## When Combination Helps vs. Hurts

**Performance Gains** (Human + AI beats both alone):
- When humans already outperform AI
- Content creation tasks
- Tasks requiring contextual integration of unstated factors

**Performance Losses** (Human + AI worse than best alone):
- Decision and classification tasks
- When AI outperforms humans alone
- Less experienced clinicians using AI (radiology finding)

**Radiology Warning** (*Nature Medicine*, 2024): AI assistance improved some radiologists but worsened others. Less experienced clinicians did not reliably benefit - poor AI calibration led them to trust false positives.

---

## Why Combination Fails

1. **Automation bias**: Humans over-weight AI recommendations even when AI is wrong
2. **Anchor effects**: AI output anchors human judgment, reducing independent thinking
3. **Calibration asymmetry**: Humans cannot reliably assess when AI is wrong vs. right
4. **Reduced cognitive engagement**: Presence of AI output reduces depth of independent analysis
5. **False confidence**: Human + AI feels more reliable than either alone, leading to less verification

---

## Where Humans Retain Genuine Advantages

- Contextual and holistic assessment integrating unstated environmental factors
- Empathy and complex situational ethics
- Detecting when a problem doesn't match known patterns (meta-recognition)
- Navigating genuinely novel situations outside any AI training distribution

**The Complementarity Principle** (Malone, 2024): "Combinations of humans and AI work best when each party can do the thing they do better than the other." But this requires knowing where the boundaries lie - which is itself an expertise problem.
