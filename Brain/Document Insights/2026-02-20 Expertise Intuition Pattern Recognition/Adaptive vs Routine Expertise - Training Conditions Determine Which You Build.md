## Core Framework

Adaptive vs. routine expertise is not a fixed trait of the learner - it is largely **produced by training conditions**. Organizations and educational systems that optimize for efficiency, accuracy, and speed produce routine experts who fail catastrophically when conditions change. The conditions that produce mastery of existing procedures actively undermine capacity to handle novel ones.

## The Core Distinction

| Routine Expertise | Adaptive Expertise |
|---|---|
| High-speed, high-accuracy, automated | All of routine expertise PLUS |
| Executes procedures brilliantly | Can modify when conditions change |
| Knows *how* | Knows *why* and *when* |
| Optimized for stable conditions | Handles genuine novelty |
| Produced by efficiency-optimized training | Requires variable context, reduced pressure |

**The sushi chef example**: Any qualified chef executes standard recipes accurately (routine). Only adaptive experts can invent dishes or improvise with unexpected ingredient shortages.

---

## Three Conditions That Develop Adaptive (Not Routine) Expertise

1. **Random/variable context** - forces adaptation rather than stereotyped response
2. **Reduced performance pressure** - rewards experimentation over error-avoidance
3. **Quality over efficiency culture** - values understanding over speed

---

## The Institutional Trap

Most education and corporate training **explicitly optimizes for routine expertise** while accidentally preventing adaptive expertise development:
- Curriculum eliminates ambiguity (prevents developing tolerance for uncertainty)
- Performance metrics reward speed and accuracy (punishes experimentation)
- Standard procedures are taught without the "why" behind them (builds fragile knowledge)
- Examinations reward recall of correct answers (not generative understanding)

**Medical residency finding** (*PMC*, 2024): Adaptive expertise requires tolerance for uncertainty. Most medical training inadvertently produces routine experts by eliminating ambiguity from training scenarios - exactly backwards from what develops doctors who can handle novel presentations.

**Schwartz, 2024** (*Educational Psychology Review*): Traditional instruction succeeds at routine tasks but consistently fails to develop adaptive expertise.

---

## The Critical Gap in Practice

This distinction has profound practical implications for:
- **AI-era skill development**: AI will increasingly handle routine expertise; adaptive expertise is the human differentiator
- **Corporate training programs**: ROI should be measured on adaptive not just routine performance
- **Medical education**: Novel disease presentations, rare conditions, cross-domain cases require adaptive expertise
- **Leadership development**: Strategic thinking under uncertainty is adaptive expertise, not routine

---

## 2024 Extension: Distributed Adaptive Expertise

"Reenvisioning adaptive expertise as a distributed phenomenon" extends Hatano-Inagaki to **team and organizational contexts**. Organizations, not just individuals, can develop adaptive vs. routine expertise - and the conditions are similar: variable inputs, psychological safety, intrinsic motivation over compliance.
