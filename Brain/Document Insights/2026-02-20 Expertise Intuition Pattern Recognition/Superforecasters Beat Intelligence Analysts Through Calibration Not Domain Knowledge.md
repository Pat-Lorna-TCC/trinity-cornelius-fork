## Core Insight

Superforecasters - recruited from the general public through a forecasting tournament - outperformed US intelligence analysts with access to classified information by **30%** on geopolitical prediction accuracy. The advantage came not from domain knowledge or information access but from specific cognitive habits: calibrated uncertainty, active open-mindedness, reference-class forecasting, and rapid feedback processing. This is the most direct demonstration that **metacognitive process matters more than domain expertise** in wicked environments.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Longitudinal forecasting tournament (20+ years, Philip Tetlock & Barbara Mellers, Wharton)
**Key Results**:
- Superforecasters: 30% more accurate than intelligence analysts with classified access
- Expert political forecasters (general): No better than random chance (Tetlock's earlier 20-year study)
- Superforecaster year-over-year consistency: 70% maintain status; r = 0.65 (genuine stable trait, not luck)
- Superforecasters vs. prediction markets: Consistently competitive with or superior to market prices

**What predicts superforecaster status**:
1. Actively open-minded thinking style
2. Probabilistic reasoning (comfort with uncertainty quantification)
3. Reference-class forecasting training (seeking base rates before case-specific analysis)
4. Rapid feedback incorporation (updating quickly on new evidence)
5. Team debate before committing to predictions (improves calibration)

---

## The Good Judgment Project Training Interventions

Most effective interventions:
- **Reference-class forecasting training**: Showed strongest correlation with accuracy - start from the base rate (how often does this type of event occur?) before considering specific case details
- **Rapid feedback loops**: Essential - forecasters improve when they get scored feedback quickly
- **Team debate**: Committing to predictions after structured team disagreement improves calibration
- **Tracking performance with explicit accuracy metrics**: What gets measured gets managed

---

## Why This Matters for Expertise Theory

This finding is critical for understanding when to trust whom:

1. **Wicked environments + domain expertise = confident error** (the expert political forecaster baseline)
2. **Wicked environments + calibrated uncertainty = significantly better performance** (superforecasters)
3. **The advantage is teachable**: Training interventions produce measurable improvement

The implication: In domains with delayed/inaccurate feedback (politics, strategy, markets), **how you reason** matters more than **what you know**. Domain knowledge without calibration metacognition is not just useless - it creates overconfidence that makes performance worse than baseline.
