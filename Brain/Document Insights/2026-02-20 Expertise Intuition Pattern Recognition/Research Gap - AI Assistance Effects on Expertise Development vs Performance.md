# Research Gap: AI Assistance Effects on Expertise Development vs Performance

## The Gap

Current research (Vaccaro et al., 2024) measures how AI assistance affects **current performance** - does human + AI perform better or worse right now? What is almost entirely unstudied is how AI assistance affects **expertise development over time** - does human + AI assistance during the learning phase produce better or worse expertise 2 years later?

The question the literature cannot yet answer: **Should beginners use AI assistance while learning a skill, or does it prevent the pattern library development that produces expert intuition?**

---

## Why This Matters

The stakes are enormous:
- **Medical education**: Residents using AI for diagnosis may improve immediate performance but prevent development of the neural chunking and DMN pattern libraries that constitute expert intuition
- **Legal training**: AI-assisted case analysis may accelerate immediate outputs but prevent development of the tacit pattern recognition that makes expert lawyers
- **Engineering**: AI-assisted design tools may produce better immediate designs but fewer engineers capable of novel design innovation

This is the developmental version of the Doctors Less Adept at Colonoscopies After Just 3 Months Using AI Tool finding - that note shows skill *decay* in experienced practitioners; the gap here is about skill *acquisition* in learners.

---

## What We Know (Related Research)

- **AI assistance degrades current performance in many contexts** (Vaccaro et al., 2024, Nature Human Behaviour)
- **AI assistance produces skill decay in experienced practitioners** after 3 months (colonoscopy study)
- **Expertise requires dopamine-mediated reinforcement** at corticostriatal synapses - this learning requires prediction errors, not smooth AI-assisted performance
- **Kind environments** produce genuine expertise through accurate feedback loops - AI assistance may contaminate feedback by removing prediction errors
- **The 10,000-hour mythology** is already wrong; the question is what the right practice conditions are; AI changes those conditions fundamentally

---

## Missing Research

**Critical Studies Needed**:
1. Longitudinal RCT: Medical residents randomized to AI-assisted vs. independent diagnosis training; measure expert performance at 5 and 10 years
2. Neural imaging: Compare chunking and DMN pattern completion in AI-trained vs. independently-trained practitioners
3. Transfer testing: Measure performance on novel cases (adaptive expertise) vs. familiar cases (routine expertise) separately by training condition
4. Dose-response: Is there a threshold of AI assistance below which expertise develops normally?

**Falsification Criteria**:
- If AI-assisted learners develop equivalent or superior expert intuition on novel cases, the concern is unfounded
- If AI-assisted learners show equivalent routine performance but worse adaptive performance, the concern is partially validated

---

## Synthesis Opportunity

This gap connects three independently powerful frameworks:
1. **Expertise neuroscience** (dopamine-mediated chunking requires prediction errors)
2. **Kind/wicked environments** (AI assistance may convert kind environments to wicked by removing valid feedback)
3. **AI deskilling research** (established in current practitioners, unstudied in learners)

The synthesis prediction: AI assistance during expertise development will produce practitioners with strong routine expertise but severely impaired adaptive expertise, particularly for novel cases.
