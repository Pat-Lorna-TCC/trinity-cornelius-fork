## Core Framework

The Default Mode Network (DMN) - long dismissed as mere "resting state" activity - is now understood as the brain's **predictive engine**, and is likely the neural substrate of expert intuition. Intuition is not a mysterious shortcut around reasoning; it is a distinct cognitive system operating through DMN-hippocampal pattern completion, parallel to rather than in conflict with prefrontal analytical reasoning.

---

## Context & Acceptance

**Field Acceptance**: Emerging consensus - reinterpretation of DMN function is now mainstream in cognitive neuroscience but the specific claim about intuition-as-DMN remains active research
**Supporting Evidence**: 20+ years of neuroimaging, 2025 systematic reviews, computational models
**Alternative Theories**: DMN still debated as to specific functions; some argue it is primarily about self-referential processing rather than prediction

## Mechanism

**The DMN as autopilot**: Research proposes the DMN contributes to an "autopilot mode" that makes memory-based predictions under established behavioral contexts, while control networks handle a "manual mode" for novel situations.

**Pattern completion and prediction**: DMN nodes (medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate cortex) show predictive coupling during narrative comprehension - they anticipate incoming information based on stored patterns. The strength of this predictive coupling correlates with comprehension performance.

**Feedforward gist signaling**: 2025 preprint directly examines the DMN's role as a predictive processing system operating through feedforward gist signaling, consistent with the view that intuition is rapid pattern-completion mediated by DMN-hippocampal circuits.

---

## Expertise and DMN-Mediated Flow

**The critical finding**: During creative performance, higher flow states were accompanied by reduced frontal activity among experts - suggesting expertise enables a **shift toward more automatic DMN-mediated processing** rather than effortful executive processing.

**Network flexibility as mastery marker**: Expert performers show enhanced network flexibility in connector hubs between different functional networks - the ability to rapidly reconfigure connections based on task demands.

**This resolves the expertise-flow paradox**: Expertise is not "doing the same thing faster with PFC" - it is restructuring which neural system carries the computation. Masters shift processing from prefrontal (slow, conscious, effortful) to DMN-hippocampal (fast, pattern-based, unconscious).

---

## Critical Distinction from Existing DMN Notes

The existing vault note on Default Mode Network (DMN) focuses on DMN's role in flow (deactivation during engagement). This insight is different: the DMN is *active* during expert intuition, serving as a predictive engine - it deactivates from self-referential narrative but activates in predictive mode. Flow involves a mode-switch within DMN function, not simple suppression.
