## Core Framework

Aristotle's distinction between three knowledge types has now been validated and extended by cognitive science. *Phronesis* (practical wisdom) - the capacity to make context-sensitive ethical judgments in irreducibly particular situations - is fundamentally different from both theoretical knowledge (*episteme*) and technical skill (*techné*). Crucially: phronesis cannot be reduced to rules, algorithms, or AI systems. It requires genuine moral responsibility, reflective practice, and embodied experience in situations with real consequences.

## Aristotle's Three Knowledge Types (Now Cognitively Grounded)

| Type | Description | Relationship to AI |
|---|---|---|
| *Episteme* | Theoretical knowledge - universal, codifiable, transferable | AI can store and retrieve |
| *Techné* | Craft knowledge - domain-specific skill, learned through practice | AI can increasingly match or exceed |
| *Phronesis* | Practical wisdom - context-sensitive ethical judgment | Cannot be codified; AI may erode conditions for its development |

---

## The Aretai Model (2024)

Phronesis as the "conductor of the virtue orchestra" - it integrates and adjudicates between competing moral, civic, and performance considerations in real situations where general principles conflict. Unlike domain expertise (*techné*), phronesis:
- Cannot be replaced by rules (rules conflict and require judgment about which applies)
- Cannot be replaced by algorithms (algorithms require the ethical framework to already be specified)
- Cannot be transferred through instruction (it requires genuine experience with real consequences)
- Develops through reflective practice in moral community, not through information acquisition

---

## The AI Concern (Tsai & Ku, 2025)

**Critical finding**: AI systems may not just fail to replicate practical wisdom - they may **actively erode the conditions** that allow it to develop in human practitioners.

**Mechanism**:
- Phronesis develops through genuine responsibility, uncertainty, and consequence
- AI assistance reduces the experience of genuine responsibility (someone else could have decided)
- AI reduces the uncertainty that forces practitioners to develop judgment under ambiguity
- AI-mediated decisions may prevent the moral community formation that supports phronesis development

**The Medical Example**: A resident who uses AI to guide every diagnostic decision may develop technical proficiency (*techné*) while never developing the judgment of when to deviate from protocol, how to handle irreducibly particular cases, or how to weigh competing ethical obligations (*phronesis*).

---

## Teaching and Medicine Research

Both fields show consistent gap: technical expertise (*techné*) - knowing content, following protocols - is trainable. Practical wisdom - knowing when to deviate, how to adapt general principles to particular situations - requires fundamentally different developmental conditions:
- Genuine responsibility (not simulated)
- Real moral community (accountability relationships)
- Reflective practice (not just repeated action)
- Tolerance for ambiguity (not elimination of uncertainty)
