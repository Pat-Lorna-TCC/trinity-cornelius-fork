# Oxytocin Requires Intact Dopamine for Trust-Based Learning

## Core Insight

Oxytocin enhances trust-based learning and reciprocal cooperation **only when dopamine signaling is intact**. This reveals a fundamental interdependency - oxytocin does not independently promote social behavior but rather modulates dopamine-based reward prediction error learning for social trust.

**Critical Finding**: When dopamine signaling was disrupted, oxytocin's trust-enhancing effects disappeared entirely.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Neurobiological research in mice with pharmacological manipulations
**Key Results**:
- Brain regions receiving dopaminergic projections (vmPFC and ventral striatum) showed increased activation to reciprocated altruism
- Same regions showed decreased activation to unreciprocated altruism
- Oxytocin effects on trust learning eliminated when dopamine system blocked
**Replication**: Builds on established oxytocin-dopamine convergence research
**Citation**: Scientific Reports (2026) - "Interdependency between oxytocin and dopamine in trust-based learning in mice"

## Mechanism

**How It Works**:
1. **Dopamine** conveys reward prediction error information that shapes judgments about individuals likely to reciprocate
2. **Oxytocin** modulates this dopamine signal to enhance social trust learning
3. Both systems converge in key regions: hypothalamus, VTA, nucleus accumbens, striatum, mPFC, amygdala
4. They mutually modulate each other's action

**Neural Substrate**: vmPFC and ventral striatum form the core "reward circuit" with high-density reciprocal axonal connections
