# Oxytocin Enables Multi-Scale Reciprocity from Dyads to Networks

## Core Insight

Oxytocin promotes nonkin cooperation at **three distinct scales**: individual prosocial tendencies, dyadic relationship strengthening, and network structure facilitation. This multi-scale operation explains how humans transitioned from small-scale direct reciprocity to large-scale indirect reciprocity.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Comprehensive review of oxytocin research across multiple scales

**Three Levels of Operation**:

1. **Individual Level**
   - Increases prosocial tendencies
   - Enhances trustworthiness judgments
   - Promotes altruistic behaviors
   - Increases empathy

2. **Dyadic Level** (Direct Reciprocity)
   - Strengthens pair bonds
   - Facilitates trust in reciprocal relationships
   - Particularly effective for individuals with low baseline trust
   - Enables initiation of reciprocal exchanges

3. **Network Level** (Indirect Reciprocity)
   - Facilitates heterogeneous network formation
   - Enables reputation-based cooperation
   - Promotes indirect reciprocity across networks
   - Works with "punitive hubs" to amplify cooperation spread

**Key Finding**: Oxytocin is **evolutionarily ancient** and structurally preserved hypothalamic neuropeptide - this explains universality of reciprocity across cultures.

**Citation**: Han & Ma, "Oxytocin in Human Social Network Cooperation," Adaptive Human Behavior and Physiology
