## Core Insight

Real-world reciprocity integrates **multiple information sources** (personal experiences + reputational information + tolerance for errors) rather than relying on pure direct or indirect reciprocity. **Tolerant integrated reciprocity** sustains cooperation more robustly than either pure strategy, particularly in noisy environments with imperfect information and errors.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Computational evolutionary game theory model

**Key Results**:
- **Integrated reciprocity** combines:
  1. Personal experiences (direct reciprocity)
  2. Reputational information (indirect reciprocity)
  3. Tolerance for errors (forgiveness mechanism)

- **Performance**: Tolerant integrated reciprocity sustains cooperation more robustly than:
  - Pure direct reciprocity (tit-for-tat)
  - Pure indirect reciprocity (reputation-only)
  - Intolerant strategies

- **Mechanism**: Tolerance prevents **punishment spirals** from mistakes
- **Context**: Particularly effective in noisy environments (imperfect information, errors)

**Implication**: Rigid tit-for-tat fails in imperfect information contexts (real-world conditions)
