# Three-Tier Reputation Systems Outperform Binary Classifications

**Source**: "Gradual reputation dynamics evolve and sustain cooperation in indirect reciprocity", Yamamoto, Okada, Suzuki, PLOS ONE, August 8, 2025
**Document Type**: Research Paper (Peer-Reviewed)
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-19
**Session**: 2026-02-19 Reciprocity Evolutionary Social Mechanisms

## Core Insight

Reputation systems operate most effectively through **three distinct levels (good-neutral-bad)** rather than binary good/bad classifications. This "gradating" social norm features gradual reputation updates and **measured responses to justified defection**, sustaining higher cooperation rates than binary systems.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Scenario-based experiments + Evolutionary game theory analysis
**Sample Size**: 1,850 responses collected November 8, 2024
**Key Results**:
- **Cooperative behavior**: Consistently improved rating by one level
- **Defection**: Typically decreased reputation by one level
- **Critical exception**: "Defection against bad recipients was an exception, as their reputation remained unchanged"
- **High cooperation rates**: Sustained when strategy of cooperating with good/neutral (while defecting against bad) becomes dominant

**Replication**: Evolutionary game theory model confirmed stability
**Citation**: PLOS ONE, DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0329742

---

## Three-Tier Dynamics

### Level Transitions

```
GOOD → [defect] → NEUTRAL → [defect] → BAD
  ↑                  ↑                  ↑
  [cooperate]        [cooperate]        [cooperate]

```

**Key Properties**:
- **Gradual degradation**: Two defections required to move from good to bad
- **Gradual redemption**: Two cooperations required to move from bad to good
- **Forgiveness mechanism**: Built-in path to reputation recovery
- **Justified defection**: Defecting against bad actors doesn't harm reputation

---

## Why Three-Tier Outperforms Binary

### Binary System Problems
- **Too harsh**: One mistake drops you from good to bad
- **No forgiveness**: Difficult path to redemption
- **No nuance**: Can't distinguish between reformed defectors and consistent cooperators
- **Exploitable**: Alternating cooperate-defect can maintain "good" status

### Three-Tier Advantages
- **Buffer zone**: Neutral provides grace period for mistakes or learning
- **Proportionate response**: Severity of reputation change matches behavior frequency
- **Distinguishes patterns**: Consistent vs. occasional defection treated differently
- **Evolutionary stability**: Strategy of "cooperate with good/neutral, defect against bad" is stable equilibrium
