# Three-Tier Reputation Systems Sustain Cooperation Better Than Binary

**Source**: "Gradual reputation dynamics evolve and sustain cooperation in indirect reciprocity" (Yamamoto, Okada, Suzuki, PLOS ONE, August 8, 2025, DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0329742)
**Document Type**: Research Paper (Peer-Reviewed)
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-19
**Session**: 2026-02-19 Reciprocity Evolutionary Social Mechanisms

---

## Core Insight

Reputation systems operate through **three distinct levels—good, neutral, and bad**—rather than binary classifications (cooperator/defector). This "gradating" social norm enables **nuanced, measured responses** to justified defection and sustains higher cooperation rates than binary reputation models.

---

## Evidence & Methodology

**Study Design**:
- Scenario-based experiments: 1,850 responses collected (November 8, 2024)
- Evolutionary game theory analysis
- Integration of higher-order information and multi-value reputation systems
- Tested against binary reputation models

**Key Results**:
1. **Cooperative behavior consistently improved rating by one level** (bad → neutral → good)
2. **Defection typically decreased reputation by one level** (good → neutral → bad)
3. **Critical exception**: "Defection against bad recipients was an exception, as their reputation remained unchanged"
4. High cooperation rates sustained when strategy of cooperating with good and neutral individuals (while defecting against bad ones) becomes dominant

---

## The "Gradating" Social Norm

**Three-Tier Structure**:
- **Good reputation**: Consistent cooperator, deserves cooperation
- **Neutral reputation**: Unknown or mixed history, worthy of conditional cooperation
- **Bad reputation**: Consistent defector, justified target for defection

**Gradual Updates**:
- Movement between levels is **incremental** (one step at a time)
- Creates "second chances" and "probationary periods"
- Allows rehabilitation from bad reputation through consistent cooperation
- Prevents single mistakes from permanently destroying reputation

**Justified Defection Recognition**:
- Defecting against bad-reputation individuals does NOT harm your reputation
- Creates "buffer zone" distinguishing free-riding from justified punishment
- Enables conditional cooperation without reputation penalty for enforcing norms

---

## Why Three-Tier Beats Binary

**Binary System Problems**:
- Single defection permanently marks as "defector"
- No distinction between justified and unjustified defection
- No path to redemption (reputation rehabilitation impossible)
- Harsh punishment discourages forgiveness and second chances
- Noise/errors create permanent reputation damage

**Three-Tier Advantages**:
- **Forgiveness pathway**: Bad → Neutral → Good through consistent cooperation
- **Error tolerance**: Single mistake drops only one level, recoverable
- **Conditional cooperation**: Can defect against bad actors without penalty
- **Realistic modeling**: Matches human intuitive reputation judgments
- **Evolutionary stability**: Sustains cooperation without strict binary enforcement
