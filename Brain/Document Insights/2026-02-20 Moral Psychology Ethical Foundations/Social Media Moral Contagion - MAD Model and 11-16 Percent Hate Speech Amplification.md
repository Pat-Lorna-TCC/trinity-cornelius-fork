## Core Insight

Each additional **moral word** in political rhetoric increases hate speech from readers by **11-16%** - and out-group animosity increases news story spread by **67%**. The MAD (Motivation, Attention, Design) model explains why moral content spreads: group identity motivations, attention capture, and platform design amplify natural evolutionary tendencies toward moral norm enforcement.

Moral outrage expression is partially **socially learned** and self-perpetuating: social reinforcement for expressing outrage increases future outrage expression, creating a dopamine-reinforced escalation spiral.

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Annual Review of Psychology synthesis across multiple empirical studies; Twitter/political rhetoric analysis
**Key Quantitative Findings**:
- +1 moral word in political rhetoric → 11-16% increase in hate speech from readers
- Out-group animosity → 67% increase in news story spread
- Moral content creates self-reinforcing cycles: social reinforcement for outrage → more future outrage

**The MAD Model Components**:
1. **Motivation**: Group identity motivations drive moral content sharing (belonging, status signaling)
2. **Attention**: Moral violations capture and hold attention (evolutionarily salient)
3. **Design**: Platform algorithms amplify content that generates moral emotional responses

**Intervention Evidence**: Moral learning treatments combining intergroup research and learning psychology consistently reduced affective polarization between Democrats and Republicans (PNAS, 2025; Social Psychological and Personality Science, 2025) - indicating moral outrage is modifiable, not fixed.
