## Core Insight

Moral reframing works not by convincing people through better arguments, but by **affirming their own values first**, which reduces psychological reactance and enables openness to opposing views. The mechanism is self-affirmation and facilitation of *intuitive processing* - not deeper analytical processing. This is counterintuitive: effective cross-partisan persuasion goes through System 1 (intuition), not System 2 (reason).

The technique: frame political messages using the *audience's* moral values, not your own. This works because it removes the identity threat embedded in cross-partisan disagreement.

---

## Evidence

**Mechanism Study** (Communication Research, 2025 - Suh & Kim):
- Moral reframing enhances persuasion through self-affirmation and facilitation of intuitive processing
- NOT through deeper analytical processing
- People become more open to opposing views when their own values are affirmed first

**Durable Effects** (PNAS, 2025 - Hall, Solomon, Voelkel, Willer et al.):
- Multiple scalable treatments show durable reduction in partisan animosity over time
- Several treatments withstand follow-up testing (not just immediate effect)

**Educational Scale** (Journal of Moral Education, 2025 - Selterman et al.):
- Perspectives program teaches people about individual and group differences in moral concerns (using MFT)
- Significantly improved moral reframing accuracy in two studies
- Causal effects on affective polarization, intellectual humility, and communication skills

**Moral Learning Treatment** (Social Psychological and Personality Science, 2025 - Moran & Walther):
- Combines intergroup research and learning psychology
- Across three experiments, decreased affective polarization at individual and party levels

## Why This Resolves a Major Puzzle

Most cross-partisan persuasion attempts fail because they:
1. Present arguments in *the speaker's* moral language (e.g., liberal framing to conservative audiences)
2. Implicitly threaten the audience's identity (your values are wrong)
3. Activate System 2 deliberative processing... which then serves identity defense, not truth-seeking

Moral reframing succeeds by:
1. Presenting arguments in *the audience's* moral language
2. Affirming their values rather than challenging them
3. Activating System 1 intuitive processing in a non-threatening way
4. Reducing identity-protective cognition before any persuasion begins

**The Core Mechanism**: Self-affirmation temporarily reduces the need to defend identity, creating a psychological window for cross-foundation communication.
