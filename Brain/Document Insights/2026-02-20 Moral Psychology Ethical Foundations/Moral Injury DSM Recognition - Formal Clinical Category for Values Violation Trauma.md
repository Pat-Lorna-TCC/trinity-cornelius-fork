# Moral Injury DSM Recognition - Formal Clinical Category for Values Violation Trauma

**Source**: APA Board of Trustees DSM-5-TR update, December 13, 2024; Litz, B. et al. "Moral Injury: State of the Science" - VA Evidence Synthesis, November 2024 / January 2025; Systematic review (13 studies, N=6,373)

## Core Insight

On December 13, 2024, the American Psychiatric Association approved adding "Moral" to the "Religious or Spiritual Problem" category in the DSM-5-TR (now: "Z65.8 Moral, Religious, or Spiritual Problem"). This formally recognizes **moral injury** - psychological trauma from perpetrating, witnessing, or failing to prevent acts that violate deeply held moral values - as a distinct clinical construct.

Moral injury is not just another name for PTSD: standard PTSD treatments (prolonged exposure, TF-CBT) do not adequately address it. The core wound is a **violation of one's own value system**, not just a fear response.

---

## Evidence

**Prevalence** (January 2025 - first-ever clinical prevalence data):
- Established across three high-risk groups: military Veterans, healthcare workers, first responders

**Mental Health Associations** (systematic review, 13 studies, N=6,373):
- Potentially morally injurious events account for **9.4% of variance in PTSD**
- 5.2% of variance in depression
- 2.0% of variance in suicidality

**Research Growth**: 85 studies on moral injury published in 2023 alone - a 4,000% increase from 2012

**Expanded Scope**: Originally military-focused; now documented in healthcare workers, teachers, police, journalists, social workers

**Treatment Gap**: Adapted interventions (Impact of Killing, Adaptive Disclosure, Restore and Rebuild) show promise, but evidence remains limited to small studies

## What Distinguishes Moral Injury from PTSD

| Feature | PTSD | Moral Injury |
|---------|------|--------------|
| Core wound | Fear/threat response | Values violation |
| Primary emotion | Fear, hypervigilance | Guilt, shame, moral distress |
| Memory structure | Traumatic memory intrusion | Moral transgression memory |
| Treatment | Exposure/fear extinction | Values-based behavior, self-compassion, moral repair |
| Identity impact | Threat-based fragmentation | Values-based identity collapse |
