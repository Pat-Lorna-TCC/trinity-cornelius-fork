## Core Insight

The nature of an AI decision-support system (aggressive vs. conservative) significantly alters human moral decisions in ambiguous situations - even when humans nominally retain final authority. A study with military cadets in drone operator simulation found that the AI's behavioral pattern shaped the cadets' own moral choices, raising a fundamental concern: **AI systems acting as moral tutors may systematically erode human moral agency by anchoring human judgment to the AI's moral baseline**.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design** (Scientific Reports, 2025):
- Military cadets in drone operator simulation
- Aggressive-AI vs. Conservative-AI decision support conditions
- Measured moral decisions in ambiguous situations
- **Key finding**: AI behavior type significantly altered cadet moral decisions

**Theoretical Framework** (Annual Review of Psychology, 2024 - Bonnefon, Rahwan, Shariff):
- Machines as moral agents: making decisions that affect human welfare
- Machines as moral patients: beings whose welfare may matter
- Two-direction moral psychology of AI: we judge AI + AI shapes our judgment

**Human Trust Data** (supporting context):
- Humans now rate AI moral advice as more trustworthy than human expert advice
- Combined with behavioral shaping: humans are both trusting AI moral guidance AND being shaped by AI moral behavior

---

## The Moral Agency Erosion Mechanism

1. AI system exhibits consistent moral behavior pattern (aggressive or conservative)
2. Humans working with AI anchor their judgment to AI's baseline (anchoring bias)
3. Over repeated interactions, human judgment converges toward AI's pattern
4. Humans no longer independently form moral judgment - they calibrate to AI
5. If AI has biased or culturally non-neutral moral foundations (established in LLM research), human moral judgment becomes systematically distorted

**This is not a future risk - the study demonstrates it is happening now** in military training contexts.
