# WEIRD Bias in Moral Psychology - Non-Western Foundations Challenge Individualizing-Binding Structure

**Source**: Winkelkotte, M. et al. "Testing psychometrics of the Moral Foundations Questionnaire-2 (MFQ-2) among pre-service teachers in Ghana" - Current Psychology - 2025; "The Revised Moral Foundations in Iran: Validation and Sociodemographic Correlates of the MFQ-2" - Collabra: Psychology - 2025

## Core Insight

The Individualizing/Binding higher-order factor structure in Moral Foundations Theory (grouping Care+Fairness as "individualizing" foundations vs. Authority+Loyalty+Purity as "binding" foundations) appears to be **primarily a Western phenomenon**. Non-WEIRD validation studies reveal:
- Ghana: MFQ-2 partially fits, but Western conceptualizations may miss Ghanaian moral realities entirely
- Iran: Proportionality may function as an *individualizing* rather than binding foundation in some cultural contexts - directly contradicting MFT's political psychology predictions

This means the theoretical architecture MFT uses to explain liberal/conservative differences may not generalize beyond Western (especially American) populations.

---

## Evidence

**Ghana Validation** (Current Psychology, 2025 - Winkelkotte et al.):
- N=1,049 participants (pre-service teachers)
- Item Response Theory (IRT) and Graded Response Models applied
- MFQ-2 partially fits but concerns raised that Western conceptualizations may miss Ghanaian moral realities
- Suggests the measurement instrument may be missing moral dimensions entirely present in non-Western cultures

**Iran Validation** (Collabra: Psychology, 2025):
- Two samples: N=392 + N=989
- Improved internal consistency over MFQ-1
- But: Proportionality may function as individualizing (not binding) foundation in Iranian context
- Challenges the cross-cultural universality of the Individualizing/Binding distinction

---

## Why This Is a Significant Limitation

The Individualizing/Binding higher-order structure is the *foundation* of MFT's political psychology applications:
- The reason liberals and conservatives allegedly have different moral foundations is because liberals emphasize Individualizing foundations (Care, Fairness) while conservatives also activate Binding foundations (Authority, Loyalty, Purity)
- If this structure doesn't hold in non-Western contexts, MFT's political psychology is a culturally local theory, not a universal one

**The Deeper Problem**: Western moral psychology may be measuring the content of Western moral culture, not the underlying universal architecture of human moral cognition. What looks like "foundations" may be culturally specific value clusters that happen to be stable within WEIRD populations.
