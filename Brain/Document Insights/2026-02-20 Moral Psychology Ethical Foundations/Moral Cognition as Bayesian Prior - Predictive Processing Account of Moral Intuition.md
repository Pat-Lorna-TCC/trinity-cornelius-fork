## Hypothesis

A growing theoretical framework (not yet empirically dominant) treats moral intuitions as **Bayesian priors encoded in hierarchical generative models**. Under this framework:
- Moral intuitions are prior probabilities about the social/moral world
- Moral violations generate **high-precision prediction errors** - this explains the automaticity and emotional strength of moral intuitions
- The strength of a moral intuition = the precision weight assigned to that prior
- Sacred values = priors with near-infinite precision (any violation generates overwhelming prediction error)

**Implication**: Moral development = updating moral priors through accumulated evidence; moral rigidity = inability to update priors despite prediction errors.

---

## Rationale

**Why This Might Be True**:
- Explains why moral intuitions feel automatic and pre-deliberative (they are priors, not conclusions)
- Explains why moral violations feel viscerally wrong before reasoning (prediction error is fast, sub-cortical)
- Explains moral learning across the lifespan (prior updating through experience)
- Explains cultural variation (different learned priors) with universal mechanism (same Bayesian architecture)
- Consistent with active inference framework (Friston) applied to social cognition
- MoralNet project (CCN 2025) is beginning to decode moral intuition categories from neural data - would provide empirical substrate

**Confidence Level**: Medium - Strong theoretical consistency but limited direct empirical validation in 2024-2025

---

## Testable Predictions

**If this hypothesis is correct, we would expect**:
1. Moral intuitions should show Bayesian properties: prior probability × likelihood → posterior moral judgment
2. Individuals with high interoceptive precision should show stronger moral intuitions (interoception = prior signal quality)
3. Moral learning should follow Bayesian updating rates: stronger prior = slower updating from counter-evidence
4. Sacred values should be formally indistinguishable from high-precision priors: treatment that reduces prior precision should reduce sacred value intensity
5. Moral prediction errors should be detectable in neural data at specific latencies (before deliberative processing)

**Falsification Criteria**:
- If moral intuitions don't track prior probability estimates (e.g., unconditional rather than probabilistic)
- If interoceptive precision doesn't correlate with moral intuition strength
- If moral learning cannot be fit by Bayesian updating models

---

## Research Needed

**Critical Studies**:
- Direct computational modeling of moral judgment as Bayesian inference (Cushman Lab would be natural)
- MoralNet fMRI decoding extended to test prediction error timing (CCN 2025 project is preliminary step)
- Interoception-moral intuition correlation studies
- Formal comparison of Bayesian vs. rule-based fits to moral learning data

**Why This Hasn't Been Tested Adequately**:
- Active inference framework is relatively new (Friston, 2010s)
- Application to social/moral cognition is even newer
- Most moral psychology lacks computational sophistication needed for formal Bayesian modeling
