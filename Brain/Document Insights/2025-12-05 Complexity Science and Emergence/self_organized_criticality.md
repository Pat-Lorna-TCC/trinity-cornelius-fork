## Core Framework

**Self-organized criticality (SOC)** is a property of dynamical systems that have a critical point as an attractor. Systems effectively **tune themselves** as they evolve towards criticality, exhibiting:
- Spatial or temporal scale-invariance
- Power-law distributions
- Avalanche dynamics on all scales
- **No external fine-tuning required**

## Foundational Definition (Bak, Tang, Wiesenfeld 1987)

> "Self-organized criticality is a property of dynamical systems that have a critical point as an attractor. Their macroscopic behavior displays the spatial or temporal scale-invariance characteristic of the critical point of a phase transition, but without the need to tune control parameters to a precise value, because the system, effectively, tunes itself as it evolves towards criticality."

**Revolutionary Aspect**: Traditional phase transitions require precise parameter tuning (e.g., temperature exactly at 0°C for ice/water). SOC systems **spontaneously** achieve critical state.

## SOC Signatures

**1. Scale-Free Degree Distributions**:
- Power law connectivity: P(k) ~ k^(-γ)
- No characteristic scale
- Hub-dominated networks

**2. Small-World Properties**:
- High clustering
- Short path lengths
- Balance of local and global connectivity

**3. Power-Law Avalanches**:
- Events at all scales with power-law frequency
- Rare large events more common than expected
- "Black swans" are structural, not anomalies

**4. Long-Memory Correlations**:
- History matters
- Temporal dependencies across long timescales
- Non-Markovian dynamics

## Economics and Finance as SOC (2024)

**Market Dynamics**:
> "Self-Organised Criticality is the mechanism by which complex systems spontaneously settle close to a critical point, at the edge between stability and chaos, and characterized by fat-tailed fluctuations and long-memory correlations."

**Market SOC Characteristics**:
1. **Fat-tailed distributions**: Extreme events more common than Gaussian (black swans)
2. **Long-memory correlations**: Past affects future (momentum, mean reversion)
3. **Avalanche propagation**: Small shocks → large crashes possible

**Quote**:
> "This scenario may explain why insignificant perturbations can generate large disruptions, through the propagation of 'avalanches' across the system."

**Implication**: Market crashes are not aberrations - they're intrinsic to SOC dynamics

## Global Supply Chains at Criticality (2025)

**Vulnerability to Black Swan Events**:
Research on COVID-19 pandemic and Russia-Ukraine war disruptions

**Finding**: Global supply chains have **self-organized to critical state**
- Optimized for efficiency (not resilience)
- Small disruptions can cascade system-wide
- Power-law distribution of disruption impacts

**Mechanism**:
- Global optimization → tight coupling
- Tight coupling → critical state
- Critical state → avalanche vulnerability

**Policy Implication**: Efficiency-robustness trade-off
- Maximum efficiency = critical state = maximum fragility
- Resilience requires moving away from criticality (adding slack)
