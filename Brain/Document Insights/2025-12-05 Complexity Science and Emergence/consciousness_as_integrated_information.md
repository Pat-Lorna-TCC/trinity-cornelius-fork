## Core Framework

**Integrated Information Theory (IIT)** proposes consciousness is identical with integrated information (Φ). System is conscious when Φ > 0. Consciousness emerges from the fusion of causal powers of a physical system and is **causally autonomous** by exerting global-to-local constraint on components.

## Five Postulates of Consciousness (IIT)

1. **Existence**: Consciousness exists (phenomenology is real)
2. **Composition**: Consciousness is structured
3. **Information**: Consciousness is specific (differentiates states)
4. **Integration**: Consciousness is unified
5. **Exclusion**: Consciousness has definite borders

---

## Emergentist Interpretation (vs. Panpsychism)

**Key Insight**: IIT can be interpreted as emergentist rather than panpsychist

**Quote**:
> "Consciousness, as integrated information, emerges from the fusion of the causal powers of a physical system, and it is causally autonomous by exerting global-to-local constraining on the system's components."

**Implications**:
- Consciousness is emergent property, not fundamental substance
- Exhibits downward causation (macro consciousness → micro neural activity)
- Organization-dependent, not substrate-dependent
- Applicable to biological AND artificial systems meeting criteria

## Synergistic Global Workspace Discovery (2024)

**Major Finding**: Combined network science with information-theoretic notion of synergy to delineate a "synergistic global workspace"

**Gateway regions**: Default Mode Network (DMN)
- Gather synergistic information from specialized modules

**Broadcaster regions**: Executive Control Network
- Distribute integrated information

**Information Flow**:
Specialized modules → Gateway (integration) → Broadcasters (distribution)

**Complexity Signature**: Consciousness emerges from synergistic information integration across hierarchical network architecture
