## Core Framework

Self-organization occurs when components of a system interact to produce a global pattern or behavior **without a leader or external controller**. Complexity is characterized by interactions that can generate novel information not present in initial or boundary conditions.

## Key Implications

**No Central Controller Required**: Pattern formation emerges from local interactions alone

**Information Generation**: Complexity creates novel information not deducible from initial conditions (related to strong emergence)

**Universal Principle**: Same mechanism operates across vastly different substrates and scales
