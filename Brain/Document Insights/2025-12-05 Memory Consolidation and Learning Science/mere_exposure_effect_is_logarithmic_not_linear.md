# Mere Exposure Effect is Logarithmic Not Linear

**Source**: The Mere Exposure Effect | Simply Put Psych

## Core Insight

The mere exposure effect follows a **logarithmic curve**, not linear progression. The first few exposures are most potent in increasing liking and familiarity, with diminishing marginal returns as exposure count increases. An optimal threshold exists beyond which additional exposures provide minimal benefit or even cause fatigue.

---

## Evidence

**Foundational Research**: Robert Zajonc (1968)
**Definition**: Formation of positive affective reaction to repeated or single exposure to a stimulus, even in absence of awareness

**Key Finding**: Simply being exposed to something regularly is sufficient to increase liking - no reward or positive consequence needed

**Pattern**:
- **Early exposures** (1-5): Steep increase in liking
- **Middle exposures** (6-15): Moderate increase
- **Late exposures** (16+): Minimal increase, potential decline

**Optimal Threshold**: Sweet spot exists where familiarity converts to trust without fatigue

**Citation**: https://simplyputpsych.co.uk/psych-101-1/the-mere-exposure-effect-how-familiarity-shapes-our-preferences

## Logarithmic Curve Characteristics

**Mathematical Pattern**:
```
Liking Score = log(exposures) × effectiveness

Exposure 1 → 2: Large jump
Exposure 2 → 3: Moderate jump
Exposure 3 → 4: Smaller jump
Exposure 10 → 11: Minimal jump
Exposure 20 → 21: Negligible or negative
```

**Implication**:
- First 3-5 exposures = 70% of total effect
- Exposures 6-11 = 25% of total effect
- Exposures 12+ = 5% of total effect (or negative if fatigue)

---

## Validation of 11-Touch Rule with Important Caveat

**Why ~11 touches makes sense**:
- **NOT** because 11th exposure is magical
- **BUT** because ~11 is where logarithmic curve flattens (diminishing returns)
- Represents empirically observed "good enough" threshold
- Beyond this, additional touches yield minimal additional benefit

**Critical Refinement**:
- **Quality of first 5 touches matters most** (70% of effect)
- Touches 6-11 are reinforcement, not primary drivers
- Going beyond 11 provides little additional benefit

**Strategic Implication**:
Invest heavily in making first 5 exposures high-quality, memorable, distinctive. Later touches can be lighter reinforcement.

---

## Mechanism: Why Logarithmic?

**Psychological Explanation**:

1. **Novelty Detection**: Brain prioritizes novel stimuli; familiarity reduces novelty signal
2. **Information Gain**: First exposure = maximum information; subsequent exposures add less
3. **Perceptual Fluency**: Processing ease increases rapidly at first, then plateaus
4. **Uncertainty Reduction**: Uncertainty drops steeply early, then levels off

**Neurological Basis**:
- Dopamine response to novelty (high on first exposure, decreases logarithmically)
- Pattern recognition (rapid early, asymptotic learning curve)
- Synaptic strengthening (LTP shows diminishing returns per additional trial)

---

## Excessive Exposure Warning

**Overexposure Effects**:
- Customer fatigue sets in beyond optimal threshold
- Reputation can decline with excessive repetition
- Irritation replaces positive affect
- Diminishing returns become negative returns

**Optimal Range**:
Research suggests 8-15 exposures hits sweet spot for most contexts
- Below 8: Insufficient familiarity
- 8-15: Optimal familiarity → trust conversion
- Above 15: Risk of fatigue

**Caveat**: Varies by content quality, spacing, and variation
