## Core Insight

Spaced repetition research has identified optimal intervals for memory consolidation: **Day 1, Day 7, Day 16, Day 35** after initial learning. These intervals align with natural memory decay patterns and long-term potentiation (LTP) consolidation windows, providing a neurological foundation for content distribution strategies.

---

## Evidence

**Recommended Optimal Intervals**:
1. **1st repetition**: 1 day after initial learning
2. **2nd repetition**: 7 days after initial learning
3. **3rd repetition**: 16 days after initial learning
4. **4th repetition**: 35 days after initial learning

**Neurological Basis**:
- **1-2 days**: Allows initial LTP to occur, strengthens synaptic connections
- **7 days**: Critical threshold before abrupt memory loss begins
- **16 days**: Reinforces before significant decay
- **35 days**: Final reinforcement before 90-day decay threshold

**Important Caveat**: Best interval varies by:
- Learner (individual memory capacity)
- Material difficulty (complex vs simple)
- Easily recalled information → wider intervals
- Difficult material → shorter intervals

## Validation of 11-Touch Rule with Optimal Timing

**Priestley's 11-Touch Rule**: ~11 exposures needed for recognition

**Optimized Version with Spacing Science**:
- Touch 1: Day 0 (initial exposure)
- Touch 2: Day 1 (1st repetition)
- Touch 3: Day 2
- Touch 4: Day 3
- Touch 5: Day 7 (2nd repetition - critical threshold)
- Touch 6: Day 9
- Touch 7: Day 11

## Why Spacing Beats Massing

**Research Finding**: Spaced training superior to massed training (short or no inter-trial intervals)

**Mechanism**: Without active recall, we lose **up to 70% of newly learned material within 24 hours** (Ebbinghaus)

**Spacing Benefits**:
- Memory consolidation
- Problem-solving enhancement
- Generalization to new situations
- Long-term retention

**At Different Interval Lengths**:

| Interval | Neurological Mechanism |
|----------|------------------------|
| Several minutes to ~1 hour | Successive trials reinforce peak kinase activity; trigger LTP of primed dendritic spines |
| ~1 hour | Allow replacement of inactivated receptors at stimulated spines (hippocampus) |
| ~1 hour or more | Align with peaks in transcription factor activity and gene expression |

## Integration with 90-Day Reset

**Critical Insight**: 35 days (4th optimal repetition) occurs **well before** 90-day reset threshold

**Strategic Implication**:
- 4 well-timed touches in 35 days > 11 random touches in 90 days
- Spacing science suggests quality of timing matters more than raw quantity
- After Day 35, maintenance touches every 2-4 weeks prevent decay

**Revised Framework**:
```
Phase 1: INTENSIVE (Days 0-35)
├─ 4 optimally-spaced exposures
├─ Strong memory consolidation
└─ Recognition established

Phase 2: MAINTENANCE (Days 35-90)
├─ Touch every 14-21 days
├─ Prevent decay
└─ Maintain top-of-mind

Phase 3: RENEWAL (Before Day 90)
├─ Re-engagement content
├─ Reset the clock with value
└─ Avoid falling into 90+ day gap
```
