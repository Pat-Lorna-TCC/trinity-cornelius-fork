## Core Insight

Memory formation is not simply about strengthening connections - it's about **selective strengthening while simultaneously eliminating noise**. A groundbreaking November 2025 PNAS study revealed that by representing each synapse as a product of two factors, the brain automatically removes inappropriate connections, tunes appropriate ones, and maintains homeostatic balance.

## Mechanism

**Two-Factor Synaptic Model**:
- Each synapse = Product of two factors (not single strength value)
- This representation enables simultaneous:
  - **Noise removal** (inappropriate connections weakened/eliminated)
  - **Signal enhancement** (appropriate connections strengthened)
  - **Homeostatic scaling** (each neuron's total input balanced)

**Implication**: The brain optimizes for **signal-to-noise ratio**, not just signal strength.

---

## Practical Implications

**For Content Strategy**:
Quality of attention during exposure matters as much as quantity. The brain actively filters noise - only meaningful, contextually appropriate exposures consolidate into memory.

**For Learning Design**:
- Focused, distraction-free learning > high-volume distracted exposure
- Context matters - content that fits existing schema strengthens, noise gets pruned
- Supports spacing over massing (allows pruning between sessions)

**For Trust Formation**:
Explains why 11 touches need to be **contextually consistent**. Contradictory or random messages get pruned as noise rather than consolidated as pattern.
