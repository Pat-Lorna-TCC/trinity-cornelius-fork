# Flow States Show Enhanced DMN-ECN Connectivity, Not Simple Suppression

**Source**: Barnett, K., & Vasiu, F. (2026). Enhanced functional connectivity between the default mode network and executive control network during flow states may facilitate creativity and emotional regulation, and may improve health outcomes. *Frontiers in Behavioral Neuroscience*, 19, 1690499.

**Document Type**: Meta-analysis (9 neuroimaging studies, 2014-2024)

**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)

**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-19

**Session**: 2026-02-19 Flow Decision-Making Selflessness

## Core Insight

Flow states are characterized by **increased functional connectivity between normally anti-correlated networks** (DMN and ECN), not simple suppression of the Default Mode Network. This creates "a brain state where task-positive networks are highly engaged and even working cooperatively with normally task-negative networks," enabling **simultaneous spontaneous ideation and goal-directed processing**.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Meta-analysis using fMRI, EEG, and NIRS

**Sample**: 9 studies (2014-2024)

**Key Results**:
- "Reduced self-referential processing in the DMN and increased cognitive control via the ECN"
- **Critical finding**: "Increased functional connectivity between networks often considered anti-correlated (e.g., DMN and ECN)" during flow
- Salience network (right anterior insula) mediates switching between exploratory and focused modes
- "Increased functional coupling with the DLPFC (executive regions) during flow" alongside "decreased coupling with the ventral striatum"
- Replication study (N=41) provided "decisive replication evidence for both canonical quadratic flow effects"

**Replication**: Yes - decisive evidence

**Citation**: https://doi.org/10.3389/fnbeh.2025.1690499

## Paradigm Shift

**Old Model (Anticorrelation)**: DMN and CEN are mutually exclusive - one must suppress the other

**New Model (Cooperative Integration)**: During flow, DMN and ECN work together in enhanced connectivity while maintaining reduced self-referential processing

**Implication for Decision-Making**: Flow may enhance decision quality through **network integration** (combining intuitive and analytical processes) rather than through suppression of one system. This suggests flow creates optimal conditions for decisions requiring both spontaneous insight and goal-directed analysis.
