# Meditation Enhances Inhibition Accuracy: Meta-Analysis of 111 RCTs Shows g=0.64 Effect

**Source**: Zainal, N. H., & Newman, M. G. (2023). Mindfulness enhances cognitive functioning: A meta-analysis of 111 randomized controlled trials. *Health Psychology Review*, 18(2), 369-395.

**Document Type**: Meta-analysis

**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor agent)

**Extraction Date**: 2026-02-19

**Session**: 2026-02-19 Flow Decision-Making Selflessness

---

## Core Insight

Mindfulness meditation produces **moderate-to-large improvements in inhibition accuracy** (g=0.643) and **moderate improvements in executive attention** (g=0.583) based on rigorous meta-analysis of 111 RCTs with 9,538 participants. These cognitive capacities are directly relevant to resisting automatic biased responses.

---

## Evidence

**Study Design**: Meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials

**Sample Size**: 111 RCTs, 9,538 total participants

**Study Quality**:
- **Publication bias**: p-uniform tests found "no publication bias observed for all cognitive subdomains examined"
- **Inter-rater agreement**: ICC = .960-1.000 (nearly perfect)
- **Treatment fidelity**: 64-65% of studies reported fidelity checks

**Effect Sizes (vs. waitlist/no-treatment)**:
- **Global cognition**: g = 0.583 (moderate)
- **Executive attention**: Significant improvement (g = 0.583)
- **Inhibition accuracy**: g = 0.643 (moderate-large) ← **KEY FINDING**
- **Shifting accuracy**: Significant improvement
- **Sustained attention**: g = 0.367 (small-moderate)
- **Working memory accuracy**: Significant improvement

**Non-Significant Outcomes**:
- Episodic memory
- Verbal fluency
- Processing speed
- Cognitive errors (surprisingly)

**Replication**: High-quality meta-analysis with no publication bias

**Citation**: https://doi.org/10.1080/17437199.2023.2248222

## Implications for Bias Reduction

**Inhibition Accuracy (g=0.64)** is the cognitive capacity to:
- Suppress automatic responses
- Resist prepotent (habitual) actions
- Override initial impulses
- Control attentional capture

**Direct Relevance to Cognitive Biases**:
- **Confirmation Bias**: Ability to inhibit seeking confirming evidence
- **Anchoring Bias**: Suppress automatic adjustment from initial anchor
- **Availability Bias**: Resist attentional capture by salient examples
- **Affect Heuristic**: Override emotional first impressions

**Mechanism**: Mindfulness-cultivated selflessness (reduced self-referential thinking) may help practitioners **suppress automatic biased responses** through enhanced inhibitory control.

## Integration with Flow States

**Shared Mechanism**:
- Both meditation and flow reduce self-referential processing
- Both involve attentional control enhancement
- Both show DMN modulation

**Hypothesis**: Flow states may leverage similar inhibitory mechanisms, enabling:
- Suppression of identity-protective biases
- Override of self-defensive responses
- Reduced confirmation seeking

**Critical Gap**: While meditation studies measure inhibition directly, flow studies don't yet assess specific bias metrics. This meta-analysis provides the empirical foundation for testing flow-bias connections.
