## Core Finding

Different breathwork practices produce **distinct and sometimes opposing autonomic profiles** - some activating sympathetic arousal (Wim Hof Method, high-ventilation breathwork) and others optimizing parasympathetic recovery (resonance/coherence breathing). These are not variations of the same thing but mechanistically different interventions with different applications.

---

## The Three Distinct Profiles

### 1. High-Ventilation Breathwork (Holotropic, Wim Hof, similar practices)

**Mechanism**: Cardiovascular sympathetic activation + haemodynamic alterations in cerebral perfusion

**Brain regions activated** (PLOS ONE, 2025):
- Left operculum/posterior insula (respiratory interoceptive representation)
- Right amygdala/anterior hippocampus (emotional memory processing)

**Phenomenology**:
- Altered states of consciousness including euphoria and bliss
- Intensity proportional to cardiovascular sympathetic activation
- Music substantially amplifies the altered state (the study included music + HVB)

**Distinct profile**: Sympathetic upregulation → arousal-based altered states

---

### 2. Resonance/Coherence Breathing (~6 breaths per minute)

**Mechanism**: Optimizes parasympathetic activity, vagal tone, baroreflex sensitivity, HRV

**Key findings** (Lindenwood, 2025):
- Even **single sessions of 2 minutes** of slow breathing produce increased HRV
- Nasal breathing delivers greater nitric oxide (NO), which positively affects:
  - Vasodilation
  - Oxygen uptake
  - Inflammation reduction
  - Autonomic regulation

**Distinct profile**: Parasympathetic optimization → physiological recovery, reduced arousal, enhanced vagal tone

---

### 3. Wim Hof Method (WHM) vs. Mindfulness

The Scientific Reports 2025 RCT (n=404, 29-day interventions) directly compared:

| Feature | Wim Hof Method (WHM) | Mindfulness Meditation |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Energy | Greater momentary improvements | - |
| Mental clarity | Greater improvements | - |
| Stress handling | Greater short-term improvements | - |
| Primary mechanism | Sympathetic upregulation with recovery | Parasympathetic stabilization |
| Sustained stability | - | Superior baseline stabilization |
| Autonomic profile | Acute arousal → recovery | Sustained calm optimization |

**Key insight**: WHM and mindfulness are COMPLEMENTARY, not competing, interventions. WHM enhances performance capacity (energy, clarity, stress response) while mindfulness enhances baseline stability. Neither is universally superior.

## Nasal vs. Mouth Breathing

The resonance breathing research highlights a clinically actionable finding:

**Nasal breathing** specifically delivers nitric oxide (NO) that:
- Acts as a vasodilator (improved blood flow)
- Enhances oxygen uptake (better O2 transfer)
- Has anti-inflammatory effects
- Improves autonomic regulation

**Mouth breathing** bypasses the nasal NO production - an important distinction for breathwork practitioners and clinical applications.
