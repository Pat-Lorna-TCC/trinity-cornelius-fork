## Core Framework

Neurophenomenology (NPh) proposes that **first-person subjective reports are scientifically valid constraints on third-person brain measurements** - not just anecdotes to explain away, but primary data that should shape what neural measures we take and how we interpret them.

The 2025 papers establish three domains:

| Domain | Content | Method |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **NPh1 - Phenomenological** | First-person experience structure | Microphenomenological interviews, experience sampling |
| **NPh2 - Computational** | Mathematical bridge between experience and brain | Formal models encoding phenomenology in computable terms |
| **NPh3 - Behavioral/Physiological** | Third-person measurements | fMRI, EEG, behavioral metrics |

**The innovation**: NPh2 provides a formal mathematical bridge - not just correlation but formal encoding of phenomenological structure that constrains neural model development.

---

## The Thick-Thin Methodological Spectrum

**Lutz et al. (2025)** propose a spectrum:

**Thick methods** (rich phenomenological description):
- Microphenomenological interviews (30-60 minute detailed exploration of a 3-5 minute experience)
- Trained practitioners report subtle shifts in consciousness with expert precision
- Enables discovery of previously undescribed experiential features
- High validity, low throughput

**Thin methods** (structured, scalable):
- Experience sampling questionnaires
- Phenomenological rating scales
- Physiological correlates
- Can be used during neuroimaging, in large samples
- Lower richness, higher reliability and scalability

**The strategic use**: Thick methods for discovery and validation; thin methods for hypothesis testing and clinical correlation.

---

## What This Enables

1. **Constraint of neural models**: Rather than fitting neural data post-hoc to phenomenology, NPh2 models encode the logical structure of experience and test specific mechanistic predictions

2. **Tracking of dynamic trajectories**: Experience sampling during retreats reveals how meditation states evolve over days - not just snapshots of "meditating vs. rest" but the full dynamic arc

3. **Clinical translation**: Standardized phenomenological tools allow comparison across labs and patient populations - essential for MBCT and other clinical meditation programs

4. **Discovery of previously unmeasured states**: Microphenomenology has revealed experiential features of meditation (micro-events during attention, transitions between states) that were not anticipated from behavioral measures alone

## Why This Matters Philosophically

The 2025 neurophenomenology papers make an important epistemological claim: **contemplative traditions have been conducting rigorous first-person investigation for millennia, and this body of knowledge is scientifically relevant**.

The Buddhist insistence on first-person methods as a source of knowledge about mind is validated - but the validation comes with a method: the first-person data must be:
- Explicitly trained (practitioners develop refined observational capacity)
- Cross-validated (multiple practitioners reporting the same structures)
- Formally encoded (NPh2 models)
- Correlated with neural measurements

This is not "any subjective experience counts" - it is "trained, refined, cross-validated first-person observation is primary data."

---

## The Hard Problem Reframed

Standard neuroscience implicitly assumes the third-person perspective is the only legitimate scientific perspective. NPh explicitly challenges this:

- Third-person measures alone cannot resolve the hard problem of consciousness (Chalmers)
- NPh proposes that first-person methods, properly constrained, can constrain the space of acceptable solutions
- Not a solution to the hard problem but a methodology that takes both sides of the brain-experience relationship seriously
