## The Gap

The contemplative neuroscience field lacks:

1. **Individual biomarkers predicting adverse effect risk** - who will experience harm from intensive practice
2. **Dose-response curves for different meditation styles** - how much of which practice type produces which effects
3. **Longitudinal studies tracking practitioners over decades** - how the brain changes over the full arc of practice
4. **Cross-cultural comparative studies** - whether neural effects of practice differ across contemplative traditions
5. **Mechanisms of the gradual jhana progression** - studies focus on individual jhana states in isolation; the transitions are unmapped
6. **Clinical translation of opioid-independent analgesia** - confirming the mechanism but not implementing it at scale

---

## Why This Matters

The adverse effects finding (60% prevalence, Van Dam et al. 2025) creates an urgent practical need for individual risk prediction. Currently:

- No validated biomarker battery for screening meditation candidates
- No dose-response model for predicting when practice transitions from beneficial to harmful
- No protocol for titrating practice intensity based on individual response
- Clinicians recommending meditation cannot assess individual risk

**The precision medicine analogy**: Just as chemotherapy requires individualized dosing and continuous monitoring, intensive meditation may require individualized "prescriptions" based on:
- Baseline DMN connectivity (higher = more risk of destabilization from rapid suppression)
- Trauma history (more = intensive practice may surface unprocessed material)
- Baseline HRV and autonomic profile (lower = less resilience for intensive practice)
- Prior psychiatric history (episodes = higher adverse effect risk)

---

## What We Know (Related Research)

**Domain A - Adverse effects (new)**:
- 60% prevalence; risk factors identified (prior mental health, trauma, intensive retreats)
- But: no predictive biomarker models yet

**Domain B - Practice effects (established)**:
- Long-term meditators show altered DMN, enhanced interoception, state-to-trait shifts
- But: no longitudinal mapping of the transition trajectory

**Domain C - Precision psychiatry (emerging)**:
- Machine learning predicts MBCT responders in social anxiety (JMIR 2025, n=191)
- Beginning of individualized meditation prescription

**Missing**: Connection between risk factors and individual neural profiles; predictive models that could prevent adverse effects

---

## Opportunities

**Immediate translatable research**:
1. Pre-practice resting-state fMRI as adverse effect predictor
2. Trauma screening + DMN connectivity combined risk score
3. Titration protocols for retreat intensity based on prior session response
4. Ecological momentary assessment during retreats for early adverse effect detection

**The precision contemplative medicine vision**: Matching specific practices (FA vs. OM vs. body scan vs. LKM), specific intensities (daily 10 min vs. 8-week MBCT vs. 10-day retreat), and specific teachers/contexts to individual neurological and psychological profiles.
