# Spaced Repetition Strengthens Retention

Spaced repetition is a learning technique in which material is reviewed at increasing intervals over time. Reviewing just as a memory begins to fade strengthens it more efficiently than repeated review in a single session.

The technique builds on the spacing effect, the observation that information is retained better when study sessions are distributed rather than massed together. Each successful recall after a delay reinforces the memory and allows the next review to be scheduled further out, so well-learned items are revisited rarely while difficult ones return often. The active effort of retrieval is central: trying to remember, rather than simply rereading, is what produces durable learning. Many study systems automate the scheduling, presenting each item at the moment review is expected to be most effective.

**Why this matters**: It offers an evidence-based way to retain knowledge long term while spending the least review effort.
