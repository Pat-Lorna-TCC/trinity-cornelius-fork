# Emergent Structure Beats Predefined Folders

Emergent structure is organization that develops from the actual relationships between notes, rather than from a folder hierarchy decided in advance. Categories form bottom-up as patterns appear, instead of being imposed top-down.

A predefined folder tree assumes one knows the right categories before the content exists, which often proves wrong as a collection grows and ideas cut across boundaries. Letting structure emerge means starting with links between individual notes and allowing themes to surface where many notes cluster. Those clusters can then be named and indexed after the fact. This approach tolerates ambiguity, since an idea that belongs to several themes simply links to several places rather than being forced into one folder. The structure stays adaptable, reshaping itself as understanding changes.

**Why this matters**: It frees a knowledge base from premature categorization, which is a common reason filing systems become rigid and unused.
