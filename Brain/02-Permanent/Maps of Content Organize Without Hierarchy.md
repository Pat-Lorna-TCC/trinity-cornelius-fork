# Maps of Content Organize Without Hierarchy

A Map of Content (MOC) is a note that gathers links to related notes on a theme, acting as a flexible index. It provides structure through curated links rather than by physically nesting files inside folders.

An MOC sits above a cluster of notes and offers an entry point into a topic, listing the notes that matter and briefly describing how they fit together. Because the organization lives in links, a single note can appear in several maps at once, and a map can be reshaped at any time without moving the underlying notes. This makes MOCs well suited to knowledge that does not fit a single tree. As a collection grows, maps can themselves be linked into higher-level maps, producing navigable layers of overview while leaving the notes free to connect in any direction.

**Why this matters**: It shows how a large note collection stays navigable without forcing every idea into one rigid category.
