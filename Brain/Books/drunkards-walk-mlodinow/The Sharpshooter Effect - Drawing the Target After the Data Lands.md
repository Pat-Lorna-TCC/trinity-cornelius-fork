# The Sharpshooter Effect - Drawing the Target After the Data Lands
**Source**: Leonard Mlodinow, *The Drunkard's Walk: How Randomness Rules Our Lives* (Pantheon, 2008) - Ch. 9
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/drunkards-walk-mlodinow

## Core Insight
Defining the boundary of a region or category AFTER observing where events fell guarantees an apparent concentration with no causal meaning. The name comes from the marksman who fires at a blank wall and then paints the bullseye around wherever the bullet happened to land - producing a "perfect shot" from pure chance. Any sufficiently flexible boundary, drawn after the fact, will enclose a "significant" cluster in random data.

## Mechanism / Why It Matters
The fallacy is letting the data choose the hypothesis and then testing the hypothesis on the same data. Significance tests assume the question was fixed BEFORE the observation; when the category, location, time window, or comparison is gerrymandered around an observed peak, the statistical machinery is invalidated, because the peak is what selected the boundary.

This is the standard mechanism behind perceived disease clusters: residents notice several sick neighbors, then draw the "affected neighborhood" line precisely around them, and the resulting elevated rate looks alarming. But the line was an artifact of the illness, not an independent unit of analysis. The same trap appears whenever an analyst slices data into ever-finer subgroups until one shows an effect, or retro-fits a pattern boundary to a streak.

The discipline it demands is pre-registration in spirit: the unit, hypothesis, and boundary must be specified before the data are seen, or the "finding" must be re-tested on fresh, independent data. Without that, post-hoc boundaries manufacture meaning from noise.

## Evidence
- Cancer clusters: communities repeatedly identify an apparent cancer concentration by noticing sick individuals first and drawing the neighborhood boundary around them - the textbook post-hoc selection that converts random spatial clumping into a spurious causal "hotspot."
- Structurally equivalent to the V2-strike and California-tract clustering data (Ch. 9): random scatter always contains regions of local elevation, and the analyst who draws the box after seeing the scatter will always find one.
