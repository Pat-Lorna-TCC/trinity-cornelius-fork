# Availability Cascades and Probability Neglect Amplify Rare Risks

**Source**: Daniel Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2011) - Part II, "Availability, Emotion, and Risk"
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/thinking-fast-and-slow-kahneman

## Core Insight
A self-sustaining media-public-policy feedback loop - an "availability cascade" (Kuran-Sunstein) - can inflate a trivial risk into a national priority. It is powered by "probability neglect": the mind handles small probabilities as all-or-nothing, vividly imagining the numerator (the catastrophe) while ignoring the denominator (how rarely it happens).

## Mechanism / Why It Matters
Minor event -> media report -> public alarm -> the alarm itself becomes the story -> more coverage, accelerated by "availability entrepreneurs" who profit from the fear. Because probability neglect strips out the base rate, the loop has no internal brake proportional to actual danger. This is the structural reason resource allocation and public fear systematically detach from statistical risk.

## Evidence
- Love Canal and the 1989 Alar-on-apples scare (Meryl Streep's testimony); the net public-health effect of the Alar panic was likely NEGATIVE as people ate fewer apples.
- Terrorism as the paradigm case: in Israel, weekly terror deaths rarely approached traffic deaths, yet dominate fear and policy.
- Debate flag: Slovic vs Sunstein on whether experts or the public should set risk priorities - democratic legitimacy versus calibrated cost-benefit.
