# Cognitive Ease Conflates Truth Familiarity and Good Mood
**Source**: Daniel Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2011) - Part 1, The Associative Machine / Cognitive Ease
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/thinking-fast-and-slow-kahneman

## Core Insight
System 1 collapses font legibility, repetition, prior exposure, and mood into ONE undifferentiated "ease" signal. Because the inputs are interchangeable and untraceable to their source, fluency produced by ANY cause masquerades as truth. Strain - disfluency - is what wakes System 2 and triggers scrutiny.

## Mechanism / Why It Matters
The ease signal is a single readout with no tag for its origin, so the mind cannot tell whether a statement feels right because it is true, because it has been heard before, or because the reader is in a good mood. This guards against treating "it feels true" as evidence: fluency is a confound, not a signal. The corollary is actionable - deliberately inducing strain (harder presentation) recruits System 2 and cuts intuitive errors.

## Evidence
Repeating "the body temperature of a chicken is..." makes an arbitrary completion feel true - illusory truth via mere repetition. Rhyming aphorisms are judged more insightful than identical non-rhyming versions. Pronounceable stock tickers (KAR) outperform unpronounceable ones (PXG) in their first week of trading. A HARD-to-read font cut errors on the bat-and-ball Cognitive Reflection Test from roughly 90% to about 35% by inducing strain that woke System 2.
