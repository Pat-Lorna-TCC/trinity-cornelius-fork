# Attribute Substitution Runs on a Mental Shotgun and Intensity Matching
**Source**: Daniel Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2011) - Part 1, Answering an Easier Question
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)

## Core Insight
This DEEPENS Attribute Substitution by supplying the HOW the existing note lacks. System 1 answers a hard question by substituting an easier one, and the machinery that makes this possible is two-part: (a) the mental shotgun - System 1 computes far more than was asked - plus (b) intensity matching - it can map any intensity onto any other scale. Together they manufacture an off-the-shelf answer to a question you did not actually understand. This is the engine under most heuristics.

## Mechanism / Why It Matters
The shotgun produces an abundance of unrequested computations, one of which is an easy proxy for the hard target question. Intensity matching then translates that proxy's strength onto the scale the hard question demanded. The transferable error: a fluent, confident answer can be the output of this swap rather than of any engagement with the actual question - so the answer's quality is unrelated to the question's difficulty.

## Evidence
Rhyme judgment is slowed when spelling mismatches (VOTE/GOAT) - an unrequested spelling computation intrudes on a sound task, demonstrating the mental shotgun. People fluently translate "Julie read fluently at age 4" into a matching adult height (around 7 feet), a matching GPA, or a matching crime severity, and they agree with one another across a culture - demonstrating intensity matching across arbitrary scales.
