# Causal Base Rates Are Used Statistical Base Rates Are Ignored
**Source**: Daniel Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2011) - Part II, "Causes Trump Statistics"
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/thinking-fast-and-slow-kahneman

## Core Insight
The mind incorporates a base rate only when it can be read as a CAUSE acting on the individual case. A merely statistical base rate - a population fact with no causal story - is ignored. The corollary asymmetry: people generalize readily from a single surprising case yet refuse to particularize from a surprising statistic.

## Mechanism / Why It Matters
This DEEPENS the prior note that the brain only weighs available, causally legible facts: it specifies the exact filter. Numbers that imply a mechanism operating on the person ("Green drivers are reckless") get used; numbers that are inert background ("85% of cabs are Green") get discarded. The asymmetry explains why vivid anecdotes update beliefs but aggregate statistics leave them untouched.

## Evidence
- Cab problem: the identical 85/15 base rate is ignored when stated as a population fact ("85% of cabs are Green") but used near-correctly when stated causally ("Green cabs cause 85% of accidents").
- Nisbett-Borgida helping study: students taught the shocking statistic (only 4 of 15 helped a seizure victim) learned nothing and predicted the filmed individuals would help anyway, "quietly exempting" them. Students shown two ordinary non-helpers immediately inferred the low general rate.
- Kahneman: "Unwillingness to deduce the particular from the general was matched only by their willingness to infer the general from the particular."
