# Preferences Are Frame-Bound Not Reality-Bound

**Source**: Daniel Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2011) - Part IV, "Frames and Reality"
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/thinking-fast-and-slow-kahneman

## Core Insight
Because System 1 reacts to descriptions rather than to states of the world, logically equivalent formulations of the same problem systematically reverse preferences - including moral and life-or-death choices. There is often no underlying "true" preference being distorted; the preference is ABOUT the frame. The broader, more inclusive frame is usually the more rational one.

## Mechanism / Why It Matters
A frame is not a lens over a pre-existing preference - frequently it constitutes the preference, because System 1 has no access to the invariant state behind a description. This dissolves the notion of a stable utility function waiting to be measured and makes default-setting and description a form of power over choice.

## Evidence
"90% survival" vs "10% mortality": 84% of PHYSICIANS chose surgery under the survival framing versus 50% under the mortality framing - expertise is no defense. The Asian disease problem: risk-averse for "200 saved", risk-seeking for "400 die", same outcomes. Organ-donation opt-out vs opt-in: roughly 86-100% consent (Sweden, Austria) versus 4-12% (Denmark, Germany), driven by the default plus System-2 laziness. MPG illusion: 12->14 mpg saves more fuel than 30->40 mpg, since gallons-per-mile is the better frame.
