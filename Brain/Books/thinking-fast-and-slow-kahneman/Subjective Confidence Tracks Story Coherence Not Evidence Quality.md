# Subjective Confidence Tracks Story Coherence Not Evidence Quality
**Source**: Daniel Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2011) - Part 1, A Machine for Jumping to Conclusions
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/thinking-fast-and-slow-kahneman

## Core Insight
This DEEPENS WYSIATI by naming the engine beneath it: confidence is produced by the coherence and cognitive ease of the story System 1 builds, INDEPENDENT of the amount or quality of evidence. Knowing LESS can raise confidence, because sparse facts fit a clean pattern more easily. WYSIATI - "What You See Is All There Is" - means the mind builds the best story from available evidence and never debits for evidence it does not have. Confidence is a feeling, not a diagnostic.

## Mechanism / Why It Matters
The associative machine generates coherence by construction, so "the world makes less sense than it seems." Because the mind cannot represent absent information, missing evidence carries zero weight - and a story with fewer competing facts is easier to make seamless. This guards against the load-bearing error of treating felt confidence as a signal of accuracy. It is instead a readout of how smoothly the story assembled.

## Evidence
Tversky's one-sided-evidence study: participants who heard only ONE side of a legal case were MORE confident in their verdict than those who heard both sides, even though they knew they had heard only one side. The Bananas/Vomit demonstration: a single phrase cascades into an embodied, causally linked story the words never stated - "the coherence comes mostly from the way your mind works," not from the input.
