## Core Framework

**Vertical thinking** deepens an existing hole: it selects a promising approach and refines it with increasing precision. It is inherently selective - you commit to a direction and dig.

**Lateral thinking** digs holes in entirely different places: it deliberately abandons a working approach to explore unexpected alternatives. It is inherently generative - you multiply starting points before committing.

De Bono's key principle: "You cannot dig a hole in a different place by digging the same hole deeper." A wrong position simply gets more deeply entrenched by vertical effort.

---

## The Labeling Problem

Naming and categorizing objects or ideas too quickly imposes rigid frames that block lateral moves. Once you label something a "table" you stop seeing the alternative uses of a flat surface with legs. Lateral thinking requires temporarily suspending the label to see raw properties.

Similarly: **random idea generation** (literally tossing unrelated concepts - "paperclips" - at a stuck problem) can dislodge the groove of vertical thought and open new entry points.

---

## Soft vs Hard Thinking

- **Soft thinking** (floodlight mode): sees similarities, uses metaphor and analogy, embraces contradiction
- **Hard thinking** (spotlight mode): sees unique qualities and distinctions, applies logic, demands consistency

Vertical thinking uses mostly hard thinking. Lateral thinking oscillates between soft and hard - generate broadly (soft), then evaluate rigorously (hard).
