# Foer's Psychological Time Dilation - Novel Experience Stretches Subjective Time

**Source**: Russell, J.M. (2020). *A Brief Guide to Smart Thinking*, pp. 155-157, summarizing Foer, J. (2011). *Moonwalking with Einstein: The Art and Science of Remembering Everything*. Penguin Press.

## Core Principle

Subjective time is not a clock - it is a **function of memory density**. The more distinct memories an interval contains, the longer it feels in retrospect.

Foer: "Monotony breeds fast time. If you don't want your life to go too fast, you need to create new memories."

And more precisely: "Creating new memories stretches out psychological time, and lengthens our perception of our lives."

---

## The Mechanism

Memory encoding is triggered by **novel, surprising, or emotionally salient** events. Routine days generate few distinct memories because they contain no new input for the encoding system to flag as significant. In retrospect, a routine week compresses to almost nothing because there is almost nothing to replay.

Novel experiences - travel, new skills, unfamiliar situations - produce dense episodic memory. In retrospect, even a single novel week can feel longer than a month of routine.

**Key implication**: the feeling that "time is speeding up" as we age is in large part a memory artifact. Adult routines generate fewer novel memories than childhood experiences, which is why childhood summers feel endless in retrospect and adult years compress.

---

## Relationship to the Method of Loci

The mechanism is the same as the one exploited by memory palace techniques: visual-spatial and episodic encoding is rich and durable precisely because it processes novelty and spatial distinctiveness. The method of loci works by creating artificial novelty (bizarre images in familiar spaces) to generate encoding events that would not otherwise occur.

Psychological time dilation and the method of loci are two applications of the same underlying principle: **the richness of experienced time is the richness of its encoded episodes**.

---

## Practical Implication

If the goal is a subjectively longer life (not just a chronologically longer one), the lever is **increasing the rate of new encodable experience** - not adding years but adding novel episodes per year. This is structurally different from hedonic happiness maximization (which often favors comfort and routine).
