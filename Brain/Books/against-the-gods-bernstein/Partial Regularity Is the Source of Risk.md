# Partial Regularity Is the Source of Risk
**Source**: Peter L. Bernstein, *Against the Gods: The Remarkable Story of Risk* (Wiley, 1996) - Introduction (Leibniz to Jacob Bernoulli)
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/against-the-gods-bernstein

## Core Insight
Leibniz wrote to Jacob Bernoulli that "Nature has established patterns originating in the return of events, but only for the most part." Risk lives precisely in that qualifier. It exists in the gap between full determinism - where everything is predictable, so there is no risk - and pure chaos - where there are no patterns to exploit, so nothing can be measured.

## Mechanism / Why It Matters
This defines the exact condition under which probabilistic reasoning is both possible and necessary: events must recur often enough to be studied, but not so reliably that the future is certain. "Only for the most part" is the operating zone of all forecasting. It also yields a clean diagnostic: before forecasting a domain, ask whether it shows partial regularity. Fully determined domains need no probability; patternless ones admit none. Misjudging which regime you are in is a primary source of forecasting failure.

## Evidence
Bernstein quotes Leibniz's correspondence with Jacob Bernoulli directly (Introduction). The phrase "but only for the most part" (Latin: *ut plurimum*) is Leibniz's own hedge, and Bernstein uses it as the conceptual hinge for why a science of risk can exist at all - patterns are real but incomplete.
