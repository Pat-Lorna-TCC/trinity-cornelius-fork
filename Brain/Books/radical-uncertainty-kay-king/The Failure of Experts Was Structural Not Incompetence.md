# The Failure of Experts Was Structural Not Incompetence

**Source**: John Kay & Mervyn King, *Radical Uncertainty: Decision-Making Beyond the Numbers* (W. W. Norton, 2020) - Ch. 2
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/radical-uncertainty-kay-king

## Core Insight

That risk models, central-bank forecasters and intelligence agencies all failed to anticipate the 2007-08 crisis and misjudged the Iraq WMD question was NOT a failure of competence, diligence or honesty but a STRUCTURAL limit. These were unmeasurable, non-stationary situations that no model could have captured, however skilled its builders. This is why a probe can be landed on Mercury with precision while forecasting next week's weather or an election outcome remains intractable - the difference is in the problem, not the analyst.

## Mechanism / Why It Matters

The diagnosis matters because it dictates the remedy. If the failure were incompetence, the fix would be better experts and better models - the "fire the forecasters / re-spec the model" reflex. But if the failure is structural - rooted in non-stationarity, reflexivity and genuine unmeasurability - then a better model cannot exist, and demanding one simply reproduces the false confidence that caused the disaster. Recognising the structural ceiling redirects effort from chasing unattainable predictive precision toward robustness, resilience and the honest acknowledgement of what is unknowable. It also defends genuine experts against scapegoating for failing at an impossible task.

## Evidence

Kay & King: "The inability of experts to anticipate the crisis was not simply the result of incompetence, or wilful blindness, but reflected much deeper problems in understanding risk and uncertainty." Their contrast case - the tractability of celestial mechanics (landing a probe on Mercury) versus the intractability of weather and elections - illustrates that predictability is a property of the system, not of the predictor's skill. The argument is structural and conceptual, drawing on the post-2008 record and the Iraq intelligence failure as illustrations.
