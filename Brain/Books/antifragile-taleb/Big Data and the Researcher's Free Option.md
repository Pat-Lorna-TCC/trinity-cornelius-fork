# Big Data and the Researcher's Free Option

**Source**: Nassim Nicholas Taleb, *Antifragile: Things That Gain from Disorder* (Random House, 2012) — Chapter 24 ("Big Data and the Researcher's Option")
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/antifragile-taleb

## Core Insight
More data yields more *false* information faster than real: spurious correlations grow convexly (nonlinearly) with the number of variables, while genuine signal grows only concavely. The researcher who can choose which statistics to report and when to stop holds a free option — so big data can deliver reliable knowledge only *via negativa* (debunking, not confirming). "Knowledge must not have an agency problem."

## Mechanism / Why It Holds
In large datasets "large deviations are vastly more attributable to noise... than to information." With enough variables, some high correlation is near-certain by chance — 200 unrelated variables will almost always contain a ~30% spurious correlation. The career incentive to "find relationships" is an agency problem that selects for the spurious: "the researcher gets the upside, truth gets the downside."

## Evidence
Ioannidis: observational/computer studies are spurious "more than eight times out of ten." ~12 years after decoding the human genome, "not much of significance" had been found. Replication failures span psychology and economics. Source-tier credible-interpreter: the core evidence is the Ioannidis result Taleb relays. NOTE: this is a 2012, pre/early-replication-crisis framing; the *direction* has since been broadly corroborated, but the specific figures are ~2012-era and the replication crisis is now far more documented.
