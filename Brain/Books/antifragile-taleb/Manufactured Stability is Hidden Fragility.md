# Manufactured Stability is Hidden Fragility

**Source**: Nassim Nicholas Taleb, *Antifragile: Things That Gain from Disorder* (Random House, 2012) — "That Time Bomb Called Stability"; "The Second Step: Do (Small) Wars Save Lives?"; "Equilibrium, Not Again"; "The Secret Thirst for Chance"
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/antifragile-taleb

## Core Insight
Artificially suppressing volatility ("stability") does not remove risk; it *transfers* it from frequent visible small shocks into rare invisible catastrophic ones — while making the system appear safest exactly when it is most fragile. Decision rule: analyze the **second step** (delayed side effects), not just the immediate loss avoided. Corollary: for organic/complex systems, **equilibrium = death** — they are dissipative structures that exist *far from* equilibrium and need a baseline of volatility and information exchange just to persist, so the economists' equilibrium goal is a category error.

## Mechanism / Why It Holds
Volatility is information and a release valve; choking it lets flammable material / silent risks accumulate beneath a calm surface until they release as a Black Swan — the longer the imposed calm, the larger the eventual blowup. "Absence of fire lets highly flammable material accumulate."

## Evidence
- ~A century of relative, heavily-armed peace in Europe bred the complacency that produced the Great War (WWI).
- **Stuart Kauffman**: for an organic system, equilibrium happens only at death (the bathtub vortex stays "far from equilibrium").
- **Ilya Prigogine**: dissipative structures are formed and maintained through energy/matter exchange in permanent nonequilibrium.
