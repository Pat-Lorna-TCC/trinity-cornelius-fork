# Iatrogenics - The Hidden Harm of Intervention
**Source**: Nassim Nicholas Taleb, *Antifragile: Things That Gain from Disorder* (Random House, 2012) — Book III, "Intervention and Iatrogenics" / "First, Do No Harm"; Book VII, "First Principle of Iatrogenics" / "Burying the Evidence" / "Guilty or Innocent"
**Document Type**: Book

## Core Insight
Iatrogenics ("caused by the healer") is the hidden or delayed net harm an intervention inflicts in excess of its benefits. The benefit is visible and trumpeted; the harm is dispersed, delayed, and uncounted. The concept generalizes far beyond medicine - into economics, policy, urban planning, and editing. It carries a burden-of-proof asymmetry: for an unnatural *via positiva* intervention, the burden lies on the intervention, not the skeptic ("absence of evidence is not evidence of absence"), because the harm is small-and-visible up front but large-delayed-and-hidden.

## Mechanism / Why It Holds
Wherever there is intervention there is iatrogenics, because intervening denies a system's natural self-healing. It is compounded by the agency problem - the intervener's incentives (justify a salary, show action) diverge from the client's welfare - and by concealed, distributed harm that never registers as an "error." Nature has vastly more statistical significance than any study, so the non-natural must justify itself: "what humans and science do is flawed until proven otherwise."

## Evidence
Medical error kills 3-10x as many people as car accidents in the US (doctors' own low-end estimate, ~2012). Pre-penicillin medicine ran a net-negative balance sheet. George Washington's death (Dec 1799) hastened by bloodletting (5-9 pounds). Trans fat, thalidomide, and DES caused harm appearing ~three-quarters of a generation later. Statins: ~50 high-risk people treated for 5 years to avoid one cardiovascular event. 1940s-50s acne/tonsil/ringworm radiation → ~7% developed thyroid cancer 2-4 decades later. (Flag: the 3-10x figure is a 2012-era claim and likely revised since.)
