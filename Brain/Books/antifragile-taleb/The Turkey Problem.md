# The Turkey Problem

**Source**: Nassim Nicholas Taleb, *Antifragile: Things That Gain from Disorder* (Random House, 2012) — "The Great Turkey Problem"; "The Idea of Becoming a Non-Turkey"; "The Inverse Turkey Problem"
**Document Type**: Book
**Extracted By**: AI (document-insight-extractor, Book Mode)
**Extraction Date**: 2026-06-28
**Scope**: Books/antifragile-taleb

## Core Insight
An observer inside a fragile system reads a lengthening run of good outcomes as rising evidence of safety, when each "confirming" day actually increases hidden exposure; confidence peaks exactly when ruin is nearest. The root error is **mistaking absence of evidence (of harm) for evidence of absence**. Corollary — the *inverse turkey*: track records mislead in opposite directions, *underestimating* antifragile activities (the unseen rare event is good) and *overestimating* fragile ones (the unseen rare event is ruinous).

## Mechanism / Why It Holds
Induction from a one-sided data series is blind to the regime that generated it. The turkey, fed for a thousand days, holds maximal confidence in "the butcher loves me" right before Thanksgiving; the same event is a Black Swan "just for the turkey, not for the butcher." One becomes a "non-turkey" by detecting which domain one is in, rather than extrapolating the past.

## Evidence
Adapted from Bertrand Russell's inductivist chicken (a metaphor — the principle, not the bird, is load-bearing). *Inverse-turkey* illustrations: a wealth survey of Mexicans missing Carlos Slim badly underestimates total wealth (he exceeds the bottom 70-90 million combined); one episode — asbestos liabilities — bankrupted families of Lloyd's underwriters, wiping out income earned over generations.
