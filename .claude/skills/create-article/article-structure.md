# Article Structure Templates

## Standard Long-Form Article Template

```markdown
# [Article Title]

**[Subtitle or hook - one compelling sentence]**

---

## TL;DR / Key Takeaways

- [Takeaway 1 - most important insight]
- [Takeaway 2]
- [Takeaway 3]

---

## Introduction

[Opening hook - start with a provocative statement, surprising data, or relatable problem]

[Bridge to main topic - connect the hook to what readers will learn]

**You will learn:**
- [Learning objective 1]
- [Learning objective 2]
- [Learning objective 3]

---

## Section 1: [The Problem/Phenomenon]

[Define the core problem or phenomenon with precision]

[Support with research or data]

> "Direct quote from research or source" - Attribution

[Explain why this matters]

---

## Section 2: [The Mechanism/Explanation]

[Explain HOW it works, not just WHAT happens]

**The mechanism:**
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [Step 3]

[Use analogy to make abstract concept concrete]

---

## Section 3: [Real-World Applications/Examples]

[Case study or concrete example]

[Data or research supporting the example]

[What this demonstrates about the principle]

---

## Section 4: [Framework/Solution]

[Introduce actionable framework]

### [Framework Name]

| Component | Description | Application |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| [Element 1] | [What it is] | [How to use it] |
| [Element 2] | [What it is] | [How to use it] |
| [Element 3] | [What it is] | [How to use it] |

[Implementation guidance]

---

## Conclusion

[Synthesize key insights]

[Restate main thesis with new depth]

[Call to action or forward-looking statement]

---

## References & Further Reading

- [Source 1]
- [Source 2]
- [Source 3]

---

**Related concepts:**
- [[Related Permanent Note 1]]
- [[Related Permanent Note 2]]
- [[Related Permanent Note 3]]
```

## Formatting Standards

### Headers
- H1: Article title only
- H2: Major sections
- H3: Subsections within major sections
- H4: Rarely used, only for deep nesting

### Lists
- Use numbered lists for sequences, steps, or ranked items
- Use bullet points for unordered collections
- Keep list items parallel in structure

### Emphasis
- **Bold** for key terms (first occurrence)
- *Italics* for emphasis or book/paper titles
- `Code` for technical terms, commands, or literal values

### Quotes
- Use blockquotes for direct citations from research
- Always include attribution

### Tables
- Use for comparisons, frameworks, or structured data
- Keep tables simple - max 4-5 columns

### Links
- Use Obsidian wiki-links `[[Note Name]]` for internal references
- Use markdown links `[text](url)` for external sources

## Length Guidelines

| Article Type | Word Count | Sections |
|--------------|------------|----------|
| Deep Dive | 2,500-4,000 | 5-7 major sections |
| Research Exploration | 2,000-3,000 | 4-6 major sections |
| Framework Introduction | 1,500-2,500 | 4-5 major sections |
| Quick Analysis | 800-1,500 | 3-4 major sections |

## Section Patterns

### Opening Section Options

1. **The Provocative Statement**
   - Start with a counterintuitive claim
   - Support immediately with evidence
   - Promise to explain the mechanism

2. **The Problem Setup**
   - Describe a relatable problem
   - Show the scale or stakes
   - Hint at the solution coming

3. **The Question**
   - Pose a central question
   - Explain why it matters
   - Preview the exploration ahead

### Closing Section Options

1. **The Synthesis**
   - Pull together key threads
   - State the unified insight
   - Point to implications

2. **The Call to Action**
   - Summarize main points
   - Provide specific next steps
   - End with forward momentum

3. **The Open Loop**
   - Acknowledge what we still don't know
   - Point to future research
   - Invite reader reflection
